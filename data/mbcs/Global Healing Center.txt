History
Global Healing Center is an
[alternative medicine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-alternative-medicine/) website that primarily sells supplements, vitamins, and nutritional products aimed at improving health. The website was founded by Dr. Edward Group III, a chiropractor (not a medical doctor) who is best known for his [relationship to Alex Jones](https://jezebel.com/alex-jones-and-his-pill-pushing-chiropractor-are-here-t-1794383993) and his [Infowars program](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/infowars-alex-jones/). According to SimilarWeb, Global Healing Center is a popular website with an estimation of 300K monthly visitors. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The website is owned by Chiropractor Edward Group III and is funded by selling so-called health and nutrition products that are not always proven to be effective.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Global Healing Center is primarily a store selling nutritional and alternative medicine products. The website also features a
[blog](https://www.globalhealingcenter.com/natural-health/) that promotes pseudoscience to sell these products. For example, they promote [Foot Detox](https://www.globalhealingcenter.com/natural-health/foot-detox-know-facts-top-benefits-recipes/), which has [absolutely zero scientific evidence](https://www.health.harvard.edu/staying-healthy/the-dubious-practice-of-detox) to [support its effectiveness](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-detoxification/). Further, Global Healing Center is on [Quackwatch’s questionable organization’s list](https://www.quackwatch.org/04ConsumerEducation/nonrecorg.html). Finally, the website frequently promotes [anti-vaccination propaganda](https://globalhealing.com/collections/search?q=vaccine#/view:content) to sell natural remedies.
