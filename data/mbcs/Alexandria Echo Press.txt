History
The Alexandria Echo Press is a daily newspaper published in digital, print, and e-edition formats in Alexandria, Minnesota. The website echopress.com features local and national news, politics, sports, entertainment, business, classifieds, and health.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Alexandria Echo Press is owned by
[Forum Communications Company](https://www.forumcomm.com/brands-overview/), which is based in North Dakota and has additional locations across South Dakota, Minnesota, and Wisconsin. Advertising and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Alexandria Echo Press reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Alexandria, MN region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[A portion of 50th Avenue in Alexandria to close Aug. 15-18 for railroad project](https://www.echopress.com/news/local/a-portion-of-50th-avenue-in-alexandria-to-close-aug-15-18-for-railroad-project). Further, the website publishes national and international news from credible sources such as the [LA Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/). Finally, all information is sourced via first-person accounts or through credible media.
Editorially, the Alexandria Echo Press reasonably represents both sides. However, a review of columnists demonstrates more favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually and with right-leaning editorial bias.
