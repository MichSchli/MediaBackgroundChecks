History
[Dr. Larry Sabato](https://centerforpolitics.org/staff/larry-j-sabato/) is the founder and director of the University of Virginia’s Center for Politics, which states its [mission](https://centerforpolitics.org/about/) as to “educate and inspire our citizenry about practical politics and civic engagement through programs that are unique, compelling, and open-minded.” The University of Virginia Center for Politics, located in Charlottesville, Virginia, was founded in 1998 by Dr. Larry Sabato. According to their about page, they have established “annual conferences, publications, programs, and curricula to advance democracy worldwide and equip people to better understand politics and government.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The University of Virginia owns the Center for Politics. When it comes to the funding, they received donations from philanthropists
[Martha and Bruce Karsh, ](https://news.virginia.edu/content/martha-and-bruce-karsh-make-largest-gift-history-uva-law-school)who also frequently [donate](https://www.opensecrets.org/search?q=Bruce+Karsh&type=donors) to Democratic candidates and causes.
Analysis / Bias
UVA Center for Politics founder Dr. Sabato is critical of Former President
[Donald Trump](https://richmond.com/news/local/education/the-republican-party-of-virginia-isnt-happy-with-larry-sabatos-tweets/article_a8233973-d8f8-566e-ad2b-48f42b57dbb5.html), stating, “Trump, who governed on the edge of insanity for four long years, has gone over the edge. Yet millions of people and 90%+ of GOP members of Congress, still genuflect before this false god.”Further, he holds favorable views of current President [Joe Biden](https://richmond.com/news/local/education/the-republican-party-of-virginia-isnt-happy-with-larry-sabatos-tweets/article_a8233973-d8f8-566e-ad2b-48f42b57dbb5.html) such as “I’m watching President-elect @JoeBiden’s press conference. It’s so refreshingly NORMAL. After 4 years of the bizarre and the disturbing, we really are going to have a return to normalcy.”
The UVA Center for Politics publishes free weekly webinars and a podcast series under
[Sabato’s Crystal Ball](https://centerforpolitics.org/americavotes/), which tracks U.S. Senate, Gubernatorial, House of Representatives, and Presidental races. In review, UVA Center for Politics publishes articles with moderately emotionally loaded wording such as [“Abortion Battlefields in a Potentially Post-Roe Political World”](https://centerforpolitics.org/crystalball/articles/abortion-battlefields-in-a-potentially-post-roe-political-world/) and [“Redistricting and Competition in Congressional Elections.”](https://centerforpolitics.org/crystalball/articles/redistricting-and-competition-in-congressional-elections/) Articles are sourced adequately from credible sources such as [Alan Guttmacher Institute,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/alan-guttmacher-institute/) [Fayetteville Observer](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fayetteville-observer/), [Associated Press,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) [Slate](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/slate/), [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Politifact](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politifact/), [The USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), [ABC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/), and nrlc.org.
Editorially, most stories favor the left with positive reporting on the Democratic party, such as this
[Republican Sweep in Virginia Sets off Alarm Bells for Democrats](https://centerforpolitics.org/crystalball/articles/republican-sweep-in-virginia-sets-off-alarm-bells-for-democrats/) and negatively on Republicans [How Trump and Biden Voters View the Events of Jan. 6](https://centerforpolitics.org/crystalball/articles/how-trump-and-biden-voters-view-the-events-of-jan-6/). In general, the Center for Politics is fact-based and holds a strong left-center editorial bias.
