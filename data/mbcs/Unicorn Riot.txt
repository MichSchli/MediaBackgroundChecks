History
[Founded in 2015](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicorn_Riot), Unicorn Riot is a non-profit independent media source. According to their [about page](https://unicornriot.ninja/about-unicorn-riot/) “Our work is dedicated to exposing root causes of dynamic social and environmental issues through amplifying stories and exploring sustainable alternatives in today’s globalized world.”
Unicorn Riot is best described as a media collective that is non-hierarchical and makes decisions based on consensus.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Unicorn Riot is a 501(c)(3) non-profit that is funded through tax-deductible donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Unicorn Riot often embeds journalists into protests such as
[Black Lives Matters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/black-lives-matter/) and other civil issues and reports from the field and through livestreams. Unicorn Riot generally promotes protests that originate from the left such as livesteaming from the George Floyd protests and as of this writing they are covering protests to prevent the Trump administration from interfering in the election [Protesters Demand Elections Remain Untampered Across United States](https://unicornriot.ninja/2020/protesters-demand-elections-remain-untampered-across-united-states/). They also frequently expose Government abuse of the poor and homeless such as this [Minneapolis Prioritizing Park Sweeps Over Sheltering the Unhoused](https://unicornriot.ninja/2020/minneapolis-prioritizing-park-sweeps-over-sheltering-the-unhoused/).
From an editorial standpoint, their coverage and viewpoints align with the progressive left.
