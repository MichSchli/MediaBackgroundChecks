History
The Kalamazoo Gazette is a newspaper that is a part of the Michigan news portal (Mlive.com) that delivers content from nine websites/newspapers. The following are the sources for the MLive website:
[The Ann Arbor News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ann-arbor-news-mlive-com-bias/), [The Bay City Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bay-city-times-bias/), [The Flint Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/flint-journal-bias/), [The Grand Rapids Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/grand-rapids-press/), [Jackson Citizen Patriot](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/jackson-citizen-patriot-bias/), Kalamazoo Gazette, Muskegon Chronicle, The Saginaw News, and Advance Newspapers. [Read our profile on USA Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
MLive Media Group owns the Kalamazoo Gazette. MLive is a part of Advance Publications Inc., controlled by the Newhouse Family, which owns numerous media publications such as Conde Nast,
[Vanity Fair](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vanity-fair/), and the [New Yorker](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-yorker/). Advertising, sponsored content, and subscriptions generate revenue for the company.
Analysis / Bias
The Kalamazoo Gazette publishes local news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Incriminating statements by Portage murder suspect not allowed as evidence](https://www.mlive.com/news/kalamazoo/2023/01/incriminating-statements-by-portage-murder-suspect-not-allowed-as-evidence.html). National and international news is reported through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Advance publications.
Editorially, they appropriately label opinion content from a wide, but never extreme, and reasonably balanced perspective. The Kalamazoo Gazette is a reliable, low-biased, and factual source of information for Michigan News.
