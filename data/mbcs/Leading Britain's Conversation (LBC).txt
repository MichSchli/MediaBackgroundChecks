History
Leading Britain’s Conversation (LBC) is a broadcast radio station in London/UK providing news, talk, and information regarding issues related to the UK and the world since 1973. LBC also has a website that reports news using
[Sky News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/sky-news/) as a source. They were initially named the London Broadcast Company.

Funded by / Ownership
LBC is owned by British Media Company Global Media and Entertainment Ltd. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, LBC radio primarily broadcasts talk and phone-in type shows. It has several right-wing presenters, such as
[Iain Dale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iain_Dale), a conservative for parliament, and [Nick Ferrari](http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/nick-ferrari-accused-of-making-racist-bigoted-comment-to-muslim-caller-a6736686.html). The broadcasting standards commission has criticized him for encouraging racism against asylum seekers. They also had Katie Hopkins, a conservative provocateur, that had to resign over a controversial [tweet](https://www.theguardian.com/media/2017/may/26/katie-hopkins-leaves-lbc-radio-final-solution-tweet-manchester-attack). Her views are similar to American conservative political commentator Ann Coulter. Recently, conservative [Nigel Farage](http://www.lbc.co.uk/radio/presenters/nigel-farage/farage-challenged-caller-over-working-class-voters/) hosted a talk show on LBC. On the left, they have [James O’Brien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_O%27Brien_(journalist)), who describes himself as a “ [champagne socialist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Champagne_socialist).”
In 2009, radio host Jeni Barnett promoted
[anti-vaccination propaganda](https://scienceblogs.com/sciencepunk/2009/02/06/jeni-barnett-mmr-show-full-t) on the air and was [harshly criticized](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-anti-vaccination-anti-vax/) by the scientific community.
