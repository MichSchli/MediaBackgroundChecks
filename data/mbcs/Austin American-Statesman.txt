History
Founded in 1871, The
[Austin American-Statesman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austin_American-Statesman) is the major daily newspaper for Austin, the capital city of Texas. [Read our profile on USA Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Austin American-Statesman is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), publishing
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Austin American-Statesman publishes local news through reporters with minimal use of loaded emotional language:
[Fires set as Austin protests against police violence spread, scatter](https://www.statesman.com/news/20200530/fires-set-as-austin-protests-against-police-violence-spread-scatter). National and international news is republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other [USA Today Network](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) stories.
Editorially, the Austin American-Statesman has
[endorsed](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Democratic Presidential candidates more often than Republicans. However, they did not endorse a candidate during the last two elections. The news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
