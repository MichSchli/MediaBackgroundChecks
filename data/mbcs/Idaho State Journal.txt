History
[Founded in 1890](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idaho_State_Journal), The Idaho State Journal is a daily newspaper published in Pocatello, Idaho, United States, that serves southeast Idaho, including Bannock, Bear Lake, Bingham, Caribou, Franklin, Power, and Oneida counties. Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper is owned by
[Adams Publishing Group](https://www.poynter.org/business-work/2017/who-are-the-adams-family-and-why-are-they-buying-newspapers-by-the-dozen/), which owns over one hundred papers across the USA. Stephen Adams is the owner and [donates to Republicans](https://web.archive.org/web/20080724023422/http://www.madcowprod.com/billboardenvy.htm), raising 1 million dollars for George W. Bush in 2000. Revenue is derived from advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Idaho State Journal covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Local man dies after driving into Blackfoot Reservoir](https://www.idahostatejournal.com/news/local/local-man-dies-after-driving-into-blackfoot-reservoir/article_d153776b-ae26-53f9-acbb-ba8e98ec7b5f.html). Local news is sourced through quotes, interviews, and accounts, while National and world news comes directly from the [Associated Press](https://www.bozemandailychronicle.com/news/city/bozeman-continues-to-foster-midtown-development/article_51464c29-97fa-57b9-9535-05375f0853c8.html) and [PRNewswire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pr-newswire/).
Editorially, the Idaho State Journal provides a balance in opinions, with slightly more favoring the right. They also tend to endorse more
[Republican candidates](https://www.idahostatejournal.com/opinion/daily_editorial/isj-editorial-bair-is-a-proven-leader-who-deserves-to-be-re-elected/article_56eb2587-b6d3-5557-800f-49a49192dbbd.html) in state elections. They generally report the news factually and with a slight right-leaning bias.
