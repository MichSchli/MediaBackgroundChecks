History
The Harlan Enterprise is a newspaper published in Harlan, Kentucky. The paper covers local, national, crime, and sports news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Harlan Enterprise is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Harlan Enterprise primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Harlan County Fiscal Court audit released](https://www.harlanenterprise.net/2023/02/09/harlan-county-fiscal-court-audit-released/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Harlan Enterprise labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Harlan Enterprise is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
