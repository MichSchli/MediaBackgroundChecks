History
[According](https://popularresistance.org/aboutus/) to the Popular Resistance mission statement, they “seek to aid in bringing movements for peace, justice, economic fairness, and environmental protection together into an independent, nonviolent and diverse movement that can end the power of concentrated wealth, shift power to the people and put human needs before corporate greed.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The fiscal sponsor for Popular Resistance is the nonprofit The Alliance for Global Justice. The goal of the AfGJ is to build a strong, grassroots movement capable of challenging the economic and foreign policies of our government and corporations. Donations and a shop that sells merchandise generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Popular Resistance uses minimal loaded words and sources the information they are referencing. They often use credible sources, but occasionally they use sources that are considered far-left, such as
[World Socialists Website](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/world-socialist-web-site/) and [TruthDig](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/truthdig/).
Editorially, Popular Resistance holds a strong progressive bias, often promoting worker’s rights such as this
[Chicago Nabisco Workers Join Strike In Four US States](https://popularresistance.org/chicago-nabisco-workers-join-strike-in-four-us-states/). They also show concern for climate change and social justice and oppose militarism, [The Crimes Of The West In Afghanistan](https://popularresistance.org/the-crimes-of-the-west-in-afghanistan/).
