History
The Waynesboro Record-Herald is a newspaper that serves Waynesboro, Pennsylvania, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. therecordherald.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Waynesboro Record-Herald. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Waynesboro Record-Herald covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Waynesboro, Pennsylvania, area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[These accidents are increasingly fatal for older Americans, according to the CDC](https://www.therecordherald.com/story/news/health/2024/02/15/older-americans-pennsylvanians-fall-death-rate-increase-prevention-tips/72029943007/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Waynesboro Record-Herald does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[A ship without a captain, compass or rudder.](https://www.therecordherald.com/story/opinion/columns/2020/09/25/mangino-column-ship-without-captain-compass-or-rudder/42689365/) After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
