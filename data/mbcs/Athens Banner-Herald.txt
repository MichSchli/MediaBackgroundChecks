History
Athens Banner-Herald serves Athens, Georgia, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. Onlineathens.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Athens Banner-Herald. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Athens Banner-Herald covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Athens, Georgia area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[GBI searching for vehicle believed linked to the slayings of 2 in Barrow County](https://www.onlineathens.com/story/news/2022/03/08/winder-barrow-county-ga-gbi-vehicle-slayings-police/9423029002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Athens Banner-Herald does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented such as this that favors the left:
[Manatees? Water quality? Home rule? Which statewide issues will dominate 2022? | Our View](https://www.onlineathens.com/story/opinion/2022/01/09/manatees-water-quality-condo-regs-among-issues-2022-our-view/9119315002/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
