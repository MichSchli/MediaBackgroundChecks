History
Founded in 1963, WQAD – Moline News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated TV station based in Moline, Illinois. wqad.com is the website for WQAD that covers news, sports, and weather.
On June 29, 2015, the Gannett Company split in two, one specializing in print media and the other specializing in broadcast and digital media. The broadcasting company retained WQAD, which took the name
[TEGNA](https://www.tegna.com/). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WQAD – Moline News is owned and operated by TEGNA Inc., which operates 66 television stations in 54 markets. They state TEGNA’s purpose is to serve the greater good of our communities. Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WQAD – Moline News reports local news primarily through TV news videos and accompanying text. Local information is presented with minimally loaded wording:
[1 killed in 2-vehicle crash in Knox County Thursday evening](https://www.wqad.com/article/news/local/2-vehicle-crash-seminary-street-knox-county-us-route-34-fatal-head-on-collision/526-807aa97b-6247-4bc7-af4f-08f96677ead7). National and International news is provided by the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other TEGNA TV stations.
Editorially, WQAD – Moline News does not produce op-eds and reports on politics with minimal bias. Finally, the website features fact checks from TEGNA’s fact check service
[VERIFY Fact check.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/verify-fact-check-bias/) In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
