History
The La Porte County Herald-Dispatch is a daily newspaper published in Michigan City, Indiana. lpheraddispatch.com delivers local news, hometown politics, business news, local events, and sports.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by
[Paxton Media Group,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paxton_Media_Group) which owns several right-leaning newspapers across the USA, such as the [Elkhart Truth](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-elkhart-truth/). Revenue is derived from advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The La Porte County Herald-Dispatch reports national news by republishing articles from credible newsagents such as the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). They report local news through the use of journalists with minimal bias using neutral headlines such as [No injuries when bullet strikes door of SUV in eastern La Porte County](https://www.lpheralddispatch.com/news/local/no-injuries-when-bullet-strikes-door-of-suv-in-eastern-la-porte-county/article_370592b4-09f0-5fe1-b947-b11cb0106af9.html). All information is properly sourced.
Editorially, there is a reasonable balance between left and right-leaning opinions, with slightly more favoring the right such as this:
[Let us never forget our solemn pledge to veterans](https://www.lpheralddispatch.com/columnists/guest_columnists/let-us-never-forget-our-solemn-pledge-to-veterans/article_97f2a4df-451f-5751-b00a-f9e68a1ef321.html). In general, the paper is fact-based and holds a slight right-leaning editorial bias.
