History
Founded in 1880, The Loveland Reporter-Herald is a daily newspaper published in Berthoud, Colorado. It carries local, regional, national, and world news. The current editor is
[John Vahlenkamp](https://www.reporterherald.com/contact-us/). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Loveland Reporter-Herald is owned by MediaNews Group, which owns nearly 100 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is, in turn, owned by
[Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York Based Hedge Fund, which has been described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former Denver Post writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Revenue is derived through subscription fees and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Loveland Reporter-Herald covers local news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Man charged with attempted murder on police following pursuit to Loveland](https://www.reporterherald.com/2020/07/11/man-charged-with-attempted-murder-on-police-following-pursuit-to-loveland/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other MediaNews Group publications cover national and international news.
Editorially, the Reporter-Herald
[endorsed](https://www.reporterherald.com/2016/10/26/guest-opinion-reporter-herald-erred-on-endorsement/) Democratic Presidential Candidate Hillary Clinton in 2016. However, a review of editorials shows a reasonable balance with a slight advantage to the left. In general, they report news factually and with minimal left-leaning bias.
