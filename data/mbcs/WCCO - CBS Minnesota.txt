History
Founded in 1949, WCCO – CBS Minnesota is a CBS-owned-and-operated television station licensed to Minneapolis, Minnesota, United States. The associated website is Minnesota.cbslocal.com that features local news, sports, weather, and crime for the greater Minneapolis Metro and Twin Cities area.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WCCO – CBS Minnesota is a subsidiary of
[ViacomCBS](https://www.viacbs.com/), which National Amusements, Inc owns [.](https://www.linkedin.com/company/national-amusements) They are operated by the third-generation leadership of the Redstone family. The President of [National Amusements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Amusements) and chairwoman of the company is [Shari Ellin Redstone.](https://www.forbes.com/sites/dennisjaffe/2019/08/27/the-labors-of-shari-redstone-reclaiming-a-family-legacy/#55acc0a951bf) Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WCCO – CBS Minnesota website publishes sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and local news for the greater Minneapolis and Twin Cities region through
[CBSN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/). Local news articles and headlines use minimal emotionally loaded wording: [66 COVID Cases Reported At Albert Lea Schools So Far This Year](https://minnesota.cbslocal.com/2021/09/04/66-covid-cases-reported-at-albert-lea-schools-so-far-this-year/). A video accompanies most stories from the TV station. National news stories are published from other [CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/) affiliates as well as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, WCCO – CBS Minnesota does not publish op-eds; however, they do report on politics with slightly more stories favoring the left, such as this
[Car Dealers Applaud Biden’s EV Executive Order, But Say Infrastructure, Incentives Needed](https://minnesota.cbslocal.com/2021/08/05/biden-electric-vehicles-car-dealers-react/). In general, the news is reported factually and with a slight left-leaning bias when covering politics.
