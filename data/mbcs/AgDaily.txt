History
[Founded in 2003](https://whois.domaintools.com/agdaily.com) (domain registration), AgDaily is a news and opinion website covering farming and agriculture. According to their [contact page](https://www.agdaily.com/contact-us/), they are based in Michigan and state, “AGDAILY, an all-digital farming, and rural lifestyle publication are among the fastest-growing agricultural outlets in the United States.” The current managing editor is Ryan Tipps. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
AgDaily is vague regarding ownership, but it appears to be a part of the
[Carbon Media Group](https://www.carbonmedia.com/what-we-do/), which claims to operate hundreds of websites. Revenue is derived through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the website publishes news and information regarding agriculture from various writers and bloggers. The website features videos and breaks the news into categories by region and sections such as lifestyle, weather, crops, livestock, and technology. Articles and headlines typically rely on low emotional wording, such as this
[GreenPoint AG announce billion-dollar agronomy merger](https://www.agdaily.com/news/greenpoint-ag-billion-dollar-merger/). This story is properly sourced to the GreenPoint AG website. In another story, [Biological control agents can protect soybeans from sudden death](https://www.agdaily.com/crops/biological-control-agents-soybeans-sds/); there is proper sourcing to a scientific journal.
Editorially, AgDaily only reports on political issues related to agriculture and farming with minimal bias such as this
[Farm groups warn Congress of major ag inspection shortfall](https://www.agdaily.com/news/farm-congress-ag-inspections-shortfall/). Further, they [support the consensus](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/) on [climate change](https://www.agdaily.com/livestock/perspective-case-climate-collaboration/) and [GMOs](https://www.agdaily.com/livestock/perspective-case-climate-collaboration/) when it comes to science. Finally, they also [combat misinformation](https://www.agdaily.com/insights/food-science-babe-risk-based-approach-food-babes-misinformation/) from purveyors of pseudoscience such as the [Food Babe](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/food-babe/). In general, the news is reported factually and from a pro-science and low-biased perspective.
