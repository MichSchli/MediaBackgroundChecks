History
[Founded in 1953](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KTUU-TV), KTUU-TV is an NBC-affiliated television station licensed to Anchorage, Alaska, United States. The website KTUU.com is the official website of the TV station covering news about Anchorage and the surrounding areas. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KTUU-TV is owned by
[Gray Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Television), which operates 145 stations across the USA. Revenue is derived through sponsored content and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KTUU.com covers local news, crime, traffic, weather, and sports. Local news is produced through reporters with minimal bias:
[Flood risk on the Kuskokwim rises with ice jam upriver](https://www.ktuu.com/content/news/Flood-risk-on-the-Kuskokwim-rises-with-ice-jame-upriver-570087471.html). A video also accompanies many stories from the TV station. National and international news is primarily covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) as well as other Gray TV networks.
Editorially, KTUU.com does not publish op-eds; therefore, news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
