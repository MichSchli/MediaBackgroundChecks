History
Well+Good is a digital publication focusing on wellness and well-being. The website includes various content categories such as health, fitness, beauty, and lifestyle.
[According](https://www.wellandgood.com/about/) to their description, they are a “market leader in the wellness space for over a decade” and aim to “inform and empower you to live a well life, inside and out.” They are based in New York City, USA. [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Well+Good states that Well+Good LLC owns it. However, they lack transparency as they do not clearly disclose that the
[Leaf Group](https://www.leafgroup.com/) actually owns them. Revenue is generated through advertising, sponsored content, and merchandise sales.
Analysis / Bias
Well+Good focuses on wellness topics and maintains a neutral tone in its articles. The website covers a range of subjects, from health and fitness to beauty and lifestyle. However, we found some instances of the website promoting pseudoscience, such as astrology
[Each Day of the Week Is Ruled by a Planet in Astrology—Here’s How To Align Your Calendar With the Cosmos](https://www.wellandgood.com/planetary-days-week/). Further, some stories are product endorsements that can bought on the site, which may be biased: [The New Brooks Ghost Max Shoes Offer Max Cushion and Comfort—No Matter What Surface You’re Running On](https://www.wellandgood.com/brooks-ghost-max-review/).
Editorially, Well+Good holds negative views of
[former President Trump](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/donald-trump-republican-presidential-candidate-bias-profile/), like this [President-Elect Biden Is So Much More Than ‘The Guy Who Isn’t Trump](https://www.wellandgood.com/president-elect-biden/), and negatively on Conservatives in general [Here’s How Mike Pence’s Views and Policies Affect Your Well-Being](https://www.wellandgood.com/mike-pence-views/). Generally, Well+Good holds progressive left viewpoints and occasionally promotes pseudoscience.
