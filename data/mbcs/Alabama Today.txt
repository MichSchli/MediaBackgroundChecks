History

Funded by / Ownership
Alabama Today lacks transparency as they do not name ownership. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Alabama Today is a website that reports on news related to Alabama and offers an opinion section for guest writers. News reporting is often presented with minimal bias in wording such as this
[Ross Marchand: USPS must deliver greater transparency](https://altoday.com/archives/43443-ross-marchand-usps-must-deliver-greater-transparency). When it comes to sourcing information, they do not use hyperlinks, relying on quotes. This is unacceptable for an online publication.
A review of editorial positions reveals that more favor the right, such as this
[Chris Talgo: Joy to the world, Build Back Better’s demise has come](https://altoday.com/archives/43471-chris-talgo-joy-to-the-world-build-back-betters-demise-has-come), and [AG Steve Marshall fights to protect Alabama land from Biden administration ‘overreach](https://altoday.com/archives/43307-ag-steve-marshall-fights-to-protect-alabama-land-from-biden-administration-overreach).’ A review of their coverage of Covid-19 indicates they are pro-science, utilizing the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and guest writers. In general, they report news factually and hold fiscally conservative viewpoints.
