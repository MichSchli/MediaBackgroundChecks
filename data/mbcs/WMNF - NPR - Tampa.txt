History
WMNF – NPR – Tampa is an NPR FM radio station (88.5 FM) based in Tampa, Florida, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WMNF – NPR – Tampa is owned and broadcast by the Nathan B. Stubblefield Foundation. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WMNF – NPR – Tampa website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Florida seeks an end to the constitutional challenge over its congressional redistricting](https://www.wmnf.org/florida-seeks-end-to-constitutional-challenge-over-congressional-redistricting/). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[Understanding Climate Change and Its Impact on Florida](https://www.wmnf.org/wmnf_events/understanding-climate-change-and-its-impact-on-florida/). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
