History
Founded in 1925, The Acadiana Advocate is an American newspaper published in Acadiana, Louisiana. theadvocate.com/acadiana/ serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Acadiana Advocate is owned by
[Georges Media Group](https://georgesmediagroup.com/), which owns businesses in the New Orleans area and other media publications. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Acadiana Advocate covers local news with minimal bias, such as
[Lafayette National Guard commander relieved of duties has history of sexual complaints](https://www.theadvocate.com/acadiana/news/national-guards-scott-desormeaux-has-history-of-complaints/article_f8297d3a-8df9-11ed-84a4-e37cb1bf3d37.html). National and international are derived from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The Acadiana Advocate properly labels opinion content and covers both sides. However, a thorough review of the opinion pages reveals more favor the left, such as this
[Our Views: An iconic mix of Spanish moss and pollution for Louisiana](https://www.theadvocate.com/baton_rouge/opinion/our_views/our-views-iconic-mix-of-spanish-moss-pollution-at-capitol/article_4dccf830-9ce2-572e-ab6c-e72f3d1aeb43.html). In general, the news is reported factually with a slight left-leaning bias.
