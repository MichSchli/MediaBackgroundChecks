History
The Nevada Current is an online news website from Nevada. Their
[about](https://www.nevadacurrent.com/about/) statement reads, “Nevada Current is a nonprofit online source of political and policy news and commentary. “ [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, the Nevada Current is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The Nevada Current is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The Nevada Current publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from Nevada. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects the people of Nevada. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through moderate, loaded language
[Child poverty dropped to a record low last year. A new report shows how to keep it that way.](https://www.nevadacurrent.com/2023/03/07/child-poverty-dropped-to-a-record-low-last-year-a-new-report-shows-how-to-keep-it-that-way/)
They properly source, linking to credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[Assembly bill aims to strengthen advocacy for domestic violence victims](https://www.nevadacurrent.com/blog/assembly-bill-aims-to-strengthen-advocacy-for-domestic-violence-victims/). On the issues, the Nevada Current promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [House Republicans go to bat for tax cheaters](https://www.nevadacurrent.com/2023/01/25/house-republicans-go-to-bat-for-tax-cheaters/). The Nevada Current generally reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
