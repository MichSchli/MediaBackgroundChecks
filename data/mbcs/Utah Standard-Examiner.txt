History
The Utah Standard-Examiner is a daily morning newspaper published in Ogden, Utah. The Standard-Examiner was founded in 1888; it is available in print, online and mobile formats. Its features include local news, events from northern Utah communities, entertainment, political commentaries, and world news.

Funded by / Ownership
The Utah Standard-Examiner is owned by
[Ogden Newspapers Inc.,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ogden_Newspapers_Inc.) which publishes numerous small-market newspapers around the eastern half of the USA. Ogden has historically donated to Republican candidates. According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), since 1992, they have [donated 93% to Republicans](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000036358&cycle=2018). [Robert Nutting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Nutting) is the current CEO. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Utah Standard-Examiner covers local news through reporters with minimal bias in wording such as this
[Weber crews fight blazes across West, train for the worst here.](https://www.standard.net/news/local/2021/aug/28/weber-crews-fight-blazes-across-west-train-for-the-worst-here/) National and international news is republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, they have a history of endorsing more Republican candidates; for example, they endorsed Republican John McCain in 2008 and Mitt Romney in 2012. A review of op-eds slightly favors the right such as this
[Lowry: Newsom deserves to be recalled.](https://www.standard.net/opinion/national-commentary/2021/aug/28/lowry-newsom-deserves-to-be-recalled/) Generally, the news is reported factually and with a right-leaning editorial bias.
