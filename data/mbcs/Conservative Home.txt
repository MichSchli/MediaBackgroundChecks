History
Founded in 2005 by Tim Montgomerie, Conservative Home is a center-right political news and analysis blog.
[Conservative Home](https://www.conservativehome.com/about) describes itself as “Britain’s leading Conservative blog for news, comment, analysis, and campaigns,” and their stances as “We are independent of the Conservative Party but supportive of it.” The blog consists of sections including ToryDiary (news analysis of the Conservative Party), Columnists, Left Watch, MPs ETC, Think Tanks, Audio and Video. Paul Goodman is the editor of the blog. They also have a sister site called [Conservative Intelligence](https://www.conservativeintelligence.com/), which provides intelligence on the Conservative Party. [Mark Wallace](https://www.conservativehome.com/thetorydiary/2020/01/new-developments-at-conservativehome-as-we-build-on-the-success-of-another-record-breaking-year.html) is the Chief Executive of Conservative Home. [View our profile on UK Media and Government](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
According to the
[About page](https://www.conservativehome.com/about), Conservative Home is owned by Michael Ashcroft and co-founded by Stephan Shakespeare. Ashcroft is a former deputy chairman of the Conservative Party. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, articles and headlines contain emotional wording that typically favors the right such as this
[“Labour’s Tory sleaze accusations look hypocritical, and – even more interestingly – aren’t landing any blows.”](https://www.conservativehome.com/leftwatch/2021/04/labours-tory-sleaze-accusations-look-hypocritical-and-even-more-interestingly-arent-landing-any-blows.html) Editorially, the Conservative Home denigrates the left such as under the section called Left Watch where they publish articles such as: [“Labour’s hypocrisy over A Level results.”](https://www.conservativehome.com/leftwatch/2020/08/labours-hypocrisy-over-a-level-results.html) In general, the Conservative Home is Right-Center biased. When it comes to sourcing, they utilize credible sources such as parliament.uk, gov.uk, and [BBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/).
When reporting on scientific issues, they support the scientific consensus on climate change such as this
[Stanley Johnson: Next year’s climate change conference should take its lead on carbon pricing from this Agreement](https://www.conservativehome.com/platform/2020/12/stanley-johnson-next-years-climate-change-conference-should-take-its-lead-on-carbon-pricing-from-this-brexit-deal.html). Further, they report accurately on Covid-19 [The new Covid-19 vaccines: what we know so far](https://www.conservativehome.com/thetorydiary/2020/11/the-new-covid-19-vaccines-what-we-know-so-far.html).
