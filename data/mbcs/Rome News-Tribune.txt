History
The Rome News-Tribune is a newspaper based in Rome, Georgia, USA. The paper mainly focuses on local news and politics, sports, and business related to the Rome area.
[Read our profile on USA media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Rome News-Tribune is owned by
[Times-Journal Inc](https://www.editorandpublisher.com/stories/times-journal-inc-acquires-six-metro-atlanta-newspapers,240906). through the Brumby family. They publish around 20 papers in the Georgia and southeast markets. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Rome News-Tribune covers local news for the Rome area through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Rome Child Murder Verdict Upheld By Ga. Supreme Court](https://www.northwestgeorgianews.com/rome/news/local/rome-child-murder-verdict-upheld-by-ga-supreme-court/article_c401044c-c4fd-11ee-9816-03e4a51b9e55.html). The paper does not cover national and international news focusing on local and regional news related to Georgia.
Editorially, story selection, and op-eds are reasonably balanced and reflect both sides. Generally, the Rome News-Tribune is fact-based and presents a minimal bias in reporting.
