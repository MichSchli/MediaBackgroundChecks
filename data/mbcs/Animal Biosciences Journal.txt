History
Animal Biosciences Journal is a review journal published by Annual Reviews that covers Animal Bioscience. According to its
[about page](https://www.annualreviews.org/journal/animal), “The Annual Review of Animal Biosciences places a particular emphasis on biotechnology, genetics, genomics, and breeding, as well as veterinary medicine, especially veterinary pathobiology, infectious diseases and vaccine development, and conservation and zoo biology.”
Founded in 1932, Annual Reviews is a nonprofit academic publishing company based in San Mateo, California. Annuals Reviews
[publishes over 50 scientific journals](https://www.annualreviews.org/action/showPublications) of review covering life, physical, biomedical, and social sciences. According to its [about page](https://www.annualreviews.org/about/what-we-do) “Annual Reviews is a nonprofit publisher dedicated to synthesizing and integrating knowledge for the progress of science and the benefit of society.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
As a nonprofit organization, Annual Reviews relies on a combination of funding sources, including subscriptions and the
[S20 model](https://www.annualreviews.org/pb-assets/Pricing%20Pages%20assets/Annual_Reviews_Pricing_Catalog_2023_Digital-1660914973360.pdf), which funds open-access publishing.
Analysis / Bias
Animal Biosciences Journal is a review journal that publishes information on Animal Bioscience. The journal primarily publishes research such as this:
[Recent Advances in Enteric Methane Mitigation and the Long Road to Sustainable Ruminant Production](https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/abs/10.1146/annurev-animal-021022-024931).
Annual Reviews is an open-access/hybrid publisher of scientific journals. There are two types of Open Access Journals, legitimate and predatory, both of which charge the author for publication.
[Predatory journals](https://predatoryjournals.com/about/) often lack peer review and are generally considered not credible. Annual Reviews is considered a legitimate, open-access publisher as they do not charge authors and rely on the s2o method for funding.
