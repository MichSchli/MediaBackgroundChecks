History
[Founded in 1891](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Seattle_Times), The Seattle Times is a broadsheet newspaper from Seattle, Washington. The paper has received 10 Pulitzer Prizes. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by
[The Seattle Times Company,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Seattle_Times_Company) which also owns local newspapers in Walla Walla and Yakima. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Seattle Times publishes local, regional, national, and international news. Local and regional news is often in-depth and utilizes low biased wording:
[Woman shot in South Delridge neighborhood near West Seattle, police search for suspects](https://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/crime/woman-shot-in-south-delridge-neighborhood-near-west-seattle-police-search-for-suspects/). Information is always properly sourced. National and international news is provided through the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Seattle Times has
[endorsed](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Democratic Presidential candidates 70% of the time since 1980. However, since 2000, they have only endorsed Democratic candidates [such as](https://www.seattletimes.com/opinion/editorials/the-seattle-times-editorial-board-recommends-joe-biden-for-u-s-president/) Democratic President [Joe Biden](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/joe-biden-democratic-presidential-candidate-bias-profile/) in 2024. A review of Op-Eds reveals more favor for the left than the right.
