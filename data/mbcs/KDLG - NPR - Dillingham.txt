History
KDLG – NPR – Dillingham is an NPR AM radio station (670) based in Dillingham, Alaska, serving the Dillingham and the Alaska Bush area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KDLG – NPR – Dillingham is owned and broadcast by Dillingham Public School District. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [‘The most extreme of all the extremes’: What’s behind Bristol Bay’s record breaking fire season](https://www.kdlg.org/environment/2023-01-09/the-most-extreme-of-all-the-extremes-whats-behind-bristol-bays-record-breaking-fire-season). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content such as this
[‘After Sappho’ brings women in history to life to claim their stories](https://www.kdlg.org/as-heard-on-npr/2023-01-26/after-sappho-brings-women-in-history-to-life-to-claim-their-stories). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
