History
[Founded in 1868](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Courier-Journal), the Louisville Courier-Journal, locally called “The Courier” or “The C-J,” is the main newspaper for Louisville, Kentucky, United States. The C-J is a part of the [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) Network. The current editor is Mike Trautmann. The newspaper has won 10 Pulitzer Prizes in its history. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Louisville Courier-Journal is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Louisville Courier-Journal covers local news, sports, entertainment, and crime through reporters with minimal bias such as this:
[Kentucky coronavirus map: How many coronavirus cases are in Kentucky? Where are they?](https://www.courier-journal.com/story/news/2020/03/09/coronavirus-kentucky-how-many-cases-and-where-they-kentucky/5001636002/). National and international are covered through the USA Today Network and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Courier-Journal has endorsed
[Democratic Presidential candidates](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) for more than 40 years. Further, a review of op-eds indicates more favor a liberal perspective. In general, they report news factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
