History
Founded in 1977, Entrepreneur is a monthly magazine focused on launching a business, managing companies, and general financial news. The associated website is entrepreneur.com, which publishes similar information and news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Entrepreneur Media, Inc, owns Entrepreneur, and revenue is derived through advertising, merchandise, and subscription sales.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Entrepreneur publishes news and information on starting and managing businesses. Many stories involve interviewing business owners. There is minimal use of loaded emotional language such as how to use artificial intelligence to boost your small business, and this
[Parler co-founder and CEO fired from his own company](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/364899). All information is sourced through hyperlinking or first-person accounts of business owners and experts.
Entrepreneur does not publish op-eds; however, they report on politics when it relates to business. Political articles are also low biased and relatively neutral such as this
[4 Ways You Can Reduce Your Taxes Like President Trump](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/357145) and this [How Will the Biden Tax Plan Affect Your Small Business?](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/358695) In general, the news is reported factually and editorially; there is a slight right-leaning bias based on lower taxes and regulations for business.
