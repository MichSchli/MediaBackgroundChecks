History
Founded in 1981, WJAB – Huntsville is an FM radio station (90.9) based in Huntsville, Alabama, that broadcasts National Public Radio (NPR).
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WJAB – Huntsville is a service of the Alabama Agricultural and Mechanical University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WJAB – Huntsville website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [‘It could just sweep us away’: This school is on the front lines of climate change](https://www.wjab.org/2022-11-13/it-could-just-sweep-us-away-this-school-is-on-the-front-lines-of-climate-change). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content such as this
[Opinion: A life lesson from these midterm elections](https://www.wjab.org/2022-11-12/opinion-a-life-lesson-from-these-midterm-elections). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
