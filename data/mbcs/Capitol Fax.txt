History
[Capitol Fax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capitol_Fax) is a daily political newsletter and website covering Illinois politics. It was founded in 1993 by Rich Miller, the editor. “Miller is widely regarded as one of the [most knowledgeable](http://igpa.uillinois.edu/press/edgarseries-miller) and connected journalists in the Illinois State Capitol. His daily intelligence from inside state government and politics influences politics and policy throughout the state.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Capitol Fax is owned by Rich Miller, who is funded by a $500 per year subscription fee for his newsletter, widely read by politicians and lawmakers in the state.
Analysis / Bias
We are only looking at the free content available on the website for this review. Capitol Fax has a blog written by Miller that is low biased and very well sourced from credible media. Along the website’s sidebar are newsfeeds from a variety of least biased and left-right leaning news sources such as
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), [NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/), and [Chicago Sun-Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/chicago-sun-times/). We could not find evidence of any failed fact checks.
