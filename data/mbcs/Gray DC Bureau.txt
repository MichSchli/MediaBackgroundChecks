History
Founded in 2014, the Gray Washington DC Bureau, based in Washington D.C., provides political news for all local Gray TV outlets. According to their
[about page](https://www.graydc.com/page/about-us/), “ [Our team of multimedia journalists, producers, and photojournalists](https://www.graydc.com/about-us/meet-the-team/) covers more than 300 members of Congress. We also report regularly from the White House and the Supreme Court when there is a story of local interest.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Gray DC Bureau is owned by
[Gray Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Television), which operates 145 stations across the USA. Sponsored content and advertising generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Gray DC Bureau provides political news for Gray TVs’ local media markets. Articles and headlines are fact-based and use minimal bias, such as this
[President Joe Biden meets with 250 mayors at White House](https://www.graydc.com/2023/01/20/president-joe-biden-meets-with-250-mayors-white-house/) and this [New cost of living numbers are out as a divided Congress must reach an agreement on a crucial decision](https://www.graydc.com/2023/01/13/new-cost-living-numbers-are-out-divided-congress-must-reach-an-agreement-crucial-decision/). All information is properly sourced.
Editorially, the Gray DC Bureau does not offer an opinion page. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
