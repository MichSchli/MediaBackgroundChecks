History
Founded in 1892,
[The Fort Bend Herald and Texas Coaster](http://www.fbherald.com/) is a daily newspaper based in Rosenberg, Texas, covering Fort Bend County, Wharton County, Austin County, and Brazoria County. The managing editor is Scott Reese Wiley. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Hartman Newspapers owns the Fort Bend Herald. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Fort Bend Herald and Texas Coaster utilize loaded language in their headlines such as “
[WHY I SECRETLY WANTED ROY MOORE TO LOSE: BROOKS 2020!”](http://www.fbherald.com/opinion/why-i-secretly-wanted-roy-moore-to-lose-brooks/article_b3c8623a-4aa1-5fef-9dbf-5f9cd6bd5958.html) and “ [Climate Change and Political Party Science.”](http://www.fbherald.com/opinion/climate-change-and-political-party-science/article_20711854-17eb-5925-943e-b6498b60ac98.html) Further, they publish right-wing [Ann Coulter](http://www.fbherald.com/search/results/?q=ANN+COULTER) opinion pieces.
A complete review of Op-eds reveals most favor the right featuring
[Erick Erickson.](https://www.fbherald.com/opinion/erick-erickson/article_7db01928-f581-5cea-b198-47f4f0947626.html) While this review is short, our analysis of 10+ more articles in 2022 shows a consistent right-leaning bias in both story selection and editorial perspectives.
