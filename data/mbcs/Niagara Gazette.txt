History
The Niagara Gazette is a daily broadsheet newspaper published in Niagara Falls, New York.
[Founded in 1854](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niagara_Gazette), the paper circulates in the city of Niagara Falls and the surrounding area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Niagara Gazette is owned by
[Community Newspaper Holdings, Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CNHI), an American publisher of newspapers and advertising-related publications throughout the United States. Subscription fees and advertising generate revenue for the paper.
Analysis / Bias
Staff writers cover local news with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Mobile clinic to provide essential services throughout Falls](https://www.niagara-gazette.com/news/local_news/mobile-clinic-to-provide-essential-services-throughout-falls/article_ee6ca7d3-bf47-5d4a-a987-8656d7adcf66.html). Coverage of national news is provided by the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other CNHI publications.
A review of opinion pieces reveals a leftward tilt with stories such as
[EDITORIAL: Need more voters, not fewer](https://www.niagara-gazette.com/opinion/editorial-need-more-voters-not-fewer/article_8df278c4-017d-50fb-82d6-ec9022bf555d.html) [,](https://www.thedailystar.com/opinion/editorials/in-our-opinion-be-a-good-neighbor-and-get-vaccinated/article_b397b5ee-1ce6-528c-80e6-52a425a5ce13.html) and [EDITORIAL: Stand back and stand by, Mr. President](https://www.niagara-gazette.com/opinion/editorial-stand-back-and-stand-by-mr-president/article_8cb40025-4b33-515a-b9aa-e9427f49eba8.html). Although The Niagara Gazette does offer some right-leaning opinions, the majority lean left in bias. In general, they report the news factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
