History
The La Junta Tribune-Democrat serves La Junta, Colorado, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. lajuntatribunedemocrat.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns La Junta Tribune-Democrat. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, La Junta Tribune-Democrat covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater La Junta, Colorado area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Cattle industry discussed at AFBF convention](https://www.lajuntatribunedemocrat.com/story/news/2022/01/12/cattle-industry-discussed-afbf-convention/9187424002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, La Junta Tribune-Democrat does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented such as this that favors the left:
[Joe Biden surveys ‘god-awful’ devastation of Colorado wildfire that caused more than $500M in damages](https://www.lajuntatribunedemocrat.com/story/news/nation/2022/01/07/colorado-wildfire-caused-513-million-damage-destroyed-1-100-homes/9128930002/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
