History
[Spectrum News – NY1](http://www.ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/about-us), known as NY1, is an American cable TV channel that first launched on September 8, 1992. The founder of NY1 is Richard Aurelio, who was the president of New York City’s Time Warner Cable. Spectrum News – NY1 is currently owned by [Charter Communications, Inc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charter_Communications). NY1 serves Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island and provides 24-hour local news, politics, weather news, and more. NY1 is headquartered in Manhattan, New York City, and shares content with its sister channels in New York City: [Manhattan](https://spectrumlocalnews.com/nyc/manhattan), [Brooklyn](https://spectrumlocalnews.com/nyc/brooklyn), [The Bronx](https://spectrumlocalnews.com/nyc/bronx), [Queens](https://spectrumlocalnews.com/nyc/queens), [Staten Island](https://spectrumlocalnews.com/nyc/staten-island), and [NY1 Noticias](https://spectrumlocalnews.com/nyc/noticias) [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Time Warner Cable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectrum_News) launched its first regional cable news channel on September 8, 1992, with the New York City-based NY1. In 2013, Charter Communications announced that they are re-branding its residential services to Charter Spectrum. In 2016, Charter acquired Time Warner Cable. Next, Time Warner Cable was phased out in favor of Spectrum, the brand used by Charter to market its services. The main revenue sources for NY1 are ads and subscription fees collected by cable operators who [package programs](https://www.cnbc.com/2015/04/20/how-do-cable-companies-make-their-money.html) in “bundles.“
Analysis / Bias
In review, Spectrum News – NY1 reports local news with minimal loaded words and bias, such as
[“Posters send message on immigration concerns across Queens.”](http://www.ny1.com/nyc/queens/news/2018/07/07/posters-send-message-on-immigration-concerns-across-queens) NY1 also republishes [Associated Press articles ](http://www.ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/politics/2018/07/10/us-to-reunite-and-release-over-50-immigrant-children-government-attorney-says)and covers stories with both points of view [represented.](http://www.ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/politics/2018/07/09/donald-trump-us-supreme-court-nominee-announcement-to-replace-anthony-kennedy) Typically, NY1 utilizes credible sources such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/); however, they also source through interviews and large quotes.
NY1 does not publish opinion pieces but does report on politics with minimal bias, such as this
[Suffolk County executive Steve Bellone, potential statewide candidate, has $1M](https://www.ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/ny-state-of-politics/2021/07/16/suffolk-county-executive--potential-statewide-candidate--has--1m). In general, NY1 covers both sides of issues and reports news factually.
