History
[The Daily Record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daily_Record_(Scotland)), part of Reach PLC, is a Scottish tabloid newspaper based in Glasgow, Scotland. It is published six days a week, and its sister paper is the Sunday Mail. It has a close kinship with the [Daily Mirror](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-mirror/), with major stories of UK significance being reported in both titles. [Read our profile on UK government influence on media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
British Publishing Company
[Reach plc](https://www.bbc.com/news/topics/c514vpxv5llt/reach-plc) (formerly Trinity Mirror) owns The Daily Record. Revenue is generated through digital advertising, print ads, and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Daily Record uses highly sensationalized bold headlines that usually tend to favor the left. The Daily Record endorses the left-leaning Labour Party. Articles and headlines sometimes contain loaded emotional wording such as this
[Coked-up biker jailed for death of fellow motorcyclist in head-on smash](https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/coked-up-biker-jailed-death-24959720).
Editorially, they frequently report negatively on conservative prime minister Boris Johnson such as this
[Triple lock on pensions scrapped as Boris Johnson performs another U-turn](https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/politics/triple-lock-pensions-scrapped-boris-24930021). In general, the Daily Record is one-sided in reporting but has not failed a fact check. For this, we rate them mostly factual due to the factual but one-sided nature of their reporting.
