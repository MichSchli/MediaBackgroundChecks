History
[Founded in 1971](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_Area_Reporter), The Bay Area Reporter is a free weekly newspaper serving the LGBT communities in the San Francisco Bay Area; it is one of the largest circulation LGBT newspapers by circulation in the United States and the country’s oldest continuously published newspaper of its kind.
According to their
[about page](https://www.ebar.com/about_the_bar/?screenID=5248): “Bay Area Reporter is America’s longest continuously-published, and highest circulation LGBTQ newspaper, and the undisputed newspaper of record serving San Francisco’s lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender communities.” [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Pride Labs, LLC owns Bay Area Reporter. Revenue is derived through advertising and classified ads.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Bay Area Reporter reports the news for the LGBTQ community in the San Francisco Bay area. Articles and headlines utilize moderately loaded wording such as this:
[Hungarian parliament bans gender recognition in landslide vote](https://www.ebar.com/news/news//292701/hungarian_parliament_bans_gender_recognition_in_landslide_vote). This article is appropriately sourced.
Editorially, they favor left-leaning positions such as LBGTQ rights,
[Climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), and negative opinions of conservatives, including President Trump such as this: [Editorial: Trump’s ‘fake news is fake news](https://www.ebar.com/news/news//264094). In general, the news is reported factually and with a strong left-leaning bias.
