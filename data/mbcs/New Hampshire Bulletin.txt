History
Launched in 2021, New Hampshire Bulletin is an online news website from New Hampshire. Their
[about](https://newhampshirebulletin.com/about/) statement reads, “The New Hampshire Bulletin is an independent, nonprofit news organization dedicated to keeping the people of the Granite State informed about the issues that matter most. Because the activities inside the halls of power are just one part of the picture, the Bulletin staff follows the threads of policy into communities throughout New Hampshire to tell the people’s stories.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, New Hampshire Bulletin is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The New Hampshire Bulletin is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The New Hampshire Bulletin publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from New Hampshire. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects New Hampshirans. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through minimally loaded language
[A sweet deal: New Hampshire wins grant to support maple syrup producers](https://newhampshirebulletin.com/2022/10/18/a-sweet-deal-new-hampshire-wins-grant-to-support-maple-syrup-producers/)
They properly source, linking to the New Hampshire Legislature and credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[Bees face many challenges – and climate change is ratcheting up the pressure](https://newhampshirebulletin.com/2022/10/14/bees-face-many-challenges-and-climate-change-is-ratcheting-up-the-pressure/). On the issues, the New Hampshire Bulletin promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [Abortion will be on the November ballot. Some Republicans who backed an exemption won’t.](https://newhampshirebulletin.com/2022/09/15/abortion-will-be-on-the-november-ballot-some-republicans-who-backed-an-exemption-wont/) In general, the New Hampshire Bulletin reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
