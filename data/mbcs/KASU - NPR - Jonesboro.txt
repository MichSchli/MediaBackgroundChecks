History
KASU – NPR – Jonesboro is an NPR FM radio station (91.9 FM) based in Jonesboro, Arkansas serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KASU – NPR – Jonesboro is owned and broadcast by Arkansas State University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Arkansas Supreme Court Justice Robin Wynne dies](https://www.kasu.org/justice-crime/2023-06-23/arkansas-supreme-court-justice-robin-wynne-dies). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[For the intersex community, ‘Every Body’ exists on a spectrum](https://www.kasu.org/health-science/2023-07-05/for-the-intersex-community-every-body-exists-on-a-spectrum). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
