History
Founded in 1985, KXXV – Waco News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Waco, Texas, United States. Kxxv.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KXXV – Waco News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E._W._Scripps_Company), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
KXXV – Waco News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Waco, Texas area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[Taylor County wildfire prompts evacuations as dry conditions raise risk in West Texas](https://www.kxxv.com/hometown/texas/taylor-county-wildfire-prompts-evacuations-as-dry-conditions-raise-risk-in-west-texas). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden awards public safety officers with Medal of Valor](https://www.kxxv.com/news/national/biden-to-award-public-safety-officers-with-medal-of-valor). In general, they present low biased, fact-based reporting.
