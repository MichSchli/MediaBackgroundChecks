History
The State Journal-Register serves Springfield, Illinois, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. sj-r.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The State Journal-Register. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The State Journal-Register covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Springfield, Illinois area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[State’s attorney: People Adam Lopez defrauded being ‘revictimized’ by IDOC actions](https://www.sj-r.com/story/news/crime/2022/04/11/states-attorney-sangamon-county-livid-idocs-moves/7237151001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The State Journal-Register does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Reject Senate Bill 482 entitling governments to virtual public meetings for any reason](https://www.sj-r.com/story/opinion/editorials/2021/10/15/illinois-should-reject-bill-allowing-virtual-public-meetings-any-reason/8460239002/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
