History
Launched in 1946,
[La Nacion](https://ticotimes.net/2006/10/20/daily-la-nacion-celebrates-60-years) is a daily newspaper published in San Jose, Costa Rica. La Nacion features general interest topics, interviews, entertainment, the latest sports news, and political analysis. [Read our profile on the Costa Rica government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/costa-rica-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Grupo Nacion](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/costa-rica-media-profile/on/empresa.html) is the owner of La Nacion; they also own several other publications such as The Tico Times and [El Financiero](https://www.ameliarueda.com/nota/grupo-nacion-cesa-director-de-el-financiero-y-anuncia-reestrucion-covid-19). The ownership information of Grupo Nación is not transparent; however, [Carlos González Jiménez](https://www.nacion.com/gnfactory/especiales/gruponacion/junta-directiva.html) serves as the chairman. Subscriptions and advertisements generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
La Nacion is known as the countries leading
[conservative](https://books.google.com/books?id=u34cTKyfIUQC&pg=PA257&lpg=PA257&dq=LA+Nacion+costa+rica+conservative&source=bl&ots=xtZs-FvCNk&sig=ACfU3U0i5zDljR3ARjJUvEsmxIgR3XAUog&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiwwc77j4_4AhWVSTABHX5VAdsQ6AF6BAgZEAM#v=onepage&q=LA%20Nacion%20costa%20rica%20conservative&f=false) paper.
In review, La Nacion publishes articles with emotionally loaded language such as “
[Carlos Alvarado raises the tone: Has anyone gone without eating avocado?”](https://www.nacion.com/el-pais/politica/alguien-se-quedo-sin-comer-aguacate-exclama-carlos/MZDLSWZNI5AZNAFYTXVHNTOKSU/story/) The article is about outgoing [center-left president](https://www.france24.com/en/20180402-centre-left-pro-gay-marriage-candidate-carlos-alvarado-wins-costa-rica-election) Carlos Alvarado; a quote from the article reads, “Immediately, the outgoing president reiterated while raising his voice: “Has anyone gone without eating avocado?”
In another article reporting about
[center-right](https://peoplesdispatch.org/2022/04/05/center-right-candidate-rodrigo-chaves-wins-presidential-elections-in-costa-rica/) president Rodrigo Chaves titled [“Rodrigo Chaves eliminates FARO tests: “It’s not that we’ve thought about it, we’ve already made the decision,”](https://www.nacion.com/el-pais/politica/rodrigo-chaves-elimina-pruebas-faro-no-es-que-lo/WYNUOWTF5FEYPI2XYORS4FVDCM/story/) the body of the article is balanced and neutral in tone. Both pieces used quotes to source, and they also did circular referencing by linking to themselves. Due to the limited availability of the articles, we could only analyze a few articles, but the articles we analyzed were balanced.
Generally, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias; however, they favor
[neoliberal](https://books.google.com/books?id=9PpdBAAAQBAJ&pg=PA27&lpg=PA27&dq=costa+rica+la+nacion+political+leaning&source=bl&ots=wo5KdiZrZi&sig=ACfU3U3ygy1IjUKVNzrCuwtfDjchAXK2uw&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiGwsHioY_4AhWkTjABHUTABacQ6AF6BAgzEAM#v=snippet&q=neo%20liberal%20economic%20La%20Nacion&f=false) economic positions editorially.
