History
The Center for Advanced Defense Studies is a nonprofit research think tank that employs academics, national security operators, and local experts from existing and emerging conflict zones worldwide. It works to address evolving and nontraditional security challenges. According to their
[about page](https://c4ads.org/about-us), they are “dedicated to providing data-driven analysis and evidence-based reporting on global conflict and transnational security issues.”
They state their primary
[focus areas](https://c4ads.org/about-us) as Transnational Organized Crime, Proliferation Networks, Threat Finance, and Supply Chain Security. The executive director is David Johnson. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Center for Advanced Defense Studies is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Revenue is derived through donations. The website does not disclose donors; however, they have received 200k from the
[Wyss Foundation](https://www.wyssfoundation.org/grants), which sponsors many left-leaning organizations that promote conservation.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Center for Advanced Defense Studies promotes action against corruption and safeguarding the environment from degradation. The website offers detailed reports such as this
[ILLICIT INFLUENCE: PART ONE](https://static1.squarespace.com/static/566ef8b4d8af107232d5358a/t/5c34ddc0cd836636f387a84f/1546968523065/Illicit+Influence+Part+One+First+Czech+Russian+Bank.pdf), highlighting money laundering and Russian influence on USA elections. They also have a blog that covers many stories, including illegal fishing, such as this: [Something Smells Fishy…](https://c4ads.org/blogposts/2019/4/2/something-smells-fishy) This blog post is properly sourced. The Center for Advanced Defense Studies promotes left-leaning positions such as conservation and environmentalism from an editorial standpoint.
