History
The Lafayette Journal & Courier serves Lafayette, Indiana, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. courierpress.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Lafayette Journal & Courier . In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Lafayette Journal & Courier covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Lafayette, Indiana area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Lafayette police investigating gunshots at Cambridge Estates Apartments](https://www.jconline.com/story/news/crime/2022/04/20/lafayette-indiana-shooting-cambridge-estates-apartment-under-investigation/7380469001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Lafayette Journal & Courier does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Opinion: Social emotional learning to address systemic injustice](https://www.jconline.com/story/opinion/2021/08/06/opinion-social-emotional-learning-address-systemic-injustice/5509837001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
