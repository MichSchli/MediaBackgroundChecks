History
[Founded in 1867](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Springfield_News-Leader), the Springfield News-Leader is a daily newspaper serving Springfield, Missouri, and the Ozarks region. The current editor is [Amos Bridges](https://www.news-leader.com/staff/4406954002/amos-bridges/). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Springfield News-Leader is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Springfield News-Leader covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Springfield, MO area. Local news is reported with minimal loaded emotional language such as this
[Police seeking second suspect in September homicide in Springfield](https://www.news-leader.com/story/news/crime/2021/01/21/springfield-police-seeking-second-suspect-homicide/4243758001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Springfield News-Leader traditionally endorses
[Democratic candidates](https://www.news-leader.com/story/opinion/editorials/2016/11/03/voice-koster-best-position-lead/93224290/). In general, the news is reported factually and with slight left-leaning bias.
