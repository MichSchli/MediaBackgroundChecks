History
The Jacksonville Daily Record is a newspaper published in Jacksonville, Florida, United States. jaxdailyrecord.com is the website for the paper covering local news, sports, crime, government, and opinion.

Funded by / Ownership
The Jacksonville Daily Record is owned and published by the
[Observer Media Group](https://www.yourobserver.com/contact-us) based in Sarasota, Florida. The Observer Media Group, founded by [Matt Walsh](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/matt-walsh-72d8) in 1995, publishes 11 newspapers and several magazines in Florida. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Jacksonville Daily Record publishes local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[City Council approves $5 million loan for 240-unit Vestcor affordable housing project](https://www.jaxdailyrecord.com/article/city-council-approves-dollar5-million-loan-for-240-unit-vestcor-affordable-housing-project). The newspaper does not publish national or international news.
Editorially, the Jacksonville Daily Record publishes an appropriate mix of right and left-leaning opinions. However, more favor the right such as this
[Review & Comment: Inflation: The insidious tax on us all](https://www.jaxdailyrecord.com/article/review-and-comment-inflation-the-insidious-tax-on-us-all). In general, they report factually and with credible sources, while op-eds are mostly balanced with differing viewpoints slightly leaning right.
