History
Founded in 1978, KSTU – Salt Lake City News is a
[FOX](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-affiliated station licensed to Salt Lake City, Utah, United States. fox13now.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KSTU – Salt Lake City News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://scripps.com/our-brands/local-media/), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
KSTU – Salt Lake City News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Salt Lake City, Utah, area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[High school student hit, killed by school bus in Sandy](https://www.fox13now.com/news/local-news/pedestrian-hit-killed-by-school-bus-in-sandy). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Bill on transgender youth health care heads to Utah governor’s desk](https://www.fox13now.com/news/politics/bill-on-transgender-youth-health-care-heads-to-utah-governors-desk). In general, they present low-biased, fact-based reporting.
