History
The Johns Hopkins Hospital (JHH) is the teaching hospital and biomedical research facility of the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine, located in Baltimore, Maryland, U.S. It was founded in 1889 using money from a bequest by philanthropist
[Johns Hopkins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johns_Hopkins). Johns Hopkins Hospital is widely regarded as one of the world’s greatest hospitals. It was ranked by [U.S. News & World Report](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/us-news-world-report/) as the best overall hospital in America for 21 consecutive years (1991–2011). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
John Hopkins Hospital is a part of the School of Medicine and is funded through donations and patient care charges.
Analysis / Bias
The website provides links and resources related to pro-science and peer-reviewed medicine. For example,
[COVID Vaccine: What Parents Need to Know](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/conditions-and-diseases/coronavirus/covid19-vaccine-what-parents-need-to-know). In general, all content is factual, peer-reviewed, and low-biased.
