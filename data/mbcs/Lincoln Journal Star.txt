History
[Founded in 1867](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lincoln_Journal_Star), the Journal Star (JS) is an online newspaper serving the Lincoln, Nebraska, metro area. It is published by Lee Enterprises. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Journal Star is owned by
[Lee Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_Enterprises), which according to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), has donated 88% of its money to [Republican politicians](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052990&cycle=2014) since 1990. The Journal Star is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Journal Star covers local news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Lincoln man in jail on $500K bond for allegedly beating woman with a cord.](https://journalstar.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/lincoln-man-in-jail-on-500k-bond-for-allegedly-beating-woman-with-a-cord/article_cc1908ab-78c7-540f-a454-c9011ae337af.html) National and international news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
A review of op-eds reveals that they promote diversity of thought. There is a reasonable balance from right to left on the op-ed page, with maybe slightly more favoring the left. It is close as this source tries to represent diverse voices, and we conclude that it is least biased.
