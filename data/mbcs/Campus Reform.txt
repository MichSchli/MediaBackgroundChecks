History
Campus Reform is a college news website.
[They claim](https://www.campusreform.org/about/) to be “a conservative watchdog to the nation’s higher education system, Campus Reform exposes liberal bias and abuse on the nation’s college campuses.” The website is a project of the [Leadership Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leadership_Institute), which is an institute founded in 1979 by conservative activist Morton C. Blackwell. Its mission is to “increase the number and effectiveness of conservative activists” and to “identify, train, recruit and place conservatives in politics, government, and media.”
The current editor-in-chief is Lawrence B. Jones III.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Campus Reform is owned by the Leadership Institute, which discloses their
[financial information](https://www.leadershipinstitute.org/aboutus/finance.cfm); however, they do not list donors. Some [notable donors](https://www.desmogblog.com/leadership-institute) include The Lynde and Harry Bradley Foundation, Adolph Coors Foundation, Charles G. Koch Charitable Foundation, and DeVos Urban Leadership Initiative. Donations generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, most articles are hostile toward liberal activists on college campuses. There is the use of some loaded words that typically favor the right such as this
[Conservatives claim victory after U Florida settles First Amendment case](https://www.campusreform.org/?ID=13521). Campus Reform typically sources their information; however, they have been flagged for making an [unproven claim](http://www.snopes.com/did-uga-professor-let-students-pick-grades/). In reviewing the journalists involved in this project, many have worked for the conservative and mixed factual [Daily Caller](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-caller/), which has [failed numerous fact checks](http://www.politifact.com/personalities/daily-caller/) and [CNS News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cns-news/) and [The Blaze](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-blaze/).
Editorially, all stories favor the right, with many
[reporting favorably](https://www.campusreform.org/?ID=13331) on President Donald Trump. When it comes to science, Campus Reform does not outright deny that [Global Warming](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/) is related to fossil fuels; however, they frequently report negatively on [Climate Change Activists](https://www.campusreform.org/?ID=13331).
