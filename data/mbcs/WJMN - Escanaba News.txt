History
Founded in 1969, WJMN – Escanaba News is a MyNetworkTv-affiliated television station licensed to Escanaba, Michigan. The associated website is upmatters.com, which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WJMN – Escanaba News is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The WJMN – Escanaba News website publishes news for the greater Escanaba, Michigan area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias, such as this:
[Escanaba votes to take back control of city pension plan](https://www.upmatters.com/news/escanaba-votes-to-take-back-control-of-city-pension-plan/)
Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Several Republicans say they were threatened for not supporting Jim Jordan for House Speaker](https://www.upmatters.com/news/washington-dc/several-republicans-say-they-were-threatened-for-not-supporting-jim-jordan-for-house-speaker/). Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
