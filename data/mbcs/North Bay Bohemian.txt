History
Established in 1979 as “The Paper” in Guerneville, Calif, the North Bay Bohemian is a weekly newspaper serving Sonoma and Napa counties. The Bohemian provides coverage of local news, arts, dining, and entertainment.
The publication changed its name to the Sonoma County Independent in 1993. In 2000, it rebranded as the North Bay Bohemian and is headquartered in Healdsburg,
[California](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/california-governor-gavin-newsom-political-bias-profile/). Metro publishes North Bay Bohemian free weekly, in wine country, as stated on the North Bay Bohemian’s [about page.](https://bohemian.com/us/) [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The website lacks transparency as they do not clearly disclose ownership. Research indicates they may be owned by
[Dan Pulcrano](https://bohemian.com/author/dan-pulcrano/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The North Bay Bohemian is divided into sections covering News & Features, Arts & Entertainment, Columns & Opinion, Food & Drink, Best Of, People, Calendar, and Find Cannabis, including stories on various topics like
[“Rob Brezsny’s ‘Astrology Is Real: Revelations from My Life as a Horoscope Columnist.'”](https://bohemian.com/rob-brezsnys-astrology-is-real-revelations-from-my-life-as-a-horoscope-columnist/) This story explores Brezsny’s work as an astrologer and horoscope columnist, viewed by some as [pseudoscience](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-astrology/). Another article titled [“Be Afraid”](https://bohemian.com/be-afraid/) can be categorized within personal growth.
Editorially, The North Bay Bohemian does not take political positions; however, they report negatively on former President Trump, such as
[“Trump’s Tweets Are Actually Emo Songs.”](https://bohemian.com/trumps-tweets-are-actually-emo-songs/) It describes Former President Trump’s tweets as angst-ridden and compares them to emo song lyrics, suggesting they evoke feelings of sadness and pity. In general, information is mostly fact-based and holds a left-leaning editorial bias in favor of a liberal perspective.
