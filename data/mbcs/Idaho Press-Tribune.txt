History
[Founded in 1883](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Idaho_Press-Tribune), The Idaho Press-Tribune is a daily newspaper published in Nampa, Idaho, United States, that serves Nampa and the surrounding area. The current publisher is Matt Davison. The website IdahoPress.com also hosts other newspapers, including [Boise Weekly](https://www.idahopress.com/boiseweekly/). [Read our profile on USA Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper is owned by
[Adams Publishing Group](https://www.poynter.org/business-work/2017/who-are-the-adams-family-and-why-are-they-buying-newspapers-by-the-dozen/), which owns over one hundred papers across the USA. Stephen Adams is the owner and [donates to Republicans](https://web.archive.org/web/20080724023422/http://www.madcowprod.com/billboardenvy.htm), raising 1 million dollars for George W. Bush in 2000. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Idaho Press-Tribune covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Man charged with two felonies in fatal hit-and-run crash](https://www.idahopress.com/news/local/man-charged-with-two-felonies-in-fatal-hit-and-run-crash/article_88c73335-d57c-5407-9c5e-e16f6714f27f.html). Local information is sourced through quotes, interviews, and accounts, while National and world news comes directly from the [Associated Press](https://www.bozemandailychronicle.com/news/city/bozeman-continues-to-foster-midtown-development/article_51464c29-97fa-57b9-9535-05375f0853c8.html).
Editorially, the Idaho Press-Tribune provides a balance in opinions, with slightly more favoring the left. They generally report the news factually and with a slight Left-leaning bias.
