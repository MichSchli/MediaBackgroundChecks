History
Founded in 1949, WHIO – Dayton News is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Dayton, Ohio, United States. The associated website whio.com covers news, sports, crime, and weather. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Cox Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cox_Enterprises) owns WHIO – Dayton News through their subsidiary Cox Media Group. They are a company that owns numerous newspapers, television stations, and radio stations, such as the [Atlanta Journal-Constitution](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/atlanta-journal-constitution/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WHIO – Dayton News covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Dayton, Ohio area. Local news is covered with minimally loaded language, such as this
[Campaign to repair landscape damaged by Memorial Day tornadoes continues for another year](https://www.whio.com/news/local/campaign-repair-landscape-damaged-by-memorial-day-tornadoes-continues-second-year/4SS3IXKF6ZB6RG7C74ROU2N3FA/). Many local news stories contain videos from the TV station. The national news is primarily republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, WHIO – Dayton News does not publish op-eds; however, when covering politics, they present info with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden, Mexican president confer on migration, diplomacy](https://www.whio.com/news/politics/biden-mexican/KKH7OD3YLJNNQ372AATEV7U65M/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
