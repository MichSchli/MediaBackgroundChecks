History
Founded in 1968, KESQ – Palm Springs News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Palm Springs, California. The associated website is kesq.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The News-Press & Gazette Company (NPG) owns KESQ – Palm Springs News and the associated website. NPG owns and publishes several TV and Radio stations and newspapers, primarily in the midwest USA. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The KESQ – Palm Springs News website publishes news for the greater Palm Springs, California area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias, such as this:
[Firefighters battle house fire in Banning](https://kesq.com/news/2022/07/02/firefighters-battle-house-fire-in-banning/) Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [CBS News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/), [CNN Newswire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/left/cnn-bias/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Joe Biden enters the second year of his presidency looking for a reset after a tumultuous first 12 months](https://kesq.com/news/national-politics/cnn-us-politics/2022/01/18/joe-biden-enters-the-second-year-of-his-presidency-looking-for-a-reset-after-a-tumultuous-first-12-months/). Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoiding using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
