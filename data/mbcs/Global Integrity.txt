History
Global Integrity is located in Washington, D.C., USA, and provides empirically-supported information that analyzes corruption and governance trends. According to their
[about page](https://www.globalintegrity.org/about/what-we-believe/), “Our mission is to help people and organizations solve complex social problems by supporting locally-led innovation, learning, and adaptation.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Global Integrity is a hybrid organization that seeks to generate earned revenue to support its public-interest mission. They are primarily funded through donations. They provide yearly reports
[here.](https://www.globalintegrity.org/annual-reports/)
Analysis / Bias
In review, the website reports news on how to improve governance through equity. The website offers a blog that is written by staff that tends to lean slightly left, such as this:
[Beating kleptocrats at their own game: Learning how to tackle kleptocracy more effectively](https://www.globalintegrity.org/2019/06/13/kleptocracy/). This story is properly sourced to [The Guardian](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-guardian/) and [Atlantic](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-atlantic/). In fact, most stories on this site are properly sourced. Global Integrity is pro-science when it comes to [climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), such as this: [Avoiding a Soylent Green Data Revolution](https://www.globalintegrity.org/2014/10/16/avoiding-a-soylent-green-data-revolution/). In general, Global Integrity slightly leans left in editorial bias, but overall is not overtly biased and retains a least biased rating.
