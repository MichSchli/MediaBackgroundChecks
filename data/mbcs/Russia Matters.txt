History
Established in 2016,
[Russia Matters](https://www.russiamatters.org/about) is an initiative of Harvard Kennedy School’s Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs. The project aims to improve understanding of Russia and U.S.-Russian relations among American decision-makers and the general public. The director of the project is [Simon Saradzhyan](https://www.belfercenter.org/person/simon-saradzhyan). Russia Matters operates from the Belfer Center at the John F. Kennedy School of Government, located in Cambridge, MA. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
Russia Matters offers well-researched news and insights on
[Russia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/russia-media-profile/) and its international relations. The article [“Russia Analytical Report, July 10-17, 2023](https://www.russiamatters.org/news/russia-analytical-report/russia-analytical-report-july-10-17-2023)” provides an in-depth analysis of Russia- [Ukraine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ukraine-media-profile/) dynamics, discussing the Ukrainian counteroffensive and Russia’s nuclear capabilities. It also explores U.S. and Russian priorities for their bilateral agenda, maintaining an unbiased tone and referencing multiple sources, including the [Wall Street Journal, ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wall-street-journal/) [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Financial Times, ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/financial-times/)and [The Washington Post.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/)
The article “
[Debate: Is U.S. Support for Ukraine Costing ‘Peanuts’ or Too Much?”](https://www.russiamatters.org/node/30739) presents two contrasting views on the cost of U.S. support for Ukraine. The article fairly presents both perspectives. The language used to describe each analyst’s arguments is neutral, and both viewpoints are given adequate space for discussion. The author avoids overtly strong language, favoring one view over the other, providing a balanced presentation of contrasting opinions. Thus, the article shows no clear indications of bias in word choices or framing.
Finally, the article titled
[“Russia’s Prime Minister Mishustin: A Quiet Technocrat Who Toes the Line and Gets Results”](https://www.russiamatters.org/analysis/russias-prime-minister-mishustin-quiet-technocrat-who-toes-line-and-gets-results) offers a balanced analysis of Mikhail Mishustin, Russia’s Prime Minister, emphasizing his technical skills and political role. The article is thoroughly sourced, drawing from a variety of sources, such as [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), and [Foreign Policy](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/foreign-policy/), including Russian sources, such as kremlin.ru, which is vital for comprehensive reporting on Russian politicians. In general, Russia Matters is fact-based and display low bias in reporting.
