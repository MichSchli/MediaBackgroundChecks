History
Founded in 2004,
[Censor.NET](https://censor.net/ru/about) is an Internet portal from Ukraine that combines social and political news with a forum. It is published in Ukrainian and Russian. Censor.NET’s founder is Yurii Butusov, and he is also the editor-in-chief. [Read our profile on Ukraine media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ukraine-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Censor.NET lacks transparency as they do not disclose ownership on the website. According to Media Ownership Monitor, Yurii Butusov is the
[“probable”](https://ukraine.mom-rsf.org/en/owners/individual-owners/detail/owner/owner/show/yurii-butusov/) owner, and the website is funded through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
[Reporters Without Borders](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reporters-without-borders/) ranks [Ukraine](https://rsf.org/en/ukraine) 96 out of 180 countries in the 2020 World Press Freedom Index.
The editor and probable owner, Yuri Butusov, is known as a
[military analyst](https://foreignpolicy.com/2015/03/26/ukraine-doesnt-have-a-warlord-problem-russia-donbas/) and has also [worked](https://ukraine.mom-rsf.org/en/media/detail/outlet/censornet/) as Chairman and co-founder of the NGO “Ukrainian Foundation for the strengthening of national security” and CEO in media holding of the Socialistic Party of Ukraine (2007).
In review, Censor.NET
[focuses](https://genshtab.info/%D0%91%D1%83%D1%82%D1%83%D1%81%D0%BE%D0%B2,_%D0%AE%D1%80%D0%B8%D0%B9_%D0%95%D0%B2%D0%B3%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87) on the Donbas conflict regarding Russia’s aggression in that region (Moscow supports separatist fighters in the Donbas region of eastern Ukraine).
Censor.NET utilizes emotionally loaded language in their headlines such as “
[Ukrainian President praises relationship with Trudeau, but mum on support for Security Council seat”](https://censor.net/en/resonance/3203567/ukrainian_president_praises_relationship_with_trudeau_but_mum_on_support_for_security_council_seat) and [“Ukraine’s new president faces a defining choice.”](https://censor.net/en/resonance/3150064/ukraines_new_president_faces_a_defining_choice) They also republish articles from a variety of left-leaning sources such as the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/) and right-leaning sources such as [The Globe and Mail](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-globe-and-mail/) and the [Financial Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/financial-times/). When reporting on politics and the Prime Minister, they report objectively, such as this [Zelenskyi announces new package of assistance to business in case of lockdown.](https://censor.net/en/news/3232919/zelenskyi_announces_new_package_of_assistance_to_business_in_case_of_lockdown)
Editorially, they produce a blog that holds left-leaning viewpoints such as this
[UN: Deforestation has brought the Carpathians to the brink of ecological disaster](https://censor.net/ru/blogs/3233387/oon_vyrubki_lesa_priveli_karpaty_na_gran_ekologicheskoyi_katastrofy) and negative reporting on Russia such as this [Cyprus: another defeat for Russia](https://censor.net/ru/blogs/3233160/kipr_esche_odno_porajenie_rossii). In general, the news is reported factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
