History
The Detroit Lakes Tribune is a newspaper published in Detroit Lakes, Minnesota. The website dl-online.com features local and national news, politics, sports, entertainment, business, classifieds, and health.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Detroit Lakes Tribune is owned by
[Forum Communications Company](https://www.forumcomm.com/brands-overview/), which is based in North Dakota and has additional locations across South Dakota, Minnesota, and Wisconsin. Advertising and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Detroit Lakes Tribune reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Detroit Lakes, MN region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[Becker County Board primary winners gearing up for Nov. 8 election](https://www.dl-online.com/news/local/becker-county-board-primary-winners-gearing-up-for-nov-8-election). Further, the website publishes national and international news from credible sources such as the [LA Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/). Finally, all information is sourced via first-person accounts or through credible media.
Editorially, The Detroit Lakes Tribune reasonably represents both sides. However, a review of columnists demonstrates more favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually and with right-leaning editorial bias.
