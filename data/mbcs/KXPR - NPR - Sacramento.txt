History
KXPR – NPR – Sacramento is an NPR FM radio station (88.9 FM) based in Sacramento, California serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KXPR – NPR – Sacramento is owned and broadcast by California State University – Sacramento. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KXPR – NPR – Sacramento website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Striking actors rally at California state Capitol](https://www.capradio.org/articles/2023/07/27/striking-actors-rally-at-california-state-capitol/). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[How new Title IX rules could affect California’s transgender and nonbinary students](https://www.capradio.org/articles/2023/01/01/how-new-title-ix-rules-could-affect-californias-transgender-and-nonbinary-students/). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
