History
[Founded in 1879](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_News-Herald_(Ohio)), The News-Herald is a newspaper distributed in the northeastern portion of Greater Cleveland, Ohio, United States, serving Lake and Geauga Counties and a section of eastern Cuyahoga County. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The News-Herald is owned by
[Digital First Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_First_Media), which owns nearly 100 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York Based Hedge Fund, which has been described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Revenue is derived through subscription fees and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the News-Herald covers local news via reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Ohio won’t go back to class for remainder of the school year](https://www.news-herald.com/news/cuyahoga-county/ohio-wont-go-back-to-class-for-remainder-of-school-year/article_a19a532d-2f9e-5e0a-9549-df10335d5270.html). National and international news is delivered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Willoughby News-Herald publishes national right-leaning columnists such as S.E. Cupp, Jonah Goldberg, and Cal Thomas. In general, the news is reported factually with a right-leaning editorial bias.
