History
The
[Argus Leader](https://ballotpedia.org/Sioux_Falls_Argus_Leader) launched as “The Argus” in 1881. It is a daily newspaper covering news information and providing local journalism in Sioux Falls, South Dakota. In 1887, by merging The Sioux Falls Argus and The Sioux Falls Leader, they became the Argus Leader. In 1955, John A. Kennedy purchased the paper, and in 1963, Speidel Newspapers Inc., a chain of 13 daily and 5 Sunday papers, acquired the paper. In 1977, Gannett purchased Speidel Newspapers Inc. According to the [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/business/1977/07/30/gannett-73-papers-and-still-counting/f5a7dec3-e17c-4fb3-860d-a5ba7762b5d2/?utm_term=.5129bb2da6dd), in 1977, this was “the second-largest newspaper purchase in U.S. history.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
In review, the Argus Leader
[endorsed,](https://ballotpedia.org/Sioux_Falls_Argus_Leader) both in 2004 and 2008, Republicans George W. Bush and John McCain. In 2018, The Argus Leader endorsed Kristi Noem for the GOP gubernatorial nomination. According to [KSFW-TV](https://www.ksfy.com/content/news/Argus-Leader-endorses-Kristi-Noem-for-GOP-nomination-484315921.html), the Argus Leader called Kristi Noem a “loyal soldier for President Trump, helping to bring home the tax reform package as a member of the Ways and Means Committee.”
The Argus Leader uses minimal emotionally loaded headlines such as
[“President Trump tweets support for South Dakota during flooding”](https://www.argusleader.com/story/news/2019/03/18/president-trump-tweets-support-south-dakota-during-flooding/3206729002/) and “ [Gov. Kristi Noem signs Capitol carry, ‘In God We Trust’ school display into law.](https://eu.argusleader.com/story/news/politics/2019/03/19/gov-kristi-noem-signs-capitol-carry-in-god-we-trust-school-law/3210635002/)” Local news is covered through staff journalists, and national news comes from the USA Today Network and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) Further, the Argus Leader sources their information through quotes, first-person accounts, and credible sources such as the [Huffington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/huffington-post/) and [Washington Post.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/) In general, they report straight news with low bias but have a right-leaning editorial bias based on endorsements of Republican candidates.
