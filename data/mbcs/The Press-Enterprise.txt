History
[Founded in 1878](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Press-Enterprise), The Press-Enterprise is a paid daily newspaper published by MediaNews Group that serves the Inland Empire in Southern California. Headquartered in downtown Riverside, California, it is the primary newspaper for Riverside County, with a heavy penetration into neighboring San Bernardino County. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Press-Enterprise is owned by MediaNews Group, which owns nearly 100 newspapers across the USA. MediaNews Group is in turn owned by
[Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund, which has been described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Revenue is derived through subscription fees and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Press-Enterprise reports news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[ After nearly 7-hour session, Riverside County supervisors vote to lift 3 coronavirus health orders](https://www.pe.com/2020/05/08/will-riverside-county-coronavirus-health-orders-be-lifted/). National and international news is covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Riverside Press-Enterprise has
[endorsed Republican](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Presidential candidates since 2000; however, in 2016, they did not endorse either candidate. A review of op-eds reveals slightly more favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually and with a right-leaning editorial bias.
