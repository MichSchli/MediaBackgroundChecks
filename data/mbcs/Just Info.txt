History
[Justice Info](https://www.justiceinfo.net/en/about-us#funding) is an online platform dedicated to covering issues related to international justice, focusing on topics such as transitional justice, tribunals, truth commissions, and reconciliation processes worldwide. The website provides news, in-depth analysis, and opinions on various international legal proceedings and human rights issues. It is unclear when the platform was launched, but its extensive coverage suggests it has been in operation for a significant period of time. The platform is based in Lausanne, Switzerland. Analysis / Bias
Justice Info’s content heavily focuses on international justice and human rights issues. The site often features articles on sensitive and complex legal matters, such as war crimes in Ukraine, the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, and genocide trials. For example, the article titled
[“MORE THAN 200 UKRAINIANS TRIED IN THE OCCUPIED TERRITORIES”](https://www.justiceinfo.net/en/128240-200-ukrainians-tried-occupied-territories.html) discusses the sentencing of Ukrainian servicemen in Russian trials since the beginning of a “special operation” in February 2022. The article offers in-depth coverage of the trials in Donetsk and Luhansk, commonly called the Donbas, outlining the charges and corresponding sentences.
The article’s portrayal of the events seems to be from a perspective sympathetic to Ukraine, evident in its focus on the sentencing of Ukrainian servicemen and questioning Russia’s tactics in prisoner exchanges. The use of phrases like “so-called Donetsk People’s Republic” and “Russian annexation” reflects a bias against the legitimacy of these entities and events. In general, while the article provides detailed information on the trials and exchanges, its language and perspective may indicate a bias toward portraying Russia’s actions in a negative light.
The article
[“ISRAEL ON TRIAL”](https://www.justiceinfo.net/en/127634-israel-on-trial.html) analyzes the legal proceedings initiated by South Africa against Israel at the International Court of Justice regarding the situation in Palestine. The piece discusses the complexity of defining genocide in international law and its implications in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. The article suggests that Netanyahu, Israel’s Prime Minister, was willing to prolong the war for personal political survival. The portrayal of Prime Minister Netanyahu’s actions as potentially driven by personal political survival and their implications for the conflict adds a critical dimension to the discussion.
The editorial stance of Justice Info is aligned with promoting awareness and understanding of international justice and human rights issues. The content is generally balanced, emphasizing factual reporting and analysis of legal proceedings and human rights matters. However, focusing on international justice and human rights can lead to a particular emphasis on these themes, potentially overshadowing other perspectives or issues.
