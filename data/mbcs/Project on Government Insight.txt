History
The Project On Government Oversight (POGO), founded in 1981, is an independent non-profit organization in the United States which investigates and seeks to expose corruption and other misconduct. POGO assists whistleblowers and investigates federal agencies, Congress, and government contractors. According to their
[about page](https://www.pogo.org/about/) they are “a nonpartisan independent watchdog that investigates and exposes waste, corruption, abuse of power, and when the government fails to serve the public or silences those who report wrongdoing.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Project on Government Ownership is a 501(c)(3) non-profit that is funded through donations and offers
[transparency](https://www.pogo.org/about/financial-information/).
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Project on Government Oversight investigates corruption and misconduct by the government. The website publishes stories that are both original and republished from other sources. Articles and headlines often contain moderately loaded emotional language that slightly favors the left such as this
[We Can’t “Look Forward” on the Trump Administration’s Abuses](https://www.pogo.org/analysis/2020/12/we-cant-look-forward-on-the-trump-administrations-abuses/). Editorially, POGO does not have much support for Donald Trump, such as this [Will Bill Barr Place His Thumb on the Scale to Tip the Election to Trump?](https://www.pogo.org/analysis/2020/11/will-bill-barr-place-his-thumb-on-the-scale-to-tip-the-election-to-trump/) In general, the information provided on this site is factual and holds a left-leaning bias.
