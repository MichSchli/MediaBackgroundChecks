History
[Founded in 2009](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin_News_Foundation), The Franklin News Foundation, formerly the Franklin Center for Government and Public Integrity is an online nonprofit news organization in the United States that publishes news and commentary from a free market, limited government perspective on state and local politics. The Franklin News Foundation publishes The Center Square, a network of state-based websites. According to their [about page](https://www.franklinnews.org/mission-vision-values/), “Franklin News Foundation’s mission is to hold government accountable through objective, balanced, citizen-focused public journalism with a taxpayer sensibility.” [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Franklin News Foundation is a nonprofit that is funded through donations. Previously, funding came from the
[Donors Trust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donors_Trust) and [Donors Capital Fund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donors_Capital_Fund). However, they lack transparency and do not clearly list how they are funded today.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Franklin News Foundation advocates for limited government and free-market capitalism. News reporting comes from their website
[The Center Square](https://www.thecentersquare.com/). Articles and headlines contain moderately loaded language, such as this [Senate committee warned Florida taxpayers at risk unless state helps private property insurers](https://www.thecentersquare.com/florida/senate-committee-warned-florida-taxpayers-at-risk-unless-state-helps-private-property-insurers/article_a38081e4-55e2-11eb-a0f3-ff3fb11bafaa.html).
According to
[Sourcewatch](http://www.sourcewatch.org/index.php/Franklin_Center_for_Government_and_Public_Integrity), the Franklin News Foundation has a right-wing position on [climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/) and has been criticized for rejecting the consensus of science. Corporations from the fossil fuel industries also fund this source.
News stories in 2022 hold right-leaning perspective on immigration, such as this
[Border Patrol union tells ACLU ‘go to Hell,’ over the migrant fight, urges votes for pro-border defense candidates.](https://www.thecentersquare.com/texas/border-patrol-union-tells-aclu-go-to-hell-over-migrant-fight-urges-votes-for-pro/article_3b6eb172-5bc9-11ed-a227-1f6fbfcfb8bb.html)
In general, information is presented from a right-libertarian perspective and sometimes does not align with the consensus of science.
