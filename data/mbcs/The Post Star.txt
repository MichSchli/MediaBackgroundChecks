History
The Post Star is a daily print newspaper published in the city of Glens Falls, New York, United States. The paper covers news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion for Glens Falls and the surrounding area. Poststar.com serves as the domain for the online version of the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Lee Enterprises owns and publishes The Post Star. According to
[Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Lee Enterprises donated 88% of its money to [Republican politicians](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052990&cycle=2014) from 1990 until 2016. However, during the 2020 election cycle, they donated 70% to Democratic candidates and causes. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Post Star reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Glens Falls, New York region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[Fort Edward police chief, sergeant placed on administrative leave](https://poststar.com/news/local/fort-edward-police-chief-sergeant-placed-on-administrative-leave/article_5e751977-d937-5b83-ada3-63a678528122.html#tracking-source=most-popular-homepage). Further, the website publishes national and international news from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). Finally, all information is sourced via first-person accounts or through credible media.
Editorially, The Post Star reasonably represents both sides. A review of editorial articles demonstrates a reasonable low bias; however, slightly more op-eds favor the right. While more op-eds favor the right, liberal perspectives have fair representation; therefore, they earn a least biased rating on the right side of the scale. In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
