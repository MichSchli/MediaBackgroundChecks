History
Founded in 1976, WBHM – Birmingham is an FM radio station (90.3) based in Birmingham, Alabama, that broadcasts National Public Radio (NPR).
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WBHM – Birmingham is a service of The University of Birmingham. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WBHM – Birmingham website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [In New Orleans, a symbolic bike ride helps fight recidivism. Here’s how it impacted the riders.](https://wbhm.org/2022/in-new-orleans-a-symbolic-bike-ride-helps-fight-recidivism-heres-how-it-impacted-the-riders/) This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to slightly lean left, as does editorial content such as this
[Conflict between Mississippi’s largest hospital, insurer a breaking point for some residents](https://wbhm.org/2022/conflict-between-mississippis-largest-hospital-insurer-a-breaking-point-for-some-residents/). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
