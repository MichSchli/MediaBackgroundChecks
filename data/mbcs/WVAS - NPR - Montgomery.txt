History
Founded in 1984, WVAS – Montgomery is an FM radio station (90.7) based in Montgomery, Alabama, that broadcasts National Public Radio (NPR).
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WVAS – Montgomery is broadcast from Alabama State University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WVAS – Montgomery website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [Looking to leave Twitter? Here are the social networks seeing new users now](https://www.wvasfm.org/business/business/2022-11-24/looking-to-leave-twitter-here-are-the-social-networks-seeing-new-users-now). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content such as this
[Alabama Constitution on ballot November 8](https://www.wvasfm.org/wvas-local/2022-11-02/alabama-constitution-on-ballot-november-8). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
