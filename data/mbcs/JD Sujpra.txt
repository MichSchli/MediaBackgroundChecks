History
[JD Supra](https://resources.jdsupra.com/what-is-jd-supra-overview) was founded in 2008 by [Aviva Cuyler](https://www.jdsupra.com/news/pdf/Aviva-Cuyler-Bio.pdf) and is headquartered in San Francisco, California. The platform provides legal news, analysis, and commentary from a network of attorneys and professionals. JD Supra also offers firms competitive insights and market intelligence by analyzing the thousands of articles read daily on the platform. Here is the [team.](https://www.jdsupra.com/services/team.aspx) [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Aviva Cuyler](https://www.jdsupra.com/services/team.aspx) is listed as the Founder & CEO of JD Supra. The company does not disclose ownership details. They generate revenue through subscriptions, but the specific charges and pricing structure are not available on their website.
Analysis / Bias
JD Supra is a
[platform](https://www.abajournal.com/news/article/new_public_website_for_lawyers_give_content_get_noticed) that allows legal professionals to publish and distribute content and search for relevant material based on jurisdiction, subject matter, and document type. Some notable [contributors include](https://etseq.law.harvard.edu/2008/03/new_to_the_net_jd_supra/) the [Electronic Frontier Foundation,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/electronic-frontier-foundation/) Morrison & Foerster, [the Cato Institute](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cato-institute/), and Mintz, Levin.
Regarding editorial and political coverage, JD Supra publishes articles from various perspectives, including policy forum announcements like the right-center-leaning Cato Institute’s forum on
[fintech](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/cato-institute-to-hold-jan-17-policy-91593/) innovation. An analysis of an article published by JD Supra titled “ [First Amendment Trumps Statutory Ban on Trademark Registration”](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/first-amendment-trumps-statutory-ban-on-9379862/) shows that it uses neutral and factual language, presenting the court’s decision without expressing personal opinions or biases. The article links to the official court [document](https://cafc.uscourts.gov/opinions-orders/20-2205.OPINION.2-24-2022_1913245.pdf) filed in the United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit.
Another article, “
[Equity, Environmental Justice, and Civil Rights: Cornerstones of the Biden Administration’s Environmental Agenda.](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/equity-environmental-justice-and-civil-7665805/) is generally objective and factual, with some potentially biased language that leans left.
