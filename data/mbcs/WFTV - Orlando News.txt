History
Founded in 1958, WFTV – Orlando News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Orlando, Florida, United States. The associated website wftv.com covers news, sports, crime, and weather. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Cox Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cox_Enterprises) owns WFTV – Orlando News through their subsidiary Cox Media Group. They are a company that owns numerous newspapers, television stations, and radio stations, such as the [Atlanta Journal-Constitution](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/atlanta-journal-constitution/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WFTV – Orlando News covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Orlando, Florida area. Local news is covered with minimally loaded language, such as this
[One dead, five hurt after plane crash lands on Florida bridge, striking car](https://www.wftv.com/news/trending/one-dead-five-hurt-after-plane-crash-lands-florida-bridge-striking-car/LS3P2ALNZJEBVEYVMLNRSYFFCU/). Many local news stories contain videos from the TV station. The national news is primarily republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, WFTV – Orlando News does not publish op-eds; however, when covering politics, they present info with minimal bias, such as this
[Congress working on legislation to help workers recover stolen wages.](https://www.wftv.com/news/local/congress-working-legislation-help-workers-recover-stolen-wages/C5WHSQNK4FD7PNSH3SXBCN7UIU/) In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
