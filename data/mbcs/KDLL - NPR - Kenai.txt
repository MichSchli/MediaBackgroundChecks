History
KDLL – NPR – Kenai is an NPR FM radio station (91.9) based in Kenai, Alaska, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KDLL – NPR – Kenai is owned and broadcast by Pickle Hill Public Broadcasting Inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KDLL – NPR – Kenai website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [Seward considers changing city manager residency requirement amid housing issues](https://www.kdll.org/local-news/2023-03-01/seward-considers-removing-city-manager-residency-requirement-amid-housing-challenges). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[Climate change is fueling more conflict between humans and wildlife](https://www.kdll.org/npr-news/2023-03-02/climate-change-is-fueling-more-conflict-between-humans-and-wildlife). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
