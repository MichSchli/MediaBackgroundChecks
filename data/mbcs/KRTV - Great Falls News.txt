History
Founded in 1958, KRTV – Great Falls News is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated station licensed to Great Falls, Montana, United States. krtv.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KRTV – Great Falls News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://scripps.com/our-brands/local-media/), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
KRTV – Great Falls News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Great Falls, Montana, area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[Officials release name of Park City teen who drowned after a crash](https://www.krtv.com/news/accident-crash/officials-release-name-of-park-city-teen-who-died-after-a-crash). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press, and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Judicial candidates Grubich and Levine debate](https://www.krtv.com/news/montana-politics/judicial-candidates-grubich-and-levine-debate). In general, they present low-biased, fact-based reporting.
