History
Bluestem Prairie publishes articles and opinions by writers living in Greater Minnesota. Bias varies by writer ranging from left to low bias. According to their
[about page](https://www.bluestemprairie.com/bluestemprairie/about-bluestemprairie.html), “Bluestem Prairie is a hip (but not cynical) rural blog for those who prefer to take their corn with a progressive chaser and tongue planted firmly in cheek.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Bluestem Praire “is
[owned](https://www.bluestemprairie.com/bluestemprairie/about-bluestemprairie.html) and edited by Sally Jo Sorensen, who lives in Maynard, Minnesota, a small town of 266 people in Chippewa County.” Revenue is derived through subscriptions and donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Bluestem Praire publishes news about the environment of Minnesota with moderately loaded wording such as this:
[No shit: manure plus fertilizer overwhelms Minnesota’s farm landscape and water](https://www.bluestemprairie.com/bluestemprairie/2020/05/no-shit-manure-plus-fertilizer-overwhelms-minnesotas-farm-landscape-and-water.html). This report is properly sourced. During the protests of George Floyd, Bluepraire often pointed out racism and bigotry by the right such as this: [Blackduck MN mayor resigns after sharing violent anti-protester social media meme](https://www.bluestemprairie.com/bluestemprairie/2020/06/blackduck-mn-mayor-resigns-after-sharing-violent-social-media-meme.html). In general, they source their information and report with a left-leaning bias.
