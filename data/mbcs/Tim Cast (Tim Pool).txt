History
Launched in
[2021](https://whois.domaintools.com/timcast.com) by Tim Pool, [TimCast.com](https://timcast.com/about/) is a conservative-leaning website offering news, videos, and podcasts. Pool gained popularity covering the [Occupy Wall Street](https://www.fastcompany.com/1796352/tim-pool-and-henry-ferry-men-behind-occupy-wall-streets-live-stream) protests in 2011 and later worked for [Vice News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vice-news/) and [Fusion](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fusion/). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Tim Pool owns and publishes timcast.com. Advertising, subscriptions, and an online shop that sells merchandise generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
Timcast.com offers a range of content, from video reports on
[Trump rallies](https://timcast.com/news/watch-thousands-gather-for-trumps-speech-in-arizona/) to neutral, well-sourced articles. Editorial content leans right, with member-only videos often featuring emotionally charged headlines against the left. Non-member opinion pieces are right-leaning but less loaded. Our analysis provides insights into Tim Pool’s political leaning and potential bias. We offer a balanced perspective on Tim Pool’s reporting, addressing key questions about his bias and political leaning.
Many articles on the website hold a conservative perspective featuring negative reporting on the
[Biden Administration](https://timcast.com/members-area/trump-slams-biden-and-harris-for-not-going-to-border-to-view-wreckage-they-created-says-he-will-visit-june-30/) and [cultural issues](https://timcast.com/members-area/brandon-straka-member-podcast-elon-posts-lbgt-pride-month-meme-crew-roasts-fake-corporate-wokeness/). For example, an article such as [FBI’s Mar-A-Lago Raid Could Stop Trump From Running in 2024, Legal Analyst Says](https://timcast.com/news/fbis-mar-a-lago-raid-could-stop-trump-from-running-in-2024-legal-analyst-says/),” sourced to credible media outlets such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), and in the link above, Cornell Law
Editorially, opinion content is labeled appropriately, with story selection heavily favoring the right and often denigrating the left. For example, video content for members only frequently uses loaded emotional headlines to frame the left negatively, such as this
[Matt Whitaker member Podcast: Kamala Does WEIRD Woke Intro, Tim Roasts Her Over Affair With Willie Brown](https://timcast.com/members-area/matt-whitaker-member-podcast-kamala-does-weird-woke-intro-tim-roasts-her-over-affair-with-willie-brown/). Whereas non-member opinion pieces are right-leaning, but not as loaded [OPINION: Former Nickelodeon Star Jennette Mccurdy’s Forthcoming Memoir Should Strike Fear in Hollywood’s Dark Underbelly](https://timcast.com/news/opinion-former-nickelodeon-star-jennette-mccurdys-forthcoming-memoir-should-strike-fear-in-hollywoods-dark-underbelly/).
Recently, TimCast.com had a dispute with media credibility rater
[Newsguard](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/newsguard/) in which they [rated](https://api.newsguardtech.com/label/timcast.com?cid=5beca98f-3100-40d0-8a9e-7adc09600d4f) the website as not gathering and presenting news responsibly. Essentially, Newsguard claims that by repeating a quote by former President Trump or any other false source, TimCast.com is making a false claim. [Tim Pool argues](https://timcast.com/video/tim-threatens-to-sue-newsguard-over-fake-news-about-timcast/) that the sources he used ( [New York Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-post/) and [Daily Mail](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-mail/)) all have Green (credible) ratings by Newsguard. Of note, Media Bias Fact Check does not consider either of these sources as highly credible. He further argues that corrections are made when evidence is presented. In our review, we found that TimCast does make appropriate corrections.
For example, in the article titled
[School District to Implement Grading System That Takes Race and Class into Account, Denies Grading System Will be Race-Based](https://timcast.com/news/illinois-high-school-to-implement-race-based-grading-system/), they rely on a source [West Cook News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/west-cook-news-bias/), which MBFC has rated as Questionable for being an imposter website from the [Metric Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/metric-media/) network that has made false claims. In this case, the story was [false](https://timcast.com/news/illinois-high-school-to-implement-race-based-grading-system/), but TimCast.com corrected the story with this statement “The original version of this story referred to the changes being made by the school district as “race-based,” we have updated the story to clarify that their goal was to “equalize test scores among racial groups” as well as socioeconomic status.”
As part of our analysis, we delve into these nuances of Tim Pool’s reporting to provide insights into his political leaning and potential bias. In general, TimCast.com’s text-based non-member news is usually fact-based, reasonably sourced, and right-leaning in story selection. We do not perform in-depth reviews of video content and rely on third-party fact-checkers to determine the factuality of videos. We could not find a failed fact check on videos but did observe hyper-partisan conservative views and loaded emotional wording in headlines.
