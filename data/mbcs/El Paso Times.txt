History
[Founded in 1881](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Paso_Times), The El Paso Times is the newspaper for the U.S. city of El Paso, Texas. The paper is currently the only English-language daily in El Paso. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The El Paso Times is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the El Paso Times covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater El Paso, Texas area. Local news is reported with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Trucks stolen from Central El Paso car dealership](https://www.elpasotimes.com/story/news/crime/2020/08/29/crime-week-trucks-stolen-el-paso-dick-poe-toyota/5669638002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the El Paso Times traditionally endorses
[Democratic candidates](https://ballotpedia.org/El_Paso_Times) since 2012. In general, the news is reported factually and with a slight left-leaning bias.
