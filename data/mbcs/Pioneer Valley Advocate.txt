History
The Valley Advocate, based in North Hampton, Massachusetts, is a news website that focuses on arts, entertainment, and culture in the Pioneer Valley region. The website covers a variety of topics including music, theater, art exhibits, and social issues. It aims to provide an alternative perspective on news and events in the area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Valley Advocate is owned by
[Newspapers of New England, Inc. (NNE)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspapers_of_New_England,_Inc.), which is a privately owned publisher of nine daily and weekly newspapers in New Hampshire and Massachusetts. The paper is funded through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
The Pioneer Valley Advocate publishes local news through staff journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Late-night lounge coming to downtown Northampton](https://valleyadvocate.com/2022/08/26/late-night-lounge-coming-to-downtown-northampton/). The paper and website do not cover national and international news.
Editorially, they feature columns that advocate for marijuana, such as this
[O Cannabis: Bedtime weed to treat insomnia and sleep apnea](https://valleyadvocate.com/2019/05/21/o-cannabis-bedtime-weed-to-treat-insomnia-and-sleep-apnea/) and promote progressive social issues [A new home for art, business and community: ‘Bloom Local’ looks to highlight LGBTQ+ artists and organizations](https://valleyadvocate.com/2023/01/27/a-new-home-for-art-business-and-community-bloom-local-looks-to-highlight-lgbtq-artists-and-organizations/). In general, the news is reported factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
