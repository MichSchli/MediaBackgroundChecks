History
Founded in 1872, The Boston Globe is an American daily broadsheet newspaper based in Boston, Massachusetts. The Boston Globe has been awarded 23 Pulitzer Prizes since 1966. The Boston Globe is one of the largest newspapers in the USA.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Globe is owned by Boston Globe Media Partners, LLC, and is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Boston Globe covers local news via journalists in the greater Boston area. The national news is also derived from journalists and republished from other sources such as the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). There is minimal use of loaded language in headlines: [Trump says he will end birthright citizenship via executive order](https://www.bostonglobe.com/news/nation/2018/10/30/trump-will-sign-order-ending-automatic-citizenship-for-children-born/k8P5qMqSIgPuF62Q9jVcAN/story.html).
Editorially, The Globe favors the left in both story selection and consistent Democratic candidate
[endorsements](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/). For example, in 2024, they [endorsed](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2023/04/25/opinion/president-biden-reelection-age-donald-trump/) Democratic Presidential candidate Joe Biden for President.
