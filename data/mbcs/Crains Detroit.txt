History
Founded in 1916, Crain’s Detroit is a weekly business magazine serving the Detroit metro area and the state of Michigan. The current editor-in-chief is
[Ken Crain](https://www.crainsdetroit.com/staff/all-staff-page). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Crain’s Detroit is owned by Crain’s Communications, a 3rd generation family-owned business based in Detriot. Revenue is derived through advertising, subscription fees, and businesses’ digital marketing.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Crain’s Detroit reports on business, covering politics, energy, health care, retail, and other sectors. Articles and headlines do not use loaded language such as this
[Eastern Michigan to rename engineering and tech college after $5 million alumni gift](https://www.crainsdetroit.com/education/eastern-michigan-rename-engineering-and-tech-college-after-5-million-alumni-gift). All stories are properly sourced to credible media outlets. National news stories are derived from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Crain’s Detroit does not specifically publish op-eds; however, they report on politics covering both sides. While slightly more lean, very slightly left, there is not enough to change their classification of least biased.
