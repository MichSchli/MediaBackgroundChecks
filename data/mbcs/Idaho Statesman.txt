History
Founded in 1864, the Idaho Statesman is the largest circulating print newspaper in Idaho.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Idaho Statesman is owned by
[Chatham Asset Management,](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-08-30/behind-trump-s-tabloid-king-a-connected-and-flush-hedge-fund) a New Jersey-based hedge fund that holds a significant equity stake in Postmedia, and majority ownership of American Media Inc., which owns the [National Enquirer](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/national-enquirer/). [David Pecker](https://www.thestar.com/business/2016/10/19/executive-from-national-enquirer-parent-joins-postmedia-board.html), who owns American Media, Inc., joined the Postmedia board and then [resigned ](https://www.canadalandshow.com/postmedia-david-pecker-donald-trump/)from the board of Postmedia in 2018 due to his involvement of hush payments on behalf of his “friends,” including President Trump and Harvey Weinstein. Advertising and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Idaho Statesman covers local news with journalists and reporters throughout the Boise and greater Idaho region with minimal bias such as this
[What do we know about University of Idaho murder charges? Here’s the newest timeline](https://www.idahostatesman.com/news/local/crime/article270810602.html#storylink=mainstage_lead). For national news, they use the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
The Idaho Statesman always sources their information properly.
[Editorially](http://www.idahostatesman.com/opinion/editorials/#navlink=subnav), there is a balance between right and left-leaning opinions with just a slight edge to the left. The Idaho Statesman typically [endorses](https://ballotpedia.org/Idaho_Statesman) Democratic candidates.
