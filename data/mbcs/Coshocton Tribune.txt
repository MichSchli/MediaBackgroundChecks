History
The Coshocton Tribune is a newspaper that serves Coshocton, Ohio, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. coshoctontribune.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Coshocton Tribune. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Coshocton Tribune covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Coshocton, Ohio, area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Coshocton Mayor Mark Mills explains why an assistant safety service director is needed](https://www.coshoctontribune.com/story/news/local/coshocton-county/2023/12/14/coshoctons-new-assistant-safety-service-director-will-earn-62500/71885849007/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Coshocton Tribune does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Climate change matters to young Republican voters. Can the GOP deal?](https://www.coshoctontribune.com/story/news/politics/elections/2023/12/15/young-republicans-climate-change-gop-candidates/71580864007/) After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
