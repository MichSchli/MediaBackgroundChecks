History
[Founded in 2014](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/about-the-daily-vox/), The Daily Vox is a South African news and opinion website that is geared toward younger citizens. The website was [founded](https://www.journalism.co.uk/news/the-daily-vox-is-training-young-reporters-to-establish-new-traditions-in-south-african-journalism/s2/a667851/) by Khadija Patel and Azad Essa. According to their [about page](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/who-we-are/) “The Daily Vox aims to put the young citizen at the centre of news. This does not mean we are a team of citizen journalists. Instead, our journalists place people at the centre of the agenda.”
For details on South African Government and Media, influence click here:
[South Africa Government and Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/south-africa-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
The Daily Vox is independently owned and funded through donations. They list their top funders
[here](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/about-the-daily-vox/our-supporters/). One notable supporter is Open Society South Africa, which is a part of George Soros’s [Open Society Foundations](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/open-society-foundations/).
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Daily Vox publishes original news and commentary covering a range of topics such as politics, education, economics, and accountability. Articles and headlines rarely use loaded emotional language such as this
[The calls for Basic Income Grant, explained](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/the-calls-for-basic-income-grant-explained/). This story, like most is properly sourced to credible outlets such as the [World Economic Forum](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/world-economic-forum/).
Editorially, they advocate for progressive left policies such as social justice for
[LGBTQ](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/lgbqtilife-navigating-life-in-student-leadership-and-the-church-as-a-gender-diverse-person/), [Income Equality](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/the-calls-for-basic-income-grant-explained/), and concern for [Climate change](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/how-we-could-have-stopped-climate-change-but-did-not/). When covering USA politics, they have a favorable view of Joe Biden and a negative view of former President Trump: [This is what Biden must do to reverse some of Trump’s worst decisions](https://www.thedailyvox.co.za/this-is-what-biden-must-do-to-reverse-some-of-trumps-worst-decisions/). In general, the news is reported factually and with a strong left-leaning bias.
