History
[Founded in 1881](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laredo_Morning_Times), The Laredo Morning Times (LMTonline.com) is a daily broadsheet newspaper located in Laredo, Texas. The website has an English and Spanish version. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Laredo Morning Times is owned by
[Hearst Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearst_Communications), which owns newspapers, magazines, and television stations, including the [San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), [Cosmopolitan](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cosmopolitan/), and [Esquire.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/) The newspaper and website are funded through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Laredo Morning Times covers local news through staff journalists with minimal bias such as this:
[Man arrested in connection with stabbing incident in South Laredo dating back to 2014](https://www.lmtonline.com/local/crime/article/Man-arrested-in-connection-with-stabbing-incident-14069579.php). National and international news is delivered through wire services such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The Laredo Morning Times has endorsed Democrats and Republicans; however, in
[2012](https://www.lmtonline.com/opinion/editorials/article/LMT-names-endorsements-for-November-election-10057349.php), they endorsed Republicans Mitt Romney and Ted Cruz. Further, editorials and op-eds tend to favor the right with articles such as this: [The post-liberal dead end](https://www.lmtonline.com/opinion/commentary/article/The-post-liberal-dead-end-13998673.php). In general, news reporting contains low bias and is properly sourced; however, editorially, there is a moderate right-leaning bias.
