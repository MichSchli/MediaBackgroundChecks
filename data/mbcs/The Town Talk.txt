History
[Founded in 1883](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Town_Talk), The Town Talk is the major broadsheet newspaper of Central Louisiana that is a part of the USA Today Network. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Town Talk is owned by
[Gannett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gannett), which publishes its flagship paper [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In 2019, Gannett was [purchased and merged](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20190805005547/en/New-Media-Investment-Group-Acquire-Gannett) with Gatehouse media, retaining the Gannett name. The paper and website generate revenue through subscriptions (paywall) and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Town Talk covers local news through staff journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Hearing set for motion to suppress evidence in Alexandria homicide](https://www.thetowntalk.com/story/news/2019/08/28/hearing-set-motion-suppress-evidence-alexandria-homicide/2143801001/). National news is derived through [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and the [Washington Post.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/)
Editorially, Town Talk no longer offers political endorsements. Further, a review of their opinion page shows a diverse set of opinions that favor both the
[left](https://www.thetowntalk.com/story/opinion/columnists/2019/08/05/donald-trumps-rhetoric-could-exacerbate-race-war/1888350001/) and [right](https://www.thetowntalk.com/story/opinion/columnists/2019/08/18/rise-anti-semitism-left/2019460001/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual, while op-eds are reasonably balanced.
