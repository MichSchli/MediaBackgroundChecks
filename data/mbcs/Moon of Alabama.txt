History
[Moon Of Alabama](http://www.moonofalabama.org/about.html) is a political blog founded in 2004. According to its about page, they are an independent and open forum for members of another blog called the Whiskey Bar. Moon Of Alabama states its purpose as “to discuss politics, economics, philosophy and blogger Billmon’s Whiskey Bar writings.” A Daily Kos [article](https://www.dailykos.com/stories/2008/2/17/458645/-) describes [Blogger Billmon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billmon) (pseudonym) as one of the earliest and leading guest bloggers of [Daily Kos](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-kos/) and a self-admitted financial writer working for a big Wall Street firm. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The editor and the person who runs the blog are anonymous, and the about page states that “Bernhard started and still runs the site, and you can reach the current administrator of this site by emailing Bernhard at MoonofA_at_aol.com (replacing _at_ with @).” The Moon of Alabama does not have advertising, but they do have a donate button, so we assume this is the primary funding method.
Analysis/ Bias
Moon of Alabama looks at the issues from a progressive perspective; for example, this
[article](http://www.moonofalabama.org/2018/03/john-bolton-a-man-with-a-hammer-looking-for-nails.html) describes Trump’s cabinet as follows: “Soon Netanyahoo will have the cabinet in place in DC he always dreamed of. A hawkish Pompeo at State, a [real torturer](http://www.moonofalabama.org/2018/03/trump-votes-for-rexit-torture-girl-to-become-cia-director.html) as head of the CIA, and now Bolton are already sufficient to protect Israel’s further expansion” The majority of blog posts utilize emotionally loaded headlines such as [“Trump Asks Russia To Roll Over – It Won’t”](http://www.moonofalabama.org/2018/04/trump-asks-russia-to-roll-over-it-wont.html) and [“John Bolton Wants No Deal With North Korea Or Iran – But Is There Any Other Choice?”](http://www.moonofalabama.org/2018/06/john-bolton-wants-no-deal-with-north-korea-or-iran-but-is-there-any-other-choice.html)
Moon of Alabama utilizes questionable sources such as
[RT News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/rt-news/), [Fox News,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news/) as well as credible sources such as [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Haaretz](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/haaretz/), [The Jerusalem Post, ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-jerusalem-post/) [Politico, ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/)cisac.fsi.stanford.edu, and blogs such as johnhelmer.org.
In this update, we find that Moon of Alabama has become sympathetic toward Russia and the war in Ukraine, often citing misinformation. For example, in this article they claim a false flag
[Neo-Nazis In Ukraine Fake Incidents To Gain More ‘Western’ Support – Updated](https://www.moonofalabama.org/2022/03/neo-nazis-in-ukraine-fake-incidents-to-gain-more-western-support.html). Media credibility rater [Newsguard](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/newsguard/) has detailed an [extensive list of false and misleading claims](https://api.newsguardtech.com/label/moonofalabama.org?cid=1a2d1594-d48e-4d8e-859b-fd76d29dcae1) regarding Ukraine. Generally, Moon of Alabama is a pro-Russian conspiracy website.
