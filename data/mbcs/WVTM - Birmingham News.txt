History
Founded in 1949, WVTM – Birmingham News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Birmingham, Alabama, United States. WVTM.com is the associated website that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime for the greater Birmingham area. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WVTM – Birmingham News is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) Hearst has donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WVTM – Birmingham News reports on news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Birmingham and Tuscaloosa areas. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias in wording such as this
[Springville police search for missing teen](https://www.wvtm13.com/article/springville-police-search-for-missing-teen/39684233#). Most stories are accompanied by a video from the TV station. National news is typically covered by the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other Hearst affiliates.
Editorially, WVTM – Birmingham News does not publish editorial pieces and when reporting on national politics there is minimal bias as the primary source is usually the Associated Press. In general, the news is reported factually with minimal editorializing.
