History
KUAF – NPR – Fayetteville is an NPR FM radio station (91.3 FM) based in Fayetteville, Arkansas serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KUAF – NPR – Fayetteville is owned and broadcast by the University of Arkansas. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [U.S. Marshals Museum opens in Fort Smith tomorrow](https://www.kuaf.com/show/ozarks-at-large/2023-06-30/u-s-marshals-museum-opens-in-fort-smith-tomorrow). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[Thinking about living with climate change](https://www.kuaf.com/show/ozarks-at-large/2023-06-09/thinking-about-living-with-climate-change). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
