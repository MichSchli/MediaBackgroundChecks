History
Fight for the Future (FFTF) is a nonprofit advocacy group in the area of digital rights founded in 2011. The group aims to promote causes related to copyright legislation and online privacy and censorship through the use of the internet.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Fight for the Future is a nonprofit funded by donations. They are transparent as they list their financials here.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Fight for the Future advocates for net neutrality and issues related to digital freedom. FFTF uses minimally loaded language in their headlines and articles. All information is well sourced to credible information and usually links to .gov and .org sites. A
[factual search](http://www.politifact.com/personalities/fight-future/) reveals only one claim that was rated Mostly True.
Editorially, they align with moderate left with articles focused on human rights such as this
[25+ human rights organizations demand Congress center the needs of marginalized people when regulating decentralized tech](https://www.fightforthefuture.org/news/2022-01-11-human-rights-big-tech-web3-letter/). In general, more stories favor the left, and news is reported factually.
