History
Worcester – Spectrum News is an American cable TV news channel based in Worcester, Massachusetts. The channel provides news across the state, from Beacon Hill to the Berkshires, and including the Massachusetts congressional delegation on Capitol Hill. Worcester – Spectrum News is currently owned by
[Charter Communications, Inc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charter_Communications). They provide 24-hour local news, politics, weather news, and more. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Time Warner Cable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectrum_News) launched its first regional cable news channel on September 8, 1992, with the New York City-based [NY1](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/spectrum-news-ny1/). In 2013, Charter Communications announced that they are re-branding its residential services to Charter Spectrum. In 2016, Charter acquired Time Warner Cable. Next, Time Warner Cable was phased out in favor of Spectrum, the brand used by Charter to market its services. The main revenue sources for Worcester – Spectrum News are ads and subscription fees collected by cable operators who [package programs](https://www.cnbc.com/2015/04/20/how-do-cable-companies-make-their-money.html) in “bundles.“
Analysis / Bias
In review, Worcester – Spectrum News reports local news with minimal loaded words and bias, such as
[Arrest warrant issued for owner of Millbury tree cutting service accused of scamming customers](https://spectrumnews1.com/ma/worcester/news/2021/07/15/tr-landscaping-millbury-warrant). Worcester – Spectrum News also republishes [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) articles and covers stories with both points of view represented. Typically, they utilize credible sources such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/); however, they also source through interviews and large quotes.
Worcester – Spectrum News does not publish opinion pieces but does report on politics with minimal bias, such as this
[U.S.-Mexico border encounters higher in June, one-third repeat attempts](https://spectrumnews1.com/ma/worcester/news/2021/07/16/border-encounters-june-higher-one-third-repeat-attempts) In general, they both sides of issues and reports news factually.
