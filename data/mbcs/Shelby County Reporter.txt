History
The Shelby County Reporter is a newspaper published in Columbiana, Alabama. shelbycountyreporter.com covers local, national, crime, and sports news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Shelby County Reporter is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Shelby County Reporter primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Pedestrian struck and killed in Pelham interstate accident](https://www.shelbycountyreporter.com/2022/09/18/pedestrian-struck-and-killed-in-pelham-interstate-accident/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Shelby County Reporter labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Shelby County Reporter is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
