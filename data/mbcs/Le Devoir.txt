History
[Le Devoir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Le_Devoir) is an independent French-language newspaper headquartered in Montreal, Canada. It is distributed in Quebec and the rest of Canada. Le Devoir’s political alignment is described as Quebec Nationalism, Quebec Independence, and Social Democracy.
According to the
[Canadian Encyclopedia](http://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/le-devoir/), Le Devoir was founded by Henri Bourassa in 1910 as a pan-Canadian-nationalist, pro-French-Canadian, pro-Catholic, anti-British, but independent newspaper.

Analysis / Bias
According to
[CBC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbc-news-canadian-broadcasting/), [Le Devoir](http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/montreal/federal-election-endorsements-quebec-newspaper-1.3276416) endorsed the Bloc Québécois under party leader Gilles Duceppe during the 2015 Canadian Federal elections. [The Bloc Québécois (BQ)](http://www.blocquebecois.org/le-parti/declaration-de-principe/) is a federal political party in Canada devoted to Quebec nationalism and promoting Quebec sovereignty.
Le Devoir publishes stories with minimally loaded emotional headlines, such as “
[Canada only has Trudeau](http://www.ledevoir.com/politique/canada/462430/sondage-leger-le-canada-n-en-a-que-pour-trudeau),” and is always well-sourced to credible media through [quotes](http://www.ledevoir.com/international/etats-unis/498878/trump-aurait-revele-des-informations-classifiees-au-chef-de-la-diplomatie-russe). When reporting USA news Le Devoir generally covers both sides of the story. Finally, a [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/) institute survey found that 71% of respondents [trust their news coverage](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2021/canada) and 7% do not, ranking them #4 in trust among the major French-language Canadian news providers.
