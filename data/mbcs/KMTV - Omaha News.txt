History
Founded in 1949, KMTV – Omaha News is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated station licensed to Omaha, Nebraska, United States. 3newsnow.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KMTV – Omaha News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://scripps.com/our-brands/local-media/), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
KMTV – Omaha News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Omaha, Nebraska, area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[Omaha Police investigating stabbing at Burke High School, 2 students injured](https://www.3newsnow.com/news/local-news/omaha-police-investigating-stabbing-at-burke-high-school-2-students-injured). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Missouri lawmakers open session focused on ballot measures](https://www.3newsnow.com/news/political/missouri-lawmakers-open-session-focused-on-ballot-measures). In general, they present low-biased, fact-based reporting.
