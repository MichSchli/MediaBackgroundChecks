History
The Grand Forks Herald is a newspaper published in Grand Forks, North Dakota. The website grandforksherald.com features local and national news, politics, sports, entertainment, business, classifieds, and health.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Grand Forks Herald is owned by
[Forum Communications Company](https://www.forumcomm.com/brands-overview/), which is based in North Dakota and has additional locations across South Dakota, Minnesota, and Wisconsin. Advertising and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Grand Forks Herald reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Grand Forks, ND region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[Sens. John Hoeven and Kevin Cramer advise Grand Forks to move on from Fufeng project](https://www.grandforksherald.com/news/north-dakota/sens-john-hoeven-and-kevin-cramer-advise-grand-forks-to-move-on-from-fufeng-project). Further, the website publishes national and international news from credible sources such as the [LA Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/). Finally, all information is sourced via first-person accounts or through credible media.
Editorially, The Grand Forks Herald reasonably represents both sides. However, a review of columnists demonstrates more favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually and with right-leaning editorial bias.
