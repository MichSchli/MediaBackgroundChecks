History
[Founded in 1876,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Gainesville_Sun) The Gainesville Sun is a newspaper published daily in Gainesville, Florida, United States, covering the North-Central portion of the state. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Gainesville Sun is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Gainesville Sun reports on local crime, business, lifestyle, and entertainment in the Gainesville region. Local news is reported with minimal bias such as this:
[High Springs woman accused of stabbing husband Friday night](https://www.gainesville.com/story/news/crime/2021/08/28/woman-accused-stabbing-husband-friday-night-high-springs/5635277001/). National and international news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Gannett publications.
