History
The Richmond Palladium-Item serves Richmond, Indiana, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. pal-item.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Richmond Palladium-Item. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Richmond Palladium-Item covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Richmond, Indiana area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Incumbent commissioner, auditor beaten in GOP primary](https://www.pal-item.com/story/news/politics/elections/2022/05/04/indiana-primary-election-results-2022-wayne-county-incumbent-commissioner-auditor-beaten-gop/9633896002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Richmond Palladium-Item does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Chuck Avery: Making amends to teachers and students](https://www.pal-item.com/story/opinion/columnists/2022/02/12/chuck-avery-making-amends-teachers-and-students/6691740001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
