History
Founded in 2000, Stuff is a New Zealand news website and is also the
[most popular](https://www.nielsen.com/nz/en/press-releases/2018/new-zealands-top-local-news-sites-july-2018-rankings/) news website in New Zealand. Stuff publishes New Zealand breaking news, weather, sport, politics, video, entertainment, business, and life and style content from Stuff Ltd’s newspapers. [Read our profile on New Zealand media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-zealand-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Stuff is owned by
[Stuff Limited](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stuff_(company)), which is a privately held news media company operating in New Zealand. They also [publish nine daily newspapers](https://www.nielsen.com/nz/en/press-releases/2018/new-zealands-top-local-news-sites-july-2018-rankings/), including New Zealand’s second and third-highest circulation daily newspapers, The Dominion Post and The Press, and the highest circulation weekly, Sunday Star-Times. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
Although political censorship exists in New Zealand, it is limited. According to
[Reporters without borders](https://rsf.org/en/new-zealand), New Zealand ranks 9 out of 180 countries in the World Press Freedom Index.
In review, Stuff covers a variety of news including “National, World, Business, Opinion, Sport, Entertainment, Life & Style.” Local and New Zealand national news is conducted through reporters and utilizes minimal bias such as this
[Man shot in Wellsford, homicide investigation under way](https://www.stuff.co.nz/national/crime/300170230/man-shot-in-wellsford-homicide-investigation-under-way). World news is reported through Stuff reporters as well as outside outlets such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/) and [The Telegraph](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-daily-telegraph-australia/).
Editorially, Stuff leans left with concern for climate change
[Working from home: What’s your carbon footprint?](https://www.stuff.co.nz/environment/climate-news/300169979/working-from-home-whats-your-carbon-footprint) and favorable reporting on the left-leaning labour party leader and former prime minister Jacinda Ardern such as this [US presidential candidate Pete Buttigieg uses Jacinda Ardern as age example during debate](https://www.stuff.co.nz/world/americas/114649627/us-presidential-candidate-pete-buttigieg-uses-jacinda-ardern-as-age-example-during-debate). In general, the news is reported factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
