History
The Delaware County Daily Times is a newspaper published in Primos, Pennsylvania, United States. delcotimes.com is the website for the paper covering local news, sports, crime, lifestyle, and opinion.
Read our profile on the
[USA government’s influence on media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
The Delaware County Daily Times is owned by
[MediaNews Group](https://www.medianewsgroup.com/our-brands/) and published by Digital First Media, which publishes 190 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Delaware County Daily Times publishes local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Donor’s $125,000 helps Brookhaven hire new police officers.](https://www.delcotimes.com/2023/03/10/donors-125000-helps-brookhaven-hire-new-police-officers/) National and international news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/).
Editorially, The Delaware County Daily Times publishes an appropriate mix of right and left-leaning opinions. In general, they report factually and with credible sources, while op-eds are mostly balanced with differing viewpoints.
