History
Founded in 1957, WTVA – Tupelo News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)/ [ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Tupelo, Mississippi, United States. WTVA.com, also known as Island News, is the associated website that provides news on crime, sports, and lifestyle for the greater Tupelo area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Allen Media LLC, a subsidiary of Entertainment Studios, owns WTVA – Tupelo News. The owner of Entertainment Studios, Byron Allen, is a frequent
[donor to Democratic](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/byron-allen-on-sinclair-politics-130236929.html) candidates and causes. Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WTVA – Tupelo News publishes news for the Tupelo and surrounding area through reporters and with minimal bias, such as this
[Alabama prison official missing after escorting inmate](https://www.wtva.com/news/alabama-prison-official-missing-after-escorting-inmate/article_84166875-9408-5bd3-aa4a-d9958ff0543b.html). Videos from the TV Station accompany many local news stories. National and international news is typically republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/).
Editorially, WTVA – Tupelo News does not publish Op-eds, and story selection is balanced.
