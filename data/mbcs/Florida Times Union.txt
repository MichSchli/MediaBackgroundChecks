History
[Founded in 1864](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Florida_Times-Union), the Florida Times-Union is a major daily broadsheet newspaper in Jacksonville, Florida, USA. Widely known as the oldest newspaper in the state. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Florida Times-Union is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, local news is covered via reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Coronavirus: All Duval County beaches to close; testing to begin at TIAA Bank Field Saturday](https://www.jacksonville.com/news/20200320/coronavirus-all-duval-county-beaches-to-close-testing-to-begin-at-tiaa-bank-field-saturday). National and international news is covered through the USA Today Network and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Florida Times-Union typically
[endorsed Republican candidates](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) under previous ownership. However, since being purchased by Gannett in 2017, there has been a moderation in editorial positions.
