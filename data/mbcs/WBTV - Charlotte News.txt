History
[Founded in 1949](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WBTV), WBTV is a CBS-affiliated television station licensed to Charlotte, North Carolina, United States. WBTV.com is the associated website that reports news, weather, sports, and crime for the Charlotte area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WBTV.com is owned by
[Gray Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Television), which operates 145 stations across the USA. Revenue is derived through sponsored content and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WBTV.com provides news coverage for the Charlotte, NC, market and the surrounding areas. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias and often has video provided by the TV station:
[Two people killed in east Charlotte shooting](https://www.wbtv.com/2020/10/19/dead-east-charlotte-shooting/). The national news is provided through other Gray Television affiliates, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and [CBS News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/). International news generally comes from [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/) and the Associated Press.
Editorially, WBTV does not offer an opinion page. When covering politics, utilizes minimally loaded words such as this
[Gov. Roy Cooper greets Presidential candidate Joe Biden during a campaign visit to N.C.](https://www.wbtv.com/2020/10/18/gov-roy-cooper-greets-presidential-candidate-joe-biden-during-campaign-visit-nc/) In general, WBTV reports news factually and with minimal bias.
