History
Founded in 1980 by
[Steve Endean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Endean), The Human Rights Campaign (HRC) is the largest LGBT civil rights advocacy group and [political lobbying](http://www.cnn.com/2007/POLITICS/08/10/gay.forum/) organization in the United States. According to their [about page](https://www.hrc.org/hrc-story/hrc-foundation), “The Human Rights Campaign represents a force of more than 3 million members and supporters nationwide. As the largest national lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer civil rights organization, HRC envisions a world where LGBTQ people are ensured of their basic equal rights and can be open, honest, and safe at home, at work, and in the community.” The current President is [Chad Griffin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad_Griffin). Analysis / Bias
In review, The Human Rights Campaign focuses on protecting and expanding rights for LGBT individuals, advocating for marriage equality, anti-discrimination and hate crimes legislation, and HIV/AIDS advocacy. HRC also publishes a blog that is primarily used to endorse or criticize political candidates running for office. The moderate use of loaded language typically favors the left, such as this:
[Kavanaugh Passes the Test of These anti-equality Organizations—And Most Certainly Fails Ours.](http://www.hrc.org/blog/kavanaugh-passes-the-test-of-these-anti-equality-organizations-but-most-cer) Blog posts are properly sourced to factual media outlets.
The Human Rights Campaign usually endorses Democratic candidates; however, they have been criticized for occasionally selecting
[Republican candidates](https://www.hrc.org/press/human-rights-campaign-endorses-senator-mark-kirk-for-re-election) that support gay marriage. Further, a [look at donations](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000000158&cycle=2010) reveals that the HRC has donated 82% of its money to Democrats since 1990, or $13,029,699.
