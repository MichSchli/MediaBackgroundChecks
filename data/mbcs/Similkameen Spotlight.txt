History
Similkameen Spotlight is a newspaper published in Similkameen, British Columbia, Canada. The website saobserver.net covers news, sports, crime, and business for the greater Similkameen River Valley, BC area.
[Read our profile on the Canadian government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Black Press Media](https://www.blackpress.ca/about-us/) owns Similkameen Spotlight. Black Press, based in British Columbia, Canada, publishes over 170 titles across Canada and the western United States. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
Similkameen Spotlight covers news for Similkameen and the surrounding area with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Fiery crash closes Highway 1 south of Cache Creek](https://www.similkameenspotlight.com/news/fiery-crash-closes-highway-1-south-of-cache-creek-4999441). National and international news is published through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-canadian-press/).
Editorially, Similkameen Spotlight does not regularly produce op-eds. However, political news coverage tends to lean left. Generally, news reporting is factual, with a left-leaning bias in story selection.
