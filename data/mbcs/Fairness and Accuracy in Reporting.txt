History
Founded in 1986, Fairness and Accuracy In Reporting (Fair) is a media criticism organization based in New York City. Jeff Cohen and Martin A. Lee founded the organization. FAIR
[monitors](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Fairness-and-Accuracy-in-Reporting) the U.S. news media for “inaccuracy, bias, and censorship” and advocates for a greater diversity of perspectives in news reporting. It is opposed to corporate ownership of media entities and calls for the break-up of media conglomerates. In other words, it holds a progressive stance. [Read our country’s media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
FAIR is a nonprofit funded through donations and a store selling products.
Analysis / Bias
In review, articles generally use moderate to low biased words in both headlines and articles such as:
[‘The Rule Is Designed to Deter People From Reporting’.](https://fair.org/home/the-rule-is-designed-to-deter-people-from-reporting/) This story is very well sourced to credible media that we rate High for factual reporting. Although FAIR stories tend to favor the left politically, they are all sourced properly and written in a mostly neutral tone.
