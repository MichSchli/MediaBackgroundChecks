History
Founded in 1870, The Greeley Tribune is a print newspaper from Greeley, Colorado, that serves the northern Colorado region. According to their about page, “We cover Weld County, the ninth-largest agricultural producing county in the United States.”
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Greeley Tribune is owned by
[MediaNews Group](https://www.medianewsgroup.com/our-brands/) and published by Digital First Media, which publishes 190 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Greeley Tribune publishes local news using journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Greeley’s police community remembers Pat Foelske, ‘a woman of many firsts’](https://www.greeleytribune.com/news/greeleys-police-community-remembers-pat-foelske-a-woman-of-many-firsts/). [National and World news](https://hosted.ap.org/greeleytrib/tags/general-news) comes straight from the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, they often
[endorse Republican candidates](https://www.greeleytribune.com/opinion/tribune-endorsements-recapping-our-picks-for-key-contested-primary-races/) and favor op-eds that support right-leaning causes.
