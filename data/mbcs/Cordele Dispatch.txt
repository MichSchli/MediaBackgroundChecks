History
The Cordele Dispatch is a newspaper published in Cordele, Georgia. The paper covers local, national, crime, and sports news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Cordele Dispatch is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Cordele Dispatch primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Eight Individuals Charged in Alleged $30 Million Unemployment Benefits Scheme](https://www.cordeledispatch.com/2022/12/01/eight-individuals-charged-in-alleged-30-million-unemployment-benefits-scheme/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Cordele Dispatch labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, Cordele Dispatch is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
