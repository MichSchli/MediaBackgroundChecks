History
Founded in 2005, La Nueva Televisora del Sur (teleSUR, English: The New Television Station of the South) is a multi-state funded, pan–Latin American terrestrial and satellite television network sponsored by the governments of Venezuela, Cuba, Ecuador, Nicaragua, Uruguay, and Bolivia that is headquartered in Caracas, Venezuela. TeleSUR has been accused of being a propaganda tool for
[Hugo Chavez](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/5046080.stm) and his successors. [Read our profile on Venezuela’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/venezuela-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
TeleSUR lacks transparency as they do not explain ownership, however, they are primarily funded through the governments of Venezuela, Cuba, Ecuador, Nicaragua, Uruguay, and Bolivia. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, TeleSur reports Latin American and world news with minimally loaded wording such as this
[Venezuela Election Update: PSUV Coalition Wins 67% of the Vote](https://www.telesurenglish.net/news/Venezuela-Election-Update-PSUV-Coalition-Wins-67-of-the-Vote-20201206-0011.html). Many stories promote Socialist government propaganda as well as very strong anti-American sentiments such as this [Two Sides of the Same Coin: VenezolanosConBiden and MAGAzuela](https://www.telesurenglish.net/opinion/Two-Sides-of-the-Same-Coin-VenezolanosConBiden-and-MAGAzuela-20200709-0006.html). Like most stories evaluated this one is completely unsourced. In general, they promote propaganda and poorly source their information.
