History
KCLU – NPR – Santa Barbara is an NPR FM radio station (1340 AM) based in Santa Barbara, California, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KCLU – NPR – Santa Barbara is owned and broadcast by California Lutheran University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KCLU – NPR – Santa Barbara website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Cal State Channel Islands gets archives of late Ventura County Supervisor Carmen Ramirez](https://www.kclu.org/2023-08-30/cal-state-channel-islands-gets-archives-of-late-ventura-county-supervisor-carmen-ramirez). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[Carbon capturing farmers and if their efforts are impacting the fight against climate change](https://www.kclu.org/theoneohone/episode6/carboncapture) Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
