History
[Founded in 1878](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Louis_Post-Dispatch) by Joseph Pulitzer, The St. Louis Post-Dispatch is a daily newspaper from St. Louis, Missouri. It is the largest daily newspaper in the metropolitan area by circulation. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by
[Lee Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_Enterprises), which according to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) has donated 88% of its money to [Republican politicians](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052990&cycle=2014) since 1990. The St. Louis Post-Dispatch is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the St. Louis Post-Dispatch reports local news, crime, and sports through local reporters with minimal bias, such as this:
[Missouri COVID-19 cases pass 7,000, as Illinois reports nearly 2,000 cases in a single day](https://www.stltoday.com/lifestyles/health-med-fit/coronavirus/missouri-covid-19-cases-pass-7-000-as-illinois-reports-nearly-2-000-cases-in/article_ea7cf4d7-e039-5824-8c15-d7cfa3fd2414.html). National and world news is derived from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the St. Louis Post-Dispatch has endorsed Democratic presidential candidates for the
[last 40 years](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/). A review of their op-ed pages also reveals more that favor the left. In general, they report news factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
