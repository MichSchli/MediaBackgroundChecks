History
[Founded in 1870](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Salt_Lake_Tribune) as the Mormon Tribune, the Salt Lake Tribune is a daily newspaper published in the city of Salt Lake City, Utah, with the largest paid circulation in the state. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Salt Lake Tribune is owned and published by The Utah Journalism Foundation. Revenue is derived through advertising, sponsored content, subscriptions, and donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Salt Lake Tribune reports local news through reporters with minimal bias such as this
[Coronavirus tests top 95,000 in Utah, no new deaths reported](https://www.sltrib.com/news/2020/04/26/coronavirus-cases-top/). National and international news is republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and the [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/).
Editorially, the Tribune has endorsed Democratic Presidential candidates in the last 3 elections (2008, 2012, 2016). A review of their opinion page reveals that they republish New York Times commentary, which typically comes from a left-leaning perspective. They also offer Tribune editorials, which are mostly balanced, however slightly more favor the left such as this:
[Tribune Editorial: How a crisis can build democracy](https://www.sltrib.com/opinion/editorial/2020/03/25/tribune-editorial-how/). In general, the Salt Lake Tribune reports news factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
