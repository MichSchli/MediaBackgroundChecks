History
According to Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington (CREW), they are a nonpartisan watchdog group that targets unethical conduct in government. CREW’s
[about page](https://www.citizensforethics.org/who-we-are/) describes itself as “Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington (CREW) uses aggressive legal action, in-depth research, and bold communications to reduce the influence of money in politics and help foster a government that is ethical and accountable.”
CREW was launched in 2003 and is headquartered in Washington DC. The co-founder and chair are Ambassador (Ret.)
[Norman Eisen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norman_L._Eisen) and Noah Bookbinder are the Executive directors. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
CREW is a nonprofit 501(c)(3) organization funded through donations. The website does not indicate top donors or financial information, thereby lacking transparency by our standards.
Analysis / Bias
CREW is best known for filing complaints alerting the public about corrupt politicians, whether it is about campaign finance, ethics, tax, and exposing both
[Democrats](https://www.citizensforethics.org/press-release/maxine-waters-named-most-corrupt-member-of-congress/) and [Republicans](https://www.citizensforethics.org/press-release/joe-walsh-named-most-corrupt-member-of-congress/). CREW also publishes its [legal filings](https://www.citizensforethics.org/legal/legal-filings/) and publishes investigation reports under the category “ [research.](https://www.citizensforethics.org/research/)”
Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington (CREW) republishes news articles from left-leaning sources such as
[Newsweek](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/newsweek/) and [The Daily Beast.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-beast/) Under their blog category, they publish stories with emotionally loaded headlines such as [“HAS WILBUR ROSS FULLY DIVESTED FROM THE CONTROVERSIAL BANK OF CYPRUS?”](https://www.citizensforethics.org/wilbur-ross-fully-divested-controversial-bank-cyprus/) However, they utilize credible least biased sources such as the [Center for Public Integrity](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-public-integrity/), state.gov.com, and other credible left-leaning mainstream sources such as [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), [Business Insider](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/business-insider/), and [Mother Jones.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/mother-jones/)
In our most recent review, we found most articles and legal actions are aimed at conservatives, such as this
[CREW files amicus brief in Trump’s lawsuit against Jan. 6 committee](https://www.citizensforethics.org/legal-action/legal-complaints/crew-files-amicus-brief-in-trumps-lawsuit-against-jan-6-committee/). In general, CREW’s information moderately favors the left through advocacy and the use of left-leaning sources.
