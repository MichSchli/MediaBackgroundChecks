History
[Founded in 1873](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foster%27s_Daily_Democrat), Foster’s Daily Democrat is a daily broadsheet newspaper serving southeast New Hampshire and southwest Maine. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Foster’s Daily Democrat is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Foster’s Daily Democrat covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in reporting such as this,
[Name of Sanford city worker killed not yet being released](https://www.fosters.com/news/20191125/name-of-sanford-city-worker-killed-not-yet-being-released). National and international news comes from other Gatehouse publications and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual.
Editorially, Foster’s has long been known as a conservative paper; however, in 2016, they
[endorsed](https://www.fosters.com/news/20161016/our-view-clinton-is-best-candidate-for-president) Democratic candidate Hillary Clinton for President. A thorough review of editorials and opinion pieces reveals a mostly balanced left-right selection, with a few more opinion pieces favoring the left.
