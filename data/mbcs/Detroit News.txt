History
[Founded in 1873](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Detroit_News), The Detroit News is a newspaper from Detroit, Michigan. They have won three Pulitzer Prizes. The current editor is Jonathan Wolman. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Detroit News is owned by
[Digital First Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_First_Media), which owns several news media outlets around the USA. They are funded through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Detroit News is a newspaper that presents local news through journalists and with minimal bias such as this:
[Baby survivor of Imlay City fire ‘breathing on his own.’](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/michigan/2019/03/08/baby-survivor-imlay-city-fire-breathing-his-own/3106700002/) The Associated Press covers national news. Editorially, the Detroit News has never endorsed a Democratic Presidential candidate, and in 2016 they endorsed [Libertarian Gary Johnson.](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/opinion/editorials/2016/09/28/endorse-johnson-president/91254412/)
