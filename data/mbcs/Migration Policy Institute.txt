History
The Migration Policy Institute (MPI) is a non-partisan think tank established in 2001 by
[Kathleen Newland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kathleen_Newland) and Demetrios G. Papademetriou. According to their [about page](https://www.migrationpolicy.org/about/about-migration-policy-institute): “The Migration Policy Institute (MPI) is an independent, nonpartisan, nonprofit think tank in Washington, DC dedicated to analysis of the movement of people worldwide.” [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
MPI is funded by the
[Carnegie Endowment for International Peace](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/carnegie-endowment-for-international-peace/).
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Migration Policy Institute has a left leaning position regarding immigration. They are in favor of easier immigration and more humane policies toward illegal immigrants and refugees. Topic selection generally favors the left and uses minimal loaded words. For example, in this article they criticize the Trump administration’s policy toward immigration without the use of loaded language:
[Judge’s Pause of Trump Travel Ban Takes Page from Republican Challenge to Obama Executive Action on DACA](https://www.migrationpolicy.org/article/judge%E2%80%99s-pause-trump-travel-ban-takes-page-republican-challenge-obama-executive-action-daca). All information is sourced to credible media or government based and/or .org websites. The Migration Policy Institute is often cited by credible fact checkers due to their well researched information.
