History
[Founded in 1863](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle_Post-Intelligencer), The Seattle Post-Intelligencer is an online newspaper and former print newspaper covering Seattle, Washington, United States, and the surrounding metropolitan area. [Read our profile on Canadian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Seattle Post-Intelligencer is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) Hearst has donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
