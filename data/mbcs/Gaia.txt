History
[Founded in 1988](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaia,_Inc.) by Jirka Rysavy, Gaia, Inc is a company that produces video and TV programming that promotes meditation, yoga, and [alternative health](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-alternative-medicine/). Gaia.com is the associated website that publishes videos and articles that sometimes promote pseudoscience. According to their [about page](https://www.gaia.com/lp/about) “, they are a member-supported media network of truth-seekers and believers empowering an evolution of consciousness.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gaia, Inc is a publicly-traded company on the NASDAQ under GAIA. Revenue is derived through paid memberships for content as well as advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Gaia.com provides videos and information that promotes Yoga, Meditation, Documentaries, and films, as well as a section that breaks down videos into categories such as
[Emotional Healing](https://www.gaia.com/topic/emotional-healing). The website also features news and information articles that contain moderately loaded language, such as [The Wonderful Benefits of Japanese Knotweed](https://www.gaia.com/article/the-wonderful-benefits-of-japanese-knotweed). This story is primarily sourced back to Gaia’s videos. It should be pointed out that the claims made in this article are not supported by medical evidence.
Gaia.com frequently promotes pseudoscience, such as the
[dangers of fluoride](https://www.gaia.com/article/the-actual-truth-about-fluoride), which has been [debunked.](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4815618/) They also encourage other pseudosciences such as [astrology](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-astrology/), pseudo-archeology, and energy healing. Further, they promote [anti-GMO propaganda](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-gmo-dangers/) such as this [Exposing GMO Agendas with Jeffrey Smith](https://www.gaia.com/video/exposing-gmo-agendas-jeffrey-smith) and [vaccinations and autism links](https://www.gaia.com/video/vaccines-autism-and-cdc-jon-rappoport), which have been [thoroughly](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-anti-vaccination-anti-vax/) [debunked](https://www.cdc.gov/vaccinesafety/concerns/autism.html). Finally, they also promote [human-influenced climate denialism](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), such as this [Waging War Against Climate Science with Tim Ball](https://www.gaia.com/video/waging-war-against-climate-science-tim-ball).
Lastly, Gaia.com reports on
[conspiracy theories](https://archive.vn/20190110040848/https://www.gaia.com/topics) such as aliens, [chemtrails](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-chemtrails/), Illuminati, and secret space programs. Gaia generally promotes a wide variety of misleading and false information regarding science, medicine, and health.
