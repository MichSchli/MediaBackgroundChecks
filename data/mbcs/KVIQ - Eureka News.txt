History
Founded in 1958, KVIQ – Eureka News is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Eureka, California, United States. The associated website kiem-tv.com covers news, sports, crime, and weather. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Cox Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cox_Enterprises) owns KVIQ – Eureka News through their subsidiary Cox Media Group. They are a company that owns numerous newspapers, television stations, and radio stations, such as the [Atlanta Journal-Constitution](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/atlanta-journal-constitution/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KVIQ – Eureka News covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Eureka, California area. Local news is covered with minimally loaded language, such as this
[TRAILER, VEHICLES,AND SHED BURN IN FIRE](https://kiem-tv.com/2022/06/06/trailer-vehiclesand-shed-burn-in-fire/). Many local news stories contain videos from the TV station. The national news is primarily republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, KVIQ – Eureka News does not publish op-eds; however, when covering politics, they present info with minimal bias, such as this
[Five Dead – Including Gunman – in Mass Shooting in Tulsa, Oklahoma](https://kiem-tv.com/2022/06/01/five-dead-including-gunman-in-mass-shooting-in-tulsa-oklahoma/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
