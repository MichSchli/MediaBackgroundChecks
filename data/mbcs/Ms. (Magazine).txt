History
Founded in 1971,
[Ms.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ms._(magazine)) is an American progressive feminist magazine co-founded by second-wave feminists and sociopolitical activists Gloria Steinem and Dorothy Pitman Hughes. According to their [about page](https://msmagazine.com/about/), “Ms. remains the most trusted, popular source for feminist news and information in print and online.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Feminist Majority Foundation owns and publishes Ms. Magazine. The Feminist Majority Foundation is a nonprofit funded through donations, subscription fees, advertising, and merchandise sales.
Analysis / Bias
Ms. Magazine covers news and opinions from a feminist perspective. There is a strong focus on social justice, health, and LGBTQ rights. Articles and headlines contain moderately loaded emotional language such as this
[The Change Women Need: Systemic Reforms Power the Path to Equality](https://msmagazine.com/2021/05/19/women-politics-systemic-reforms-equality/). This original article, like most, is properly sourced to credible media outlets such as [World Economic Forum](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/world-economic-forum/) and [The 19th News.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-19th-news/) Ms. Magazine also features news from other sources such as Feminist Daily News.
Editorially, opinion aligns with the progressive left with commentary such as this
[Complacency Is Not An Option: Why States Must Protect Reproductive Rights Under The Biden Administration](https://msmagazine.com/2021/05/19/mississippi-abortion-states-supreme-court-roe-v-wade-biden-administration/). Politically they have an unfavorable view of former President Trump [How Trumpism Fostered Anti-Choice Violence](https://msmagazine.com/2021/02/09/trump-capitol-riots-women-abortion-anti-choice-violence-anti-abortion-extremists-propaganda/).
