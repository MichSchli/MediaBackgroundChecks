History
The Edwardsburg Argus is a newspaper published in Edwardsburg, Michigan. The paper covers local, national, crime, and sports news.

Funded by / Ownership
The Edwardsburg Argus is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Edwardsburg Argus primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Cass County voters approve school operating millages](https://www.leaderpub.com/2023/05/02/cass-county-voters-approve-school-operating-millages/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Edwardsburg Argus labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Edwardsburg Argus is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
