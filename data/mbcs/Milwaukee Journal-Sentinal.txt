History
[Founded in 1837](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milwaukee_Journal_Sentinel), The Milwaukee Journal-Sentinel is a daily morning broadsheet printed in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. It is the primary newspaper in Milwaukee, the largest newspaper in Wisconsin, and is distributed widely throughout the state. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Milwaukee Journal-Sentinel is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Milwaukee Journal-Sentinel covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in reporting such as this; Tommy
[ Bartlett Show in Wisconsin Dells is closing permanently because of business losses.](https://www.jsonline.com/story/money/business/2020/09/16/tommy-bartlett-show-wisconsin-dells-closing-permanently/5817069002/) National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual.
Editorially, the Milwaukee Journal-Sentinel
[stopped making endorsements in 2012.](https://madison.com/wsj/news/local/govt-and-politics/elections/journal-sentinel-to-stop-making-political-endorsements/article_0cd20f3a-1fa2-11e2-b436-0019bb2963f4.html) However, they do cover politics with reasonable balance though more stories favor the left.
