History
Founded in 1885, The Billings Gazette is the
[largest newspaper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billings_Gazette) in Montana. It is geographically one of the most widely distributed newspapers in the nation. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by
[Lee Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_Enterprises), which, according to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), has donated 88% of the money to [Republican politicians](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052990&cycle=2014) since 1990. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Billings Gazette covers local news with journalists and reporters throughout Montana. For national news, they use the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). All news is reported with minimal loaded words and bias. The Billings Gazette always sources its information properly. Editorially, there is a right-center bias. For example, during the 2016 elections, they endorsed Hillary Clinton, but for local Montana candidates, they almost always [endorsed Republican candidates](http://billingsgazette.com/news/opinion/editorial/gazette-opinion-election-endorsements/collection_3e787ba1-964d-5db5-b1db-54cd325c1875.html). The news is generally reported factually and with a right-leaning editorial bias.
