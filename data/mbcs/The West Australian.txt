History
[The West Australian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_West_Australian), widely known as The West, is the only locally edited daily newspaper published in Perth, Western Australia, and is owned by Seven West Media (SWM). The West is the second-oldest continuously produced newspaper in Australia, having been published since 1833. [Read our profile on Australia’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/australia-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Seven West Media Limited owns the West Australian. “Seven West Media was
[created](https://www.linkedin.com/company/west-australian-newspapers) through the acquisition of Seven Media Group by West Australian Newspapers Holdings Limited (WAN).” [Kerry Stokes](http://www.sevenwestmedia.com.au/about-us/board-of-directors/) is Executive Chairman and [James Warburton](http://www.sevenwestmedia.com.au/assets/pdfs/SWM-Leadership-Succession-190816.pdf), Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer of Seven West Media Limited. Advertising derives revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The West Australian publishes news for Perth Australia and the surrounding region. Seven West Media is known to have a moderate conservative bias through the support of conservative Prime Minister Scott Morrison. Articles and headlines offer moderate loaded language such as this
[Australia has no choice but to be involved in a China war](https://thewest.com.au/opinion/comment-australia-has-no-choice-but-to-be-involved-in-a-china-war-c-2729410). World news is derived from other sources such as [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/).
Editorially, op-eds favor the Morrison government through negative reporting on the labor party such as this
[Is McGowan’s GST battle all bluster?](https://thewest.com.au/opinion/paul-murray/paul-murray-first-it-was-the-pandemic-now-mark-mcgowan-has-positioned-the-gst-as-the-new-threat-to-wa-ng-b881831732z) In general, the news is reported factually and with a right-leaning editorial bias.
