History
According to the New Humanitarian
[about page](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/content/about-us), they are humanitarian news service and analysis site that focuses on humanitarian stories in regions that are often forgotten, under-reported, misunderstood or ignored.
The New Humanitarian was launched in 1995 Nairobi /Kenya and until January 2015, the New Humanitarian was a project of the
[United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Office_for_the_Coordination_of_Humanitarian_Affairs)” In January 2015, they re-launched as an independent, non-profit media venture that is headquartered in Geneva, and governed by Swiss law. The New Humanitarian is led by Heba Aly and [Howard French](http://www.irinnews.org/content/irin-board-members) is the president.
The New Humanitarian is best known for alerting the public and the humanitarian community on crises before they happen, they indicate their
[mission statement](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/content/about-us) as: “the New Humanitarian delivers unique, authoritative and independent reporting from the frontlines of crises to inspire and produce a more effective humanitarian response.” [Read our profile on Switzerland’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/switzerland-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The New Humanitarian is a non-profit that is funded through donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the New Humanitarian publishes analysis with minimally loaded words in their headlines “
[UN bid to improve migrant, refugee response flounders as political will evaporates”](http://www.irinnews.org/analysis/2017/11/30/un-bid-improve-migrant-refugee-response-flounders-political-will-evaporates) and they utilize credible government sources and other credible UN humanitarian information portals such as [ReliefWeb.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ReliefWeb) However, they also utilize left-biased sources such as [Democracy Now](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/democracy-now/), and left-leaning sources such as [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Foreignpolicy](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/foreign-policy/), and [hrw.org.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/human-rights-watch/) Further, they are critical of the Trump administration’s lack of attention to humanitarian problems: “ [New relief chief, Harvey deportations, and Trump M.I.A. on Africa: The Cheat Sheet.”](https://www.irinnews.org/news/2017/09/01/new-relief-chief-harvey-deportations-and-trump-mia-africa-cheat-sheet)
