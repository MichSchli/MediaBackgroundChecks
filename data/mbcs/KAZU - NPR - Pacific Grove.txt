History
KAZU – NPR – Pacific Grove is an NPR FM radio station (90.3 FM) based in Pacific Grove, California, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KAZU – NPR – Pacific Grove is owned and broadcast by California State University – Monterey Bay. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KAZU – NPR – Pacific Grove website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Vega and Lewis Road Evacuated for Vegetation Fire](https://www.kazu.org/kazu-news/2023-08-30/vega-and-lewis-road-evacuated-for-vegetation-fire). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as
[Climate Costs: The High Price of Climate Change for California Communities](https://www.kazu.org/show/kazu-sunday-sound-adventures/2022-04-06/climate-costs-the-high-price-of-climate-change-for-california-communities). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
