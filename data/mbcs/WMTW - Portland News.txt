History
Founded in 1954, WMTW – Portland News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Poland Spring, Maine, United States. WMTW.com is the associated website that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime for the greater Portland, Maine area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WMTW – Portland News is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Hearst donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WMTW – Portland News reports on news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Portland, Maine area. Local news is covered by reporters with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Maine man charged in New Hampshire crime spree](https://www.wmtw.com/article/maine-man-charged-new-hampshire-crime-spree/40794548). A video accompanies most stories from the TV station. The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Hearst affiliates typically cover the national news.
Editorially, WMTW – Portland News does not publish editorial pieces, and when reporting on national politics, there is minimal bias as the primary source is usually the Associated Press. In general, they present fact-based, low-biased news.
