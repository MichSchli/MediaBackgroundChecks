History
[Founded in 1961](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_(magazine)), Los Angeles Magazine is a monthly publication covering Los Angeles. According to their [about page](https://www.lamag.com/about-us/), “Los Angeles Magazine covers the people, food, culture, arts and entertainment, fashion, lifestyle, and news that defines Southern California with a signature mix of feature-length writing, service journalism, investigative reporting, and design.” The current editor-in-chief is Maer Roshan. LAmag.com provides original coverage of culture, food, fashion, local history, and news. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Los Angeles Magazine is owned by Los Angeles Magazine, LLC, a subsidiary of Hour Media Group LLC. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Los Angeles Magazine publishes original content on news, food, entertainment, and culture for the Los Angeles area. News reporting utilizes minimally loaded language such as: Will
[ the City Buy Out Private Buildings to Protect Affordable Housing?](https://www.lamag.com/citythinkblog/gil-cedillo-eminent-domain-affordable-housing/) The website does not cover national news stories. However, they do report on politics concerning California, often with a left-leaning bias such as this, [Trump Bro and Failed Maxine Waters Opponent Arrested on Stalking Charges](https://www.lamag.com/citythinkblog/omar-navarro-stalking/). This story is sourced to the left-leaning [Daily Beast](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-beast/). The magazine and website also offer long-form investigative journalism, such as [The Conjugal Visit](https://www.lamag.com/longform/the-conjugal-visit/).
Editorially, they have a negative view of the former Trump administration, as evidenced by this
[Court Shoots Down Trump’s Bid to Delay Defamation Suit by Sex Assault Accuser](https://www.lamag.com/citythinkblog/trump-defamation-assault/). LAmag also holds left-leaning viewpoints such as pro-immigration: [Federal Immigration Officials Arrested 100 People, and Angelenos Aren’t Having It](https://www.lamag.com/culturefiles/undocumented-immigrants-just-arrested-angelenos/), and concern for [climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), [Climate Change Could Kill Off Southern California’s Joshua Trees](https://www.lamag.com/citythinkblog/joshua-tree-climate-change/). Los Angeles Magazine generally reports news factually and with a moderate left-leaning editorial bias.
