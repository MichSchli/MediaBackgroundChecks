History
[Founded in 1857](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Times_Union_(Albany)), the Albany Times-Union is an American daily newspaper serving the Capital Region of New York, United States. Although the newspaper focuses on Albany and its suburbs, it covers all parts of the four-county area, including the cities of Troy, Schenectady, and Saratoga Springs. Funded by / Ownership
The Albany Times-Union is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Hearst donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Albany Times-Union reports local news through journalists with minimal bias such as this:
[Albany County offering employees $15K buyout](https://www.timesunion.com/news/article/Albany-County-offering-employees-15K-buyout-15498094.php). The national news is derived from other Hearst papers and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Albany Times-Union tends to favor Democratic candidates, as evidenced by their
[endorsement](https://ballotpedia.org/Albany_Times_Union) of Democratic Presidential Candidate Hillary Clinton in the 2016 Presidential election.
