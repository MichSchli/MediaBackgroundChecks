History
[Founded in 2006](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boy_Genius_Report), Boy Genius Report (also referred to as BGR) is a technology-influenced website covering topics ranging from consumer gadgets to entertainment, gaming, and science. BGR also covers politics as it relates to technology. Jonathan S. Geller launched the website at the age of 19. According to their [about page](https://bgr.com/about-bgr/), “Geller regularly provides expert analysis for acclaimed news programs on networks such as [CNBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnbc/), [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/), [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), and [NBC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/), and BGR has been quoted by countless globally recognized media outlets including [The New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), CNN, [The Wall Street Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wall-street-journal/), [MSNBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/msnbc/), [TIME Magazine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/time/), [Barron’s](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/barrons/), [Los Angeles Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and many others.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Boy Genius Report is owned by
[Penske Media Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penske_Media_Corporation), which also owns [Variety Magazine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/variety-magazine/) and [Deadline](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/deadline-hollywood/). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Boy Genius Report is a technology website that focuses on Tech, Entertainment, Business, Science, and Lifestyle. Primarily, this is a technology website. Most information is related to advances in tech with minimal bias, such as this
[T-Mobile confirms 19 devices that will stop working on its network this month](https://bgr.com/posts/5899926/a43cc4-t-mobile-confirms-19-devices-that-will-stop-working-on-its-network-this-month/). This story, like all others, is properly sourced.
Editorially, they do not produce political op-eds; however, they report on politics related to tech and science, such as
[Dr. Fauci is finally fed up with Trump’s continued attacks over coronavirus](https://bgr.com/2020/10/27/trump-vs-fauci-insults-coronavirus-response-nonise/) and this [Trump campaign demands Biden pull attack ad that’s full of fake news](https://bgr.com/2020/09/14/donald-trump-cease-and-desist-letter-to-biden-campaign-over-ad/). While most of this site is not related to politics, the stories that do involve politics tend to favor the left slightly. Generally, story selection favors the left slightly and is sourced and factual.
