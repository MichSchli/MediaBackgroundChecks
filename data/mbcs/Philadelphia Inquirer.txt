History
[Founded in 1829](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer), The Philadelphia Inquirer is a morning daily broadsheet newspaper that serves the Philadelphia metropolitan area of the United States. The current editor is Gabriel Escobar. The Inquirer has won 20 Pulitzer Prizes. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper and website are held by The Philadelphia Inquirer LLC, which is owned by
[The Philadelphia Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Foundation), a nonprofit organization in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Philadelphia Inquirer covers local news, breaking news, sports, and politics. Local news is covered with minimal bias, such as this
[2 Philadelphia men killed in Bucks County campground altercation.](https://www.inquirer.com/news/bucks-county-double-homicide-philadelphia-homestead-family-campground-20191019.html) National and International news is primarily covered by the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and the [Washington Post.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/) They also offer opinion pieces that are clearly labeled and placed in their own section.
Editorially, over the last 20 years, the Inquirer has
[endorsed](https://ballotpedia.org/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer) Democratic Presidential candidates. A review of op-eds reveals more lean left such as this: [Trump deserves impeachment process | Editorial](https://www.inquirer.com/opinion/editorials/nancy-pelosi-trump-impeachment-inquiry-ukraine-joe-biden-20190924.html). In general, the Philadelphia Inquirer reports news accurately and with minimal bias; however, editorially, they are moderately liberal.
