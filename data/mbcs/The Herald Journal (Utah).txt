History
Founded in 1931, The Herald-Journal or hjnews is a daily newspaper based in Logan, Utah, United States. Its features include local news, events from northern Utah communities and Southeastern Idaho, entertainment, as well as political commentaries, and world news.
[Read our profile on USA Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper is owned by
[Adams Publishing Group](https://www.poynter.org/business-work/2017/who-are-the-adams-family-and-why-are-they-buying-newspapers-by-the-dozen/), which holds over one hundred papers across the USA. Stephen Adams is the owner and [donates to Republicans](https://web.archive.org/web/20080724023422/http://www.madcowprod.com/billboardenvy.htm), raising 1 million dollars for George W. Bush in 2000. Revenue is derived from advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Herald-Journal covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in wording such as this cache
[ Valley schools welcome state employees as substitute teachers](https://www.hjnews.com/news/government/cache-valley-schools-welcome-state-employees-as-substitute-teachers/article_2c35788b-edb5-5309-a287-60dbddc2c32c.html). Local news is sourced through quotes, interviews, and accounts, while National and world news comes directly from the [Associated Press](https://www.bozemandailychronicle.com/news/city/bozeman-continues-to-foster-midtown-development/article_51464c29-97fa-57b9-9535-05375f0853c8.html).
Editorially, the Herald-Tribune provides a balance in opinions. In general, they report the news factually and with minimal bias.
