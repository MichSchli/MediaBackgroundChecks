History
The Hawaii Tribune-Herald is a daily newspaper published in Hilo, Hawaii. The website hawaiitribune-herald.com covers news, sports, crime, and business for the greater Hilo area.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Black Press Media](https://www.blackpress.ca/about-us/) owns the Hawaii Tribune-Herald. and is operated by Oahu Publications Inc. Black Press, based in British Columbia, Canada, publishes over 170 titles across Canada and the western United States. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Hawaii Tribune-Herald covers news for Hilo and the surrounding area with minimal bias in wording such as this
[Ige signs five bills to bolster education services](https://www.hawaiitribune-herald.com/2022/07/08/hawaii-news/ige-signs-five-bills-to-bolster-education-services/). National and international is published through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Hawaii Tribune-Herald does not regularly produce op-eds. However, political news coverage tends to lean left, such as this
[Biden sees chance of ‘rational’ Republican approach on guns](https://www.hawaiitribune-herald.com/2022/05/31/nation-world-news/biden-sees-chance-of-rational-republican-approach-on-guns/). Generally, news reporting is factual with a left-leaning bias in story selection.
