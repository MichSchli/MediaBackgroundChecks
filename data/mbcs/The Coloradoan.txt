History
[Founded in 1873](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Collins_Coloradoan), The Coloradoan is a daily newspaper serving Fort Collins, Colorado, and the surrounding area. The newspaper covers local news, sports, business, and lifestyle. The current editor is Eric Larsen. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Coloradoan is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Coloradoan reports local news for the Fort Collins, Colorado region with minimal use of loaded emotional language such as this
[Colorado to begin vaccinating 65-plus teachers](https://www.coloradoan.com/story/news/2021/01/29/covid-vaccine-colorado-next-phase-educators-seniors-eligible/4313494001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, op-eds tend to be reasonably balanced, presenting opinions from both sides. However, in reviewing several, more favor the left such as this

The editorial board typically does not endorse presidential candidates, but they endorse local candidates. In 2018, they [endorsed both Democrats and Republicans](https://www.coloradoan.com/story/opinion/2018/10/15/colorado-2018-election-heres-who-coloradoan-editorial-board-endorsed-governor-ballot-issues/1515781002/); however, more democrats were endorsed overall. In general, they report the news factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
