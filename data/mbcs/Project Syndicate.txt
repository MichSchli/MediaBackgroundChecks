History
Founded in 1995, Project Syndicate is a non-profit organization that provides readers with original commentaries by prominent political leaders and thinkers, scholars, and economists. Their headquarters is in Prague, Czech Republic.
[Project Syndicate](https://www.theguardian.com/business/series/project-syndicate-economists) includes nearly 500 media outlets in more than 150 countries. They indicate their [purpose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_Syndicate) as “Promotion of media freedom and increased accessibility to a variety of global opinions.”
According to their
[about page](https://www.project-syndicate.org/about): “Project Syndicate produces and delivers original, high-quality commentaries to a global audience. Featuring exclusive contributions by prominent political leaders, policymakers, scholars, business leaders, and civic activists worldwide, we provide news media and their readers cutting-edge analysis and insight, regardless of ability to pay.” [Read our profile on Czech Republic Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/czech-republic-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Project Syndicate is a nonprofit that charges full price for original content to developed countries and provides it free or low cost to developing countries. Revenue is also derived through sales of their yearly print magazine as well as donations. Project Syndicate
[lists](https://www.project-syndicate.org/about) that they have received funding from the following: [Open Society Foundations](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/open-society-foundations/), the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, the MasterCard Foundation, the European Climate Foundation, the European Journalism Centre, the Children’s Investment Fund Foundation, the Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Knowledge Foundation, the Heinrich Böll Stiftung, the Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung, GAM, the Google Digital News Initiative, McKinsey Global Institute, the Nature Conservancy, and the Sustainable Development Solutions Network.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Project Syndicate publishes op-eds for syndication by numerous columnists from various countries and backgrounds. Headlines and articles vary in wording, with some having a moderately emotional tone, such as this:
[Is America Going Fascist?](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/trump-fascist-parallels-unhelpful-by-daron-acemoglu-2020-01) This story is properly sourced to the [Anti-Defamation League](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/anti-defamation-league/), [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), and [NBC News](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/trump-fascist-parallels-unhelpful-by-daron-acemoglu-2020-01).
Editorially, Project Syndicate covers both left-center and right-center positions; however, there are frequent negative pieces regarding nationalism, such as this
[Johnson’s Win Is a Loss for British Power](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/boris-johnson-dfid-by-gordon-brown-2019-12). They also report unfavorably on former President Trump, such as this: [Trump Will Make China Great Again](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/us-china-strategic-rivalry-decoupling-by-nouriel-roubini-2019-12). Finally, they frequently report on the [dangers of climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/): [Climate Change Is also a Health Crisis](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/climage-change-health-crisis-by-maria-neira-2019-11). In general, information on Project Syndicate is properly sourced and factual; however, editorially, they align with globalist and environmentalist perspectives.
