History
Founded in 1900, The Daily Express is a daily conservative national tabloid newspaper in the United Kingdom. The paper is published by
[Northern & Shell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_%26_Shell), and the current editor is [Gary Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Jones_(journalist)). [Read our profile on UK’s Government and Media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Daily Express is owned by
[Reach plc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reach_plc) (formerly known as Trinity Mirror), a British newspaper, magazine, and digital publisher. Subscriptions, newsstand sales, and advertising generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Daily Express is a British right-wing tabloid newspaper that routinely utilizes sensational headlines such as this:
[NATO TERRIFIED: Trump may ‘seriously undermine’ alliance in Putin meeting – world on ALERT](https://www.express.co.uk/news/world/989524/Donald-Trump-Vladimir-Puting-meeting-Helsinki-latest-news-Nato-Russia-election-hacking). Articles are typically written by journalists or correspondents and are reasonably sourced to credible information. However, like many tabloids, the Daily Express also publishes conspiracy and pseudoscience articles such as these: [‘Portal OPENS’ above New Jersey with some fearing it’s a sign of PLANET X](https://www.express.co.uk/news/weird/986586/portal-opens-new-jersey-planet-x-nibiru-youtube-video) and [Could this strange corpse be the world’s first proof aliens have visited Earth?](https://archive.is/ZCFrt#selection-1379.0-1379.79)
The paper’s
[editorial stances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daily_Express) have often been seen as aligned to the UK Independence Party (UKIP), Euroscepticism, and many other right-wing factions, including the Conservative Party’s right-wing. Finally, they have frequently promoted misinformation regarding Covid-19. See fact checks below.
