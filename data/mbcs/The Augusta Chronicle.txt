History
[Founded in 1785](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Augusta_Chronicle), The Augusta Chronicle is a daily newspaper serving Augusta, Georgia, and the surrounding area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Augusta Chronicle is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Augusta Chronicle covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Augusta, Georgia area. Local news is reported with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[ Motorcycle crash in Augusta parking lot kills South Carolina man](https://www.augustachronicle.com/story/news/2021/08/25/south-carolina-man-dead-motorcycle-crash-augusta-parking-lot/5576701001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Augusta Chronicle previously
[endorsed Republican](http://www.morris.com/content/ceo-message-our-readers/) Presidential candidates; however, in 2020, they did not endorse a candidate. A review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left: [Critical race theory: Is it really a threat to our country?](https://www.augustachronicle.com/story/opinion/columns/guest/2021/07/23/writer-misguided-panic-flourishing-over-critical-race-theory/8040799002/) and this favoring the right [Biden administration calls for more oil from OPEC. What happened to energy independence?](https://www.augustachronicle.com/story/opinion/2021/08/17/biden-calls-opec-produce-more-oil-time-when-administration-wants-invest-u-s-economy-russia-iran/8105249002/)
. Overall, in viewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a more liberal perspective. In general, the news is reported factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
