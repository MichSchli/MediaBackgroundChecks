History
Established in 2007,
[Environment America](https://environmentamerica.org/about/) is a coalition of state-level organizations in the U.S. focused on environmental advocacy. The organization focuses on areas like waste & recycling, toxins, and clean water & air. It advocates for pro-environmental policies at the state and federal levels. It is a non-profit organization not affiliated with any political party but financially supports Democratic candidates who share its environmental values. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Environment America is a 501(c)(4) non-profit organization. According to their
[financial information page](https://environmentamerica.org/center/about/financial-information/), The organization has received foundation support from various entities in fiscal year 2022, including the 444S Foundation, Alaska Wilderness League, American Heart Association, Bydale Foundation, Colcom Foundation, and many others.
Analysis / Bias
Environment America is an organization that supports environmental causes and works with politicians to promote pro-environment policies. They tend to provide more financial support to
[Democratic candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/environment-america/recipients?id=D000025699) than Republican candidates, indicating a preference for the Democratic Party. [The Environment America, Voter Action page](https://environmentamerica.org/environment-america-voter-action/) shows the organization’s willingness to engage in political advocacy to further its environmental goals. It indicates a strategic approach to achieving environmental objectives, including aligning with specific political parties or candidates that share their environmental concerns.
Furthermore, Environment America Voter Action has contributed over $470,000 to candidates in federal, state, and local races. Most contributions go to Democratic candidates such as
[Joe Biden](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/joe-biden-democratic-presidential-candidate-bias-profile/) & Kamala Harris for the presidential ticket and various Democratic candidates for the US Senate and House. There are also some Moderate Republicans on the list, such as Lisa Murkowski and Brian Fitzpatrick.
An article titled
[“What can bring Democrats and Republicans together? Protecting America’s Wildlife”](https://environmentamerica.org/updates/what-can-bring-democrats-and-republicans-together-protecting-americas-wildlife/) discusses bipartisan support for the Recovering America’s Wildlife Act, which aims to conserve wildlife. This article highlights that Environment America’s primary focus is on environmental conservation, irrespective of political affiliation, as long as the cause is supported. Lastly, Environment America offers valuable insights into wildlife conservation in the United States. For example, their article [“Jewels of the Sky” ](https://environmentamerica.org/center/articles/jewels-of-the-sky/) is fact-based, aligns with the organization’s environmental advocacy focus, and cites reputable sources like Pollinator.org, adding to its credibility.
