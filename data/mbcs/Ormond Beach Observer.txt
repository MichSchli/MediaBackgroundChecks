History
The Ormond Beach Observer is a newspaper published in Ormond Beach, Florida, United States. ormondbeachobserver.com is the website for the paper covering local news, sports, crime, government, and opinion.
Read our profile on the
[USA government’s influence on media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
The Ormond Beach Observer is owned and published by the
[Observer Media Group](https://www.yourobserver.com/contact-us) based in Sarasota, Florida. The Observer Media Group, founded by [Matt Walsh](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/matt-walsh-72d8) in 1995, publishes 11 newspapers and several magazines in Florida. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Ormond Beach Observer publishes local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this: Police arrest
[Ormond man who was reported to cause nightly disturbances in his neighborhood.](https://www.ormondbeachobserver.com/article/police-arrest-ormond-man-who-was-reported-to-cause-nightly-disturbances-in-his-neighborhood) The newspaper does not publish national or international news.
Editorially, the Ormond Beach Observer publishes an appropriate mix of right and left-leaning opinions. However, more favor the right such as this
[My view: City’s answers about the runway extension are disappointing.](https://www.ormondbeachobserver.com/article/my-view-citys-answers-about-the-runway-extension-are-disappointing) In general, they report factually and with credible sources, while op-eds are mostly balanced with differing viewpoints slightly leaning right.
