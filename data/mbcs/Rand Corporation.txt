History
[Founded in 1946](https://www.rand.org/about/faq.html#when-was-rand-created-some-of--), The RAND Corporation is a global policy think tank whose mission is to improve policy and decision-making through research and analysis. The RAND Corporation is headquartered in Santa Monica, California, U.S. According to their [“about page,”](https://www.rand.org/about.html) The RAND Corporation is a “research organization that develops solutions to public policy challenges to help make communities throughout the world safer and more secure, healthier, and more prosperous.” [The RAND Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAND_Corporation) was founded by Henry Arnold, Donald Douglas, Major General Curtis LeMay, and the current President and CEO is Michael D. Rich. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
According to a
[US News article](https://www.usnews.com/news/articles/2011/03/03/think-tank-employees-tend-to-support-democrats) dated 2011, the political leaning of the RAND Corporation is Centrist. [Opensecrets.org](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) indicates that RAND Corporation’s [total contributions by party of recipient](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000036957&cycle=2018) donate more to Democratic candidates.
RAND reports commentaries, periodicals, and
[blog posts.](https://www.rand.org/blog/2018/04/staying-cool-as-the-globe-warms.html) Their commentaries utilize credible sources such as [NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/), [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [columbia.edu](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/columbia-reports/), and pro-science sites such as [HowStuffWorks.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/howstuffworks/) Rand also [republishes](https://www.rand.org/blog/2018/06/how-do-you-define-terrorism.html) articles from credible right-center sources such as [The National Interest (TNI)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/national-interest/) and [least biased](https://www.rand.org/blog/2018/06/north-korea-is-not-like-libya.html) sources such as [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), utilizing neutral headlines such as “ [How Do You Define Terrorism?](https://www.rand.org/blog/2018/06/how-do-you-define-terrorism.html)”. Further, RAND reports research findings regarding public policy areas, including those related to (peer-reviewed) [Environmental Science](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RR2442.html), [climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), energy and water, and other natural resources.
