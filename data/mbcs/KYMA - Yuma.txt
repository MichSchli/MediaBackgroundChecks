History
Founded in 1962, KYMA – Yuma is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Yuma, Arizona. The associated website is kyma.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Blackhawk Broadcasting LLC owns KYMA – Yuma TV, while the News-Press & Gazette Company (NPG) owns the associated website. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The KYMA – Yuma website publishes news for the greater Yuma, Arizona area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[Calexico teachers union and district still at odds over pay](https://kyma.com/news/top-stories/2021/09/23/calexico-teachers-union-and-district-still-at-odds-over-pay/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [CBS News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/), [CNN Newswire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/left/cnn-bias/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Two former Arizona governors endorse new gubernatorial candidate](https://kyma.com/news/2021/09/22/two-former-arizona-governors-endorse-new-gubernatorial-candidate/). Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
