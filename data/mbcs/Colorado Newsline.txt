History
The Colorado Newsline is an online news website from Colorado. Their
[about](https://alaskabeacon.com/about/) statement reads, “Colorado Newsline is a nonprofit, nonpartisan, independent source of online news. It launched in July 2020. It provides fair and accurate reporting on politics, policy and other stories of interest to Colorado readers.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, the Colorado Newsline is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The Colorado Newsline is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The Colorado Newsline publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from Colorado. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects the people of Colorado. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through moderate, loaded language
[Democrat Adam Frisch announces another challenge to Rep. Lauren Boebert](https://coloradonewsline.com/briefs/adam-frisch-challenge-lauren-boebert/).
They properly source, linking to credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[To tackle climate change, we need to make housing more affordable](https://coloradonewsline.com/2023/02/13/to-tackle-climate-change-we-need-to-make-housing-more-affordable/). On the issues, the Colorado Newsline promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [White nationalism gets a hearing in the Republican House](https://coloradonewsline.com/2023/02/09/white-nationalism-gets-a-hearing-in-the-republican-house/). The Colorado Newsline generally reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
