History
The Mitchell Daily Republic is a newspaper published in Mitchell, South Dakota. The website mitchellrepublic.com features local and national news, politics, sports, entertainment, business, classifieds, and health.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Mitchell Daily Republic is owned by
[Forum Communications Company](https://www.forumcomm.com/brands-overview/), which is based in North Dakota and has additional locations across South Dakota, Minnesota, and Wisconsin. Advertising and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Mitchell Daily Republic reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Mitchell, SD region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[Downtown Mitchell property owners hit with higher tax due to increased property valuations](https://www.mitchellrepublic.com/news/local/downtown-mitchell-property-owners-hit-with-higher-tax-due-to-increased-property-valuations). Further, the website publishes national and international news from credible sources such as the [LA Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/). Finally, all information is sourced via first-person accounts or through credible media.
Editorially, The Mitchell Daily Republic reasonably represents both sides. However, a review of columnists demonstrates more favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually and with right-leaning editorial bias.
