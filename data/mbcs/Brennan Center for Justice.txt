History
[Brennan Center for Justice](https://ballotpedia.org/Brennan_Center_for_Justice) at New York University School of Law is a [nonprofit](https://ballotpedia.org/Nonprofit_organization) law and policy institute that focuses on improving U.S systems of democracy and justice. They were founded in 1995 by the family and former Supreme Court law clerks of [Justice William J. Brennan,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_J._Brennan_Jr.) who served as an Associate Justice of the United States Supreme Court from 1956 to 1990. President Eisenhower nominated Justice Brennan, known for his progressive views, including opposition to the death penalty and support for abortion rights. [The Brennan Center](https://www.bloomberg.com/profiles/companies/0224119Z:US-brennan-center-for-justice) is headquartered in New York, and Robert A. Atkins and Patricia Bauman co-chair the board. [Michael Waldman](https://www.brennancenter.org/expert/michael-waldman), a former speechwriter/assistant to President Bill Clinton, is the Brennan Center for Justice president. Analysis / Bias
According to their
[about page,](https://www.brennancenter.org/about) Brennan Center for Justice operates as ”part think tank, a part advocacy group, part cutting-edge communications hub.” Their main focus is voting rights and elections, campaign finance reform, money in politics, government and court reform, justice for all, liberty, and national security.
In review, Brennan Center for Justice excerpts and cross-posts articles from credible sources such as the
[New York Times](https://www.brennancenter.org/blog/how-purges-could-disenfranchise-voters-fall). They also publish research and analysis related to their main focus issues such as voting rights: [“How Purges Threaten to Disenfranchise Voters Under the Radar”](https://www.brennancenter.org/blog/how-purges-threaten-disenfranchise-voters-under-radar) and money in politics such as [“Money Is Not Destiny](https://www.brennancenter.org/blog/money-not-destiny).” Story and analysis selection moderately favor the left based on the issues covered. Generally, information is sourced from reasonably credible sources such as [The New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Huffington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/huffington-post/), [Daily Beast,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-beast/) and [Open Secrets.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/)
