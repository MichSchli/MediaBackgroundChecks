History
KUNC – NPR – Greeley is an NPR FM radio station (91.5 FM) based in Greeley, Colorado, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KUNC – NPR – Greeley is owned and broadcast by Community Radio for Northern Colorado. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KUNC – NPR – Greeley website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Colorado makes some progress on adult education amid a lack of funding, legislative ‘oversight’](https://www.kunc.org/podcast/inthenoco/2023-10-27/colorado-makes-some-progress-on-adult-education-amid-a-lack-of-funding-legislative-oversight). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[The Climate For The Fight Against Climate Change](https://www.kunc.org/2021-01-22/the-climate-for-the-fight-against-climate-change). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
