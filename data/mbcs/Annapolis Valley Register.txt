History
The Annapolis Valley Register is a newspaper published in Annapolis Valley, Nova Scotia, Canada. The News delivers breaking news, opinion, sports, lifestyles, and crime.
[Read our profile on Canadian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by a Canadian newspaper publishing company,
[SaltWire Network](https://www.saltwire.com/), and is funded through subscriptions and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
The Annapolis Valley Register has a right-center bias in reporting as many stories
[slightly favor the right](https://www.saltwire.com/cape-breton/news/update-ns-fuel-update-aug-5-2022-gas-diesel-prices-drop-interrupter-clause-to-be-invoked-100760572/). When reporting world news, they typically use [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), and with national news, they use [Postmedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ottawa-sun/) publications, which lean right.
Opinion and editorial pieces also slightly favor the right such as this
[EDITORIAL: Trudeau should have consulted more before declaring holiday](https://www.saltwire.com/nova-scotia/opinion/editorial-trudeau-should-have-consulted-more-before-declaring-holiday-100773834/). However, news reporting is usually accurate and holds a slight conservative bias.
