History
Founded in 1971,
[Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/features/what-you-need-to-know-about-earthjustice) is a nonprofit U.S. environmental law organization. EarthJustice is headquartered in San Francisco, CA. The president is [Trip Van Noppen](https://earthjustice.org/about/staff/trip-van-noppen), who states its mission as “using the courts to protect our environment and people’s health.” [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Earthjustice was founded by
[Phil Berry, Fred Fisher and Don Harris](https://earthjustice.org/features/founders). Funding for the organization comes from individual, fully tax-deductible contributions and [foundations.](https://earthjustice.org/give/foundations) Their financial statements and supporting foundations are available [online](https://earthjustice.org/financial-statements). EarthJustice has [been awarded top ratings ](https://earthjustice.org/your-donation-at-work#charityratings)from charity watchdogs such as [Charity Navigator](http://www.charitynavigator.org/index.cfm?bay=search.summary&orgid=3638#.VCBr4fldV8E), [GuideStar](http://www.guidestar.org/organizations/94-1730465/earthjustice.aspx), BBB, and [CharityWatch.](https://www.charitywatch.org/ratings-and-metrics/earthjustice/251)
According to their
[annual report](https://earthjustice.org/sites/default/files/files/2017-annual-report.pdf), as of 2017, Earthjustice had $132,970,573 in Liabilities and Assets and 300 full-time staff members, including about 120 full-time attorneys, which include legal and research analysts, policy and legislation experts, staff scientists, and communications staff.
Analysis / Bias
Earthjustice provides expertise in
[Coal](https://earthjustice.org/about/offices/coal), [Clean Energy](https://earthjustice.org/about/offices/clean-energy), and [Oceans Programs](https://earthjustice.org/about/offices/oceans). They also advocate on behalf of the public’s interest on Capitol Hill. On their website, you can view their recent [court wins and legal advances](https://earthjustice.org/about/victories). The main criticism of EarthJustice comes from right-wing groups such as this: [How Environmentalism Weakens U.S. National Security ](https://capitalresearch.org/article/earthjustice-legal-defense-fund-how-environmentalism-weakens-u-s-national-security/)
In review, EarthJustice utilizes emotionally loaded
[headlines](https://earthjustice.org/news/press/2018/trump-administration-resorts-to-soviet-style-takeover-of-private-energy-markets-to-keep-dirty-uneconomic-coal) such as “Trump Administration Resorts To Soviet-style Takeover Of Private Energy Markets To Keep Dirty, Uneconomic Coal Plants Running” and [“Scott Pruitt’s EPA Just Shut the Door on Independent Scientists.”](https://earthjustice.org/blog/2017-october/scott-pruitt-s-epa-just-shut-the-door-on-independent-scientists?_ga=2.70166928.1642281962.1528238861-885567832.1528238861) However, EarthJustice utilizes credible sources such as [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/) [,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/) and pro-science blogs such as [blog.ucsusa.org.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/union-of-concerned-scientists/)
