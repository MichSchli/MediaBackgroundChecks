History
The Hartford Courant is the
[largest daily](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hartford_Courant) newspaper in the U.S. state of Connecticut and is often recognized as the oldest continuously published newspaper in the United States. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Hartford Courant is owned by
[MediaNews Group](https://www.medianewsgroup.com/our-brands/) and published by Digital First Media, which publishes 190 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Hartford Courant covers local news with field journalists and national news through the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). All articles are sourced from credible information and sourced.
Editorially, the Hartford Courant publishes an appropriate mix of right and left-leaning opinions. In general, they report factually and with credible sources, while op-eds are mostly balanced with differing viewpoints, though more favor the left.
