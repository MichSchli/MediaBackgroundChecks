History
[iMediaEthics,](https://www.imediaethics.org/about-us/) formerly known as StinkyJournalism.org is a non-partisan media watchdog group that targets unethical conduct, and incorrect or unethical media practices. iMediaEthics states its [mission](https://www.imediaethics.org/about-us/) as “iMediaEthics promotes the media’s use of scientific methods and experts before publication. Our journalists, student interns, and contributors publish media ethics news and investigative reports based on in-depth research.” The Founder, Publisher & Editor-in-chief is [Rhonda Roland Shearer](https://www.imediaethics.org/our-staff/rhonda-roland-shearer/). [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
iMediaEthics is part of the not-for-profit 501(c)(3),
[Art Science Research Laboratory](https://asrlab.org/). iMediaEthics is funded through donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, iMediaEthics focuses on investigative reports, daily media ethics news, case studies, and fact checks. Under their commentary category, they publish stories with emotionally loaded headlines such as
[“How Racist Are We Americans? What an AP Poll Suggests.”](https://www.imediaethics.org/racist-americans-ap-poll-suggests/) They typically utilize credible sources such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [The Daily Beast](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-beast/). Under the section called PollCheck, polling expert David Moore examines the polling standards for news outlets. [David Moore](https://www.imediaethics.org/our-staff/david-w-moore/) was former Vice President of the [Gallup Organization](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/gallup/). iMediaEthics also conducts polls such as this one: “ [Public Evenly Divided over Impeachment of Donald Trump: USA Today/iMediaEthics Poll](https://www.imediaethics.org/imediaethics-usatoday-poll-public-divided-impeachment-donald-trump/)” in which they source and collaborate with [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) They provide full topline results and crosstabs for this poll. iMediaEthics also produces [investigative reports](https://www.imediaethics.org/new-york-daily-news-review-kevin-deutsch-no-red-flags-not-able-comprehensively-confirm-every-fact-source/) such as the [Kevin Deutsch story](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kevin_Deutsch)
