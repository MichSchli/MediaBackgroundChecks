History
The Bedford Times-Mail serves Bedford, Indiana, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. tmnews.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Bedford Times-Mail. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Bedford Times-Mail covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Bedford, Indiana area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Inmate dies by apparent suicide at Lawrence County Jail](https://www.tmnews.com/story/news/local/2022/05/10/inmate-dies-apparent-suicide-lawrence-county-jail/9718629002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Bedford Times-Mail does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[October is Domestic Violence Awareness month: Eliminating intimate partner violence](https://www.tmnews.com/story/opinion/2021/10/08/eliminating-intimate-partner-violence/5946079001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
