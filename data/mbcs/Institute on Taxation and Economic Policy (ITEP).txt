History
The
[Institute on Taxation and Economic Policy (ITEP)](https://itep.org/about/) is a non-profit, non-partisan think tank that works on state and federal tax policy issues. ITEP was founded in 1980, and is a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization. ITEP describes its mission as striving to “keep policymakers and the public informed of the effects of current and proposed tax policies on tax fairness, government budgets and sound economic policy.” ITEP is a partner with the left-leaning [Citizens for Tax Justice](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/citizens-for-tax-justice/). ITEP is a non-profit organization funded through donations.
Analysis / Bias
ITEP has been sourced by both Republicans and Democrats, however, they favor a more
[liberal economic policy](http://www.pewtrusts.org/en/research-and-analysis/blogs/stateline/2013/07/10/immigration-overhaul-could-bring-states-localities-2-billion-a-year). ITEP also has a blog that reports on taxes and economic policy. There is little use of loaded language and all information is properly sourced. A [factual search](https://factualsearch.news/#gsc.tab=0&gsc.q=itep&gsc.sort=&gsc.ref=fact_checking&gsc.page=1) reveals that they have never failed a fact check. In fact, ITEP is frequently used as a [source for fact checks](http://www.politifact.com/punditfact/statements/2014/jul/28/jamie-weinstein/are-states-without-income-tax-doing-better-ones-do/) regarding tax policy.
