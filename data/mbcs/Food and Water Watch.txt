History
[Founded in 2005](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_%26_Water_Watch), Food and Water Watch is a Washington, D.C.-based non-governmental organization group that focuses on corporate and government accountability relating to food, water, and corporate overreach. The organization was founded by staff from [Public Citizen](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/public-citizen/). [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Food and Water Watch is a
[non-profit](https://www.foodandwaterwatch.org/ways-give) that does not take government or corporate donations. [Charity Watch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charity_Watch) rates it an “A” grade.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Food and Water Watch have a history that includes stopping fossil fuel and fossil fuel extraction, regulating factory farms, advocating for renewable energy, fighting water privatization, stopping bad trade deals, increasing transparency in our food system, and standing up for human rights.
Food and Water Watch routinely use emotionally loaded words such as this:
[When His Father Died, He Googled Teflon And Cancer And Found Out About PFAS.](https://www.foodandwaterwatch.org/insight/when-his-father-died-he-googled-teflon-and-cancer-and-found-out-about-pfas?ms=fwws_fp_02282019_michael-hickey-cancer-teflon-water-act&oms=fwws_fp_02282019_michael-hickey-cancer-teflon-water-act) A review of their campaign tag reveals an almost 100% left bias toward environmental issues such as fracking and climate change. We found that sourcing usually leads back to Food and Water Watch and also leads to factually mixed sources such as [Think Progress](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/think-progress/). While Food and Water Watch clearly supports the consensus of science when it comes to [climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), they [do not](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-gmo-dangers/) with [GMOs.](https://www.foodandwaterwatch.org/insight/heavy-price-tag-gmo-contamination)
