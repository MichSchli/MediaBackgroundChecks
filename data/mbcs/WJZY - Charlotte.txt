History
Founded in 1986, WJZY – Charlotte is a
[Fox](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-affiliated television station licensed to Charlotte, North Carolina. The associated website is fox46.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WJZY – Charlotte is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
WJZY – Charlotte website publishes news for the greater
[Charlotte](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/charlotte-observer/), North Carolina area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this: [Couple found shot to death inside Newton newspaper office: Police](https://www.fox46.com/news/u-s/north-carolina/catawba-county/newton/two-killed-in-apparent-shooting-at-newton-newspaper-business-police-say/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden promises ‘relentless diplomacy’ to skeptical allies](https://www.fox46.com/news/world-news/biden-aims-to-enlist-allies-in-tackling-climate-covid-more/). Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
