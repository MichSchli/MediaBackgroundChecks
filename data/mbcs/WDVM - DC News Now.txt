History
Founded in 1970, WDVM – DC News Now is a CW-affiliated television station licensed to Hagerstown, Maryland. The associated website is dcnewsnow.com.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WDVM – DC News Now is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The WDVM – DC News Now website publishes news for the greater Hagerstown and Washington, DC, area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias, such as this:
[Mother of infant that died of fentanyl surrenders to police](https://www.dcnewsnow.com/news/local-news/maryland/mother-of-infant-that-died-of-fentanyl-overdose-surrenders-to-police/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large, credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[What’s next for the $1 trillion bipartisan infrastructure bill?](https://www.dcnewsnow.com/news/capitol-review/whats-next-for-the-1-trillion-bipartisan-infrastructure-bill/)
Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides, and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
