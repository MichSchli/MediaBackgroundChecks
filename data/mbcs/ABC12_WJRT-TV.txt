History
Founded in 1958, WJRT-TV is an ABC-affiliated television station based in Flint, Michigan, USA. ABC12.com is the website for the TV station and provides news for Flint, Michigan, and the surrounding region.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WJRT/ABC12.com is owned by
[Gray Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Television), which operates 145 stations across the USA. Revenue is derived through sponsored content and advertising.

In review, ABC12.com provides news coverage for the Flint, Michigan market and the surrounding areas, encompassing several counties. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias and often has video provided by the TV station:
[Whitmer: ‘Very unlikely’ that Michigan students will return to classrooms.](https://www.abc12.com/content/news/Whitmer-Very-unlikely-that-Michigan-students-will-return-to-classrooms-569162841.html) The national news is provided through other Gray Television affiliates and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). International news generally comes from [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/) and the Associated Press.
Editorially, WJRT does not offer an opinion page and, when covering politics, utilizes minimally loaded words such as this
[Washington set to deliver $2.2 trillion virus rescue bill](https://www.abc12.com/content/news/Senate-passes-22T-coronavirus-aid-plan-House-votes-Friday-569125091.html). In general, WJRT/ABC12 reports news factually and with minimal bias.
