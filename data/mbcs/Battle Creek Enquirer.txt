History
The Battle Creek Enquirer is a
[daily newspaper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_Creek_Enquirer) in Battle Creek, Michigan. It is the only daily paper serving Calhoun County, Michigan and parts of four neighboring counties. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper is owned by the
[Gannett Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gannett_Company) (USA Today) and is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Battle Creek Enquirer covers local news via local journalists and uses minimal loaded words and proper sourcing. National/International news comes from the
[USA Today Network](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). Editorially, the Battle Creek Enquirer typically [endorses more Democratic candidates](https://www.battlecreekenquirer.com/story/opinion/editorials/2016/11/06/endorsement-hillary-clinton-president/93293550/). Story selection of editorials leans slightly left, but does cover a range of perspectives. In general, news reporting is low biased, and opinions are left-leaning.
