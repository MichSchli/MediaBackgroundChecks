History
Based in New York City, Al-Sura is an English-language news website that focuses on Iraq and the Middle East. The site describes itself on its
[about page](https://al-sura.com/about-us/) as a “news platform for journalists, social media activists focusing on political analysis and news about Iraq and the Middle East.” However, no ownership or management information is given on the site’s about page, and the media contact page leads back to the home page. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The website does not disclose ownership, but according to its
[about page, ](https://al-sura.com/about-us/)“Al-Sura does not receive funding from any source and depends solely on the efforts and donations of its contributors.” However, a website review shows that there are approximately 5 online ads per page.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Al-Sura tends to publish stories utilizing moderately emotionally loaded wording such as
[“IS IDLIB NEXT?”](https://al-sura.com/is-idlib-next/) and “ [THE BLOODY SHIFT TO HOSTAGES & EXTORTION TACTICS BY ISIS.”](https://al-sura.com/the-bloody-shift-to-hostages-extortion-tactics-by-isis/) Editorially, Al-Sura demonstrates a [negative opinion](https://al-sura.com/us-president-trump-doesnt-want-immigrants-from-shithole-countries-like-africa-and-haiti-instead-wants-norwegians/) of President Trump and being critical of the [Saudi Government](https://www.haaretz.com/middle-east-news/jordan/jordan-denies-king-s-brothers-ousted-from-army-over-saudi-ties-1.5629999). Al-Sura also reports with a [pro-environment stance](https://al-sura.com/deforestation-leading-to-the-next-civil-war-in-the-middle-east/).
When it comes to sourcing, Al-Sura simply does not source at all. There was no hyperlinking, quotations, or citations on the articles reviewed.
