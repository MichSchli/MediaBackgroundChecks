History
The Denver Post is a daily broadsheet newspaper published in Denver, Colorado, United States,
[since 1892](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Denver_Post). The Denver Post has won nine Pulitzer Prizes.
In 2018, several Denver Post writers formed a new publication called the Colorado Sun in response to being let go by Digital First Media.

Funded by / Ownership
The Denver Post is owned by
[MediaNews Group](https://www.medianewsgroup.com/our-brands/) and published by Digital First Media, which publishes 190 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund, described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former Denver Post writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Denver Post publishes local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Denver sheriff’s deputy disciplined for napping in supply closet while on duty at the downtown jail](https://www.denverpost.com/2019/12/03/denver-sheriff-deputy-napping-jail/). National and international news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/).
Editorially, the Denver Post has favored Democratic candidates by
[endorsing](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) a majority since 1980. Further, most editorials lean left with reporting such as this: [Editorial: All evidence points to a president who compromised American foreign policy objectives for political goals](https://www.denverpost.com/2019/11/18/editorial-all-evidence-points-to-a-president-who-compromised-american-foreign-policy-objectives-for-political-goals/). In general, the Denver Post reports factually and with credible sources. However, from an editorial perspective, the paper leans moderately left.
