History
The Quint is an Indian digital newspaper based in India founded by Raghav Bahl, who favors right-wing politics. The Quint covers topics ranging from politics, policy, entertainment, and sports.
[Read our profile on Indian Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/india-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Quint Digital Media Limited owns The Quint. Membership fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
According to
[Reporters without Borders](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reporters-without-borders/), India’s journalists are “Attacked online and physically” For example, the [report states](https://rsf.org/en/india), “Those who espouse Hindutva, the ideology that gave rise to Hindu nationalism, are trying to purge all manifestations of “anti-national” thought from the national debate.
The Quint reports international news with low bias as they use credible mainstream sources such as
[NBC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/). Editorially, they are critical of conservative Prime Minister Modi such as this [In Modi Govt’s Unique Health ID Push, Consent And Privacy Are Missing](https://www.thequint.com/voices/opinion/in-pm-narendra-modi-govts-unique-health-id-push-consent-and-privacy-are-missing). The publication also promotes [women’s rights](https://www.thequint.com/news/hot-news/womens-rights-activist-poet-author-kamla-bhasin-no-more) and expresses concerns for [Hindu Nationalism](https://www.thequint.com/news/law/madras-hc-rejects-hyper-nationalism-while-upholding-national-pride).
During the Covid-19 pandemic, they have reported factually on the disease and vaccines. They also operate a fact-checking service called WebQoof that is certified by the
[International Fact-Checking Network](https://www.exchange4media.com/media-others-news/the-qunits-webqoof-becomes-verified-signatory-of-poynter-institute-code-of-principles-94384.html). In general, The Quint is a left-leaning source that produces reliable news and fact checks.
