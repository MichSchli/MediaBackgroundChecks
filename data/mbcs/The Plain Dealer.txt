History
Founded in 1842, The Plain Dealer is a broadsheet newspaper from Cleveland, Ohio. The paper and website cover local, national, sports, business, and lifestyle news.

[USA government’s influence on media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
The Plain Dealer is owned by Advance Publications. The company dates to the early 1920s and is owned by the
[Newhouse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Irving_Newhouse,_Sr.) family. Donald Newhouse, together with his brother Samuel “Si” Newhouse Jr. owns Advance Publications. The [New Yorker](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-yorker/), [Vanity Fair](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vanity-fair/), and Reddit are all produced by Advance Publications. Revenue is generated through advertising and subscription sales.
Analysis / Bias
The Plain Dealer covers local news through journalists with minimal bias such as this
[Cleveland woman dies in one-vehicle crash on Interstate 480 on Christmas Eve](https://www.cleveland.com/crime/2019/12/cleveland-woman-dies-in-one-vehicle-crash-on-interstate-480-on-christmas-eve.html). National and international news is covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Cleveland Plain Dealer has
[endorsed](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Democratic Presidential candidates since 2008, including Hillary Clinton in 2016 and [Joe Biden in 2020](https://www.cleveland.com/opinion/2020/10/joe-biden-for-president.html). The op-ed pages primarily consist of letters to the editor versus regular columnists. Although there is reasonable balance in the columns, some do offer loaded language and strong left-leaning perspectives such as this: [The GOP’s transformation from party to cult is now complete](https://www.cleveland.com/opinion/2019/12/the-gops-transformation-from-party-to-cult-is-now-complete.html). In general, the news is reported factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
