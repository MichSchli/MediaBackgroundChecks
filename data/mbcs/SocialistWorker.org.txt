History
Launched on 2008,
[SocialistWorker.org](https://socialistworker.org/get-involved) is the online version of the monthly print edition of Socialist Worker, which is published by the [International Socialist Organization.](https://www.internationalsocialist.org/) It is modeled after the British publication of the same name. According to their [about page](https://socialistworker.org/about) they describe themselves “As a newspaper and now a daily Web site, Socialist Worker has been committed to giving a voice to those struggling for a better world.” [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
SocialistWorker.org is funded through online advertising.
Analysis / Bias
SocialistWorker.org publishes articles and analyses of current events in the world and the USA from a Marxist perspective. Typically, their analysis and articles are well-sourced and they utilize credible sources such as
[PBS,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/) [Jacobin](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/jacobin/) [www.bls.gov](http://www.bls.gov). [http://cepr.net](http://cepr.net). and the [Washington Post.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/) SocialistWorker.org also uses loaded language in their headlines and the body of the article that favors the left, such as [“Who will go bust in the Trumponomics casino?”](https://socialistworker.org/2018/02/07/who-will-go-bust-in-the-trumponomics-casino) They occasionally utilize less known sources such as itsaboutfreedom.org and rarely a factually mixed source such as [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/).
Failed Fact Checks
- None in the Last 5 years
Overall, when covering political issues,
[SocialistWorker.org](https://socialistworker.org/get-involved) has a left bias in story selection and economic theory. We also rate them High for factual reporting due to extensive sourcing and providing links to their claims. (M. Huitsing 2/26/2018) Updated (08/08/2023)
