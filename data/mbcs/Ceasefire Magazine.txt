History
[Ceasefire](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/about/) is a British-based quarterly journal established in 2002. Ceasefire covers news, politics, reviews, analysis, art, and culture. In their own words, they cover “a wide range of topics – from Arthouse to Žižek.” Hicham Yezza is the founder and editor-in-chief. For a list of all writers and staff, see [here](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/about/).
[Read our profile on UK government influence on media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Hicham Yezza is the founder and owner of Ceasefire. They
[state](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/about/), “We are entirely reader-sustained and accept no advertising or corporate/government funding of any kind.” They accept donations from readers to generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Ceasefire publishes articles with emotionally loaded headlines such as “
[Has the world forgotten about Jamal Khashoggi?”](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/jamal-khashoggi-article/)
They also publish articles strongly biased against the Labor party, such as this:
[“What kind of Labour anti-racist movement do we want?](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/kind-labour-anti-racist-movement-want/) A quote from the article reads, “Given the history of Labour and its role as an administrator of capitalism within the British state, something that isn’t going to change with Corbyn” and against the Conservative party such as [“Hollow Man: Boris Johnson’s delusions of grandeur won’t survive reality.”](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/hollow-man-boris-johnsons-delusions-grandeur-survive-reality/) and this [Chronicle of a Coup Foretold: Boris Johnson’s assault on democracy must be stopped.](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/chronicle-coup-foretold-boris-johnsons-assault-democracy-stopped/) Boris Johnson is serving as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom and [Leader](https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2019/jul/21/boris-johnson-route-to-number-10) of the Conservative Party.
In the past, Ceasefire published scientific articles such as
[“Beyond the Higgs Boson: can future choices really affect the past?”](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/higgs-boson-hype-future-choices-affect-past/) However, the last article was published in 2012. Ceasefire also conducts interviews such as this with Former Greek Minister of Finance Yanis Varoufakis: [“Never before have we had so much money, yet so little investment in what humanity needs.”
](https://ceasefiremagazine.co.uk/yanis-varoufakis-never-money-investment-humanity-needs/)
Ceasefire utilizes credible sources such as
