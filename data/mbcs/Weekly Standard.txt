History
The Weekly Standard is an American neoconservative opinion magazine published 48 times per year. Its founding publisher, News Corporation, debuted the title on September 18, 1995. The Weekly Standard
[has been called](https://www.economist.com/united-states/2005/09/15/the-neocon-bible) the “Neo-Con Bible” by several sources. The founders are [William Kristol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Kristol) and [Fred Barnes.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fred_Barnes_(journalist)) The current editor is [Stephen F. Hayes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_F._Hayes).
Funded by / Ownership
The Weekly Standard is owned by
[Clarity Media Group](https://www.sourcewatch.org/index.php/Clarity_Media_Group), which also owns other right-leaning publications such as the [San Francisco Examiner](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-examiner/) and the [Washington Examiner](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-examiner/). The Magazine is funded through a [subscription](http://subscribe.weeklystandard.com/?iKey=I%2A%2AE24) and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
The Weekly Standard has a solid reputation for well written conservative journalism featuring
[prominent writers](https://www.weeklystandard.com/about-us) such as Tucker Carlson, Brit Hume, and PJ O’Rourke. The Weekly Standard typically publishes articles that are favorable to the right and uses moderately loaded wording such as this: [Are Conservatives Giving Up On Democracy?](https://www.weeklystandard.com/arch-puddington/donald-trump-vladimir-putin-and-the-conservative-case-for-democracy) When [reporting on President Trump](https://www.weeklystandard.com/irwin-m-stelzer/is-the-trump-economy-sustainable) the Weekly Standard is neither openly for, or against him, but rather reports factually with a conservative-leaning opinion. When it comes to sourcing information, they tend to use quotes as they are a print magazine. Articles on the website are properly sourced with hyperlinks to credible media outlets.
