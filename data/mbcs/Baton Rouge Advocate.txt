History
Founded in 1925, The Baton Rouge Advocate is an American newspaper published in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. theadvocate.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Baton Rouge Advocate is owned by
[Georges Media Group](https://georgesmediagroup.com/), which owns businesses in the New Orleans area and other media publications. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Baton Rouge Advocate covers local news with minimal bias, such as
[Louisiana regulators continue allowing weak insurers to take on risky policies](https://www.theadvocate.com/baton_rouge/news/louisiana-continues-to-let-weak-insurers-take-risky-policies/article_62400e2f-8c89-52b8-8a3c-1f7c3c750fe1.html). National and international are derived from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The Baton Rouge Advocate properly labels opinion content and covers both sides. However, a thorough review of the opinion pages reveals more favor the left, such as this
[Our Views: An iconic mix of Spanish moss and pollution for Louisiana](https://www.theadvocate.com/baton_rouge/opinion/our_views/our-views-iconic-mix-of-spanish-moss-pollution-at-capitol/article_4dccf830-9ce2-572e-ab6c-e72f3d1aeb43.html). In general, the news is reported factually with a slight left-leaning bias.
