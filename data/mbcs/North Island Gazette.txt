History
The North Island Gazette is a newspaper published in Port Hardy, British Columbia, Canada. The website northislandgazette.com covers news, sports, crime, and business for the greater North Island area.
[Read our profile on the Canadian government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Black Press Media](https://www.blackpress.ca/about-us/) owns the North Island Gazette. Black Press, based in British Columbia, Canada, publishes over 170 titles across Canada and the western United States. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the North Island Gazette covers news for Port Hardy and the surrounding area with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[‘Potentially dangerous’: Arctic front bringing icy winds, freezing rain to much of B.C.](https://www.northislandgazette.com/news/arctic-front-bringing-icy-winds-freezing-rain-to-much-of-b-c/) National and international is published through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-canadian-press/).
Editorially, the North Island Gazette does not regularly produce op-eds. However, political news coverage tends to lean left. Generally, news reporting is factual, with a left-leaning bias in story selection.
