History
Estevan Mercury is an English-language newspaper in Estevan, Saskatchewan, Canada. sasktoday.ca/south/estevan-mercury is the online domain covering news, sports, business, and lifestyle.
[Read our profile on Canada’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Glacier Media Group](https://www.glaciermedia.ca/brands/media-directory#Community+Media) owns the Estevan Mercury. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Estevan Mercury publishes sports, weather, national, world, and local news for the greater Estevan, Saskatchewan region. Local news is reported factually and with minimal bias, such as this
[Estevan team wins provincial 8-ball championship](https://www.sasktoday.ca/south/local-sports/estevan-team-wins-provincial-8-ball-championship-6897052). The national news is typically republished from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2018/06/15/fact-check-of-trumps-canadian-trade-stats/), while world news is from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, Estevan Mercury’s Op-eds favor the left with stories such as this
[Column: Holidays don’t have to hurt the environment](https://www.sasktoday.ca/south/opinion/column-holidays-dont-have-to-hurt-the-environment-6275752). The paper generally offers a moderately liberal perspective and reports news factually.
