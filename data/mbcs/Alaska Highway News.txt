History
Alaska Highway News is an English-language newspaper in Fort St. John, British Columbia, Canada. alaskahighwaynews.ca is the online domain covering news, sports, business, and lifestyle.
[Read our profile on Canada’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Glacier Media Group](https://www.glaciermedia.ca/brands/media-directory#Community+Media) owns the Alaska Highway News. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Alaska Highway News publishes sports, weather, national, world, and local news for the greater Fort St. John, BC region. Local news is reported factually and with minimal bias, such as this
[Fort St. John property sales down in 2022](https://www.alaskahighwaynews.ca/local-real-estate/fort-st-john-property-sales-down-in-2022-6352795). The national news is typically republished from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2018/06/15/fact-check-of-trumps-canadian-trade-stats/), while world news is from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, Alaska Highway News’s Op-eds favor the left with stories such as this
[Watt’s Happening: COP15 a message of hope for life on Earth](https://www.alaskahighwaynews.ca/opinion/watts-happening-cop15-a-message-of-hope-for-life-on-earth-6321901). The paper generally offers a moderately liberal perspective and reports news factually.
