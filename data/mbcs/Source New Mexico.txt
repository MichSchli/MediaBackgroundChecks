History
The Source New Mexico is an online news website from New Mexico. Their
[about](https://sourcenm.com/about/) statement reads, “Source New Mexico is an independent, nonprofit news organization that shines a light on governments, policies and public officials so you get the information you need to make choices — about yourself, your family, your neighborhoods and communities. “ [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, the Source New Mexico is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The Source New Mexico is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The Source New Mexico publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from New Mexico. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects the people of New Mexico. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through moderate, loaded language
[Despite GOP pushback, bill protecting reproductive, gender identity health care passes Roundhouse](https://sourcenm.com/2023/03/18/despite-gop-pushback-bill-protecting-reproductive-gender-identity-health-care-passes-roundhouse/)
They properly source, linking to credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[Legislation to give low-income New Mexicans cheaper utility rates passes Senate](https://sourcenm.com/2023/03/15/legislation-to-give-low-income-new-mexicans-cheaper-utility-rates-passes-senate/). On the issues, the Source New Mexico promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [How the judge who could ban the abortion pill won confirmation in the U.S. Senate](https://sourcenm.com/2023/03/02/how-the-judge-who-could-ban-the-abortion-pill-won-confirmation-in-the-u-s-senate/). The Source New Mexico generally reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
