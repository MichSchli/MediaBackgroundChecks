History
Founded in 1964 by the real estate giant
[Bramalea Ltd](https://www.nytimes.com/1992/12/24/business/company-news-bankruptcy-for-bramalea.html), [Brampton Guardian](https://www.bramptonguardian.com/news-static/2545708-brampton-guardian-about-us/) (Originally called The Bramalea Guardian) is a community newspaper that focuses on community events, people, neighborhoods, and community life, serving the City of Brampton, Ontario, Canada, and the surrounding area.
In 1966, the Brampton Guardian was sold to The Toronto Telegram. After the Telegram
[closed](https://www.cbc.ca/archives/this-is-it-the-day-the-toronto-telegram-folded-in-1971-1.4884371) in 1971, Douglas Bassett (son of [John Bassett](https://archive.macleans.ca/article/1962/5/19/how-john-bassett-corporate-executive-became-a-celebrity)) sold the Brampton Guardian to Inland Publishing, a [subsidiary](https://www.mississauga.com/news-story/5701148-50th-anniversary-our-fourth-publisher-elio-agostini/) of the Telegram Corporation. In 1981, Inland Publishing was purchased by Metrospan and merged to become Metroland.

Analysis / Bias
In 2015 and
[2021](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2021/09/19/the-stars-editorial-board-endorses-liberals-for-2021-federal-election.html), the [Toronto Star](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/toronto-star/) (Nordstar), which owns the Barrie Advance publisher, Metroland Media Group, [endorsed](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2015/10/09/for-justin-trudeau-and-the-liberal-party-editorial.html) the Liberal Party under Justin Trudeau.
In review, the Brampton Guardian uses low biased wording when reporting local news such as this:
[Former Brampton Liberal MP Raj Grewal undecided on election](https://www.bramptonguardian.com/news-story/9341868-former-brampton-liberal-mp-raj-grewal-undecided-on-election/). In an opinion and editorial pieces, there is the moderate use of loaded emotional language that favors the left, such as this: “ [‘Build a wall around the GTA’: Brampton councilor channels Donald Trump in campaign against pot shops](https://www.bramptonguardian.com/news-story/9126525--build-a-wall-around-the-gta-brampton-councillor-channels-donald-trump-in-campaign-against-pot-shops/) “ and [“Editorial: Ford can seize opportunities on carding and sex ed.”](https://www.bramptonguardian.com/opinion-story/9110366-editorial-ford-can-seize-opportunities-on-carding-and-sex-ed/)
Doug Ford is the current
[Premier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_(Canada)) of Ontario, which serves as the provincial prime minister. He is the head of the center-right [Progressive Conservative Party of Ontario.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doug_Ford) In general, editorials do not have a favorable opinion of Doug Ford, such as this: [Premier can prove his government is “for the people” — all the people.](https://www.bramptonguardian.com/opinion-story/9110366-editorial-ford-can-seize-opportunities-on-carding-and-sex-ed/)
