History
The Port Clinton News-Herald is a newspaper that serves Port Clinton, Ohio, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. portclintonnewsherald.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Port Clinton News-Herald. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Port Clinton News-Herald covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Port Clinton, Ohio, area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Mayor Sanchez has a plan for putting proposed Fremont road tax to work](https://www.portclintonnewsherald.com/story/news/local/2023/10/26/fremont-road-tax-proposal-was-reduced-for-third-try-on-ballot/71289321007/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Port Clinton News-Herald does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[What are Ohio lawmakers doing to protect gay people? Not much.](https://www.portclintonnewsherald.com/story/news/local/2015/07/19/ohio-lawmakers-protect-gay-people-much/30381509/) After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
