History
Verywell Family is a medical and health website
[launched by About.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/parulguliani/2016/04/26/about-com-unveils-new-health-vertical-verywell/#19447dca7599) in 2017. The website provides factual, evidence-based information on pregnancy and parenting. According to their [about page](https://www.verywellfamily.com/about-us), “Verywell Family is a modern resource that offers a realistic and friendly approach to pregnancy and parenting. Our library of more than 5,000 pieces of content, created and refined over the past 20+ years, has been written by more than 100 healthcare professionals and industry experts, including experienced pediatricians and parenting coaches, and is then vetted by board-certified physicians.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Verywell Family is owned and published by
[Dotdash](https://www.verywellhealth.com/about-us), formerly About.com. Dotdash owns the VeryWell network, which consists of the websites [Verywell Health](https://www.verywellhealth.com/), [Verywell Fit](https://www.verywellfit.com/), [Verywell](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/verywell/), and [Verywell Mind.](https://www.verywellmind.com/) They also own the website [Investopedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/investopedia/). Revenue is derived through advertising and affiliate commissions for some of the products they recommend.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Verywell Family provides news and information about getting pregnant, pregnancy, and parenting. They also publish news with minimal bias, such as this
[Fertility May Be Delayed After Stopping Certain Types of Birth Control.](https://www.verywellfamily.com/fertility-may-be-delayed-after-stopping-birth-control-5088139) This article is properly sourced to the [British Medical Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-bmj-british-medical-journal/) and Conception Journal, and other credible medical journals. The website provides information on the Coronavirus from a pro-science perspective, such as this [Ongoing COVID-19 School Closures Could Impact Academic Achievement](https://www.verywellfamily.com/ongoing-covid-19-school-closures-could-impact-academic-achievement-new-research-suggests-5087544).
Editorially, VeryWell Family employs editors involved in medicine or the related field and fact-checkers who ensure the articles are accurate and evidence-based. In general, information is factual and presented with limited bias.
