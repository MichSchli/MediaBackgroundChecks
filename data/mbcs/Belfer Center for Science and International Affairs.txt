History
Founded in 1973,
[The Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs](http://www.belfercenter.org/about) is the hub of Harvard Kennedy School’s research, teaching, and training in international security and diplomacy, environmental and resource issues, and science and technology policy. In 2017, for the fourth year in a row, the Belfer Center was ranked the world’s #1 University Affiliated Think Tank by the University of Pennsylvania’s Think Tanks and Civil Societies Program. Funded by / Ownership

Analysis / Bias
The Belfer Center is a think tank that promotes international security and diplomacy, environmental and resource issues, and science and technology policy. The website features research and analysis that holds a left-center bias, such as this
[Permafrost Carbon Feedbacks Threaten Global Climate Goals](https://www.belfercenter.org/publication/permafrost-carbon-feedbacks-threaten-global-climate-goals). Other stories focus on the positives of immigration, such as these [Three Notable Immigrants Who Served Their Adoptive Land](https://www.belfercenter.org/publication/three-notable-immigrants-who-served-their-adoptive-land) and this covering LBGT rights [Remarks by Ambassador Samantha Power on Making LGBT Rights Universally Respected Human Rights](https://www.belfercenter.org/publication/remarks-ambassador-samantha-power-making-lgbt-rights-universally-respected-human-rights). All stories reviewed are properly sourced.
In general, The Belfer Center is a pro-science source that leans left politically.
