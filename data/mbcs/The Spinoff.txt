History
Founded in 2014, The Spinoff is a New Zealand-based independent media organization. It began with a focus on New Zealand TV and culture, later expanding to include business, politics, Māori perspectives, and more.
[Read more on New Zealand media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-zealand-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Spinoff Group owns the Spinoff. The platform derives revenue through advertising, content partnerships, sponsorships, and a podcast network.
Analysis / Bias
The Spinoff offers a broad spectrum of content, including daily news, features, and opinions on topics like society, media, and sports. It also features exclusive video and podcast series. Articles and headlines use moderately loaded language such as this
[Let’s Get Wellington Moving was a giant waste of time](https://thespinoff.co.nz/politics/19-12-2023/lets-get-wellington-moving-was-a-giant-waste-of-time). This story, like all others reviewed, is properly sourced from credible outlets like [Stuff](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/stuff/).
Although it doesn’t have a separate opinion section, opinion pieces are clearly marked. Further, they frequently report on politics and tend to cover both sides; however, our review indicates more favor the left, such as this in support of workers’ rights
[The hard-won, and swiftly lost, dream of Fair Pay Agreements](https://thespinoff.co.nz/politics/22-12-2023/the-hard-won-and-swiftly-lost-dream-of-fair-pay-agreements) and social issues [Congratulations, She’s a Boy: I have my very first shaving lesson](https://thespinoff.co.nz/society/22-08-2017/congratulations-shes-a-boy-i-had-my-very-first-shaving-lesson). In general, The Spinoff holds left-leaning editorial biases and reports news accurately.
