History
[Founded in 1812](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Haven_Register), The New Haven Register is a daily newspaper published in New Haven, Connecticut. The Register covers 19 towns and cities within New Haven and Middlesex counties, including New Haven. The current publisher is Mike Deluca. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funding / Ownership
The New Haven Register is owned by
[Hearst Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearst_Communications), which owns more than 300 media outlets across the United States, including the [San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Cosmopolitan](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cosmopolitan/). Revenue is generated through subscriptions, display ads, and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the New Haven Register covers local news through staff reporters with minimal wording bias:
[Hospitals gear up with coronavirus ‘triage centers’](https://www.nhregister.com/politics/article/Hospitals-gear-up-with-triage-centers-to-15111068.php?src=nhrhpcp). National and international news is covered through outside sources such as [Business Insider](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/business-insider/), [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, they have frequently endorsed Democratic candidates such as
[Barack Obama in 2012](https://www.nhregister.com/news/article/ENDORSEMENT-President-Barack-Obama-should-be-11513880.php). A review of the opinion page demonstrates more political positions favored by the left. Generally, the news is reported factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
