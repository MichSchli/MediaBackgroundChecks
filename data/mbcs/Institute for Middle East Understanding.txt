History
Founded in 2005 and based in Tustin, California, the
[Institute for Middle East Understanding (IMEU)](https://imeu.org/about) is a pro-Palestinian non-profit organization that provides analysis and facts about Palestine and Palestinians. A “group of concerned Americans founded it.” It described its mission as “to provide journalists with quick access to information about Palestine and the Palestinians, as well as expert sources, both in the United States and in the Middle East.”
Funding / Ownership
The Institute for Middle East Understanding (IMEU) is a non-profit organization that receives funding from the left-leaning
[Rockefeller Brothers Fund](https://www.rbf.org/grantees/institute-middle-east-understanding) and private donations, which can be viewed [here](https://imeu.networkforgood.com/).
Analysis / Bias
The Institute for Middle East Understanding assists journalists working on stories about Palestine or the Palestinians by providing quick access to the latest news stories, expert analysis, images, photographs, and other visuals. IMEU’s coverage includes commentaries, analysis,
[photo essays](https://imeu.org/photo_essay/volunteering-under-israeli-fire), and stories regarding Palestine-related issues, both political and cultural.
In review, the Institute for Middle East Understanding focuses on Palestinian affairs, emphasizing a Palestinian viewpoint. They publish well-sourced news stories from reputable, mostly left-leaning news organizations such as republishing
[news analysis](https://imeu.org/topic/category/news-analysis) from the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [The Nation](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-nation/), and least biased [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In their articles/interviews, the IMEU utilize neutral headlines [“Expert Q&A: On Trump’s Pledge to Move the US Embassy to Jerusalem,”](https://imeu.org/article/expert-qa-on-trumps-pledge-to-move-the-us-embassy-to-jerusalem) and in the body of articles, the IMEU thoroughly source utilizing credible sources such as [GovTrack](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/govtrack/) (enables its users to track the bills and members of the United States Congress), unispal.un.org, [Wall Street Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wall-street-journal/) and, [Jewish Telegraphic Agency (JTA).](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/jewish-telegraphic-agency-jta/)
