Analysis / Bias
In review, WSAZ News Channel provides news coverage for the Charleston-Huntington market and the surrounding areas, encompassing 61 counties. Local news is covered by reporters with minimal bias and often has video provided by the TV station:
[FBI agents searching area where remains were found](https://www.wsaz.com/content/news/FBI-agents-searching-area-where-remains-were-found--568592991.html). The national news is provided through other Gray Television affiliates and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). International news generally comes from [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/) and the Associated Press.
Editorially, WSAZ does not offer an opinion page and, when covering politics, utilizes minimally loaded words such as this
[Trump names Rep. Mark Meadows, his new chief of staff](https://www.wsaz.com/content/news/Trump-names-Rep-Mark-Meadows-his-new-chief-of-staff-568578611.html). In general, WSAZ reports news factually and with minimal bias.
