History
Founded in 1985, WFTX – Fort Myers News is a
[Fox](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-affiliated station licensed to Cape Coral, Florida, United States. fox4now.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WFTX – Fort Myers News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E._W._Scripps_Company), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
WFTX – Fort Myers News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Fort Myers, Florida area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[Deputies locate subject after disturbance that prompted school lockout](https://www.fox4now.com/news/local-news/lee-county/deputies-locate-subject-after-disturbance-that-prompted-school-lockout). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press, and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Final week, final pitches for candidates in Florida’s primary](https://www.fox4now.com/news/political/final-week-final-pitches-for-candidates-in-floridas-primary). In general, they present low biased, fact-based reporting.
