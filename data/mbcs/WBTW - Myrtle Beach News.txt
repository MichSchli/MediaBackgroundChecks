History
Founded in 1954, WBTW – Myrtle Beach News is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Myrtle Beach, South Carolina. The associated website is wbtw.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WBTW – Myrtle Beach News is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The WBTW – Myrtle Beach News website publishes news for the greater Myrtle Beach, South Carolina area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[Raymond Moody confessed, led officials to Brittanee Drexel’s body, deputies say](https://www.wbtw.com/brittanee-drexel/raymond-moody-confessed-led-officials-to-brittanee-drexels-body-deputies-say/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[‘White supremacy is a poison’: Biden urges Americans to not stay silent after Buffalo attack.](https://www.wbtw.com/news/washington-dc-news/white-supremacy-is-a-poison-biden-urges-americans-to-not-stay-silent-after-buffalo-attack/) Reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides, and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
