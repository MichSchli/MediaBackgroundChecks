History
Founded in 2014 and based at Cornell University, the Alliance for Science is a non-profit foundation that promotes the use of GMOs for food sustainability. They describe their
[mission](https://allianceforscience.cornell.edu/about/mission/) as “The Cornell Alliance for Science seeks to promote access to scientific innovation as a means of enhancing food security, improving environmental sustainability, and raising the quality of life globally.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Alliance for Science is a non-profit whose primary source of support is a grant from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. They also
[list all donors](https://allianceforscience.cornell.edu/about/funders/) who gave over $1000.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Alliance for Science publishes news and studies that align with the
[consensus of science](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-gmo-dangers/) regarding GMOs. Articles are well-sourced and use minimally loaded language such as this: [Kenya reconsidering GMO crop ban to support food security](https://allianceforscience.cornell.edu/blog/2019/04/kenya-reconsidering-gmo-crop-ban-support-food-security/). The website also features educational resources that simply and clearly explain GMO such as this [10 Myths about GMO’s.](https://allianceforscience.cornell.edu/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/mythsFINAL.pdf)
Although the Alliance for Science supports the consensus of science, they have faced criticism as well:
[Why is Cornell University hosting a GMO propaganda campaign?](https://theecologist.org/2016/jan/22/why-cornell-university-hosting-gmo-propaganda-campaign)
