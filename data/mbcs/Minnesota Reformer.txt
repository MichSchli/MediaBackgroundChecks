History
The Minnesota Reformer is an online news website from Michigan. Their
[about](https://minnesotareformer.com/about/) statement reads, “The Minnesota Reformer is an independent, nonprofit news organization dedicated to keeping Minnesotans informed and unearthing stories other outlets can’t or won’t tell. “ [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, the Minnesota Reformer is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The Minnesota Reformer is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The Minnesota Reformer publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from Minnesota. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects the people of Minnesota. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through moderate, loaded language
[DFL Legislature moving quickly on a range of issues three weeks into session](https://minnesotareformer.com/briefs/dfl-legislature-moving-quickly-on-a-range-of-issues-three-weeks-into-session/).
They properly source, linking to credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[DFL trifecta brings renewed effort in Legislature to ban conversion therapy for minors](https://minnesotareformer.com/2023/01/18/dfl-trifecta-brings-renewed-effort-in-legislature-to-ban-conversion-therapy-for-minors/). On the issues, the Minnesota Reformer promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [U.S. House GOP would make it easier for feds to give public lands away to states](https://minnesotareformer.com/2023/01/17/u-s-house-gop-would-make-it-easier-for-feds-to-give-public-lands-away-to-states/). The Minnesota Reformer generally reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
