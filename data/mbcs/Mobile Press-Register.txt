History
Founded in 1813, The Mobile Press-Register is a newspaper from Mobile, Alabama. The paper and website cover local, national, sports, business, and lifestyle news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Advance Publications own the Mobile Press-Register. The company dates to the early 1920s and is owned by the
[Newhouse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Irving_Newhouse,_Sr.) family. Donald Newhouse and his brother Samuel “Si” Newhouse Jr. own Advance Publications. The [New Yorker](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-yorker/), [Vanity Fair](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vanity-fair/), and Reddit are all produced by Advance Publications. Revenue is generated through advertising and subscription sales.
Analysis / Bias
The Mobile Press-Register covers local news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Alabama closes some oystering areas, sparking complaints](https://www.al.com/news/mobile/2022/12/alabama-closes-some-oystering-areas-sparking-protests.html). National and international news is covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, they have a slight right-leaning bias and employ known right-leaning columnists, such as Cameron Smith, Vice President of the
[R Street Institute](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/r-street-institute/), a conservative and libertarian think tank based in Washington, D.C. They generally report the news factually and with a moderate conservative bias.
