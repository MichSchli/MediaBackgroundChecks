History
Founded in 1946, WBAL – Baltimore News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Baltimore Maryland, United States. Wbaltv.com is the associated website that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime for the greater Baltimore, MD area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WBAL – Baltimore News is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Hearst donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WBAL – Baltimore News reports on news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Baltimore, Maryland area. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Fire officials: 7 people — including officers, EMTs — hospitalized after explosion in Woodlawn](https://www.wbaltv.com/article/building-fire-in-woodlawn-baltimore-county/40015166#). A video accompanies most stories from the TV station. The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Hearst affiliates typically cover the national news.
Editorially, WBAL – Baltimore News does not publish editorial pieces, and when reporting on national politics, there is minimal bias as the primary source is usually the Associated Press. In general, they present fact-based, low-biased news.
