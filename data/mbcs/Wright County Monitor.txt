History
The Wright County Monitor is a newspaper published in Eagle Grove,
[Indiana](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-governors-bias-ratings/), United States. The paper covers news, sports, lifestyle, and opinion for Wright County and the surrounding area. clarionnewsonline.com serves as the domain for the online version of the newspaper. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Mid America Publishing owns and publishes the Wright County Monitor. Mid America Publishing publishes
[several community newspapers](https://www.midampublishing.com/content/mid-america-publications) throughout Indiana. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Wright County Monitor reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Wright County, Indiana region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[New Water Treatment Facility Officially Broke Ground, the Future for City Water](http://www.clarionnewsonline.com/content/new-water-treatment-facility-officially-broke-ground-future-city-water). The newspaper and website do not publish national news, focusing on local news reporting.
Editorially, the Wright County Monitor reasonably represents both sides. A review of op-eds demonstrates a reasonably low bias; however, slightly more op-eds favor the right. While more op-eds favor the right, liberal perspectives have fair representation; therefore, they earn a least biased rating on the right side of the scale. In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
