History
Frontiers in Audiology and Otology is a peer-reviewed open-access journal that covers the fields of Audiology and Otology. According to its
[about page,](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/audiology-and-otology/about) “Frontiers in Audiology and Otology is a leading multidisciplinary open access journal for the publication of high-quality and cutting-edge clinical, basic and translational research, peer-reviewed by experts, with the aim of fostering and supporting a collaborative global community of scientist and clinicians driven to decipher the mysteries of the inner ear.” [Read our profile on Switzerland’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/switzerland-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Frontiers in Audiology and Otology is owned by
[Frontiers Media SA](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals), which is a for-profit corporation. Funding comes from digital sales of content and author fees for publication.
Analysis / Bias
Frontiers in Audiology and Otology is an open-access journal that publishes information and research related to the inner ear. The journal primarily publishes research such as this
[Specialty Grand Challenge: Smarter Solutions for Tinnitus](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fauot.2023.1101233/full).
[Frontiers Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/frontiers/) has been criticized for being a predatory open-access publisher. [In short](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-019-03759-y), Predatory Open Access publishing is an exploitative open-access publishing business model that involves charging publication fees to authors without providing the editorial and publishing services associated with legitimate journals.
However, Frontiers in Audiology and Otology is a credible scientific journal, as we have not found any controversy or significant retractions. Although, Frontiers Media has
[retracted](https://retractionwatch.com/2014/09/26/publisher-issues-statement-of-concern-about-hiv-denial-paper-launches-investigation/) some studies in other publications that were found to be of poor methodology and not thoroughly peer-reviewed.
