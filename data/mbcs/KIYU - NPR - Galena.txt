History
KIYU – NPR – Galena is an FM radio station (88.1) based in Galena, Alaska, that broadcasts National Public Radio (NPR).
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KIYU – NPR – Galena is broadcast by the Big River Public Broadcasting Corporation and repeated in the following markets: KRBY Ruby / KOYU Koyukuk / KNUL Nulato / KALG Kaltag / KHUS Huslia / KTYU Tanana/ KHUU Hughes / KKET Allakaket / KSYU St. Mary’s. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KIYU – NPR – Galena website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [A dangerous storm disrupts travel and knocks out power across the U.S](https://www.npr.org/2022/12/24/1145419116/winter-storm-travel-power-outage-dangerous-conditions). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content such as this
[Migrants warmed by the community as freezing temperatures linger in El Paso](https://www.npr.org/2022/12/24/1145245308/el-paso-migrants-church-shelters-christmas-freezing). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
