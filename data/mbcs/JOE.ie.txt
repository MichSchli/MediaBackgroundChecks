History
Launched in 2010,
[JOE.ie](https://www.joe.ie/about) is an Irish lifestyle website initially founded to provide an [“online outlet for Irish males.”](https://universitytimes.ie/2015/01/not-all-talk/) The website covers various topics such as news, sports, business, classifieds, and lifestyle. The format is similar to [BuzzFeed](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/buzzfeed/), including lists, [quizzes](https://www.joe.ie/quiz), and [viral](https://www.joe.ie/joe/viral) videos. Her.ie, is a sister site aimed at young women in Ireland. The UK operation is called JOE.co.uk. Niall McGarry is the founder. [View our profile on Ireland’s media and government](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ireland-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The venture capital firm
[Greencastle capital](https://www.irishtimes.com/business/media-and-marketing/greencastle-completes-purchase-of-joe-and-her-media-brands-1.4376893) is the owner of JOE.ie. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription.
Analysis / Bias
In review, JOE.ie publishes listicles such as
[“17 times Obama and Joe Biden proved they have the world’s greatest bromance.”](https://www.joe.ie/news/17-times-obama-joe-biden-proved-worlds-greatest-bromance-573535) They also cover a variety of topics, including politics, most often with emotionally loaded language such as “ [Joe Biden tells audience: “I may be Irish, but I’m not stupid”](https://www.joe.ie/politics/joe-biden-tells-audience-may-irish-im-not-stupid-688643) and [“A simple warning for the Green Party: don’t screw us on this.”](https://www.joe.ie/politics/a-simple-warning-for-the-green-party-dont-screw-us-on-this-697109)
Editorially, story selection tends to slightly favor the left with stories such as this
[WATCH: Dublin Bus publish delightful video focusing on Ireland’s older LGBT people for Pride](https://www.joe.ie/news/watch-dublin-bus-674221) and report negatively on conservatives like this [Liz Truss gets absolutely skewered on UK local radio over fracking questions](https://www.joe.ie/news/liz-truss-thought-shed-get-an-easy-ride-on-local-radio-then-she-got-absolutely-skewered-755892).
Regarding sourcing, they often use extensive quotes and credible sources such as
[The Irish Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-irish-times/), [The Irish Examiner](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-irish-examiner/), [United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP),](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-nations-environment-programme-unep/) and [Irish Independent](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/irish-independent/). In general, JOE.ie holds a left-leaning editorial bias and is factual when reporting political news. However, the lists they create are highly subjective and sensational, resulting in a Mostly Factual rating for overall reporting.
