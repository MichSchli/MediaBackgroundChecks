History
[Founded in 1987](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roosevelt_Institute), The Roosevelt Institute is a liberal American think tank. According to the organization, it exists “to carry forward the legacy and values of Franklin and Eleanor Roosevelt by developing progressive ideas and bold leadership in the service of restoring America’s promise of opportunity for all.” It is headquartered in New York, New York. The current President and CEO is Felicia Wong. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Roosevelt Institute is a non-profit that is funded through donations. Some
[donors](https://www.masslive.com/news/2015/02/amherst_colleges_roosevelt_ins.html) include the Democratic Alliance, the Ford Foundation, the William and Flora Hewlett Foundation, the MacArthur Foundation, and the Bauman Foundation. According to [OpenSecrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) the Roosevelt Institute donates [100% to Democratic candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000051071&cycle=2020) and causes.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Roosevelt Institute is a liberal think tank that advocates for progressive and democratic policy. Some of the issues they champion are climate change, income inequality, support for labor, and higher education. The website features a blog that publishes news with a left-leaning bias such as this:
[The Trump Economy: Not What It May Seem](https://rooseveltinstitute.org/trump-economy-not-what-it-may-seem/). This story is very well sourced to government data as well as [hbr.org](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/harvard-business-review/). Editorially, the Roosevelt Institute consistently favors the left.
