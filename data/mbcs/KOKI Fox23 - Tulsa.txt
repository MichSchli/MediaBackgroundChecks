History
Founded in 1980, KOKI Fox23 – Tulsa is a
[FOX](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-affiliated television station licensed to Tulsa, Oklahoma, United States. The associated website fox23.com covers news, sports, crime, and weather. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Cox Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cox_Enterprises) owns KOKI Fox23 – Tulsa through their subsidiary Cox Media Group. They are a company that owns numerous newspapers, television stations, and radio stations, such as the [Atlanta Journal-Constitution](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/atlanta-journal-constitution/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KOKI Fox23 – Tulsa covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Tulsa, Oklahoma area. Local news is covered with minimally loaded language, such as this
[Driver arrested in connection with crash that killed an Oklahoma high school student](https://www.fox23.com/news/oklahoma/driver-arrested-connection-with-crash-that-killed-an-oklahoma-high-school-student/EZ23MQSBAFFBLEP7UIMLO72PMI/). Many local news stories contain videos from the TV station. The national news is primarily republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, KOKI Fox23 – Tulsa does not publish op-eds; however, when covering politics, they present info with minimal bias, such as this
[Proposed bill outlaws high school student-professor relationships](https://www.fox23.com/news/proposed-bill-outlaws-high-school-student-professor-relationships/YPL5KLCR5ND6HGSPC2SV6OL3XQ/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
