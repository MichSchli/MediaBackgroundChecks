History
United States Courts (uscourts.gov) is a website maintained by the Administrative Office of the U.S. Courts on behalf of the
[Federal Judiciary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_judiciary_of_the_United_States). The website provides news and information related to the federal court system. [Chief Justice John G. Roberts, Jr](https://www.supremecourt.gov/about/biographies.aspx#:~:text=John%20G.%20Roberts%2C%20Jr.,two%20children%20%2D%20Josephine%20and%20Jack.) is the head of the Federal Judiciary. [Read our profile on the United States government and media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
The Federal Judiciary owns, publishes, and funds the uscourts.gov website.
[The budget](https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/IF/IF11842#:~:text=The%20FY2023%20judiciary%20budget%20request,funds%20for%20judicial%20retirement%20accounts.) for the Federal Judiciary for the fiscal year 2023 is 8.64 billion dollars. It is unknown how much funding is directed at uscourts.gov.
Analysis / Bias
The uscourts.gov website provides information and news regarding the Federal Court System. For example, the website has a section for learning
[ about the Federal Courts](https://www.uscourts.gov/about-federal-courts), a section dedicated to learning about the [judges](https://www.uscourts.gov/judges-judgeships), another describing [services and forms](https://www.uscourts.gov/services-forms), statistics and reports, rules and policies, and the ability to [look up court cases](https://www.uscourts.gov/court-records) via PACER.
To review bias, we look at the Judiciary News section for story selection balance and wording. Articles and headlines reviewed typically do not contain emotional language such as this
[Just the Facts: Revocations for Failure to Comply with Supervision Conditions and Sentencing Outcomes](https://www.uscourts.gov/news/2022/06/14/just-facts-revocations-failure-comply-supervision-conditions-and-sentencing-outcomes) and [Bankruptcy Filings Continue Steady Drop](https://www.uscourts.gov/news/2022/05/17/bankruptcy-filings-continue-steady-drop). All information is properly sourced. In general, uscourts.gov is an informational website with minimal bias.
