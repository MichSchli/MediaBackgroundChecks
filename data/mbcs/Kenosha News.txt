History
[Founded in 1894](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenosha_News), The Kenosha News is a daily newspaper published in Kenosha, Wisconsin, United States, covering southeastern Wisconsin and northeastern Illinois. The paper covers news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. Kenoshanews.com serves as the domain for online newspaper content. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Lee Enterprises owns and publishes the Kenosha News. According to
[Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Lee Enterprises donated 88% of its money to [Republican politicians](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052990&cycle=2014) from 1990 until 2016. However, during the 2020 election cycle, they donated 70% to Democratic candidates and causes. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Kenosha News reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Kenosha, Wisconsin region. Reporters cover local news with minimal bias, such as this
[Kenosha man wanted in officer shooting](https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/kenosha-man-wanted-in-officer-shooting/article_59c674e8-f6d8-51d8-b194-c1ef3c54b983.html#tracking-source=home-breaking). The website publishes national and international news from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [CNN](https://www.kenoshanews.com/news/local/kenosha-man-wanted-in-officer-shooting/article_59c674e8-f6d8-51d8-b194-c1ef3c54b983.html#tracking-source=home-breaking).
Editorially, the Kenosha News often endorses Democratic Presidential candidates such as John Kerry in 2004, Barack Obama in 2008, and
[Hillary Clinton in 2016](https://www.kenoshanews.com/opinion/we-endorse-hillary-clinton-for-president/article_b671c791-97aa-57cf-a94d-6ea6eea0e4a5.html). A review of editorial articles demonstrates that slightly more favor a liberal perspective such as this [Editorial: Conservatives on the Supreme Court look set to trash Roe v. Wade’s viability line — but what would they replace it with?](https://www.kenoshanews.com/opinion/editorial/editorial-conservatives-on-the-supreme-court-look-set-to-trash-roe-v-wade-s-viability/article_7ba74dcf-1427-5d3b-9406-8bd6e7891095.html). The news is generally reported factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
