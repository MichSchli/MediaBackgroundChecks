History
KUCB – NPR – Unalaska is an NPR FM radio station (89.7) based in Unalaska, Alaska, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KUCB – NPR – Unalaska is owned and broadcast by Unalaska Broadcasting, Inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KUCB – NPR – Unalaska website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Army Corps to begin cleanup efforts at Fort Learnard in Unalaska Bay](https://www.kucb.org/regional/2023-05-05/army-corps-to-begin-cleanup-efforts-at-fort-learnard-in-unalaska-bay). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[Opinion: Alaska Airlines And Ravn Air Travel Decisions Harm The Community Of Unalaska](https://www.kucb.org/opinion/2019-11-18/opinion-alaska-airlines-and-ravn-air-travel-decisions-harm-the-community-of-unalaska). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
