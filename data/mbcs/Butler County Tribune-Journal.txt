History
The Butler County Tribune-Journal is a newspaper published in Allison, Indiana, United States. The paper covers news, sports, lifestyle, and business for Butler County and the surrounding area. butlercountytribune.com serves as the domain for the online version of the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Mid America Publishing owns and publishes the Butler County Tribune-Journal. Mid America Publishing publishes
[several community newspapers](https://www.midampublishing.com/content/mid-america-publications) throughout Indiana. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Butler County Tribune-Journal reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Butler County, Indiana region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[Supervisors informed of fairground renovation project](http://www.butlercountytribune.com/content/supervisors-informed-fairground-renovation-project). The newspaper and website do not publish national news, focusing on local news reporting.
Editorially, the Butler County Tribune-Journal does not produce op-eds and rarely interjects opinions in news stories. In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
