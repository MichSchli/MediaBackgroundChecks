History
Founded by Joe Martino 2009, Collective-Evolution is a clickbait, conspiracy, and quackery-level pseudoscience website. According to their
[about page](https://www.collective-evolution.com/about-us/), “CE has grown to become one of the world’s most popular conscious media outlets that provide readers and viewers an opportunity to expand their consciousness, unlock their potential and reshape their everyday way of being.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Collective-Evolution is owned by Joe Martino and is funded through advertising, paid subscriptions to
[CE-TV](https://cetv.one/?utm_source=website&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=cetv-home&utm_content=Subscribe-today), and donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Collective-Evolution promotes numerous conspiracy theories and pseudoscience. For example, they promote 9/11 conspiracies such as this:
[Important 9/11 Investigation Reveals Undeniable Truth](https://www.collective-evolution.com/2013/04/28/dr-daniele-ganser-incredible-911-investigation-reveals-telling-truth-about-911/). Some other topics you will find are [alien visitations](https://www.collective-evolution.com/2014/07/07/more-wikileaks-documents-surface-confirming-the-existence-of-extraterrestrial-life-ufos/), [chemtrails](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-chemtrails/), [miracle cancer cures](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-alternative-cancer-treatments/), [anti-GMO propaganda](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-gmo-dangers/), and of course, [anti-Vaxx articles](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-anti-vaccination-anti-vax/) such as this: [Why You Can’t Trust the CDC on Vaccines](https://www.collective-evolution.com/2019/01/27/why-you-cant-trust-the-cdc-on-vaccines/). There are many others with the common theme that almost none of it is supported by evidence.
