History
The Pasadena Star-News is a newspaper published in Pasadena, California, United States. pasadenastarnews.com is the website for the paper covering local news, sports, crime, lifestyle, and opinion.

Funded by / Ownership
The Pasadena Star-News is owned by
[MediaNews Group](https://www.medianewsgroup.com/our-brands/) and published by Digital First Media, which publishes 190 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund, described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Pasadena Star-News publishes local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Irwindale Speedway property is sold, but don’t expect speedway to go away overnight](https://www.pasadenastarnews.com/2022/10/01/irwindale-speedway-property-is-sold-but-dont-expect-speedway-to-go-away-overnight/). National and international news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/).
Editorially, the Pasadena Star-News publishes an appropriate mix of right and left-leaning opinions. In general, they report factually and with credible sources, while op-eds are mostly balanced with differing viewpoints.
