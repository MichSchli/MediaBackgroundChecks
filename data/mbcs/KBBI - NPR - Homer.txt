History
KBBI – NPR – Homer is an NPR AM radio station (890) based in Homer, Alaska, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KBBI – NPR – Homer is owned and broadcast by Kachemak Bay Broadcasting Inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KBBI – NPR – Homer website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [Borough supports Kasilof River bridge study](https://www.kbbi.org/2023-02-15/borough-supports-kasilof-river-bridge-study). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content such as this
[Come rain or come shine: How is climate change impacting precipitation along Kachemak Bay](https://www.kbbi.org/local-news/2023-02-03/come-rain-or-come-shine-how-is-climate-change-impacting-precipitation-along-kachemak-bay)? Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
