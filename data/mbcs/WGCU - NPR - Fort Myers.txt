History
WGCU – NPR – Fort Myers is an NPR FM radio station (90.1 FM) based in Fort Myers, Florida, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WGCU – NPR – Fort Myers is owned and broadcast by Florida Gulf Coast University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Drought gripping Southwest Florida; record-setting likely in Sarasota, Venice, Bradenton](https://news.wgcu.org/top-story/2023-11-22/drought-gripping-southwest-florida-record-setting-likely-in-sarasota-venice-bradenton).
