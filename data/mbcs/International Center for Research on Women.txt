History
[Founded in 1976](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Center_for_Research_on_Women), The International Center for Research on Women (ICRW) is a non-profit organization that promotes gender equitable development within the field of international development. According to their [about page](https://www.icrw.org/about/), their mission is “Anchored in the principle of human dignity, ICRW advances gender equity, inclusion, and shared prosperity. To this end, ICRW works with non-profit, government, and private sector partners to conduct research, develop and guide strategy and build capacity to promote evidence-based policies, programs, and practices.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The International Center for Research on Women (ICRW) is a non-profit organization headquartered in Washington, D.C., United States, with a regional office in New Delhi, India. ICRW has project offices in Mumbai and Hyderabad, India and is establishing a formal presence in East Africa. Foundations, public charities, and individuals provide funding.
Analysis / Bias
In review, ICRW promotes gender equity, inclusion, and the alleviation of poverty worldwide. The website provides information to learn more about individual issue areas; explore
[research programs](https://www.icrw.org/research-programs/) by issue area, progress status, and keyword; or browse or search a curated collection of [resources and publications](https://www.icrw.org/resources-publications/). The ICRW website does not really publish news, but rather research and analysis such as this [IN BRIEF: HOW HAVE FERTILITY DECLINES BENEFITTED WOMEN’S LIVES IN LOW- AND MIDDLE-INCOME COUNTRIES?](https://www.icrw.org/publications/in-brief-how-have-fertility-declines-benefitted-womens-lives-in-low-and-middle-income-countries/) The writing is scholarly and presents a minimal bias in wording, and the information is properly sourced. ICRW earns a Left-Center bias rating based on its advocacy for liberal social policy.
