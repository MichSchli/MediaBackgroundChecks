History
Population Connection is a non-profit organization in the United States that raises awareness of population challenges and advocates for improved global access to family planning and reproductive health care. The organization was
[founded in 1968](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_Connection) by [Paul R. Ehrlich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_R._Ehrlich), Richard Bowers, and Charles Remington in the wake of Ehrlich’s best-selling book, [The Population Bomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Population_Bomb). Their [mission statement](http://www.populationconnection.org/us/statement-policy/) is: “Overpopulation threatens the quality of life for people everywhere. Population Connection is the national grassroots population organization that educates young people and advocates progressive action to stabilize world population at a level that Earth’s resources can sustain.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Population Connection is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit funded through donations. They are transparent as they disclose their
[finances](http://populationconnection.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/2020-Consolidated-FS.pdf).
Analysis / Bias
In review, Population Connection publishes articles from their monthly magazine and a blog. All headlines and articles use minimally loaded language such as:
[UNTANGLING THE CONNECTIONS BETWEEN CLIMATE CHANGE AND REPRODUCTIVE JUSTICE.](http://www.populationconnection.org/climate-repro-justice/) Each article and blog entry is sourced through a combination of links, direct quotes, or MLA citations at the bottom of the article. Politically, the issues reported on this website are championed by the left.
Editorially, Population Connection
[advocates](http://www.populationconnection.org/population-connection-press/) for the following issues: Global access to contraception and reproductive health care, Population, and the environment, Reproductive rights, Comprehensive sex education for teens, U.S. funding for international family planning, Support for the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA) and Elimination of the Global Gag Rule.
