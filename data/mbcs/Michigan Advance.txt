History
The Michigan Advance is an online news website from Michigan. Their
[about](https://michiganadvance.com/about/) statement reads, “The Michigan Advance is a hard-hitting, nonprofit news site covering politics and policy across the state. We feature in-depth stories, briefs and social media updates, as well as top-notch progressive commentary. “ [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, the Michigan Advance is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The Michigan Advance is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The Michigan Advance publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from Michigan. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects the people of Michigan. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through moderate, loaded language
[Three Michigan 2020 presidential electors file suit against 16 GOP fake electors](https://michiganadvance.com/blog/three-michigan-2020-presidential-electors-file-suit-against-16-gop-fake-electors/).
They properly source, linking to credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[Rick Haglund: Michigan Dems offer a refreshing change to a people-based economic growth strategy](https://michiganadvance.com/2023/01/15/rick-haglund-michigan-dems-offer-a-refreshing-change-to-a-people-based-economic-growth-strategy/). On the issues, the Michigan Advance promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [Susan J. Demas: Don’t be fooled. Conservatives are still on a mission to destroy LGBTQ+ rights.](https://michiganadvance.com/2021/08/05/susan-j-demas-dont-be-fooled-conservatives-are-still-on-a-mission-to-destroy-lgbtq-rights/) The Michigan Advance generally reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
