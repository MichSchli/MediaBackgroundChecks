History
Launched in 2009,
[Odyssey](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/this-is-odyssey), also known as The Odyssey Online, is a news and entertainment website headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. The website was founded by [Evan Burns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odyssey_(publication)), who was fired and is now run by Eoin Townsend. Odyssey’s coverage features news, politics, fashion, relationships, sports, ideas, tech, and the future. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Odyssey Online utilizes a
[crowdsourcing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crowdsourcing) model, described by Fortune Magazine as “receiving articles from unpaid college kids already well-versed in social media.” According to the [Fortune article](http://fortune.com/2017/06/03/odyssey-media-layoffs/), “editors told Fortune they began creating fake profiles and writing under pseudonyms to increase their article count—whatever it took to meet the arbitrary content targets.”
Analysis / Bias
Odyssey’s coverage features news, politics, fashion, relationships, sports, ideas, tech, and the future.
The Odyssey Online covers tabloid-style news, emphasizing sensational headlines such as “
[10 Ways Young Adult Women Can Conceal Carry And Still Be Hot AF](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/women-conceal-carry-sexy).”
In review, their political reporting utilizes emotionally loaded words in their headlines, such as “
[Comey’s Failure”](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/comeys-failure) and [“A Love Letter To You, Paul Ryan.”](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/love-letter-paul-ryan) The Odyssey Online typically sources credible sources such as [ABC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/), [Rolling Stone](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/rolling-stone/), [The Guardian](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-guardian/), [NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/), and [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/); however, they also occasionally use factually mixed sources such as [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/).
The Odyssey Online on domestic matters favors the left, usually supporting
[socially liberal values](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/sex-marriage-affect-catholic-woman) such as: “One of our civil liberties as United States citizens is the freedom of religion. If you believe your religion doesn’t support homosexuality, that’s OK. What isn’t OK is thinking that your religious beliefs should dictate others’ lives. What isn’t OK is using your religion or your beliefs to take away rights from those who chose to live their life differently than you.”
