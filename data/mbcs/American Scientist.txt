History
[American Scientist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Scientist) (informally abbreviated AmSci) is an American bimonthly science and technology magazine published since 1913 by Sigma Xi, The Scientific Research Society. Each issue includes four to five feature articles written by prominent scientists and engineers who review research in fields from molecular biology to computer engineering. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
American Scientist is published by
[Sigma Xi: The Scientific Research Honor Society](https://ecommerce.sigmaxi.org/ecom/#Subscribe), a non-profit membership society, [501(c)3](https://www.guidestar.org/profile/06-0655494), founded in 1886. Donations, memberships, and subscriptions generate revenue.
