History
Founded in 1978, KFTA – Northwest Arkansas News is a
[Fox](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-affiliated television station licensed to Fort Smith, Arkansas. The associated website is nwahomepage.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KFTA – Northwest Arkansas News is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The KFTA – Northwest Arkansas News website publishes news for the greater Fort Smith, Arkansas area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[Fort Smith police arrest man accused of driving off, dragging officer by car](https://www.nwahomepage.com/news/fort-smith-police-looking-for-man-accused-of-driving-off-dragging-officer-by-car/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden administration to launch new program to help Ukrainian refugees amidst sanctions and military support](https://www.nwahomepage.com/washington/washington-dc/biden-administration-to-launch-new-program-to-help-ukrainian-refugees-amidst-sanctions-and-military-support/). Reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides, and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
