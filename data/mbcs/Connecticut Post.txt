History
[Founded in 1883](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Connecticut_Post), The Connecticut Post is a daily newspaper located in Bridgeport, Connecticut. It serves Fairfield County and the Lower Naugatuck Valley. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Connecticut Post is owned by
[Hearst Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearst_Communications), which owns newspapers, magazines, and television stations, including the [San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), [Cosmopolitan](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cosmopolitan/), and [Esquire.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/) The newspaper and website are funded through advertising, classified sales, and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Connecticut Post reports local news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Councilwoman with troubled finances seeks campaign funds](https://www.ctpost.com/local/article/Councilwoman-with-troubled-finances-seeks-14475524.php?src=posthpcp). National news is derived from other Hearst papers and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Connecticut Post tends to favor Democratic candidates, as evidenced by their
[endorsement](https://www.ctpost.com/opinion/slideshow/Connecticut-Post-2016-Endorsements-137057.php) of Democratic Presidential Candidate Hillary Clinton in the 2016 Presidential election. Further, a review of op-eds shows that the majority lean left, such as this: [Editorial: No time to waste on impeachment](https://www.ctpost.com/opinion/article/Editorial-No-time-to-waste-on-impeachment-14464245.php). In general, straight news is presented with low bias and proper sourcing, while editorial content favors the left.
