Funded by / Ownership The Cell Journal is published by Cell Press which is a subsidiary of [Elsevier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elsevier), a Dutch information and analytics company and one of the world’s foremost providers of scientific, technical, and medical information. It was established in 1880 as a publishing company. Elsevier’s parent company is RELX plc, a British multinational information and analytics company headquartered in London, England. The journal generates revenue through subscription fees and fees for open-access publishing. Analysis / Bias Cell Journal is a hybrid (some open-access) journal that publishes information and research related to cellular biology. The journal primarily publishes research such as this [Opioid-induced fragile-like regulatory T cells contribute to withdrawal](https://www.cell.com/cell/fulltext/S0092-8674(22)01573-2). Cell Press is an open-access/hybrid publisher of scientific journals. There are two types of Open Access Journals, legitimate and predatory, both of which charge the author for publication. [Predatory journals](https://predatoryjournals.com/about/) often lack peer review and are generally considered not credible. The Cell Journal is considered a [legitimate, open-access publisher](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5017830/) that is frequently indexed in [PubMed](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pubmed/). However, Cell Press has been criticized for [retracting some studies.](https://retractionwatch.com/category/by-journal/cell/) Although Cell Press has retracted some studies in other publications, Cell Journal publishes credible scientific studies that are factual and appropriately peer-reviewed.
