History
The Dunfermline Press is a newspaper based in Dunfermline, UK. The paper mainly focuses on topics such as news and politics, lifestyle and culture, and local events and news related to the Dunfermline area.
[Read our profile on UK media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Dunfermline Press is operated by
[Newsquest Media Group Ltd](https://www.newsquest.co.uk/)., owned by the American mass media holding company [Gannett](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/), the publisher of [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Dunfermline Press covers local news for the Dunfermline area through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Dunfermline: Former BAE training school to be demolished](https://www.dunfermlinepress.com/news/23715157.dunfermline-former-bae-training-school-demolished/). Regarding sourcing, they rely on first-person accounts and large quotes, which is appropriate for a print publication.
National and international news is usually republished through reliable sources such as
[PA Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pa-media-uk-bias/) (national news agency for the UK and Ireland) and [The Sunday Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-times-of-london/).
Editorially, story selection and op-eds are reasonably balanced and reflect both sides. Generally, the Dunfermline Press is fact-based and presents a minimal bias in reporting.
