History
[Founded in 1878](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WEHCO_Media), The Arkansas Democrat-Gazette is the newspaper of record in the U.S. state of Arkansas, printed in Little Rock with a northwest edition published in Lowell. It is distributed for sale in all 75 of Arkansas’ counties. The website lists the paper’s [core values](https://www.arkansasonline.com/corevalues/) which include: “Impartiality means reporting, editing, and delivering the news honestly, fairly, objectively, and without personal opinion or bias. Credibility is the greatest asset of any news medium, and impartiality is the greatest source of credibility.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Arkansas Democrat-Gazette is owned by
[WEHCO Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WEHCO_Media), which publishes 9 other newspapers in the region. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Arkansas Democrat-Gazette reports local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Toddler, woman, killed in crash on U.S. 67 near I-40 in North Little Rock, authorities say](https://www.arkansasonline.com/news/2020/feb/24/toddler-woman-37-die-crash-near-i-40-north-little-/). National and international news is derived from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Arkansas Democrat-Gazette has traditionally endorsed Republican candidates; however, in 2016, they refused to
[endorse](https://www.arkansasonline.com/news/2016/oct/09/not-for-the-donald-20161009/) Donald Trump or any candidate for that matter. A review of [editorials](https://www.arkansasonline.com/news/editorials/) indicates a reasonable balance; however, many favor right-leaning positions. Generally, news reporting is factual, while editorials tend to lean right.
