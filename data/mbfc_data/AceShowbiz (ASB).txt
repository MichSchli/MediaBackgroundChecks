History
Launched in 2005,
[AceShowbiz (ASB)](https://www.aceshowbiz.com/site/about.php) is an online entertainment magazine covering topics ranging from celebrity gossip, interviews, news pertaining to TV, movies, music, and lifestyle. The co-founder/publisher is [Denny Lee](https://www.zoominfo.com/p/Denny-Lee/1062955050), and the co-founder/editor is Anita Yang.
The website lacks transparency as they do not clearly indicate ownership or location. For example, Denny Lee’s profile states that AceShowbiz is from Hong Kong; however, at the bottom of all pages on the website, it indicates they are a part of the
[Kanal247.com](https://www.kanal247.com/) & [WowKeren.com](https://www.wowkeren.com/) networks, which originates from Indonesia. Further, they do not publish author names or bios. [Read our profile on Indonesian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/indonesia-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
AceShowbiz (ASB) does not openly disclose ownership; however, it is presumed to be owned and published by Denny Lee and Anita Yang. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
AceShowbiz (ASB) typically publishes articles with short text and big colorful pictures. They often publish sensational stories with emotionally loaded language such as
[“Lil Wayne’s Daughter Reginae Carter Almost Got Into Trouble With Her Mom For Posing In Lingerie”](https://www.aceshowbiz.com/news/view/00153765.html) and [“The ‘Cravings’ Author Is ‘Bummed And Hit Hard.'”](https://www.aceshowbiz.com/news/view/00153622.html) When it comes to sourcing AceShowbiz (ASB) hyperlinks to themselves or utilizing sources such as [New York Post’s](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-post/) gossip column Page Six, and when reporting news, they most often use large quotes from [CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/), [MSNBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/msnbc/), and [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/).
When covering political news such as President Trump, AceShowbiz (ASB) uses strong emotionally loaded language and
[sources through quotes ](https://www.aceshowbiz.com/news/view/00153696.html)“This Is Not The First Time For Trump To Patronize CBS’ Weijia Jiang As During A COVID-19 News Conference As Last Month, POTUS Told Her To ‘Relax’ And ‘Keep Your Voice Down.” Under the [Interview section](https://www.aceshowbiz.com/interviews/), we couldn’t find any interviews. In general, AceShowbiz demonstrates a left-leaning bias through story selection and sometimes does not source properly or name authors.
