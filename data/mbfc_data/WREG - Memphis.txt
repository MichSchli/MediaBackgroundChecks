History
Founded in 1956, WREG – Memphis is a CBS-affiliated television station licensed to Memphis, Tennessee. The associated website is wreg.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WREG – Memphis is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
WREG – Memphis website publishes news for the greater Memphis, Tennessee area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[Family identifies victims of Oak Court Mall shooting](https://www.wreg.com/news/local/family-identifies-victims-of-oak-court-mall-shooting/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden: US ‘considering’ diplomatic boycott of China Olympics](https://www.wreg.com/2022-olympics/biden-us-considering-diplomatic-boycott-of-china-olympics/). Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoidings using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
