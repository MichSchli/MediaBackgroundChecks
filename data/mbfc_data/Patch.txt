History
[Launched in 2007](https://patch.com/about), Patch is a hyperlocal news and information site which was formerly part of AOL. It is an online news and information portal for 1200 neighborhood and community websites across the USA. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Currently, Patch is owned by investment firm Hale Global. Warren St. John is the CEO. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Patch Media runs a large US-based hyperlocal network of sites. Patch primarily covers tabloid-style news and news that generates the most traffic, such as police and local sports, with an emphasis on sensational headlines such as “
[What Could Cause A Superintendent To Poop On A Track?”](https://patch.com/new-jersey/holmdel-hazlet/what-caused-nj-superintendent-allegedly-poop-track) Although headlines are somewhat sensational, the content of articles uses a [low biased neutral tone](https://patch.com/virginia/oldtownalexandria/protest-nra-draw-alexandria-students) that does not favor the right or left.
In their national news reporting, they utilize listicle style headlines such as “
[Stormy Daniels Payoff: 5 Things To Clear Your Confusion,”](https://patch.com/us/white-house/stormy-daniels-payoff-5-things-clear-your-confusion) and typically they source to credible sources such as the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [NBC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/), [citizensforethics.org](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/citizens-responsibility-ethics-washington-crew/), [commoncause.org](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/common-cause/), [Star-Ledger](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/star-ledger/), and occasionally factually mixed sources such as [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/) and [Fox News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news/). In addition, they republish stories from [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
