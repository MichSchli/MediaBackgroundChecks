History
The Albert Lea Tribune is a newspaper published in Alber Lea, Minnesota. The paper covers local, national, crime, and sports news.

Funded by / Ownership
The Albert Lea Tribune is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Albert Lea Tribune primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Albert Lea woman injured in crash on I-35](https://www.albertleatribune.com/2023/05/albert-lea-woman-injured-in-crash-on-i-35/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Albert Lea Tribune labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Albert Lea Tribune is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
