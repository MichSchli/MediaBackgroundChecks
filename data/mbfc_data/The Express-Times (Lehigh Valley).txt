History
[Founded in 1855](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Express-Times) as the Easton Daily Express, The Express-Times is a daily broadsheet newspaper serving Easton, Pennsylvania, and the greater Lehigh Valley Region. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Express-Times is owned by
[Advance Publications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advance_Publications), which is in turn owned by the [Newhouse Family](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Newhouse-family). According to [Open Secrets,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) Advance Publications has [donated](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000041920&cycle=2014) over 1 million dollars to political campaigns, with 91% going to Democrats. The Newspaper is funded through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Express-Times covers local news through the use of journalists with minimal loaded words and bias, such as this:
[Route 22 East crash briefly closes both lanes in Easton.](https://www.lehighvalleylive.com/easton/2019/02/route-22-east-crash-closes-both-lanes-in-easton.html) All original reporting names the author and provides sources such as interviews, quotes or links. National news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [Tribune News Service](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/tribune-news-service/), and the [Washington Post.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/)
Editorially, the paper covers a mix of left and right-leaning views, with perhaps a slight advantage to the left. They have also
[endorsed](https://www.lehighvalleylive.com/joe-owens/index.ssf/2008/10/barack_obama_gets_endorsement.html) Democratic Candidates such as Barack Obama in 2008.
