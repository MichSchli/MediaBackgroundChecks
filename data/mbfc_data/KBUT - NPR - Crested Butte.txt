History
KBUT – NPR – Crested Butte is an NPR FM radio station (90.3 FM) based in Crested Butte, Colorado, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KBUT – NPR – Crested Butte is owned and broadcast by the Crested Butte Mtn Educal. Radio Inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KBUT – NPR – Crested Butte website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Big snow brings big blooms to Gunnison Valley](https://kbut.org/local-news/big-snow-brings-big-blooms-to-gunnison-valley/). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[Heidi Steltzer: Mountain Scientist on Climate Change and the Power of Narrative](https://kbut.org/west-elk-word/heidi-steltzer-mountain-scientist-on-climate-change-and-the-power-of-narrative/). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
