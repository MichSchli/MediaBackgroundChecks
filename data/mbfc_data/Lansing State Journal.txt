History
[Founded in 1855](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lansing_State_Journal), The Lansing State Journal is a daily broadsheet newspaper published in Lansing, Michigan.
Funded by / Ownership
The Lansing State Journal is owned by the Gannett Company, which controls the
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) Network. Revenue is generated from advertising and subscription sales.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Lansing State Journal reports local news via staff journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Nearly 13,000 without power in Greater Lansing area as more storms approach](https://www.lansingstatejournal.com/story/news/local/2019/07/20/power-outage-lansing-bwl-dte-consumers-energy-storm-restoration/1784895001/). National and International news comes via the USA Today Network or the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the LSJ mostly
[endorses Democratic candidates](https://www.lansingstatejournal.com/story/opinion/editorials/2016/10/20/missed-endorsement-roundup-lsjs-2016-election-picks/92364516/). [Op-Eds](https://www.lansingstatejournal.com/opinion/) tend to slightly favor the left but provide a reasonable balance of non-extreme opinions. In general, this is a low-biased, factual news source that leans just slightly left editorially.
