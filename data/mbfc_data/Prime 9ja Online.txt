History
Prime 9ja Online is a Nigerian news website based in
[Benin City, Edo, Nigeria](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/prime9jaonline-media). The topics covered by Prime 9ja Online include politics, business, culture, entertainment, sports news, and news from Africa.
According to their
[about page](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/p/about.html), Prime 9ja Online states its mission as “to remain Nigeria’s primary source of digital news. Every day, we deliver factual news to keep millions of people around the world posted.” [Read our profile on Nigerian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nigeria-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their
[about page](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/p/about.html), “Prime 9ja Online (P9O) is a media/news website owned by Prime 9ja Online Media, a registered corporation managed by the founder, Chima Joseph Ugo in Edo, Nigeria.” Advertising and donations generate [revenue](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/p/funding.html).
Analysis / Bias
In review, Prime 9ja Online reports news on Nigerian national and local news. Articles and headlines often contain loaded emotional wording such as
[“Niger Delta leaders urge Muhammadu Buhari to scrap NDDC interim management](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/2022/07/niger-delta-leaders-urge-muhammadu.html).” Prime 9ja Online sources poorly, rarely using hyperlinks and often utilizing quotes. Online-only news sources must provide hyperlinks to the information they are presenting.
Prime 9ja Online reports positively on Nigeria’s center-right PDP party with loaded emotional wording
[“PDP: Atiku Abubakar’s 2023 triumph will surprise pollsters.”](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/2022/12/pdp-atiku-abubakars-2023-triumph-will.html) Atiku Abubakar is the presidential candidate of the center-right PDP for the 2023 general election. Their coverage of the governing center-left party APC and current president of Nigeria, Muhammadu Buhari, holds a negative tone, such as “ [Suspend Buhari’s Account Now, Trump Was Suspended For Lesser Issue — Nigerians Tell Twitter Boss.”](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/2021/06/suspend-buharis-account-now-trump-was.html)
When reporting on
[world news](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/2020/11/trump-campaign-asks-court-to-suspend.html), they rely on credible news outlets like the [AFP](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/afp-agence-france-presse/).
Editorially, Prime 9ja Online has a dedicated section for
[opinion pieces.](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/search/label/Opinion) A review of op-eds shows they cover all sides of the political debate, such as this [2023: Campaign promises and the issue of trust](https://www.prime9ja.com.ng/2022/10/2023-campaign-promises-and-issue-of.html). In general, the website is fact-based and provides balanced editorial perspectives.
