Analysis / Bias
In review, local news is handled by reporters covering crime, sports, weather, traffic, and lifestyle. There is minimal use of loaded language as evidenced by this,
[St. Louis police wrack up $14M in overtime, audit finds](https://www.kmov.com/news/st-louis-police-wrack-up-14m-in-overtime-audit-finds/article_254b78cd-0e13-58d5-8e26-c4b6b1861f3a.html). National and international news is often handled by other Gray properties and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, KMOV does not offer opinion pieces; however, they report on politics in a mostly neutral tone, such as this
[Trump announces SCOTUS pick](https://www.kmov.com/trump-announces-scotus-pick/video_286ff02e-5bb1-59e9-86d6-1f5bb80d1d5b.html). In general, news reporting is factual with minimal editorial bias.
