History
Inforum is the website for The Forum of Fargo-Moorhead, a daily broadsheet newspaper printed in Fargo, North Dakota. The Forum of Fargo-Moorhead was
[founded in 1891](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Forum_of_Fargo-Moorhead) and is the primary daily paper for southeast North Dakota and also much of northwest Minnesota. The Forum is also co-owned with radio station [WDAY AM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WDAY_(AM)) and TV stations [WDAY-TV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WDAY-TV) and [WDAZ-TV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WDAZ-TV). The website also publishes content from these sources. The current editor is Matthew Von Pinnon.
Analysis / Bias
Inforum is owned by the
[Forum Communications Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forum_Communications), which is a media firm based in Fargo, North Dakota. Forum Communications owns numerous newspapers within North Dakota and Minnesota. Revenue is derived from advertising, sponsored content, and [subscriptions](https://allaccess.forumcomm.com/allaccess/inforum) to deliver The Forum of Fargo-Moorhead.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Inforum covers news from the greater Fargo-Moorhead area. Local news is covered through the use of journalists from The Forum of Fargo-Moorhead newspaper and reporting from WDAY TV and Radio as well as WDAZ-TV. In general, Local reporting uses minimally loaded language in headlines such as this:
[Man arrested for firing a gun within Jamestown city limits](https://www.inforum.com/news/crime-and-courts/2731471-Man-arrested-for-firing-a-gun-within-Jamestown-city-limits). National and World news are delivered through third-party providers such as the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), and others.
Editorially, Inforum presents a
[reasonable balance](https://www.inforum.com/opinion/columns/) between left and right-leaning opinions, with slightly more leaning right. According to [Ballotpedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ballotpedia/), The Forum historically [endorses Republican candidates](https://ballotpedia.org/The_Forum_of_Fargo-Moorhead); however, in 2016, they chose [not to endorse a candidate for President](https://www.inforum.com/news/4142785-historically-rare-move-forum-editorial-board-doesnt-endorse-republican-president). Lastly, all information on the website is properly sourced.
