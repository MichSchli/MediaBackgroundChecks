History
Founded in 1982, WVNS – Bluefield News is a
[CBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Lewisburg, [West Virginia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/west-virginia-governor-jim-justice-political-bias-profile/). The associated website is wvnstv.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WVNS – Bluefield News is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The WVNS – Bluefield News website publishes news for the greater Lewisburg and Bluefield, West Virginia, area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias, such as this:
[Beckley firefighter offers fireworks safety tips](https://www.wvnstv.com/news/beckley-firefighter-offers-fireworks-safety-tips/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large, credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Supreme Court to decide on student loan forgiveness](https://www.wvnstv.com/washington/washington-dc/supreme-court-to-decide-on-student-loan-forgiveness/). Reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides, and avoids using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
