History
[The Varsity,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Varsity_(newspaper)#cite_note-5) founded in 1880, is a student newspaper from the University of Toronto/Canada. They have six sections which are news, comment, features, arts, science, and sports. [The Varsity](https://www.linkedin.com/company/varsity-publications-inc.) is entirely student-run and is funded through advertising and a student levy. Funded by / Ownership
The paper is funded through the school, and revenue is through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
The Varsity, in its editorials, uses minimally loaded headlines such as “
[Condemning hatred in Charlottesville is just the beginning. Media organizations and educational institutions must avoid equivocation when encountering white supremacy](https://thevarsity.ca/2017/08/28/condemning-hatred-in-charlottesville-is-just-the-beginning/)”. They typically link to verifiable media sources such as [CNBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnbc/) and [BBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/). They also source through quotations and interviews. With story selection, The Varsity tends to favor the left slightly.
