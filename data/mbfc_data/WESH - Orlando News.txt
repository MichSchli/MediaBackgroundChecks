History
Founded in 1956, WESH – Orlando News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Daytona, Florida, United States. WESH.com is the associated website that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime for the greater Orlando, FL area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WESH – Orlando News is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Hearst donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WESH – Orlando News reports on news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Orlando, FL area. Local news is covered by reporters with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Orange County deputies using simulators to train for active shooters](https://www.wesh.com/article/orange-county-deputies-active-shooter-training/40257526). A video accompanies most stories from the TV station. The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Hearst affiliates typically cover the national news.
Editorially, WESH – Orlando News does not publish editorial pieces, and when reporting on national politics, there is minimal bias as the primary source is usually the Associated Press. In general, they present fact-based, low-biased news.
