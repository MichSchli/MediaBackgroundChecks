History
Founded in 1992,
[FairVote ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FairVote)(formerly the Center for Voting and Democracy) is a 501(c)(3) organization that advocates electoral reform in the United States. FairVote advocates using [Ranked choice voting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ranked_choice_voting) (RCV), in elections. Under this system, voters rank candidates in order of preference, in contrast to the more widely used [plurality voting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plurality_voting) system. FairVote also wants to abolish the electoral college. According to their [about page](https://www.fairvote.org/about), “FairVote is a nonpartisan champion of electoral reforms that give voters greater choice, a stronger voice, and a representative democracy that works for all Americans.” [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
In review, FairVote advocates for the abolition of the Electoral College with a mission of fair representation for the citizens of the USA. FairVote publishes news stories containing minimally loaded words like this:
[How to increase voter turnout? New report suggests automatic registration, ranked choice voting.](https://www.fairvote.org/how_to_increase_voter_turnout_new_report_suggests_automatic_registration_ranked_choice_voting) All information on the website is sourced properly. FairVote generally publishes low-biased information; however, they advocate for significant changes in the voting system, which is opposed to the conservative view.
