History
WNIJ – NPR – DeKalb is an NPR FM radio station (89.5 FM) based in Dekalb, Illinois, serving the surrounding area. The website northernpublicradio.org also serves as the news page for WNIU, WNIE, WNIQ, and WNIW.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WNIJ – NPR – DeKalb is owned and broadcast by Northern Illinois University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WNIJ – NPR – DeKalb website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Ahead of Pritzker’s budget address, pressures that mounted last year intensify](https://www.northernpublicradio.org/illinois/2024-02-20/ahead-of-pritzkers-budget-address-pressures-that-mounted-last-year-intensify). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[Supreme Court weighs challenge to EPA rule; student loans wiped under SAVE plan](https://www.northernpublicradio.org/2024-02-21/supreme-court-weighs-challenge-to-epa-rule-student-loans-wiped-under-save-plan). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
