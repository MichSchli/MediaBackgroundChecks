History
[Israel War Room](https://www.israelwarroom.com/) is a website that advocates for the interests of the State of Israel and aims to combat what it perceives to be anti-Israel misinformation on social media and the internet by fact-checking. The website does not provide specific details about its inception, founders, or the geographical location of its headquarters. However, a [domain search](https://whois.domaintools.com/israelwarroom.com) indicates they were founded in 2020 with the domain registered at a United States IP address. [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The website lacks transparency as they do not provide clear information regarding its funding and ownership.
Analysis / Bias
Israel War Room strongly defends Israel by debunking perceived misconceptions and addressing the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, democracy status, and apartheid accusations. The site’s editorial stance is characterized by a strong nationalistic and pro-Israel bias, which often counters criticisms of Israeli policies and reflects a perspective that aligns closely with Israeli governmental and pro-Israel advocacy positions. The content on the site engages in counter-narratives to prevalent critiques of Israel in international discourse.
The website’s selective use of information strongly suggests a bias in support of a pro-Israel viewpoint despite claiming factual accuracy.
In review, the article
[“Palestinians Are Supporting Russia in the War Against Ukraine”](https://www.israelwarroom.com/tp/palestinians-are-supporting-russia-in-the-war-against-ukraine) uses emotionally loaded language and a one-sided perspective, lacking in balanced representation. The article mentions “new reports” indicating Palestinian involvement in the Ukraine conflict on Russia’s side but does not provide specific evidence or citations for these claims. This framing could influence readers’ perceptions, potentially leading to a skewed understanding of the events described. Further, the article presents and interprets facts to support a specific viewpoint, using selective information from various sources, such as [Israel Hayom](https://www.israelhayom.com/2022/05/18/senior-pa-official-meets-with-lavrov-in-moscow/), [Israel 21C](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/israel21c/), [The Algemeiner Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-algemeiner-journal/), natanrelief.org (Not rated), [The Jerusalem Report](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-jerusalem-report/), [BBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/), [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), [Foundation for Defense of Democracies – FDD ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/foundation-defense-democracies/) which indicates a tailored narrative.
Similarly, “
[Israel is not an Apartheid State”](https://www.israelwarroom.com/tp/israel-is-not-an-apartheid-state) asserts that Israel is not an apartheid state, distinguishing between the rights of Arab citizens of Israel and the status of Palestinians in the West Bank without providing external citations to support its claims. This stance contrasts with human rights organizations’ [reports](https://www.hrw.org/report/2021/04/27/threshold-crossed/israeli-authorities-and-crimes-apartheid-and-persecution), which often cite evidence when discussing Israel’s policies and their alignment with apartheid definitions under international law.
In general, the Israel War Room is a dedicated advocacy platform with a clear pro-Israel stance, engaging in counter-narratives against perceived anti-Israel misinformation. Its content is focused on defending and promoting Israel’s image and policies. While it claims to fact-check and provide context, the site’s strong advocacy role suggests that its factual presentation is aligned with its pro-Israel bias. Further, there is evidence they have published false information.
