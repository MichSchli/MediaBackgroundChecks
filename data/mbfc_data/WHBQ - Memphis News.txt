History
Founded in 1953, WHBQ – Memphis News is a
[FOX](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-affiliated television station licensed to Memphis, Tennessee, United States. The associated website fox13memphis.com covers news, sports, crime, and weather. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Cox Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cox_Enterprises) owns WHBQ – Memphis News through their subsidiary Cox Media Group. They are a company that owns numerous newspapers, television stations, and radio stations, such as the [Atlanta Journal-Constitution](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/atlanta-journal-constitution/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WHBQ – Memphis News covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Memphis, Tennessee area. Local news is covered with minimally loaded language, such as this
[Man indicted in carjacking, murder as police search for more suspects](https://www.fox13memphis.com/news/local/man-indicted-carjacking-murder-police-search-more-suspects/ARONEEUKPVHBZL5JANQQZZ2PAQ/). Many local news stories contain videos from the TV station. The national news is primarily republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, WHBQ – Memphis News does not publish op-eds; however, when covering politics, they present info with minimal bias, such as this
[Abortion rights may rest on governor’s races in some states](https://www.fox13memphis.com/news/politics/abortion-rights-may/HJYU7DWZF4SVPBA64MFSQ5H2NU/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
