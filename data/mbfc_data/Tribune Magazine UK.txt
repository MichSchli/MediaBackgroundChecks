History
Tribune Magazine is a democratic socialist
[fortnightly newspaper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribune_(magazine)), founded in 1937 and published in London.
Funded by / Ownership
Since the autumn of 2016 the journal has been owned by businessman
[Owen Oyston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Owen_Oyston), who acquired its parent company London Publications Ltd. The paper is subscription based.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Tribune Magazine typically favors the Labour Party but remains independent. The articles on the website use minimally loaded words and are generally properly sourced. Story selection strongly favors the left, especially as it relates to economics, which is aligned with democratic socialism. Editorially, the paper does not have a favorable view of
[President Trump](http://www.tribunemagazine.org/2018/03/trump-embarrassed-into-expulsions/) or conservatives. A [factual search](https://factualsearch.news/#gsc.tab=0&gsc.q=tribunemagazine.org&gsc.sort=&gsc.ref=fact_checking) reveals they have not failed a fact check.
