History
[Founded in 1906](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Worth_Star-Telegram), The Fort Worth Star-Telegram is a U.S. daily newspaper serving Fort Worth and the western half of the North Texas area known as the Metroplex. The Star-Telegram has won two Pulitzer Prizes in its history. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Fort Worth Star-Telegram is operated by
[The McClatchy Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McClatchy) and is owned by Chatham Assets Management which owns numerous papers across the United States, including the [Fresno Bee](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fresno-bee/). Revenue is derived from advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Star-Telegram covers local news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[13 new coronavirus cases confirmed in Tarrant County, bringing total to 42](https://www.star-telegram.com/news/coronavirus/article241397056.html#storylink=mainstage_lead). National and international news is provided by the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/) and [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Star-Telegram has mostly endorsed
[Republican candidates](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/); however, in 2008, they endorsed Democratic Presidential Candidate Barack Obama. A review of Op-Eds reveals a slight rightward lean. In general, the Star-Telegram reports news factually with a right-leaning editorial bias.
