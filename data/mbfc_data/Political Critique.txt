History
Founded in 2002 by
[Stanisław Brzozowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Brzozowski_(writer)), Krytyka Polityczna (Political Critique) is a quarterly magazine that is part of the largest Central and Eastern European liberal network of institutions and activists. The aim of Krytyka Polityczna is to revive the tradition of engaged Polish intelligentsia. [Read our profile on Poland’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/poland-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Political Critique is owned and published by Stanisław Brzozowski Association. Revenue is derived through donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Political Critique publishes news and information related to social science, culture, and politics. Articles often align with the left and utilize moderately loaded language such as
[The Future of European Gender Politics](http://politicalcritique.org/cee/hungary/2019/the-future-of-european-gender-politics/). This article is sourced from [Salon](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/salon/).
Politically, Political Critique leans strongly left with
[concerns](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/) for [climate change](http://politicalcritique.org/cee/poland/2019/polands-changing-climate-activists/), [social justice](http://politicalcritique.org/cee/poland/2019/under-attack-reproductive-rights-in-poland/) and also presents [pro-science positions](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-anti-vaccination-anti-vax/) on [vaccines](http://politicalcritique.org/cee/poland/2019/the-rise-and-fall-of-the-polish-anti-vaccination-movement/). In general, most stories favor the left and are factual.
