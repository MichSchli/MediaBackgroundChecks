History
[Founded in 1954](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WMUR-TV), WMUR9 is an [ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Manchester, New Hampshire, United States. WMUR.com is the associated website that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime for the greater Manchester area. WMUR is also a part of the Boston, Massachusetts market. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WMUR is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) Hearst has donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WMUR.com reports on news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Manchester and Boston areas. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias in wording such as this
[Governor: First shipment of vaccine set to arrive in NH Monday.](https://www.wmur.com/article/governor-first-shipment-of-vaccine-will-be-in-nh-monday/34954131) Most stories are accompanied by a video from the TV station. National news is typically covered by the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other Hearst affiliates.
Editorially, WMUR does not publish editorial pieces and when reporting on national politics there is minimal bias as the primary source is usually the Associated Press. In general, the news is reported factually with minimal editorializing.
