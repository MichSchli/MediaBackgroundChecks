History
The Alliance Review is a newspaper that serves Alliance, Ohio, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. the-review.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Alliance Review. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Alliance Review covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Alliance, Ohio, area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Massillon Latter-day Saints church faces foreclosure; all started with unpaid $2,101 fee](https://www.the-review.com/story/news/local/massillon/2023/09/07/latter-day-saints-church-lost-exemption-over-unpaid-2101-assessment/70616792007/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Alliance Review does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[State Rep. Bob Young makes mockery of the law, public service](https://www.the-review.com/story/opinion/columns/2023/09/06/state-rep-bob-young-makes-mockery-of-the-law-public-service/70716220007/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
