History
The Doylestown Intelligencer is a newspaper that serves Doylestown, Pennsylvania, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. phillyburbs.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Doylestown Intelligencer. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Doylestown Intelligencer covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Doylestown, Pennsylvania, area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[A $15M wall could stop flooding in Bristol, but what about the Delaware River view?](https://www.phillyburbs.com/story/news/local/2024/01/04/bristol-borough-continues-to-struggle-with-flooding-delaware-river-mill-street-lions-park-wharf/72085363007/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Doylestown Intelligencer does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Fetterman stands with Wabtec workers, backs bill to ensure strikers have access to food](https://www.phillyburbs.com/story/opinion/columns/2023/07/27/john-fetterman-wabtec-strike-snap-bill-ue-post-gazette-labor-wages-economy-senate-casey-sherrod/70475639007/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
