History
Frontiers in Communication is a peer-reviewed open-access journal that covers communication. According to its
[abo](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/cognition/about)u [t page,](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/cognition/about) “A cross-disciplinary journal that advances our understanding of the global communication revolution and its relevance across social, economic and cultural spheres.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Frontiers in Communication is owned by
[Frontiers Media SA](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals), which is a for-profit corporation. Funding comes from digital sales of content and author fees for publication.
Analysis / Bias
Frontiers in Communication is an open-access journal that publishes information and research related to communication. The journal primarily publishes research like this
[Insights into the time course of evidentiality processing in Turkish heritage speakers using a self-paced reading task](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fcomm.2023.1070510/full).
[Frontiers Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/frontiers/) has been criticized for being a predatory open-access publisher. [In short](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-019-03759-y), Predatory Open Access publishing is an exploitative open-access publishing business model that involves charging publication fees to authors without providing the editorial and publishing services associated with legitimate journals.
However, Frontiers in Communication is a credible scientific journal, as we have not found any controversy or significant retractions. Although, Frontiers Media has
[retracted](https://retractionwatch.com/2014/09/26/publisher-issues-statement-of-concern-about-hiv-denial-paper-launches-investigation/) some studies in other publications that were found to be of poor methodology and not thoroughly peer-reviewed.
