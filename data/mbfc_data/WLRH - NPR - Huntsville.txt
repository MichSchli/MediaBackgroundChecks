History
Founded in 1976, WLRH – Huntsville is an FM radio station (89.3) based in Huntsville, Alabama, that broadcasts National Public Radio (NPR).
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WLRH – Huntsville is broadcast from the UAH Campus. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WLRH – Huntsville website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [Albany bishop accused of abuse has asked the pope to remove him from the priesthood](https://wlrh.org/NPR-News/albany-bishop-accused-abuse-has-asked-pope-remove-him-priesthood). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content such as this
[Pelosi shattered the marble ceiling and leaves a historic leadership legacy](https://wlrh.org/NPR-News/pelosi-shattered-marble-ceiling-and-leaves-historic-leadership-legacy). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
