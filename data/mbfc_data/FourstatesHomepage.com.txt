History
FourStatesHomepage.com is a news website associated with KSNF-TV and KODE-TV in Joplin, Missouri.
[KSNF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KSNF) is an [NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/) affiliate launched in 1968, while [KODE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KODE-TV) is an [ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/) affiliate launched in 1954. The website reports local news, weather, sports, and crime news. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
FourStatesHomepage is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns over 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the FourStatesHomepage.com website publishes news for the greater Joplin, Missouri area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[The Joplin mask ordinance is expiring](https://www.fourstateshomepage.com/news/the-joplin-mask-ordinance-is-expiring/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Stations. The national news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire and other large media outlets. Lastly, the FourStatesHomepage website does not publish op-eds. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
