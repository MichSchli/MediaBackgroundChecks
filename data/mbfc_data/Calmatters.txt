History
Calmatters.org was
[founded in 2015](https://calmatters.org/about/) by Simone Coxe, Chris Boskin, and David Lesher. It is a non-profit news organization that covers politics, policy, and issues that affect California. The website is based in Sacramento, California. According to their about page, CalMatters.org aims to “engage and inform Californians about public policy in their state” and “produce high-impact journalism that educates and engages the public, and holds those in power accountable.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership:
CalMatters.org is a non-profit organization that relies on donations from individuals, foundations, and other organizations to fund its operations. According to their
[financial disclosures](https://calmatters.org/about/funding/), they receive funding from various sources, including The James Irvine Foundation and the Knight Foundation. The organization claims that it has a diverse funding base to maintain its editorial independence.
Analysis / Bias
CalMatters.org primarily publishes news related to California politics and public policy. The news is written with moderate bias through loaded emotional language such as this
[The California bills on a business ‘kill list](https://calmatters.org/newsletters/whatmatters/2023/03/california-business-job-killer-bills/),’ and they utilize credible sources such as government websites, academic institutions, and expert interviews.
Editorially, CalMatters.org tends to lean left, with a focus on progressive policies. For example, they have published articles that support concerns with
[climate change](https://calmatters.org/environment/2022/11/california-revises-climate-change-plan/) and [LGBTQ](https://calmatters.org/health/2019/06/reproductive-rights/) issues, and they have criticized the former [Trump administration’s immigration policies](https://calmatters.org/immigration/2019/08/orange-county-lawmaker-condemns-trump-immigration-plan-vietnam-public-charge/).
CalMatters.org promotes pro-science, accurate information on climate change and the environment. They have published numerous articles on the subject, emphasizing the need for California to take action on climate change.
CalMatters.org is generally a credible source that provides comprehensive coverage of California politics and policy. While they have a moderate left-leaning bias, they support their claims with credible sources. CalMatters.org has not failed any fact-checks and is a trustworthy source for information on California politics and policy.
