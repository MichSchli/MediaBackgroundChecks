History
[The State](http://www.thestate.com/customer-service/about-us/) is a daily newspaper published in Columbia, South Carolina. The State Media Company was founded on February 18, 1891, by the Gonzales brothers (N.G., Ambrose, and William). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The State is owned by
[Chatham Asset Management,](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-08-30/behind-trump-s-tabloid-king-a-connected-and-flush-hedge-fund) a New Jersey-based hedge fund with a significant equity stake in Postmedia and majority ownership of American Media Inc. In 2020, Chatham Asset Management bought the McClatchy Company, which owned numerous newspapers around the USA and has won several Pulitzer Prizes. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The State covers local news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Picnicking couple attacked by bear multiple times on Blue Ridge Parkway, rangers say.](https://www.thestate.com/news/state/north-carolina/article254658687.html) National and international comes from other Chatam papers and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The State tends to favor Democratic candidates through endorsements. They generally report the news factually and with a slight left-leaning bias.
