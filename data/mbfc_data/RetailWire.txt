History
Launched in February
[2002](https://retailwire.com/discussion/the-epic-tale-of-retailwire-and-its-co-founders-can-now-be-told/), [RetailWire](https://retailwire.com/about-us/) is an online forum for the retail industry. It presents trending topics for discussion by its [“BrainTrust](https://retailwire.com/braintrust/),” a panel of retail industry experts and thought leaders who regularly provide insights and analysis at RetailWire. RetailWire, co-founded by [Rick Moss](https://retailwire.com/author/rick-moss/), Al McClain, and Santi Briglia, hosts daily commentaries forming a virtual roundtable on key retail trends. RetailWire is based in Brooklyn, New York. [Read our profile on USA Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Chase Binnie](https://retailwire.com/author/chase-binnie/) is the owner and CEO of RetailWire. The operations are financially supported through [sponsorships](https://retailwire.com/about-us/) from leading retail tech solutions providers and service organizations.
Analysis / Bias
RetailWire, a website with sponsored content in its “Resources” section, promotes specific products like cloud retail management solutions. It also republishes articles from various sources in its “Retail News” section, including
[The Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [The Wall Street Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wall-street-journal/), [MarketWatch](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/marketwatch/), [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/), [Business Insider](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/business-insider/), [Milwaukee Journal Sentinel](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/milwaukee-journal-sentinel/), [CNBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnbc/), [Toronto Star](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/toronto-star/), among others.
We reviewed several articles for bias and factuality.
[“Has Nike Learned It Just Can’t Do It With DTC Sales Alone?”](https://retailwire.com/discussion/has-nike-learned-it-just-cant-do-it-with-dtc-sales-alone/) provides a balanced discussion of Nike’s business strategies, citing [Motley Fool](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-motley-fool/) as its source. [“Shopify Dumps Trump’s Stores from its Platform”](https://retailwire.com/discussion/shopify-dumps-trumps-stores-from-its-platform/) uses emotionally loaded language in its title, suggesting a negative portrayal of Former President Trump, and cites sources like uscp.gov and [TechCrunch](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/techcrunch/). The next article, [“Retailers Call on Trump to End the National Chaos He Created,”](https://retailwire.com/discussion/retailers-call-on-trump-to-end-the-national-chaos-he-created/) also uses emotionally loaded language and presents Mr.Trump negatively. Given the word choices and use of credible sources, RetailWire is rated as Left-Center Biased with a High factual reporting rating due to proper sourcing.
