History
The News Journal Media Group launched
[Delaware online](http://www.delawareonline.com) in 1996. Delaware Online states its focus is “to provide our readers with another product to keep them informed about news, real estate, entertainment, and much more in the state of Delaware” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Delaware Online is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Delaware Online covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the state of Delaware. Local news is reported with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[25-year-old man shot near New Castle after fight breaks out at Route 13 intersection](https://www.delawareonline.com/story/news/2021/09/11/new-jersey-man-shot-at-fight-on-route-13-near-new-castle/8297775002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, Delaware Online does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented such as this that favor the left. Overall, in viewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a more liberal perspective. Generally, the news is reported factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
