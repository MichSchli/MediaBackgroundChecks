History
WPRO – 99.7 FM – Providence is a Providence, Rhode Island, talk radio station. The radio station features conservative news talk shows, while the website publishes local and political news. The web domain for the station is 997wpro.com.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WPRO – 99.7 FM – Providence is owned by
[Cumulus Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cumulus-media-bias/), a publicly-traded American broadcasting company that owns over 400 radio stations. Cumulus Media is owned by its shareholders, including hedge funds and institutional investors. A review of [political donations](https://www.opensecrets.org/campaign-expenditures/vendor?cycle=2020&vendor=Cumulus+Media) indicates more favoring Republican candidates and PACS. Advertising through radio and the web generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
WPRO – 99.7 FM – Providence publishes local news stories that utilize minimally loaded language, such as this:
[RI fugitive accused of faking his death to avoid rape charge in Utah is extradited from Scotland](https://www.997wpro.com/2024/01/05/ri-fugitive-accused-of-faking-his-death-to-avoid-rape-charge-in-utah-is-extradited-from-scotland/). This story, like all other local stories, did not provide hyperlinked sourcing to the information.
The radio station and website broadcasts a daily lineup of news radio hosts and shows that generally hold moderate conservative perspectives.
Editorially, the majority of radio hosts hold moderate conservative viewpoints. While they do not publish op-eds, political news reporting is low biased, such as this
[Harvard president Claudine Gay resigns amid plagiarism claims, backlash from antisemitism testimony](https://www.997wpro.com/2024/01/02/harvard-president-claudine-gay-resigns-amid-plagiarism-claims-backlash-from-antisemitism-testimony/). Again, this story lacks hyperlinks to credible sources. In general, WPRO – 99.7 FM – Providence holds slightly right-leaning biases and is mostly fact-based.
