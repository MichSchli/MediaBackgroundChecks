Funded by / Ownership
WTAE-TV and WTAE.com are owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Hearst donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WTAE Action News reports on news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Pittsburgh area. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Coronavirus in Western Pa.: Over 6,700 cases in 14 counties, according to PA Dept. of Health](https://www.wtae.com/article/coronavirus-cases-pittsburgh-western-pennsylvania/31552064). A video accompanies most stories from the TV station. The national news is typically covered by [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/), [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other Hearst affiliates.
Editorially, WTAE reports on politics and produces usually balanced commentary; however, slightly more favor a liberal perspective such as this
[WTAE Editorial: Protests in, around Pittsburgh held to combat racial injustice](https://www.wtae.com/article/wtae-editorial-protests-in-around-pittsburgh-held-to-combat-racial-injustice/32853571). Generally, the news is reported factually with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
