History
Federal News Network (Radio)
[was founded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WFED) in the late 1990s and became the first internet-only, all-news radio station in February of 2000. In 2004, Federal News Network moved onto the radio airwaves at 1050 AM. In 2008, it moved again to a Clear Channel 50,000-watt station at 1500 AM. Funded by / Ownership
Federal News Network and the website are owned by
[Hubbard Radio](http://corporate.hubbardradio.com/). The website and radio programming are funded through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Federal News Network website publishes news and analysis on US government employees, agencies, and private contractors.
Federal News Network reports news with minimal bias in wording such as this:
[Bill authorizing government reorganization now has a House companion](https://federalnewsradio.com/reorganization/2018/09/bill-authorizing-government-reorganization-now-has-a-house-companion/). Further, all information is properly sourced to Government and Federal Agencies or credible media outlets. Federal News Network is affiliated with the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [ABC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/), [Voice of America](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/voice-of-america/), and WUSA-TV.
