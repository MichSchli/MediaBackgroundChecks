History
[Founded in 1851](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Mercury_News), The San Jose Mercury News is a morning daily broadsheet newspaper published in San Jose, California, the largest city in Silicon Valley. The newspaper has earned several awards, including two Pulitzer Prizes. The current editor is Neil Chase. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The San Jose Mercury News is published by the
[Bay Area News Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_Area_News_Group), a subsidiary of [Digital First Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_First_Media). Digital First Media owns nearly 100 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund, which has been described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former Denver Post writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Revenue is derived through subscription fees and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the San Jose Mercury News reports original local and regional news through reporters with minimal bias such as this:
[Bay Area renters displaced during holiday season highlight ‘loopholes’ in new California law](https://www.mercurynews.com/2019/12/19/renters-displaced-during-holiday-season-highlight-loopholes-in-new-state-law/). National and International news primarily comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, Mercury News has endorsed Democratic Presidential Candidates since 1992. Further, the paper offers
[local election endorsements](https://www.mercurynews.com/tag/endorsements/) that usually favor Democratic candidates. Lastly, the paper’s op-eds often negatively report on former President Trump, such as this: [Editorial: Impeach Trump. No American is above the rule of law](https://www.mercurynews.com/2019/12/15/editorial-impeach-trump-no-american-is-above-the-law/). In general, The Mercury News reports news factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
