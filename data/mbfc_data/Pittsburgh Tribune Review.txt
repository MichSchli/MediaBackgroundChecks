History
Founded in 1811, The Pittsburgh Tribune-Review, also known as “the Trib,” was the second-largest daily printed newspaper serving metropolitan Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, in the United States until it transitioned to an all-digital format on December 1, 2016, as triblive.com.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Trib Total Media owns the website. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, local news is covered with minimal bias by reporters on the ground, and national/world news is aggregated from various sources, including the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/). Editorially, there is a right-leaning bias as most stories favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually with a right-leaning bias editorially.
