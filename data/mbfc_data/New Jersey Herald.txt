History
The New Jersey Herald is a newspaper that serves Newton, New Jersey, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. njherald.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The New Jersey Herald. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The New Jersey Herald covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Newton (Sussex County), New Jersey area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Judge gives suspended Franklin fire chief a shot at redemption following arrest](https://www.njherald.com/story/news/courts/2023/02/02/franklin-fire-chief-arrest-judge-gives-60-days-prove-self/69865948007/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The New Jersey Herald does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Getting every New Jersey kid guaranteed health coverage remains essential | Opinion](https://www.njherald.com/story/opinion/2023/01/19/nj-kids-health-insurance-cover-all-kids/69819114007/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
