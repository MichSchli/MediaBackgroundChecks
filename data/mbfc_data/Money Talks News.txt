History
Founded in 1991,
[Money Talks News](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/about/) is a personal finance news website that provides money-saving and investing tips and information, personal finance news, business news, analysis, and affiliate links. Stacy Johnson is the founder and CEO. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Stacy Johnson is the owner of Money Talks News through
[J&G Publications Inc](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/about/ownership-funding-info/). The website generates revenue through online advertising, membership fees, affiliate links, and the sale of online courses.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Money Talks News provides financial news, personal finance news, and advice. In general, they publish articles made up of a series of facts and tips, such as this article: “
[7 Reasons Not to Take Social Security at Age 62](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/slideshows/reasons-to-delay-claiming-social-security-retirement-benefits/).” Money Talks News provides an advertising disclosure, and they often source back to themselves and utilize credible sources such as Social Security Administration (SSA), bls.gov, and [whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/).
When reporting on politicians, headlines generally utilize loaded words, such as when reporting on the Biden administration, 7
[ Ways President Biden Plans to Fight Inflation](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/slideshows/7-ways-president-biden-plans-to-fight-inflation/), [How the Biden Presidency Could Affect Your Money](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/how-the-biden-presidency-could-affect-your-money/), and [ 4 Ways Biden’s New Plan Could Lower Your Taxes.](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/4-ways-bidens-new-plan-could-lower-your-taxes/) When reporting on former Republican President Donald Trump, they usually display a negative tone: [“12 Ways Donald Trump Is Crushing Consumers.”](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/12-ways-donald-trump-crushing-consumers/) A quote from the article reads, “Love him or hate him, you can’t deny that President Trump is attacking regulations and funding that was intended to help regular American consumers.” Other example headlines are [“Taxpayers Paying a Fortune to Protect Trump Family](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/heres-how-much-protecting-the-trump-family-is-costing-taxpayers/)” and [“Watch This: How Did Donald Trump Get Rich?”](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/watch-this-how-did-donald-trump-get-rich/)
Editorially, Money Talks News tends to favor the left regarding economic philosophy:
[15 States With the Largest Carbon Footprint](https://www.moneytalksnews.com/slideshows/15-states-with-the-largest-carbon-footprint/). In general, they are fact-based and lean left editorially.
