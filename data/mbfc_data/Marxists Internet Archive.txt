History
Launched in 1990,
[The Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/admin/intro/history/index.htm) (MIA) is a digital repository offering various Marxist, anarchist, and socialist literature in various languages. Initially formed by an individual known as the Zodiac, it grew through the efforts of numerous volunteers. In 1998, the marxists.org domain was established, solidifying its presence as an online resource for Marxist and non-Marxist literature. Funded by / Ownership
MIA is a
[California-based](https://www.marxists.org/admin/legal/nonprofit/status01.jpg) non-profit, 501(c)(3) organization., primarily sustained through [donations](https://www.marxists.org/admin/donate/index.htm), possibly [book sales](https://www.marxists.org/admin/books/index.htm) and [volunteer](https://www.marxists.org/admin/volunteers/volunteers.htm) support. They maintain transparency in their financial operations by publicly sharing detailed financial reports accessible at [MIA Financial Reports.](https://www.marxists.org/admin/budget/index.htm)
Analysis / Bias
The Marxists Internet Archive (MIA) provides left-leaning content from a Marxist, anarchist, and socialist viewpoint. The articles range from academic texts to opinion-based editorials. While its political bias is apparent, it does not necessarily compromise the factual accuracy of its historical and academic content. Many articles on MIA come with extensive lists of sources cited, often including historical texts, speeches, and writings by notable figures in socialist, Marxist, and anarchist thought.
For example, writings may reference works by
[Karl Marx](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/m/a.htm#marx), [Friedrich Engels](https://www.marxists.org/archive/lenin/works/1895/misc/engels-bio.htm), or contemporary leftist thinkers. The tone of MIA’s content can vary. Academic and historical pieces like Lenin’s [“On the Eve”](https://www.marxists.org/archive/lenin/works/1906/jun/08.htm#bkV11E001) article have a formal and informative tone. However, opinion pieces or editorials might exhibit a more emotionally loaded or persuasive tone, aiming to advocate for certain viewpoints or interpretations consistent with leftist ideologies.
An example of this type of article is
[“Sanders, Trump and the US Working Class,”](https://www.marxists.org/history/etol/writers/trudell/2016/xx/sanders-trump.html) which was originally published in International Socialism, a journal known for its socialist perspective. This publication typically presents analyses and commentaries on political and economic issues through a socialist lens. MIA republished this article on its platform, which examines the political dynamics of the 2016 U.S. presidential election, focusing on Bernie Sanders and Donald Trump. It approaches the subject from a clearly defined socialist perspective, analyzing how each candidate’s campaign and policies relate to the interests and struggles of the working class in the U.S.
The article uses emotionally loaded language such as: “The most striking element of his campaign so far is his insistence that his candidature is part of a movement.” This quote reflects a positive view of Sanders’s campaign, emphasizing its nature as a movement rather than just a political campaign. The sources referenced, such as
[Pew Research Center](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pew-research/), [NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/), and [The Atlantic](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-atlantic/), are generally considered credible and reliable for factual information. References to speeches by public figures like Bernie Sanders and analyses from known publications add to the credibility of the information presented.
The article’s sources are generally factual, but its editorial perspective may influence source selection. It includes articles from socialist publications like
[“Against the Current”](https://againstthecurrent.org/atc228/door-opens-to-return-of-jim-crow/), evident in pieces like “ [Trump and the Middle East,](https://www.marxists.org/history/etol/newspape/atc/4968.html)” indicating a left-leaning bias. In general, MIA predominantly republishes content from socialist or left-wing sources, such as the [International Socialism Journal](https://isj.org.uk/about/), associated with the Socialist Workers Party. This focus may limit the presentation of diverse viewpoints. For a more balanced approach, sourcing content from various political and ideological perspectives would be beneficial.
