History
Founded in 1865, The San Francisco Chronicle is a California newspaper serving the San Francisco Bay area and surrounding suburbs. The newspaper has won six Pulitzer Prizes. The current editor is
[Audrey Cooper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audrey_Cooper).
Funded by / Ownership
The San Francisco Chronicle is owned by
[Hearst Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearst_Communications), which owns more than 300 media outlets across the United States, including [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Cosmopolitan](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cosmopolitan/). Subscriptions and display ads generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The San Francisco Chronicle covers local news through staff journalists with minimal bias such as this:
[Faced with eviction, disabled Vacaville couple get help with move to new housing](https://www.sfchronicle.com/bayarea/article/Faced-with-eviction-disabled-Vacaville-couple-14906137.php). National and international news is delivered through wire services such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). Editorially, The San Francisco Chronicle has [endorsed](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Democratic presidential candidates since 1996.
