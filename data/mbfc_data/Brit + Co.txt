History
[Brit + Co](https://www.brit.co/about/) is a lifestyle and entertainment website launched in 2011. It was founded by Brit Morin, who also serves as CEO. The website is geared toward millennial women and covers topics in [popular culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_culture), love, friendship, careers, style, food, online classes, and gender issues. According to its contact page, the company’s headquarters is in [California](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/california-governor-gavin-newsom-political-bias-profile/). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Brit Morin](https://www.brit.co/u/brit_morin) owns Brit + Co through Brit Media, Inc. Revenue is derived through advertising, sponsored content, and online classes. According to [SFGate](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/sfgate/), Brit + Co had to [lay off](https://www.sfgate.com/business/article/Brit-Co-online-publication-women-layoffs-SF-13793209.php) most of its employees in 2019 due to financial difficulties.
Analysis / Bias
Brit + Co publishes news and information regarding various topics, including beauty, fashion, food, and home. It features online classes and categorizes news into sections like design, food, pop culture, and style. Brit + Co publishes articles with emotionally loaded headlines such as
[ “Lizzo Is #BodyGoals Because, Well, She Has A Body.”](https://www.brit.co/lizzo-body-positivity/) This story discusses body positivity. In another story, “ [7 Budget-Friendly Ways to Update Your Backyard And Outdoor Space,](https://www.brit.co/backyard-and-outdoor-space-upgrades/)” there are tips for home improvement and affiliate links to Amazon.
In addition to lifestyle and creativity-related topics, Brit &Co, based on the content and topics covered under the “Transgender” tag, exhibits a progressive bias as they provide a platform for diverse voices and perspectives, particularly focusing on issues relevant to the LGBTQ+ community, which aligns with progressive values of inclusivity and representation. These articles include personal stories, such as
[“Meet the Trans Military Vet Who’s Mentoring Trans Youth in America”](https://www.brit.co/giovanni-santiago-transgender-veteran-mentor/) and [“I’m a Trans Woman with Gender Dysmorphia, and This Is What it Feels Like](https://www.brit.co/transgender-gender-dysmorphia/),” as well as news and societal issues, like [“President Trump’s Transgender Military Ban Has Been Put on Hold”](https://www.brit.co/president-trumps-transgender-military-ban-has-been-put-on-hold/) and [“Anchorage, Alaska Voters 1st in US to Reject Anti-Trans Bathroom Bill.”](https://www.brit.co/anchorage-alaska-voters-1st-in-us-to-reject-anti-trans-bathroom-bill/)
When it comes to sourcing Brit + Co sources from civil rights organizations focusing on lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender communities, such as The Lambda Legal Defense and Education Fund,
[BBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/), [Associated Press (AP News)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and [Bustle Magazine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bustle-magazine/). In general, Brit+Co is mostly fact-based and holds a strong left-leaning bias; however, there is evidence of pseudoscience promotion such as this: [Here Are The Zodiac Signs That Cancer Is Most Compatible With](https://www.brit.co/cancer-compatibility/).
