History
[Founded in 1870](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asheville_Citizen-Times), The Asheville Citizen-Times is a major daily newspaper from Asheville, North Carolina. The domain for the online paper is citizen-times.com. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Asheville Citizen-Times is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett, becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Asheville Citizen-Times covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Asheville, NC area. Local news is reported with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Man shot by police after knife incident on city bus identified, in critical condition](https://www.citizen-times.com/story/news/local/2020/08/20/man-barricaded-bus-knife-passengers-evacuated/5616940002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Asheville Citizen-Times traditionally endorses
[Democratic candidates](https://www.citizen-times.com/story/opinion/editorials/2016/09/30/view-clinton-qualified-pick-th-us-president/91315090/). In general, the news is reported factually and with a slight left-leaning bias.
