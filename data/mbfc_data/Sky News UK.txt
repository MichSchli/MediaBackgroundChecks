History
[Founded in 1989](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sky_News) by Rupert Murdoch, Sky News is a British news organization, which operates a TV network of the same name, a radio news service, and distributes news through online channels. Sky News has sister outlets worldwide, such as Australia, Arabia, and Ireland. Sky News has won numerous awards, including in 2018, being named [Royal Television Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Television_Society) News Channel of the Year, the eleventh time the channel had [won the award](https://www.tvnewsroom.org/news-coverage/rts-television-journalism-awards-2018-results-187384/). Funded by / Ownership
Sky News is owned by
[Comcast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comcast) as of November 2018 and is funded through advertising. Rupert Murdoch is no longer affiliated with Sky News UK; however, Newscorp still has a share of [Sky News Australia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/sky-news-australia/), which reports with a right-leaning bias.
Analysis / Bias
This review covers UK website content only. Sky News reports on the
[UK](https://news.sky.com/uk), [World](https://news.sky.com/world), [Politics](https://news.sky.com/politics), [US](https://news.sky.com/us), [Ocean Rescue](https://news.sky.com/ocean-rescue), [Science & Tech](https://news.sky.com/technology), [Business](https://news.sky.com/business), [Ents & Arts](https://news.sky.com/entertainment), and [Offbeat](https://news.sky.com/strangenews). Sky News utilizes moderate to minimally loaded language in headlines and articles like this: [Climate change demos in London: Police face challenges dealing with protesters](https://news.sky.com/story/climate-change-demos-in-london-police-face-challenges-dealing-with-protesters-11696691). In another article, there is the use of mild loaded language that covers both sides, [May and Corbyn highlight persecution of Christians and refugee crisis in Easter messages](https://news.sky.com/story/pm-highlights-persecution-of-christians-in-easter-message-11699669). Like most on Sky News, this article does not provide hyperlinked sourcing but instead relies on quotes and first-person accounts. A review of the [opinion page](https://news.sky.com/opinion) reflects mostly balanced coverage, with some clearly in favor of the [left](https://news.sky.com/story/horrendous-anti-lgbtq-laws-may-force-me-to-leave-brunei-11683960).
News reporting is generally balanced and low-biased, with op-eds having a slight lean left. Under Rupert Murdoch, Sky News was frequently accused of having a right-wing bias; however, the left-leaning
[New Statesman](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-statesman/) disagrees and concludes that [Sky News is impartial](https://www.newstatesman.com/blogs/mehdi-hasan/2010/05/sky-news-murdoch-labour). Further, a [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/) institute survey found that 54% of respondents [trust their news coverage](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2021/united-kingdom) and 19% do not, ranking them #5 in trust of the major UK news providers.
