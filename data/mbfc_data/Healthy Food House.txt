History
[Founded in 2012](http://whois.domaintools.com/healthyfoodhouse.com), Healthy Food House is a website that publishes news on a diet, lifestyle, and [alternative medicine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-alternative-medicine/). According to their [about page](https://www.healthyfoodhouse.com/about/), they state “We publish articles that provide the newest information in the field of medicine and nutrition on a daily basis. We tend to provide important educational content on various themes, including Natural Remedies, Diet and Weight loss, Health Benefits of Fruits and Vegetables, Healthy Recipes, Health Tips, and many more.”
Funded by / Ownership
Although Healthy Food House does not disclose ownership, a search indicates the site is owned by Aleksandar and Borce Velkovski, also known as the
[Healthy Brothers](https://www.wired.com/2017/02/veles-macedonia-fake-news/) in Veles, Macedonia. The website earns revenue through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Healthy Food House publishes alternative health news that is frequently classified as pseudoscience. For example, they often publish dangerous information related to
[miracle cancer cures](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-alternative-cancer-treatments/) and prevention such as these:
[10 Plants Native Americans Used to Cure Everything And Even Cancer](https://www.healthyfoodhouse.com/10-plants-native-americans-used-cure-everything-even-cancer/) [Cancer Patient Given 18 Months to Live is Cured by Cannabis Oil](https://www.healthyfoodhouse.com/cancer-patient-given-18-months-live-cured-cannabis-oil/) [This Oncologist Makes Ground-Breaking Discovery: How to Cure Cancer With Baking Soda](https://www.healthyfoodhouse.com/oncologist-makes-ground-breaking-discovery-cure-cancer-baking-soda/)
Healthy Food House also promotes
[anti-vaccination propaganda](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-anti-vaccination-anti-vax/), the [dangers of GMOs](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-gmo-dangers/), and publishes ridiculous information such as [placing a bar of soap under your sheets](https://www.healthyfoodhouse.com/put-bar-soap-sheets-night/) to prevent leg cramps.
