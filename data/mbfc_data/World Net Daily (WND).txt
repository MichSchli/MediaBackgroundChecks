History
Founded in 1997, World Net Daily or WND is a politically conservative news and opinion website. WND was founded by
[Joseph Farah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Farah), who also serves as editor-in-chief. WND also publishes books under the imprint WND Books. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WND is owned by WorldNetDaily.com, Inc. and is funded through online advertising, book publishing, and an online store that sells conservative-related merchandise.
Analysis / Bias
Based on reviews by all of our researchers, WND is a Questionable online news source that has a far-right bias and dabbles in right-wing conspiracies such as
[President Obama’s birth certificate](http://www.wnd.com/2009/07/103137/). They also use misleading clickbait headlines that do not always match the article’s content (See M. Allen’s review below). WND also has an abysmal track record with fact-checkers: [“California To Register Illegal Aliens To Vote – Automatically.”](https://www.politifact.com/california/statements/2018/jan/24/worldnetdaily/no-california-didnt-pass-law-allowing-undocumented/)– Pants on Fire [“A U.S. Government Accountability Office report says Planned Parenthood Federation of America cannot find some $1.3 billion given to it by the federal government from 2002 through 2008.”](https://www.politifact.com/truth-o-meter/statements/2011/mar/25/worldnetdaily/planned-parenthood-funding-did-gao-really-find-mil/)– Pants on fire [White House spokesman Robert Gibbs “lied” when he said President Obama’s birth certificate is posted on the Internet.](https://www.politifact.com/truth-o-meter/statements/2009/jul/28/worldnetdaily/birthers-claim-gibbs-lied-when-he-said-obamas-birt/)– False [Chobani CEO Hamdi Ulukaya vowed to “drown the United States in Muslims” and is “importing them to Idaho 300 at a time to work in his factory.”](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/chobani-ceo-importing-300-muslim-refugees-per-month/)– Mostly False [Elena Kagan Tied to Obama’s Birth Certificate](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/kaganroo-court/)– False [A town in New Jersey has forbidden residents from criticizing Muslims or Islam in keeping with sharia law.](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/did-a-new-jersey-town-forbid-residents-from-criticizing-islam/)– Mostly False [In early 2019, vandals targeted 12 churches in France over seven days.](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/12-churches-france/)– Mostly False [“1 million COVID-vaccine injuries now reported on CDC’s database,](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/factcheck/2022/01/05/fact-check-1-million-covid-19-vaccine-adverse-events-unverified/9103381002/)” – False [Arctic sea ice reached a 30-year high in May 2022](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/factcheck/2022/06/28/fact-check-arctic-sea-ice-losing-area-not-30-year-high/10002807002/)– False
