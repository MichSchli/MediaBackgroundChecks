History
Launched in 2016, Mississippi Today is a nonprofit news and analysis website covering the state of Mississippi. According to their
[about page](https://mississippitoday.org/about-us/), they are a “statehouse watchdog, Mississippi Today’s roots in Capitol coverage have grown to encompass a myriad of beats beyond politics and policy, including education, public health, justice, environment, equity, and, yes, sports. Mississippi Today is now one of the largest newsrooms in Mississippi, proving that free, nonpartisan news is the antidote to apathy and an essential key to government accountability.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Mississippi News and Information Corporation](https://mississippitoday.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/2019-990-Mississippi-Today.pdf) own and publish Mississippi Today. The nonprofit organization was founded in 2014 by the former chairman of [NBC News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/) and [MSNBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/msnbc/), [Andrew Lack](https://www.niemanlab.org/2016/06/mississippi-today-backed-by-an-nbc-exec-aims-to-be-the-texas-tribune-of-its-undercovered-state/). The organization is funded through donations and advertising. Notable donors [include](https://mississippitoday.org/who-funds-us/) the American Journalism Project, Comcast NBCUniversal Foundation, and John S. and James L. Knight Foundation. The website is fully transparent regarding ownership and funding.
Analysis / Bias
Mississippi Today publishes news and analysis on the Mississippi government, politics, criminal justice, sports, and education. The website also has dedicated coverage of Covid-19 and Mississippi’s controversial abortion law. Articles and headlines contain moderately emotional wording, such as this
[Nancy and Zach New plead guilty to federal charges in private school scheme](https://mississippitoday.org/2022/04/21/nancy-new-zach-new-federal-plea-agreement/). This article, like all reviewed, is fact-based and utilizes credible sources such as court documents and [Gallup](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/gallup/) polling.
Editorially, they appropriately label opinion content as “Analysis.” When analyzing politics and government Mississippi Today favors a liberal perspective with articles such as this
[Speaker Philip Gunn is holding $1.8 billion hostage — and could give Gov. Tate Reeves a big win](https://mississippitoday.org/2022/03/14/mississippi-philip-gunn-tate-reeves-spending-tax-cut/). They also support social issues such as [pro-choice on abortion](https://mississippitoday.org/2021/09/09/mississippi-pro-choice-activists-prepare-for-america-without-roe-v-wade/), concern for [climate change](https://mississippitoday.org/coast-fishing-industry/), and [racial equity](https://mississippitoday.org/2022/03/09/von-gordon-talks-critical-race-theory-in-mississippi/). In general, reporting is fact-based and holds a left-leaning political bias.
