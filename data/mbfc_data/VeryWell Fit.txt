History
VeryWell Fit is a medical and health website
[launched by About.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/parulguliani/2016/04/26/about-com-unveils-new-health-vertical-verywell/#19447dca7599) in 2017. The website provides factual, evidence-based information on fitness and nutrition. According to their [about page](https://www.verywellmind.com/about-us-5184564), “Our expert team is dedicated to empowering you with the facts behind the fads to help you focus on progress, not perfection. We provide verified, evidence-based answers to your most pressing fitness and nutrition questions.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
VeryWell Fit is owned and published by
[Dotdash Meredith](https://www.verywellhealth.com/about-us), formerly About.com. Dotdash Meredithowns the VeryWell network, which consists of the websites [Verywell Health](https://www.verywellhealth.com/), [Verywell Mind](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/verywell-mind-bias/), and [Verywell](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/verywell/). They also own the website [Investopedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/investopedia/). Revenue is derived through advertising and affiliate commissions for some of the products they recommend.
Analysis / Bias
In review, VeryWell Fit provides news and information about fitness and nutrition. They also publish news with minimal bias, such as this
[Exercise Reduces Risk of Type 2 Diabetes, Studies Show](https://www.verywellfit.com/exercise-reduces-risk-of-type-2-diabetes-studies-show-5219858). This article is properly sourced from a study in the journal Metabolites, and other credible journals.
Editorially, VeryWell Fit employs editors involved in fitness and nutrition or the related field and fact-checkers who ensure the articles are accurate and evidence-based. In general, information is factual and presented with limited bias.
