History
The Lake City Graphic-Advocate is a newspaper published in Lake City, Indiana, United States. The paper covers news, sports, lifestyle, and opinion for Calmar and the surrounding area. thegraphic-advocate.com serves as the domain for the online version of the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Mid America Publishing owns and publishes the Lake City Graphic-Advocate. Mid America Publishing publishes
[several community newspapers](https://www.midampublishing.com/content/mid-america-publications) throughout Indiana. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Lake City Graphic-Advocate reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Lake City, Indiana region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[Judge rules on Hinners evidence](http://www.thegraphic-advocate.com/content/judge-rules-hinners-evidence). The newspaper and website do not publish national news, focusing on local news reporting.
Editorially, the Lake City Graphic-Advocate reasonably represents both sides. A review of op-eds demonstrates a reasonably low bias; however, slightly more op-eds favor the right. While more op-eds favor the right, liberal perspectives have fair representation; therefore, they earn a least biased rating on the right side of the scale. In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
