History
The
[Syrian Observatory for Human Rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_Observatory_for_Human_Rights) (SOHR), founded in May 2006, is a UK-based information office documenting human rights abuses in Syria; it has focused since 2011 on the Syrian Civil War. It is frequently quoted by major Western news media, such as [Voice of America](http://mediabiasfactcheck.com/voice-of-america/), [Reuters](http://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), [BBC](http://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/), [CNN](http://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/), and [National Public Radio](http://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/), since the beginning of the uprising about daily numbers of deaths from all sides in the conflict and civilians killed in airstrikes in Syria. SOHR has been described as being “pro-opposition” and anti-Assad. [Read our profile on UK Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
SOHR is a nonprofit NGO that does not disclose funding.
Analysis / Bias
Our review shows minimal use of loaded words and a clean fact-check history. The source has a left-center bias in reporting, but for the most part, has been factual in an area where valid information is hard to find. This is a good source, but we recommend that you use this site in conjunction with the major outlets listed above.
