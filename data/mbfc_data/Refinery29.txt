History
[Founded](https://observer.com/2017/06/refinery-29-founders-piera-gelardi-christene-barberich/) in 2005 as a “localized [New York guide](https://www.businessoffashion.com/community/people/philippe-von-borries-justin-stefano) for independent boutiques and shops” by Justin Stefano, Philippe von Borries, Piera Gelardi, and Christene Barberich, [Refinery29](https://corporate.r29.com/about#about-intro) (R29) today is a Millenial female-focused lifestyle entertainment website that is based in New York. They also publish a UK and German edition. Refinery29 [defines](https://corporate.r29.com/about#about-intro) its aim to “inspire, entertain, and empower our audience through optimistic and diverse storytelling.” Refinery29 [covers](https://company.vice.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/VICE-R29-Media-Alert.pdf) a wide variety of topics from fashion, beauty, money to lifestyle, film and TV projects, art, and fashion [events](https://www.forbes.com/sites/stevebaltin/2018/12/07/behind-refinery29s-impressive-and-thought-provoking-29rooms-installation/#8dc166538bf0) (29Rooms). Vice Media Group acquired Refinery29 in 2019. Nancy Dubuc is the chief executive of Vice. It is not clear who is the director or editor of Refinery29. Funded by / Ownership
Refinery29 is owned by the
[Vice Media Group](https://company.vice.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/VICE-R29-Media-Alert.pdf), which publishes [Vice News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vice-news/) and several other websites. Revenue is derived through advertising and an online shop that sells merchandise.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Refinery29 publishes articles with emotionally loaded language such as
[“New Bodycam Video Shows Georgia Police Attempting To Tase Ahmaud Arbery In 2017”](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/05/9825422/ahmaud-arbery-bodycam-video-police-taser-car-search) and [“What It’s Really Like To Be Undocumented & In College”](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2016/10/122357/undocumented-student-daca-harvard-experience) Further, Refinery29 typically utilizes credible sources such as [American Psychological Association (APA)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-psychological-association-apa/), [USAToday](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), [Migration Policy Institute](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/migration-policy-institute/), uscis.gov. [Wall Street Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wall-street-journal/), and [AP News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). Refinery29 also publishes essays such as [“Why Should Single Women “Settle Down”?”](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/05/9796841/settling-down-meaning-single-women-problem) and [“Reddit’s Dirtiest Pick-Up Lines Will Make You Blush.”](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/dirty-pick-up-lines)
Editorially, Refinery29 holds left-leaning social positions such as advocacy for
[feminism](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/03/9527956/famous-quotes-international-womens-day), [LGBTQ Rights](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/youtube-fundamental-lgbtq-rights-georgia), [abortion rights](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/04/9618456/abortion-clinic-toledo-ohio-covid-19), and concern for [human-influenced climate change](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2019/09/8467683/how-to-help-climate-change-greta-thunberg). Further, when reporting on conservatives, they often hold negative views such as this [Here’s The Latest COVID-19 Conspiracy Theory, Courtesy Of Eric Trump.](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/05/9824419/eric-trump-coronavirus-conspiracy-jeanine-pirro-fox) In general, the news is reported mostly factually with a strong left-leaning bias.
