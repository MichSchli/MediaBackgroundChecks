History
Founded in 1818, the Tuscaloosa News serves Tuscaloosa, Alabama, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. Tuscaloosanews.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Tuscaloosa News. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Tuscaloosa News covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Tuscaloosa, Alabama area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Memorial dedicated at Alabama girls home honors 8 crash victims](https://www.tuscaloosanews.com/story/news/2021/12/15/memorial-tallapoosa-girls-home-dedicated-interstate-65-crash-victims/8911296002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Tuscaloosa News does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented such as this that favors the left:
[AT LARGE: Democrats face tough, but not impossible, task in midterm elections](https://www.tuscaloosanews.com/story/opinion/2021/12/11/large-democrats-face-tough-but-not-impossible-task-2022/6448878001/). And this favoring the right [Bob Dole one of the great political leaders of the last century](https://www.tuscaloosanews.com/story/opinion/2021/12/13/bob-dole-one-great-political-leaders-last-century/6494597001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
