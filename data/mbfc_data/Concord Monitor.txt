History
[Founded in 1864](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concord_Monitor), The Concord Monitor is the daily newspaper for [Concord](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concord,_New_Hampshire), the state capital of New Hampshire. It also covers surrounding towns in Merrimack, most of Belknap county, and portions of Grafton, Rockingham, and Hillsborough counties. The Monitor has been named one of the best small papers in America several times. The current editor is Steve Leone. Funded by / Ownership
The owner of the Concord Monitor is the
[Newspapers of New England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspapers_of_New_England), which owns and publishes nine newspapers in Massachusetts and New Hampshire. The President and CEO of Newspapers of New England are [Aaron Julien](https://www.linkedin.com/in/aaron-julien-58005917). The newspaper is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, local news is covered by journalists and national news through syndication of the
[Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) There is minimal use of loaded words in headlines, and all information is appropriately sourced. Editorially, there is a left-leaning bias as the paper primarily endorses Democratic candidates, and the opinion pages slightly favor the left in story selection.
