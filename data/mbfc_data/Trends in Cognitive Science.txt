History
Trends in Cognitive Science is a peer-reviewed open-access journal published by Cell Press that covers Cognitive Science. According to its
[about page](https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/aims), “Essential reading for those working directly in the cognitive sciences or in related specialist areas, Trends in Cognitive Sciences provides an instant overview of current thinking for scientists, students and teachers who want to keep up with the latest developments in the cognitive sciences.”
Cell Press, based in Cambridge, Massachusetts, publishes over
[50 scientific journals](https://www.cell.com/). Cell Press Journals are 50% open-access as of 2023. [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Trends in Cognitive Science is published by Cell Press, which is a subsidiary of
[Elsevier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elsevier), a Dutch information and analytics company and one of the world’s foremost providers of scientific, technical, and medical information. It was established in 1880 as a publishing company. Elsevier’s parent company is RELX plc, a British multinational information and analytics company headquartered in London, England. The journal generates revenue through subscription fees and fees for open-access publishing.
Analysis / Bias
Trends in Cognitive Science is a hybrid (some open-access) journal that publishes information on Cognitive Science. The journal primarily publishes research such as this:
[Consciousness in the cradle: on the emergence of infant experience](https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(23)00214-0).
Cell Press is an open-access/hybrid publisher of scientific journals. There are two types of Open Access Journals, legitimate and predatory, both of which charge the author for publication.
[Predatory journals](https://predatoryjournals.com/about/) often lack peer review and are generally considered not credible. The Trends in Cognitive Science is considered a [legitimate, open-access publisher](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5017830/) that is frequently indexed in [PubMed](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pubmed/). However, Cell Press has been criticized for [retracting some studies.](https://retractionwatch.com/category/by-journal/cell/)
Although Cell Press has retracted some studies in other publications, Trends in Cognitive Science publishes credible scientific studies that are factual and appropriately peer-reviewed.
