History
Founded in 1925 (originally named The Sarasota Herald), the
[Herald-Tribune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarasota_Herald-Tribune) is a regional daily newspaper based in Sarasota, Florida. The Herald-Tribune delivers news from politics and business to sports, lifestyle, and entertainment.
[Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Sarasota Herald-Tribune. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Herald-Tribune reports local news with minimally loaded words and bias using credible media sources through quotes such as
[“More affordable housing coming to Sarasota’s Rosemary District”](http://www.heraldtribune.com/news/20180601/more-affordable-housing-coming-to-sarasotas-rosemary-district?start=1) and [“Florida Education Association grades legislators.”](http://www.heraldtribune.com/news/20180601/florida-education-association-grades-legislators) Further, their editorials are balanced, such as: “ [Editorial: School security is worth special session.”](http://www.heraldtribune.com/opinion/20180524/editorial-school-security-is-worth-special-session) For National News, the Herald-Tribune republishes the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) Generally, all articles are well sourced from credible media through quotes and are least biased in their wording.
