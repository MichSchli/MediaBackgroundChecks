History
[Founded in 1895](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Record_(Stockton,_California)), The Record is a daily newspaper based in Stockton, California, serving San Joaquin and Calaveras Counties. The current editor is Don Blount. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Record is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Record of Stockton covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in reporting such as this,
[Veterinary project on a mission to help pets in need](https://www.recordnet.com/news/20200704/veterinary-project-on-mission-to-help-pets-in-need). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In general, news reporting is low biased and factual.
Editorially, they endorsed Democratic Presidential Nominee Hillary Clinton in 2016:
[Record Endorsements, President: Clinton best for country](https://www.recordnet.com/opinion/20161008/record-endorsements-president-clinton-best-for-country). However, a review of op-eds shows a balance between left and right opinions. In general, they report news factually and with minimal editorial bias.
