History
KMXT – NPR – Kodiak is an NPR FM radio station (100.1) based in Kodiak, Alaska, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KMXT – NPR – Kodiak is owned and broadcast by Kodiak Public Broadcasting Corp. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes moderately loaded language, such as this [Kodiak’s state legislators update on fishing, ferries, and the PFD](https://kmxt.org/2023/03/kodiaks-state-legislators-update-on-fishing-ferries-and-the-pfd/). In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
