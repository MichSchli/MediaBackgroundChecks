History
The Selma Times-Journal is a newspaper published in Selma, Alabama. selmatimesjournal.com covers local, national, crime, and sports news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Selma Times-Journal is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Selma Times-Journal primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this Selma man killed Monday morning. The
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Selma Times-Journal labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Luverne Journal is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
