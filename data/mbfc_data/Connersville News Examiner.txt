History
The Connersville News Examiner is a daily newspaper published in Connersville, Indiana. newsexaminer.com delivers local news, hometown politics, business news, local events, and sports.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by
[Paxton Media Group,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paxton_Media_Group) which owns several right-leaning newspapers across the USA, such as the [Elkhart Truth](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-elkhart-truth/). Revenue is derived from advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Connersville News Examiner reports national news by republishing articles from credible newsagents such as the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). They report local news through the use of journalists with minimal bias using neutral headlines such as [Indiana receives federal approval to continue extending postpartum Medicaid coverage to one year](https://www.newsexaminer.com/news/indiana-receives-federal-approval-to-continue-extending-postpartum-medicaid-coverage-to-one-year/article_e39401c9-f308-5209-9f28-df75897fb3f1.html). All information is properly sourced.
Editorially, there is a reasonable balance between left and right-leaning opinions, with slightly more favoring the right such as this:
[Drawing the line between censorship and age-appropriateness](https://www.newsexaminer.com/opinion/drawing-the-line-between-censorship-and-age-appropriateness/article_3a091197-97db-5125-b3f9-87259abc01b5.html). In general, the paper is fact-based and holds a slight right-leaning editorial bias.
