History
[Founded in 1988](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NDTV), New Delhi Television Limited (NDTV) is an Indian Television Media Company headquartered in New Delhi, India.
New Delhi Television Limited (NDTV) presents world affairs from an Indian perspective, providing National News critical of Right-wing populist current PM Narendra Modi. According to an article published by the
[NY Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/05/world/asia/india-ndtv-raids-narendra-modi-prannoy-roy.html), NDTV was raided by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI), searching the residences of the NDTV’s co-founders Dr. Prannoy Roy and Mr. Nariman.

Analysis / Bias
According to
[Reporters without Borders](https://rsf.org/en/india), the media environment in India is threatened by increasing nationalism. India ranks 142nd out of 180 countries on the World Press Freedom Index.
In review, New Delhi Television Limited (NDTV) republishes stories from
[The Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [AFP](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/afp-agence-france-presse/), and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/). For national news, they republish stories from the [Press Trust of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Press_Trust_of_India), which is the largest news agency in India, and the [Indo Asian News Service.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Asian_News_Service)
New Delhi Television Limited (NDTV) utilizes minimally loaded headlines such as
[“Editorial Freedom Must Be Used Wisely, PM Modi Says In Message To Media](https://www.ndtv.com/india-news/editorial-freedom-must-be-used-wisely-pm-narendra-modi-says-in-message-to-media-1771707),” however, their articles are sourced through quotes and rarely linked to those sources; instead, they note “With agency inputs” at the end of the article. Editorially, they often report [somewhat negatively](https://www.ndtv.com/india-news/pakistan-denies-use-of-airspace-to-pm-narendra-modi-again-report-2123366) on right-leaning Prime Minister Narendra Modi.
