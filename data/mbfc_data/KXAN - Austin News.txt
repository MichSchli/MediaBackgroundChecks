History
Founded in 1965, KXAN – Austin News is a NBC-affiliated television station licensed to Austin, Texas. The associated website is kxan.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KXAN – Austin News is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The KXAN – Austin News website publishes news for the greater Austin, Texas area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[Man killed after daylong SWAT situation, Bastrop Halloween display criticized](https://www.kxan.com/news/local/weekend-read-man-killed-after-daylong-swat-situation-bastrop-halloween-display-criticized/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[U.S. Rep. Joaquin Castro proposes bill to prevent natural gas price gouging during emergencies](https://www.kxan.com/news/political-news/u-s-rep-joaquin-castro-proposes-bill-to-prevent-natural-gas-price-gouging-during-emergencies/). Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoiding using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
