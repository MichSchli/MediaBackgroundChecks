History
[ABC](https://about.abc.net.au/abc-history/#2010s) launched in 1932. ABC News is the news division of The Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC), established on 22 July 2010.
They are headquartered in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. Justin Milne, former ABC chairman,
[resigned](https://www.theguardian.com/media/2018/sep/27/justin-milne-resigns-and-denies-government-interference-in-abc) after allegations that he compromised the independence of ABC by asking managing director Michelle Guthrie to fire the chief economics correspondent, Emma Alberici, and the political editor, Andrew Probyn, due to the government not liking them.
“They [the government] hate her,” Milne wrote in a leaked email about Alberici. “Get rid of her.”
According to a
[Guardian article](https://www.theguardian.com/media/2018/sep/27/justin-milne-resigns-and-denies-government-interference-in-abc), Milne is also a close friend of former Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull (Liberal Party/Centre-Right). [Dr. Kirstin Ferguson](https://about.abc.net.au/profile/dr-kirstin-ferguson/) is the Acting Chair. The Board appoints the Managing Director, and David Anderson is the acting Managing Director. According to their about page, “Up to seven Directors are appointed by the Governor-General on the recommendation of the Government.” [Here](https://about.abc.net.au/who-we-are/the-abc-board/) is the complete list of the Board members and the appointment process.

Funded by / Ownership
ABC is funded mainly by the Australian government, and in 2018, funding for ABC was
[cut by $84m](https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2018/may/08/abc-funding-slashed-by-84m-in-budget-to-help-broadcaster-live-within-their-means). The website does not feature advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, ABC News uses emotionally loaded headlines about US Politics, such as this
[“Donald Trump kicked off his re-election campaign by making socialism into 2020’s dirty word.”](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2019-02-08/donald-trump-wants-socialism-to-be-2020s-dirty-word/10793734) ABC News has a section underworld news dedicated to US news titled “Trump’s [ America](https://www.abc.net.au/news/donald-trumps-america/),” with most stories being Anti-Trump. When it comes to national politics, ABC News publishes articles with loaded headlines and would criticize the former center-right government [“Scott Morrison said all the right things after Christchurch attack, but his history tells another story.”](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2019-03-23/scott-morrison-end-of-tribalism-christchurch/10930682) Further, ABC News sources its information from credible sources such as judiciary.house.gov, [NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/), [Gallup](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/gallup/), [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Sky](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/sky-news/) [News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/sky-news/), [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Finally, a
[Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/) Institute survey found that 70% of respondents [trust their news coverage](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2021/australia) and 12% do not, ranking them #1 in trust of the major Australian news providers.
