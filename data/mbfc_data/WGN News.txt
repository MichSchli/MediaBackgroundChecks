History
[Founded in 1948](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WGN-TV), WGN-TV is an independent television station licensed to Chicago, Illinois. The associated news website wgntv.com covers local news for Chicago and the surrounding area. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WGN-TV is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns over 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). Revenue is generated through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the wgntv.com website publishes news for the greater Chicago, Illinois area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias, such as this:
[United Center begins COVID-19 vaccine distribution](https://wgntv.com/news/coronavirus/united-center-to-begin-administering-covid-19-vaccines-tuesday/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Stations. The national news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire and other large media outlets. Lastly, the WGN-TV website does not publish op-eds. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
