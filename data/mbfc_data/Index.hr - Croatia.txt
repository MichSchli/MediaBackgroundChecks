History
Launched in 2002,
[Index.hr](https://cjenik.index.hr/) is an online tabloid-style news portal based in Zagreb, Croatia. Index.hr publishes news and commentaries, politics, entertainment, gossip, sports, and business news. [Read our profile on Croatian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/croatia-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Index.hr is owned by journalist and entrepreneur
[Matija Babić](https://www.timeout.com/croatia/things-to-do/matija-babic-interview-the-world-on-a-plate). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
Reporters Without Borders (RSF) ranked Serbia 56th out of 180 countries in its World Press Freedom Index. In their review of Croatia,
[Reporters Without Borders](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reporters-without-borders/) points out, “Croatian journalists who investigate corruption, organized crime or war crimes are often subjected to harassment campaigns.”
Index.hr journalists currently face 65 active lawsuits. In
[Croatia,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/croatia-media-profile/) defamation is a criminal offense used by politicians to intimidate and harass journalists. For instance, Branimir Glavaš filed defamation lawsuits after Index. hr referred to him as a “war criminal.” [Glavaš](https://ipi.media/croatia-wave-of-abusive-legal-actions-against-index-hr/) was a general during Croatia and Serbia war and was convicted and served five years in prison. According to a [report](https://www.digitalnewsreport.org/survey/2020/croatia-2020/) published by [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), “In regard to media brands, the two online portals identified with the right-wing (Dnevno. hr) and left/liberal (Index.hr) have the highest levels of distrust.”
In review, Index.hr publishes articles with loaded headlines such as
[“Lavrov: This situation will strengthen relations between Russia and China”](https://www.index.hr/vijesti/clanak/lavrov-ova-situacija-ojacat-ce-odnose-rusije-i-kine/2348744.aspx?index_ref=naslovnica_vijesti_teme_i_komentari_d) and [“Military analyst: Russia’s losses are incredible, they gave up on Kyiv](https://www.index.hr/vijesti/clanak/vojni-analiticar-rat-nece-biti-tako-brzo-gotov-samo-vojska-moze-maknuti-putina/2348713.aspx?index_ref=rubrika_vijesti_najcitanije_d).” Index.hr reports world news in a neutral tone such as [“These are the conditions Putin wants to discuss the end of the war at all.”](https://www.index.hr/vijesti/clanak/ovo-su-uvjeti-koje-putin-zeli-kako-bi-uopce-razgovarao-o-kraju-rata/2348765.aspx) On the issues such as immigration, index.hr publishes articles with a neutral tone [“Yes, Europe is less and less white. No, it’s not because of a big conspiracy.” ](https://www.index.hr/vijesti/clanak/da-europa-je-sve-manje-bijela-ne-to-nije-zbog-velike-urote/2112426.aspx)
Index.hr sources through quotations and utilizes local sources such N1 television. They often use Hina (official Croatia news agency) and also use sources such as
[FAZ.net.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/frankfurter-allgemeine-zeitung-faz/) Further, they also conduct interviews with high-profile guests and politicians. In general, Index.hr publishes factual news from a moderately liberal perspective.
