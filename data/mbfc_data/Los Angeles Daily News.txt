History
Founded in 1911, the
[Los Angeles Daily News](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Daily_News) (LA Daily News) is a broadsheet and the second-largest-circulating paid daily newspaper of Los Angeles, California. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Los Angeles Daily News is owned by
[Digital First Media,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_First_Media) which is a management company that specializes in newspapers. Digital First Media runs numerous newspapers within the USA that range from Right-Center to Left-Center depending on the market. The newspaper is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Los Angeles Daily News reports local news through the use of field journalists and national/world news through syndication of the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). Headlines utilize minimally loaded language, and information is properly sourced. Editorially, there is a mix of left and right-wing opinions and the paper has endorsed both Republican and Democratic candidates; however, there is [slight favoritism](https://www.dailynews.com/tag/endorsements/) of right-leaning candidates.
