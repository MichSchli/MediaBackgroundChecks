History
[Founded in 1831](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detroit_Free_Press), The Detroit Free Press is the largest daily newspaper in Detroit, Michigan, USA. The Free Press has received ten Pulitzer Prizes and four Emmy Awards. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
In review, the Detroit Free Press reports local news about the greater Detroit area with minimal bias, such as this
[Grosse Ile supervisor: We had ‘almost no notice’ on parkway bridge closure](https://www.freep.com/story/news/local/michigan/wayne/2019/11/15/grosse-ile-bridge-closure-toll-bridge/4202474002/). National and international news is derived through USA Today.
Editorially, the Detroit Free Press has
[endorsed](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Democratic Presidential candidates since at least 1980. Op-Ed’s also currently favor the left.
