History
[PressReader](https://www.torontopubliclibrary.ca/pressreader-help/) is a digital aggregator, [an “all-you-can-read platform of newspapers and magazines,”](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/pressreader-and-the-economist-deepen-partnership-301692105.html) that offers online access to newspapers and magazines from various countries and languages. The website was launched in 1999 [as Newspaper Direct](https://about.pressreader.com/about-us/) and was later rebranded as PressReader in 2014. PressReader converts newspapers and magazines into digital formats and offers access to various publications, including [The Economist](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-economist/), [Bloomberg](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bloomberg/), [Foreign Affairs](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/foreign-affairs/), [Los Angeles Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/), and [Forbes](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/forbes/). PressReader [headquarters](https://techcrunch.com/2019/08/20/pressreader-acquires-news360/) is in Vancouver, Canada. [Read our profile on Canadian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Alexander Kroogman](https://about.pressreader.com/pressreleases/2022/10/pressreader-announces-ceo-transition/) is the co-founder and Executive Director, and Ruairí Doyle is the CEO. The website does not disclose ownership. PressReader generates revenue from advertising and [subscription fees.](https://www.pressreader.com/subscribe) The website offers various subscription plans to users who wish to access premium content.
Analysis / Bias
[PressReader](https://about.pressreader.com/about-us/) is a digital newsstand that offers users access to a [wide range](https://about.pressreader.com/libraries-institutions/) of newspapers and magazines worldwide. PressReader collects and delivers full issues of premium newspapers and magazines from various sources, including [The Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [The Guardian](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-guardian/), [The Globe and Mail](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-globe-and-mail/), [El Pais](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/el-pais/), [Le Figaro](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/le-figaro-bias/), [Newsweek](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/newsweek/), [Vogue](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/teen-vogue/), Bloomberg Businessweek, and [People](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/people-magazine/).
There is no evidence of bias in its reporting or editorial content, as it is an aggregator of news and information rather than a content creator. The website does not present its own editorial opinions or take a stance on political issues. In general, PressReader relies on mostly credible sources; however, some of the sources they use have failed fact checks, such as the Guardian.
