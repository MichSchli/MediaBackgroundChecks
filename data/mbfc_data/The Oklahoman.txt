History
Founded in 1889, The Oklahoman is the largest daily newspaper in Oklahoma and is the only regional daily that covers the Greater Oklahoma City area.
[Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Oklahoman is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Oklahoman covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in reporting such as this; Horn
[ opposes her party’s $3 trillion coronavirus relief package](https://oklahoman.com/article/5662345/horn-opposes-her-partys-3-trillion-coronavirus-relief-package). National and international news comes from other Gatehouse/Gannett publications and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual.
Editorially, the Oklahoman has long been known as a conservative paper, and although in 2016 they did not endorse a Presidential candidate, they did make
[this statement](https://oklahoman.com/article/5525599/recapping-our-endorsements-in-the-2016-election): “it’s crucial voters elect Republican majorities in Congress to counteract the bad policies likely to come from our next president.” A thorough review of editorials and opinion pieces reveal a mostly balanced left-right selection with a few more opinion pieces favoring the right. In general, news reporting is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
