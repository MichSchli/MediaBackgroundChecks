History
Founded in 1980, WGBA – Green Bay News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated station licensed to Green Bay, Wisconsin, United States. nbc26.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WGBA – Green Bay News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://scripps.com/our-brands/local-media/), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
WGBA – Green Bay News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Green Bay, Wisconsin, area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[Oshkosh Area School District without buses Thursday due to catalytic converter thefts](https://www.nbc26.com/news/local-news/oshkosh-area-school-district-without-buses-thursday-due-to-catalytic-converter-thefts). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden promotes electric vehicles, announces EV charging network](https://www.nbc26.com/news/national/biden-to-promote-electric-vehicles-charging-network-in-visit-to-detroit). In general, they present low-biased, fact-based reporting.
