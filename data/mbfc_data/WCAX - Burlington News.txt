History
[Founded in 1954](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WCAX-TV), it is a CBS-affiliated television station licensed to Burlington, Vermont, United States, serving Northern Vermont’s Champlain Valley and Upstate New York’s North Country, including Plattsburgh. WCAX.com is the news website for the TV Station that covers local news, sports, crime, weather, and lifestyle. [Read our profile on USA media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WCAX.com is owned by
[Gray Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Television), which operates 145 stations across the USA. Revenue is derived through sponsored content and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WCAX.com provides news coverage for the Plattsburgh market and the surrounding areas in northern Vermont. Local news is covered by reporters with minimal bias and often has video provided by the TV station:
[Burlington school leaders call on state to fund PCB contamination cleanup](https://www.wcax.com/2020/09/18/burlington-school-leaders-call-on-state-to-fund-pcb-contamination-cleanup/). National news is provided through other Gray Television affiliates and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). International news generally comes from [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/) and the Associated Press.
Editorially, WCAX3 does not offer an opinion page and, when covering politics, utilizes minimally loaded words such as this
[Trump to Senate: Vote ‘without delay’ on his high court pick](https://www.wcax.com/2020/09/19/mcconnell-pledges-to-bring-trumps-scotus-pick-up-for-a-vote/). In general, WCAX3 reports news factually and with minimal bias.
