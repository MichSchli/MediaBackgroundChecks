History
WHYY – NPR – Philadelphia is an NPR FM radio station (90.9 FM) based in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WHYY – NPR – Philadelphia is owned and broadcast by WHYY, Inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WHYY – NPR – Philadelphia website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Delco officials are conducting the county’s first community health assessment](https://whyy.org/articles/delaware-county-pa-health-department-community-assessment/). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[Too many LGBT youth depend on abusive relationships for a home](https://whyy.org/articles/too-many-lgbt-youth-depend-on-abusive-relationships-for-a-home/). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
