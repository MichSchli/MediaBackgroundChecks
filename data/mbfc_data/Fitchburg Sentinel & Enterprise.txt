History
The Fitchburg Sentinel & Enterprise is a newspaper published in Fitchburg, Massachusetts, United States. sentinelandenterprise.com is the website for the paper covering local news, sports, crime, lifestyle, and opinion.

Funded by / Ownership
The Fitchburg Sentinel & Enterprise is owned by
[MediaNews Group](https://www.medianewsgroup.com/our-brands/) and published by Digital First Media, which publishes 190 newspapers across the USA. Digital First Media is owned by [Alden Global Capital LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alden_Global_Capital), a New York-Based Hedge Fund described as “ [Vulture Capitalists](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/07/business/media/denver-post-opinion-owner.html)” by former [Denver Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/denver-post/) writers for the cuts made to newsrooms. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Fitchburg Sentinel & Enterprise publishes local and regional news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Andrew Shepherd to challenge 1st Middlesex District state rep. race result in court.](https://www.sentinelandenterprise.com/2022/12/29/andrew-shepherd-to-challenge-1st-middlesex-district-state-rep-race-result-in-court/) National and international news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/).
Editorially, the Fitchburg Sentinel & Enterprise publishes an appropriate mix of right and left-leaning opinions. In general, they report factually and with credible sources, while op-eds are mostly balanced with differing viewpoints.
