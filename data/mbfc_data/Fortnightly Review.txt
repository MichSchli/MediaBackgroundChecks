History
[The Fortnightly Review](https://fortnightlyreview.co.uk/about/) was one of the most prominent and influential magazines in nineteenth-century England. The magazine mainly covered topics on Politics, Literature, Philosophy, Science, and Art. The Fortnightly Review is known to publish many articles, stories, and essays of famous literary figures such as Oscar Wilde, George Orwell, James Joyce, W. B. Yeats, and Ezra Pound. The magazine also published several articles on science and astronomy. The print magazine ceased publication in 1954. In 2009, a group of [British and American scholars ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fortnightly_Review)and writers began publishing a “new series” online at The Fortnightly Review.
