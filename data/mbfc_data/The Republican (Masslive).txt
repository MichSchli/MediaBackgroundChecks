History
[Founded in 1824](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Republican_(Springfield,_Massachusetts)), The Republican (Masslive.com) is a daily broadsheet newspaper located in Springfield, Massachusetts. The paper covers local, national, business, sport,s and weather for the Springfield area. Analysis / Bias
The Republican (Masslive.com) reports local news through journalists with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Robert D. Smith of Pittsfield sentenced to prison in hit-and-run that killed bicyclist](https://www.masslive.com/news/2019/06/robert-d-smith-of-pittsfield-sentenced-to-prison-in-hit-and-run-that-killed-bicyclist.html). The paper also covers news from all parts of the state and region, with news being broken into city categories such as Boston, Springfield, Cape Code, Etc.
National and international news comes from other sources, such as the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The Republican has endorsed both Republican and Democratic Presidential candidates. In 2008, they endorsed Republican John McCain; in 2012, Democrat Barack Obama and in 2016,
[Democrat Hillary Clinton](https://www.masslive.com/opinion/2016/10/the_republican_endorses_hillar.html). A review of editorials and op-eds shows a reasonable balance between left and right-leaning opinions, with perhaps a slight lean left. The Republican reports straight news factually and editorially is reasonably balanced.
