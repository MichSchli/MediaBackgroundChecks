History
[First Draft News](https://firstdraftnews.org/about/) was launched by the First Draft Coalition in 2015. As one of the founding organizations, Google News Lab also assisted in developing and maintaining [firstdraftnews.org](https://firstdraftnews.org/), supported the creation of new content, and coordinated the [community of practice](https://firstdraftnews.org/community-of-practice/). First Draft News is a nonprofit organization whose goal is to help newsrooms, scholars, fact-checkers, and journalism schools. According to their about page, their goal is “to raise awareness, perform research, and address challenges relating to trust and truth in media in the digital age.” [Claire Wardle](https://learno.net/instructors/claire-wardle) is the Executive Director of First Draft. In October 2017, First Draft moved to the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University. It continues its work as a project of the [Shorenstein Center on Media, Politics, and Public Policy](https://shorensteincenter.org/).
Funded by / Ownership
In 2019 First Draft became an independent 501(c)(3) non-profit. Donations generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
[First Draft News](https://firstdraftnews.org/about/) features training resources and a [catalog](https://firstdraftnews.org/curriculum-resources/) for journalist [industry news.](https://firstdraftnews.org/verification-workstation/) It is designed both as a best practice guide and analysis focused on specific news such as elections worldwide. For example, the [UK Election](https://firstdraftnews.org/uk-real-time-factchecking/) and [German Election](https://firstdraftnews.org/wahlcheck17-correctiv/). They typically publish very well-sourced articles, and they utilize credible sources such as [Columbia Journalism Review](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/columbia-journalism-review/), [shorensteincenter.org](https://shorensteincenter.org/first-draft-joins-shorenstein-center/), [NPR,](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and the [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/). In their headlines, they typically use neutral language such as [“Why we need to understand misinformation through visuals.”](https://firstdraftnews.org/understanding-visual-misinfo/) Further, First Draft News has a partnership with credible UK fact-checkers, [Full Fact](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/full-fact-uk/).
