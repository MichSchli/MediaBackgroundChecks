History
[Thetruth.com](https://www.thetruth.com/terms-of-service) is a website for the Truth Initiative, founded in 1999 as a result of the Tobacco Master Settlement Agreement (MSA). The Truth Initiative’s signature program is its Truth campaign, a youth smoking prevention public education program credited with reducing teen smoking. [Leidos](https://investors.leidos.com/news-and-events/news-releases/press-release-details/2020/Leidos-Wins-2020-Halo-Social-Impact-Award/default.aspx) (Fortune 500 information technology) supports the Truth Initiative opioid education and prevention campaign. Robin Koval is the CEO and President, and Mike Moore is the chair of the Truth Initiative. For a complete list of the board of directors and staff, please see [here.](https://truthinitiative.org/who-we-are/our-team) [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
The website publishes articles with emotionally loaded headlines, such as
[“Spotlight: Lincoln Mondy”](https://www.thetruth.com/video/read-between-the-lies-lincoln-mondy-discusses-black-lives-black-lungs) and [“VAPING + TOBACCO IS A SOCIAL JUSTICE ISSUE.”](https://www.thetruth.com/take-action/campaign/socialjustice) In the article, Thetruth.com also highlights Big Tobacco’s history of systemic discrimination, such as funding a summer program that gave them access to Black children. When sourcing Thetruth.com, utilize credible sources, such as industrydocuments.ucsf.edu, [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2018/12/20/678915071/altria-buys-35-percent-stake-in-e-cigarette-maker-juul), [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/).
Editorially, Thetruth.com takes action against the tobacco industry by initiating campaigns such as the “Mental Health Campaign,” “Social Justice Campaign,” and the “Environment Campaign.” In general, TheTruth.com is fact-based and holds left-leaning editorial positions.
