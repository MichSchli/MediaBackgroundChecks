History
Founded in August 2022,
[The Los Angeles Innocence Project (LAIP)](https://www.innocencela.org/ourwork) is a legal organization focusing on exonerating the wrongly convicted in Central and Southern California. It stands separate from the National [Innocence Project](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/innocence-project-bias/) and is distinct in its approach, combining legal and forensic science expertise. LAIP partners with [Cal State LA’s California Forensic Science Institute](https://www.calstatela.edu/hhs/crim/faculty-katherine-roberts-phd) and School of Criminal Justice and Criminalistics, emphasizing the use of scientific evidence in wrongful conviction cases. Complete details on all funding sources are not publicly disclosed. [Paula Mitchell](https://www.innocencela.org/paulamitchell) is the Director and a Board Member of the Los Angeles Innocence Project (LAIP).
Analysis / Bias
The Los Angeles Innocence Project (LAIP) displays a progressive bias based on its
[mission statement](https://www.innocencela.org/mission), which emphasizes systemic reform, racial justice, and non-carceral solutions, aligning with values typically favored by progressive ideologies.
Their commitment to using scientific methods for exoneration is seen in their collaboration with scientific institutions and in cases like
[Maurice Hastings](https://www.innocencela.org/maurice-hastings), where DNA testing was pivotal. LAIP’s reliance on scientific evidence and its partnerships with academic and forensic institutions emphasize its pro-science approach, lending credibility to its work.
As reported by
[CBS News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/kdka-cbs-pittsburgh/), the organization’s recent involvement in the high-profile [Scott Peterson](https://www.cbsnews.com/losangeles/news/la-innocence-project-takes-up-scott-petersons-murder-case-of-pregnant-wife-laci/) case further illustrates their adherence to their mission. Despite the case’s notoriety, LAIP’s decision to take it on is consistent with its objective of addressing potential wrongful convictions using scientific methods. Their engagement in such a high-profile case indicates a consistent application of their principles, regardless of the case’s public perception or complexity.
This case indicates that LAIP is committed to its mission to challenge convictions where there is a plausible claim of innocence, maintaining its focus on forensic science and legal scrutiny as central to its approach. The organization’s consistent commitment to addressing wrongful convictions and advocating systemic reform in the criminal justice system reinforces its credibility.
Finally, the Los Angeles Innocence Project (LAIP) does not regularly publish articles; therefore, our assessment of their bias and credibility is based on their mission statement, approach to cases, and public statements.
