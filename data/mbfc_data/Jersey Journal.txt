History
Founded in 1867, The Jersey Journal is a newspaper based in Secaucus, New Jersey. The paper and website cover local, national, business, crime, and sports news for the Hudson County area.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Advance Publications owns the Jersey Journal. The company dates to the early 1920s and is owned by the
[Newhouse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Irving_Newhouse,_Sr.) family. Donald Newhouse, together with his brother Samuel “Si” Newhouse Jr. owns Advance Publications. The [New Yorker](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-yorker/), [Vanity Fair](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vanity-fair/), and Reddit are all produced by Advance Publications. Revenue is generated through advertising and subscription sales.
Analysis / Bias
The Jersey Journal reports local news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Sixth-grader arrested after bringing pellet gun to Jersey City school, officials say](https://www.nj.com/hudson/2023/02/sixth-grader-arrested-after-bringing-pellet-gun-to-jersey-city-school-officials-say.html). National/international news is covered via the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Advance publications.
Editorially, The Jersey Journal does not publish opinion content online; however, story selection tends to slightly favor the left, such as this
[Hamilton school principal receives MLK community spirit award](https://www.nj.com/mercer/2023/01/hamilton-school-principal-receives-mlk-community-spirit-award.html). Generally, the news is factual and holds a slight left-leaning bias in story selection.
