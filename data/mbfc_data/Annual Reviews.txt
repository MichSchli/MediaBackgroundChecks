History
Founded in 1932, Annual Reviews is a nonprofit academic publishing company based in San Mateo, California. Annuals Reviews publishes over 50 scientific journals of review covering life, physical, biomedical, and social sciences. According to its
[about page](https://www.annualreviews.org/about/what-we-do) “Annual Reviews is a nonprofit publisher dedicated to synthesizing and integrating knowledge for the progress of science and the benefit of society.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
As a nonprofit organization, Annual Reviews relies on a combination of funding sources, including subscriptions and the
[S20 model](https://www.annualreviews.org/pb-assets/Pricing%20Pages%20assets/Annual_Reviews_Pricing_Catalog_2023_Digital-1660914973360.pdf), which funds open-access publishing.
Analysis / Bias
Annual Reviews is a hybrid publisher combining peer-reviewed research as well as open-access. The focus is on summarizing and synthesizing peer-reviewed research, which is subject to rigorous academic standards. The Annual Reviews website serves as a
[portal](https://www.annualreviews.org/action/showPublications) for the journals they publish. They feature links to all of their journals as well as a [search](https://www.annualreviews.org/action/doSearch?AllField=mrna&ConceptID=) to find topics of research they have published.
In general, Annual Reviews finds and reviews the most important and relevant research and publishes them in its journals. This leads to minimal bias and the likelihood of publishing the most credible research.
Annual Reviews is an open-access/hybrid publisher of scientific journals. There are two types of Open Access Journals, legitimate and predatory, both of which charge the author for publication.
[Predatory journals](https://predatoryjournals.com/about/) often lack peer review and are generally considered not credible. Annual Reviews is considered a legitimate, open-access publisher as they do not charge authors and rely on the s2o method for funding.
