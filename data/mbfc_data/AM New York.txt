History
Founded in 2003, AM New York is a morning-free community daily newspaper that is published in New York City, formerly by
[Newsday](http://mediabiasfactcheck.com/newsday/). The paper is primarily distributed in enclosed newspaper holders (“honor boxes”) located on sidewalks and street corners with high pedestrian traffic and the website. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
AM New York is owned by Schneps Media, which owns dozens of small community newspapers. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, AM New York covers New York City government, sports, transportation, and crime stories. Most stories are original from reporters and journalists with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Woman found dead in trunk of Queens car went missing three months ago: NYPD](https://www.amny.com/news/cops-find-womans-body-in-the-trunk-of-an-abandoned-car-in-queens/). The Associated Press covers more national stories.
Editorially, Op-eds cover both sides, highlighting the left, such as this
[Op-Ed | Union jobs can help New Yorkers thrive during the pandemic](https://www.amny.com/opinion/op-ed-union-jobs-can-help-new-yorkers-thrive-during-the-pandemic/). In general, the information is factual and has a moderate left-leaning editorial bias.
