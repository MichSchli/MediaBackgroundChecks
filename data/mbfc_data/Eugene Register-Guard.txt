History
[Founded in 1867](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Register-Guard) as The Guard, the Eugene Register-Guard is a daily newspaper in the northwestern United States, published in Eugene, Oregon. From 1927 to 2018, it was owned by the Baker family of Eugene, and members of the family served as both editor and publisher for nearly all that time period. The current editor is Michelle Maxwell. Funded by / Ownership
The Register-Guard has been owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media) since 2018, which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Register-Guard covers news, sports, lifestyle, and crime for the greater Eugene, Oregon area. Local news is reported with minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Missing man dies, found Saturday at scene of motorcycle crash](https://www.registerguard.com/story/news/2020/10/25/motorcyclist-killed-saturday-single-vehicle-accident/6033767002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
