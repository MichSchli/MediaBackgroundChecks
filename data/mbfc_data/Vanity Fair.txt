History
[Founded in 1983](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)), Vanity Fair is a magazine of popular culture, fashion, and current affairs published by [Condé Nast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cond%C3%A9_Nast_Publications) in the United States. They currently publish five international editions of the magazine, and the editor is [Radhika Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radhika_Jones). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Vanity Fair is owned by
[Condé Nast Inc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cond%C3%A9_Nast)., which attracts more than 164 million consumers across its 19 brands and media. Revenue is generated through subscription sales and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Vanity Fair primarily covers culture, fashion, and current affairs. They occasionally report on politics using very strong, emotionally loaded words such as this:
[TRUMP LOSES HIS MIND IN 60-TWEET TIRADE AFTER FIREFIGHTERS ENDORSE BIDEN](https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2019/05/trump-60-tweet-tirade-firefighters-biden). This story is properly sourced to well-known media outlets such as the [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/). Editorially, story selection almost always favors the left, and they routinely report [negatively on the right.](https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2019/04/donald-trump-staff-note-taking)
