History
The Stanford Interior Journal is a newspaper published in Stanford, Kentucky. The paper covers local, national, crime, and sports news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Stanford Interior Journal is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Stanford Interior Journal primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[Centre names Spencer VP for enrollment management](https://www.theinteriorjournal.com/2023/01/26/centre-names-spencer-vp-for-enrollment-management/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Stanford Interior Journal labels its opinion section appropriately, with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Stanford Interior Journal is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
