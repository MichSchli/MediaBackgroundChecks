History
Founded in 1911, American Psychoanalytic Association (APsaA) is an association of psychoanalysts in the United States. APsaA serves as a scientific and professional organization focusing on education, research, and membership development. The APsaA also publishes a peer-reviewed scientific journal called the
[Journal of the American Psychoanalytic Association](http://journals.sagepub.com/home/apa).
According to t
[heir vision statement](https://apsa.org/content/apsaa-mission-vision#overlay-context=about-apsaa), “The American Psychoanalytic Association, valuing the development and complexity of the mind and human relationships seeks to advance clinical, scholarly, scientific and social progress.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[The American Association for the Advancement of Science](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-association-for-the-advancement-of-science-aaas/) is the parent organization and is funded through donations and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
The American Psychoanalytic Association (APsaA) promotes its association and events on its website. They also publish news articles related to the field, such as
[Juneteenth and the APsaA Community](https://apsa.org/content/juneteenth-and-apsaa-community). Editorially, they generally promote a left-leaning agenda such as this [APsaA Denounces Court Decision on Conversion Therapy](https://apsa.org/content/apsaa-denounces-court-decision-conversion-therapy). This report is considered pro-science, as Conversion therapy is considered a [dangerous pseudoscience](https://www.nbcnews.com/feature/nbc-out/american-medical-association-backs-nationwide-conversion-therapy-ban-n1088731). In general, they are factual and support the consensus of science with a left-leaning bias.
