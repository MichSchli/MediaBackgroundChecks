History
KSJD – NPR – Cortez is an NPR FM radio station (91.5 FM) based in Cortez, Colorado, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KSJD – NPR – Cortez is owned and broadcast by the Community Radio Project. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KSJD – NPR – Cortez website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Solar development and land use stirs community discussion in Norwood, Colorado](https://www.ksjd.org/2023-10-19/solar-development-and-land-use-stirs-community-discussion-in-norwood-colorado). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[A book delivery initiative will help people in Florida receive banned books](https://www.ksjd.org/2023-10-20/a-book-delivery-initiative-will-help-people-in-florida-receive-banned-books). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
