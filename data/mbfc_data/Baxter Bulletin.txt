Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Baxter Bulletin. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett, becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Baxter Bulletin covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Mountain Home, Arkansas area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Baxter County voters receiving updated ID cards](https://www.baxterbulletin.com/stories/baxter-county-voters-receiving-updated-id-cards,26161). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Winter Haven News Chief does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented such as this that favors the left:
[Omicron makes its mark on the New Year](https://www.baxterbulletin.com/stories/omicron-makes-its-mark-on-the-new-year,25768?). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
