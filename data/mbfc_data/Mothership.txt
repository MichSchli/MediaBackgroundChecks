History
Founded in 2013,
[Mothership](https://mothership.sg/about-us/) is a social news website and [content aggregator](http://www.yushengteo.com/coding/ms-branding/about.html) based in Singapore. The website focuses on delivering entertainment and lifestyle-related news, that caters to millennials. [Read our profile on Media and Government in Singapore](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/singapore-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Mothership does not disclose ownership on the website; however, according to The
[Straits Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-straits-times/), they are [funded](https://www.straitstimes.com/singapore/mda-asks-website-mothershipsg-to-register-under-broadcasting-act) through a “Social enterprise fund called Project Fisher-men.” A notable Mothership contributor is the former foreign minister [George Yeo](https://mothership.sg/author/georgeyeo/), and the chair of Project Fisher-men is [Philip Yeo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_Yeo). Mothership generates revenue through advertising and [“sponsored content](https://mothership.sg/about-us/#sales).
Analysis / Bias
In review, Mothership publishes articles with a mix of serious news such as
[“China to shut rare earths exports to US but risks international isolation”](https://mothership.sg/2019/06/china-rare-earths-restrict/) as well as clickbait and listicles, [“I met a hardcore fan of local celebs IRL to find out how hardcore the community really is.”](https://mothership.sg/2018/09/mediacorp-idol-chaser-singapore/) Mothership relies on virality to promote stories with a progressive leaning on social issues such as [this](https://www.facebook.com/MothershipSG/posts/2433342593372045).
They also publish articles that originate from social media, such as this
[“S’pore FB group urges Muslims who cannot fast to eat in private, commenters disagree](https://mothership.sg/2019/05/muslim-fasting-private-debate/)” and [“Mother in S’pore allegedly denied Manhattan Fish Market Mother’s Day promo because “the father is missing](https://mothership.sg/2019/06/manhattan-fish-market-mother-promotion/).” When it comes to sourcing, they utilize sources such as gossip sites and credible sources such as [Nikkei Asian Review](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nikkei-asian-review/), [BBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/) as well as social media sites.
