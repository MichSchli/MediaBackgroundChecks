History
[Founded in 1880](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Kansas_City_Star), The Kansas City Star is a broadsheet newspaper based in Kansas City, Missouri, in the United States. The paper has been the recipient of eight Pulitzer Prizes. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Kansas City Star is operated by
[The McClatchy Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McClatchy) and is owned by Chatham Assets Management which owns numerous papers across the United States, including the [Fresno Bee](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fresno-bee/). Revenue is derived from advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Kansas City Star reports local news through the use of journalists and National news through the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). There is minimal use of loaded language in news reporting, and all information is properly sourced.
Editorially, the Kansas City Star almost always
[endorses](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) Democratic Presidential candidates.
