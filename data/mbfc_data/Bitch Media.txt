History
Founded in 1996 by Andi Zeisler and Lisa Jervis,
[Bitch Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bitch_Media) is a non-profit multimedia organization based in Portland, Oregon. It is best known for producing Bitch (magazine), a quarterly publication. Bitch Media has grown to encompass several podcasts, a fellowship for up-and-coming writers, and a community lending library. Its editor-in-chief is [Evette Dionne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evette_Dionne). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The website and magazine are owned by Bitch Media, with revenue derived through advertising an online shop and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
Bitch Media has a left-leaning bias in story selection and political affiliation by promoting Feminist causes. All information is sourced accurately and is credible. There is only minimal use of loaded words in headlines and articles. This is a credible source of feminist information.
Update: On 4/12/2022, they announced they will
[cease publishing](https://www.bitchmedia.org/article/were-saying-goodbye) in June 2022 but will remain online as an archive.
