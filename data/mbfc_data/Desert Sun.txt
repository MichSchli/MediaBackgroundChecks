History
[Founded in 1927](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Desert_Sun), The Desert Sun is a local daily newspaper serving Palm Springs and the surrounding Coachella Valley in Southern California. The current editor is Julie Makinen. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Desert Sun is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Desert Sun reports local news with low bias, such as this
[Three desert schools apply with Riverside County to return students to classrooms](https://www.desertsun.com/story/news/education/2020/08/26/three-private-schools-palm-springs-area-ask-county-class-waivers/5638875002/). National and international news comes from the USA Today Network as well as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). An examination of op-eds reveals slightly more favor the right. Generally, the news is reported factually, with just slightly more favoring the right.
