History
The Wire (thewire.in) is an English Indian news and media website established in 2015 and based in New Delhi, India. It provides daily coverage of politics, economy, local news, world news, and more. The Wire India is edited by Siddharth Varadarajan, M K Venu, and Sidharth Bhatia. The Wire is published by the Foundation for Independent Journalism (FIJ), a non-profit Indian company.
[Read our report on how media is influenced by Government in India.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/india-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Wire is
[owned](https://thewire.in/media/how-the-wire-is-funded) by the Foundation for Independent Journalism (FIJ), which is a non-profit that provides funding. They have also received funding from the [Independent and Public Spirited Media Foundation](http://ipsmf.org/). Additional revenue is derived from advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, articles such as
[“India Deporting Rohingya Refugees Is on Same Lines of Islamophobia as Trump’s Executive Orders”](https://thewire.in/168398/rohingya-refugees-india-trump-executive-order/) uses left bias when reporting USA news, however, they use credible sources such as [Center For Migration Studies](http://cmsny.org/trumps-executive-orders-immigration-refugees/) and government organizations such as the [Press Information Bureau Government of India Ministry of Home Affairs](http://pib.nic.in/newsite/PrintRelease.aspx?relid=108152). When reporting Indian national news The Wire is critical of the right-leaning Modi Government such as [Modi to Set Up Committee to Study Simultaneous Polls, Opposition Cold to Idea.](https://thewire.in/politics/pm-to-set-up-panel-to-look-into-issue-of-simultaneous-polls-rajnath-singh) All News published is generally sourced to credible media outlets. In general, The Wire publishes credible, factual news that leans left editorially.
