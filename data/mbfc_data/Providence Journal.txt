History
[Founded in 1829](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Providence_Journal), The Providence Journal is a daily newspaper from Providence, Rhode Island. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Providence Journal is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Providence Journal covers local news through reporters with minimal bias such as this
[ Worcester church lawsuit on hold](https://www.providencejournal.com/news/20200516/worcester-church-lawsuit-on-hold). National and international news is derived from [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The Providence Journal has endorsed
[Democratic Presidential Candidates](https://noahveltman.com/endorsements/) since 2008. A review of op-eds shows balance but slightly favors the left. In general, they report news factually and with a slight left-leaning bias.
