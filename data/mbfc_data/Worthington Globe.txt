History
The Worthington Globe is a newspaper published in Worthington, Minnesota. The website dglobe.com features local and national news, politics, sports, entertainment, business, classifieds, and health.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Worthington Globe is owned by
[Forum Communications Company](https://www.forumcomm.com/brands-overview/), which is based in North Dakota and has additional locations across South Dakota, Minnesota, and Wisconsin. Advertising and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Worthington Globe reports on news, sports, weather, crime, and entertainment for the greater Worthington, MN region. Reporters demonstrate minimal bias when reporting local news, such as this
[District 518 school board votes to demolish West Learning Center](https://www.dglobe.com/news/local/district-518-school-board-votes-to-demolish-west-learning-center). Further, the website publishes national and international news from credible sources such as the [LA Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/). Finally, all information is sourced via first-person accounts or through credible media.
Editorially, The Worthington Globe reasonably represents both sides. However, a review of columnists demonstrates more favor the right. In general, the news is reported factually and with right-leaning editorial bias.
