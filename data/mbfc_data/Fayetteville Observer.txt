History
[Founded in 1816](https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn00059022/), the Fayetteville Observer is published daily in Fayetteville, North Carolina. It is also the oldest newspaper in North Carolina. The current news editor is Lorry Williams and the opinion editor Myron B. Pitts. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Fayetteville Observer is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Fayetteville Observer covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in reporting such as this; Cooper
[ delays decision on N.C. school reopening plans](https://www.fayobserver.com/news/20200701/cooper-delays-decision-on-nc-school-reopening-plans). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual.
Editorially, the Fayetteville Observer publishes a mix of left and right opinions with slightly more favoring the left.
