History
[Founded in 1872](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Republic_(Columbus,_Indiana)), The Republic is an American daily newspaper published in Columbus, Indiana. It covers the city of Columbus and several nearby communities in Bartholomew and Jennings counties. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Republic is owned by
[AIM Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AIM_Media_Indiana), which is an American printer and publisher of daily and weekly newspapers based in Columbus, Indiana. The paper derives revenue from subscriptions and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Republic covers local news via journalists and with minimal bias, such as this
[Cummins employee misses world record by 20 seconds](http://www.therepublic.com/2019/09/30/cummins-employee-misses-world-record-by-20-seconds/). The greater state, national, and international news is covered by the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, they do not publish Op-Eds.
