History
The Greenville Advocate is a newspaper published in Greenville, Alabama. greenvilleadvocate.com covers local, national, crime, and sports news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Greenville Advocate is owned by
[Boone Newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boone_Newspapers), which owns dozens of community newspapers and magazines. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Greenville Advocate primarily covers local news, sports, weather, and entertainment through the use of local journalists. Articles and headlines use minimal loaded emotional language, such as this
[37-year-old man arrested for robbery in McKenzie](https://www.greenvilleadvocate.com/2022/08/10/37-year-old-man-arrested-for-robbery-in-mckenzie/). The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) typically serves national and international news.
Editorially, the Greenville Advocate labels its opinion section appropriately with more op-eds favoring the right. In general, the Greenville Advocate is factual with a right-leaning editorial bias.
