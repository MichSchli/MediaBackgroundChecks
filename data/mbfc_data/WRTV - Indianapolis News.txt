History
Founded in 1949, WRTV – Indianapolis News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated station licensed to Indianapolis, Indiana, United States. wrtv.com is the associated website for the TV station that publishes news, sports, weather, and crime. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WRTV – Indianapolis News is owned by
[The E. W. Scripps Company](https://scripps.com/our-brands/local-media/), an American broadcasting company founded in 1878 by Edward Willis “E. W.” Scripps. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
WRTV – Indianapolis News covers local news, sports, weather, entertainment, and crime for the greater Indianapolis, Indiana, area. Local news is reported with minimal bias, such as this:
[Driver in deadly crash where family raised concerns over investigation charged with reckless homicide](https://www.wrtv.com/news/local-news/crime/driver-in-fatal-crash-that-killed-lyft-passenger-now-charged-after-family-raises-concerns-about-investigation). Like most stories, there is an embedded video from the TV station. The national news is derived from various sources, most coming from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and Scripps National.
Editorially, they do not offer op-eds; however, they cover political news through the Associated Press and local journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden addresses possibility of son facing federal charges](https://www.wrtv.com/news/national/biden-addresses-possibility-of-son-facing-federal-charges). In general, they present low-biased, fact-based reporting.
