History
[Founded in 2002](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackson_Free_Press), The Jackson Free Press (JFP) is a for-profit tabloid community magazine available free of charge at various retail establishments in Jackson, Mississippi. The current editor is Donna Ladd.
Funded by / Ownership
The Jackson Free Press is owned by
[Donna Ladd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donna_Ladd) and [ Todd Stauffer.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Stauffer) They are funded through sponsors and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Jackson Free Press is primarily a lifestyle and entertainment magazine for the Jackson, Mississippi area, covering dining, entertainment, music, sports, and news. For the purpose of this review, we will focus on their news section, which uses minimal to moderate loaded language in headlines such as this:
[Senate Passes $1,000 Teacher Pay Raise, Seeking To Address Teacher Shortage Crisis.](http://www.jacksonfreepress.com/news/2019/feb/19/senate-passes-1000-teacher-pay-raise-seeking-addre/) This story is properly sourced to credible media outlets and information. The JFP also has a [From the Wire Section](http://www.jacksonfreepress.com/news/wire/) that curates news from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) related to Mississippi.
Editorially, the JFP tends to
[lean left](http://www.jacksonfreepress.com/news/opinion/) in story selection and political affiliation. In general, this is a lifestyle tabloid, dedicated to Jackson, Mississippi that has a left-leaning bias in story selection.
Update: they appear to have stopped publishing new content in May 2022.
