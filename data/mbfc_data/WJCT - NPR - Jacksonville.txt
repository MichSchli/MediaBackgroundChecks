History
WJCT – NPR – Jacksonville is an NPR FM radio station (89.9 FM) based in Jacksonville, Florida, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WJCT – NPR – Jacksonville is owned and broadcast by the WJCT inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [On Monday’s show: ICARE’s plan to tackle gun violence](https://news.wjct.org/show/first-coast-connect/2023-12-08/first-coast-connect-gun-violence-lewis-black). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[LGBT Group Turns To Rule Making For Discrimination Protection](https://news.wjct.org/state-news/2018-10-01/lgbt-group-turns-to-rule-making-for-discrimination-protection) Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
