History
Founded in 2008, The Cut is a standalone website that is published by
[New York Magazine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-magazine/). The Cut covers fashion, lifestyle, culture, and politics from a primarily female perspective. According to their [media kit](http://mediakit.nymag.com/the-cut/) “The Cut is the premier destination for women with sharp, stylish minds. No matter the subject, we address our readers’ lives head on, with generous wit, unflinching honesty and undeniable power.” [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Cut is owned by
[Vox Media,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vox_Media) which is a digital publishing network founded by [Jerome Armstrong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerome_Armstrong), Tyler Bleszinski, and [Markos Moulitsas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markos_Moulitsas) and based in Washington, D.C. According to a [Nieman Lab ](http://www.niemanlab.org/2016/01/built-on-passion-how-vox-media-grew-from-its-roots-as-an-oakland-as-blog-into-one-of-the-internets-biggest-publishers/)article, Vox Media had eight editorial brands and a custom advertising division. These are (sports-focused) SB Nation, (tech site) [The Verge](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-verge/), (real estate blog) Curbed, (food and nightlife) Eater, (technology news) Racked, (news hub) [Vox](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vox/) and (technology business) [Recode](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/recode/). However, in 2019 they merged with New York Media adding [New York Magazine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-magazine/), [Vulture](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vulture/) and others. Further, a [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/) [article](https://www.nytimes.com/2015/08/13/business/media/nbcuniversal-invests-200-million-in-vox-media.html) dated 2015 states that NBC Universal, which is owned by Comcast, invested $200 Million in Vox Media. Revenue for The Cut is generated through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Cut publishes news and opinion articles covering fashion, lifestyle, and politics. Non-political articles often use moderately loaded language such as this
[5 Up-and-Coming Designers Changing the Fashion Industry](https://www.thecut.com/2020/10/designers-changing-the-fashion-industry.html). All stories reviewed linked to credible sources such as the New York Times and the [World Health Organization](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/world-health-organization-who/).
Under the section called “Power” they publish articles related to feminism and politics. The articles in this section are fact-based and also rely on moderately loaded language such as this
[It’ll Have to Be Completely Destroyed ](https://www.thecut.com/2020/10/amy-coney-barrett-affordable-care-act-aca.html)
Editorially, most opinions favor the left and often have a negative opinion of President Trump such as this
[Sure, Let Him Talk for a Whole Hour](https://www.thecut.com/2020/10/nbc-schedules-trump-town-hall-to-rival-biden.html). The website also promotes a [feminist perspective](https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/rbg-complicated-legacy.html#_ga=2.121820429.701165123.1602867842-973190618.1602006077). When it comes to science they have [promoted](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-gmo-dangers/) [GMO alarmism](https://www.grubstreet.com/2016/12/fda-okays-gmo-pink-pineapples.html#_ga=2.123009165.701165123.1602867842-973190618.1602006077) and published horoscopes that are considered pseudoscience. In general, the news is reported factually with a moderate left-leaning bias in editorial content.
