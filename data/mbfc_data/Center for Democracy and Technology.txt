History
[Center for Democracy & Technology (CDT)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_for_Democracy_and_Technology) is a Washington, D.C.-based 501(c)(3) non-profit organization whose mission is to promote an open, innovative, and free Internet. CDT utilizes an expertise-based advocacy model and acts as a non-partisan convener to bring all parties with a stake in the internet to the table. They advise government officials, agencies, corporations, and civil society on policy actions that will maintain the open and accessible nature of the internet. CDT often works with legislators on controversial legislation. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)

Analysis / Bias
In review, the Center for Democracy and Technology reports news about free speech and the internet. All entries use minimal loaded words and are well-sourced. The website also advocates for law, technology, and policy that strengthen and protect fundamental privacy rights and protect more robust legal controls on government surveillance by finding practical and innovative solutions to public policy challenges while protecting civil liberties. Both sides value the protection of these rights to some degree, and therefore, we rate them as Least Biased.
