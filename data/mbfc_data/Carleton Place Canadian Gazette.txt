Analysis / Bias
In 2015 and
[2021](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2021/09/19/the-stars-editorial-board-endorses-liberals-for-2021-federal-election.html), the [Toronto Star](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/toronto-star/) (Nordstar), which owns the Carleton Place Canadian Gazette publisher, Metroland Media Group, [endorsed](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2015/10/09/for-justin-trudeau-and-the-liberal-party-editorial.html) the Liberal Party under Justin Trudeau.
In review, the Carleton Place Canadian Gazette uses low biased wording when reporting local news such as this:
[2 charged with impaired driving in Carleton Place and Lanark Highlands](https://www.insideottawavalley.com/news-story/10657082-2-charged-with-impaired-driving-in-carleton-place-and-lanark-highlands/). The national news is derived through the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-canadian-press/), while world news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, a review of op-eds slightly favors the left. In general, they are fact-based and hold a left-leaning editorial bias.
