History
[Founded in 1909](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Herald-Dispatch), The Herald-Dispatch is a daily broadsheet newspaper that serves Huntington, West Virginia, and neighboring communities in southern Ohio and eastern Kentucky. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Herald-Dispatch is owned by
[HD Media Co., LLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HD_Media), which owns the [Charleston Gazette-Mail](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/charleston-gazette-mail/). The paper is funded through an advertising and subscription model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Herald-Dispatch covers local news through the use of reporters and journalists. National news is covered by the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). With local news, there is minimal use of loaded language such as this: [West Virginia population drops for 6th straight year.](http://www.herald-dispatch.com/_recent_news/west-virginia-population-drops-for-th-straight-year/article_d63d7c26-04b4-11e9-af87-c747b06803f9.html) All information is properly sourced from credible media.
Editorially, the Herald-Dispatch tends to
[endorse](http://www.herald-dispatch.com/opinion/endorsement-editorial-richard-ojeda-is-not-afraid-to-fight-for/article_ab9f669f-d565-54c3-88fb-42819cd08145.html) more Democratic candidates. The [opinion pages](http://www.herald-dispatch.com/opinion/) also reflect a reasonable balance of views with a slight advantage to left-leaning views.
