History
[Founded in 2003](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/indexmundi), IndexMundi is a comprehensive data portal with country-level facts and statistics presented through easy-to-use maps and charts. The data mainly relates to economic and financial data. However, they cover other areas such as health.
According to their
[about page](https://www.indexmundi.com/about.html), the website was founded by Miguel Barrientos and Claudia Soria. They state their mission “is to turn raw data from all over the world into useful information for a global audience. We capture statistics that are scattered or otherwise hidden and present them via user-friendly maps, charts, and tables which allow visitors to understand complex information at a glance.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
IndexMundi lacks transparency as they do not openly disclose ownership or how they are funded. Revenue appears to be derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, IndexMundi publishes data from around the world on various topics. Though most of the data is related to financial information, they also cover education, health, and poverty, to name a few. Information is derived from credible sources. For example, the
[Income share held by the highest 20%](https://www.indexmundi.com/facts/indicators/SI.DST.05TH.20) is sourced to the World Bank and the Luxembourg Income Study database.
IndexMundi is currently tracking Covid-19 cases and deaths using the
[European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control](https://www.ecdc.europa.eu/en). In general, a thorough review indicates that the data provided on this website is derived from credible sources and presented with minimal bias.
IndexMundi also features a blog that is low biased and data-driven such as this
[Wholesale Prices of Agricultural Products](https://www.indexmundi.com/blog/index.php/2019/02/21/wholesale-prices-of-agricultural-products/).
