History
KNAU – NPR – Flagstaff is an NPR FM radio station (88.7 FM) based in Flagstaff, Arizona, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KNAU – NPR – Flagstaff is owned and broadcast by Northern Arizona University Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KNAU – NPR – Flagstaff website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Study: Some groundwater demand in Phoenix won’t be met in next 100 years](https://www.knau.org/knau-and-arizona-news/2023-06-01/study-some-groundwater-demand-in-phoenix-wont-be-met-in-next-100-years). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this
[Transgender Killings Spur Calls For Police Reform](https://www.knau.org/2018-09-01/transgender-killings-spur-calls-for-police-reform). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
