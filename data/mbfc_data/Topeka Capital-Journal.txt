History
The Topeka Capital-Journal serves Topeka, Kansas, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. cjonline.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns the Topeka Capital-Journal. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Topeka Capital-Journal covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Topeka, Kansas area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[ Farmers could soon see tax relief for fences destroyed by wildfires](https://www.cjonline.com/story/business/agricultural/2022/01/24/kansas-plans-tax-cut-fencing-materials-destroyed-farmers-ranchers-2021-wildfires/6598389001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Topeka Capital-Journal does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented such as this that favors the left:
[Legislators must move on from voter suppression laws](https://www.cjonline.com/story/opinion/2022/01/14/more-voter-suppression-laws-unnecessary-kansas-legislative-session/6523694001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
