History
The Lebanon Enterprise is a newspaper published in Lebanon, Kentucky. pmg-ky2.com/lebanon/ delivers local news, hometown politics, business news, local events, and sports.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The paper is owned by
[Paxton Media Group,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paxton_Media_Group) which owns several right-leaning newspapers across the USA, such as the [Elkhart Truth](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-elkhart-truth/). Revenue is derived from advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Lebanon Enterprise reports national news by republishing articles from credible newsagents such as the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). They report local news through the use of journalists with minimal bias using neutral headlines such as [School Board hears KSA data presentation, approves first reading of school calendar](https://www.pmg-ky2.com/lebanon/news/school-board-hears-ksa-data-presentation-approves-first-reading-of-school-calendar/article_294fd0a5-a7eb-529d-976d-ebbcbebce54f.html). All information is properly sourced.
Editorially, there is a reasonable balance between left and right-leaning opinions, with slightly more favoring the right. In general, the paper is fact-based and holds a slight right-leaning editorial bias.
