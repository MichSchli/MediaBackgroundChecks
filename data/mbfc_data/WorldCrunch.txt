History
Worldcrunch is a Paris-based English-language news website that curates and translates news from international media sources or partner organizations. It was launched in 2011 and was founded by Jeff Israeli, former bureau chief for
[Time Magazine ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/time/)in Europe, and Irène Toporkoff, former CEO of [Ask.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ask.com) France and Angie Interactive. [Read our profile on France’s Media and Government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/france-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Worldcrunch does not specifically state how they are funded; however, they do have website advertising and a for-pay subscription service.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the
[content includes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worldcrunch) direct translations, articles that are shortened or fitted with additional context, and in-house productions summarizing current world affairs. Worldcrunch uses moderately loaded words in their headlines and stories such as this: [In Southern Italy, A City’s Perfect Storm Of Populism](https://www.worldcrunch.com/world-affairs/in-southern-italy-a-city39s-perfect-storm-of-populism). Most stories are sourced through direct translations from credible foreign-language news sources. A [factual search](https://factualsearch.news/#gsc.tab=0&gsc.q=worldcrunch&gsc.sort=&gsc.ref=fact_checking) reveals that Worldcrunch has not failed a fact check.
