History
Founded in 1957, WSOC – Charlotte News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Charlotte, North Carolina, United States. The associated website wsoctv.com covers news, sports, crime, and weather. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Cox Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cox_Enterprises) owns WSOC – Charlotte News through their subsidiary Cox Media Group. They are a company that owns numerous newspapers, television stations, and radio stations, such as the [Atlanta Journal-Constitution](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/atlanta-journal-constitution/). Advertising and sponsored content generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WSOC – Charlotte News covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle for the greater Charlotte, North Carolina area. Local news is covered with minimally loaded language, such as this
[Police investigating shooting that killed one person in Monroe](https://www.wsoctv.com/news/local/police-investigating-shooting-that-killed-one-person-monroe/6NH33NPPMNAPLG6APYUMJVCFBE/). Many local news stories contain videos from the TV station. The national news is primarily republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, WSOC – Charlotte News does not publish op-eds; however, when covering politics, they present info with minimal bias, such as this
[Mark Meadows removed as North Carolina registered voter](https://www.wsoctv.com/news/local/mark-meadows-removed-north-carolina-registered-voter/X2RQIZLUTZFLXLJEU4HORXC3UI/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
