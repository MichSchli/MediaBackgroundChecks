History
Launched in 1947, KTTV – Fox 11 – Los Angeles is a
[Fox](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-owned-and-operated television station licensed to Los Angles, California, United States, and serving the LA Area. foxla.com is the associated website covering news, sports, crime, and weather for the Los Angeles area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KTTV – Fox 11 – Los Angeles is owned by Fox Television Stations, a subsidiary of Fox Corporation and, in turn, owned by Rupert Murdoch. Fox Corporation owns 30 Fox Television Stations. Revenue is derived through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KTTV – Fox 11 – Los Angeles reports news, sports, crime, and weather for the Los Angeles area. Local news is provided with minimal bias in wording, such as
[Former Dallas Cowboys TE Gavin Escobar dies in rock climbing accident in Riverside County](https://www.foxla.com/news/former-dallas-cowboys-te-gavin-escobar-dies-in-rock-climbing-accidents-in-riverside-county). Most news reports also contain video footage from the TV station. The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Fox properties provide national and international news.
Editorially, they do not offer opinion content, and they cover politics with minimal bias, often citing the Associated Press for national politics. In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
