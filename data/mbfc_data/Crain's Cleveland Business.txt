History
Founded in 1916, Crain’s Cleveland Business is a weekly business magazine serving the Cleveland metro area and the state of Ohio. Crainscleveland.com covers business, finance, Real Estate, and political news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Crain’s Cleveland Business is owned by Crain’s Communications, a 3rd generation family-owned business based in
[Detriot](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/crains-detroit/). Advertising, subscription fees, and businesses’ digital marketing generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Crain’s Cleveland Business reports on business, covering politics, energy, health care, retail, and other sectors. Articles and headlines do not use loaded language such as this
[Ohio positioned well as trade deal enforcement ramps up](https://www.crainscleveland.com/government/ohio-positioned-well-trade-deal-enforcement-ramps). All stories are properly sourced to credible media outlets. National news stories are derived from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Crain’s Cleveland Business publishes op-eds under the Voices category, such as this
[Opinion: Why is the cost of rent going up? Ask the Fed](https://www.crainscleveland.com/opinion/opinion-why-cost-rent-going-ask-fed). While more lean, very slightly left, there is not enough to change their classification of least biased.
