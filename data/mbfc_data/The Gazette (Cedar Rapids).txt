History
[Founded in 1883](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Gazette_(Cedar_Rapids)), The Gazette is a daily broadsheet newspaper published in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. The newspaper is distributed throughout northeastern and east-central Iowa, including the Cedar Rapids and Iowa City metropolitan areas. It was formerly called The Cedar Rapids Gazette. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper is owned by
[The Gazette Company](https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=4385136) and is funded through an advertising and subscription model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The newspaper presents news stories with minimal bias and always sources properly. There is minimal use of loaded language in straight news headlines such as this:
[Allegiant announces nonstop flights from Cedar Rapids to Nashville](https://www.thegazette.com/subject/news/business/allegiant-announces-nonstop-flights-from-cedar-rapids-to-nashville-eastern-iowa-airport-20190212). National and World news is republished from sources such as the [LA Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/la-times-los-angeles-times/) and the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/).
Although the newspaper endorsed Hillary Clinton in 2016, it has
[endorsed Republicans](http://www.thegazette.com/subject/opinion/staff-editorial/gazette-presidential-endorsements-1884-2012-20161023) in every election since 1964. Therefore, the editorial bias typically favors the right.
In general, straight news reporting is minimally biased, and editorials tend to favor the right.
