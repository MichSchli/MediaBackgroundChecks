History
The Prince George Citizen is an English-language newspaper in Prince George, British Columbia, Canada. princegeorgecitizen.com is the online domain covering news, sports, business, and lifestyle.
[Read our profile on Canada’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Glacier Media Group](https://www.glaciermedia.ca/brands/media-directory#Community+Media) owns the Prince George Citizen. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
The Prince George Citizen publishes sports, weather, national, world, and local news for the greater Prince George, BC region. Local news is reported factually and with minimal bias, such as this
[City project aims to shift perception of downtown, unhoused population](https://www.princegeorgecitizen.com/local-news/city-project-aims-to-shift-perception-of-downtown-unhoused-population-7324132). The national news is typically republished from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2018/06/15/fact-check-of-trumps-canadian-trade-stats/), while world news is from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, Prince George Citizen’s Op-eds favor the left with stories such as this
[Don Wright: Why did Justin Trudeau switch sides in the ‘class struggle?’](https://www.princegeorgecitizen.com/opinion/don-wright-why-did-justin-trudeau-switch-sides-in-the-class-struggle-7305313). The paper generally offers a moderately liberal perspective and reports news factually.
