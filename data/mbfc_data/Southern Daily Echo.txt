History
The Southern Daily Echo is a newspaper based in Southhampton, UK. The paper mainly focuses on topics such as news and politics, lifestyle and culture, and local events and news related to the Southhampton area.
[Read our profile on UK media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Southern Daily Echo is operated by
[Newsquest Media Group Ltd](https://www.newsquest.co.uk/)., owned by the American mass media holding company [Gannett](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/), the publisher of [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Southern Daily Echo covers local news for the Southhampton area through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Southampton: Teen released on bail after Thornhill stabbing](https://www.dailyecho.co.uk/news/23650541.southampton-teen-released-bail-thornhill-stabbing/). Regarding sourcing, they rely on first-person accounts and large quotes, which is appropriate for a print publication.
National and international news is usually republished through reliable sources such as
[PA Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pa-media-uk-bias/) (national news agency for the UK and Ireland) and [The Sunday Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-times-of-london/).
Editorially, story selection and op-eds are reasonably balanced and reflect both sides. Generally, the Southern Daily Echo is fact-based and presents a minimal bias in reporting.
