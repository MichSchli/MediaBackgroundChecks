History
Launched in 1969,
[The Center for Law and Social Policy (CLASP)](https://www.clasp.org/about/) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Washington, D.C., that focuses on “advancing policy solutions to improve the lives of people with low incomes.” Their about page describes their founding purpose as “promoting racial and economic justice and opportunity from vision to policy to practice.” Bruce Terris, James Moorman, Geoffrey Cowan, and Charles Halpern are the [founders](https://www.clasp.org/50years/#history). Analysis / Bias
The Center for Law and Social Policy (CLASP) republishes articles from sources such as
[Business Insider](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/business-insider/) and Duke Chronicle, [“N.C. Senators Burr and Tillis propose bill to prevent Biden administration from forgiving student loan debt](https://www.clasp.org/press-room/news-clips/n-c-senators-burr-tillis-propose-bill-to-prevent-biden-administration-from-forgiving-student-loan-debt/).” They also publish briefs with emotionally loaded headlines such as [“Trump Administration Immigration Policies Are Harming Children and Families in the Carolinas.”](https://www.clasp.org/publications/report/brief/carolinas-immigration-2020/)
Right-leaning
[Influence Watch](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/influence-watch/) criticizes The Center for Law and Social Policy (CLASP) for “promoting LGBTQ-themed sex education,” with articles such as [“How Queer Visibility Threatens White Power](https://www.clasp.org/blog/how-queer-visibility-threatens-white-power/).”
In general, The Center for Law and Social Policy (CLASP) articles are fact-based but typically favor the left and portray the right negatively.
