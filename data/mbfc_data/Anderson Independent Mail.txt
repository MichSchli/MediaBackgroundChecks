History
[Founded in 1899](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Anderson_Independent-Mail), the Anderson Independent Mail is the local newspaper for Anderson County in South Carolina. The current editor is Stephen S. Mullins. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Anderson Independent-Mail is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Anderson Independent Mail covers news, sports, weather, and crime through local reporters in and around the Anderson County area. Headlines and articles use minimal loaded emotional language such as this:
[Anderson County officials preparing to deal with coronavirus](https://www.independentmail.com/story/news/local/2020/03/13/coronavirus-anderson-county-officials-preparing-deal-virus/5038107002/). National and international news is provided through other newspapers in the USA Today Network and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, the Anderson Independent-Mail has a
[history](http://p2004.org/cands/natendorse5.html) of endorsing Democratic Presidential candidates. A review of their op-ed page demonstrates a diversity of opinions with only a slight lean to the left, such as this: [Opinion: How bill to allow SC to dissolve school boards would ‘trample on’ voters’ rights](https://www.independentmail.com/story/opinion/2020/02/02/opinion-bill-let-sc-dissolve-school-boards-wrong/4608969002/). Generally, news reporting is factual, with reasonably balanced editorials.
