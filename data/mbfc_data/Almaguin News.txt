History
The Almaguin News is a community newspaper published (online) in Almaguin Highlands, Ontario, Canada. Guelphmercury.com provides local news, lifestyle, things to do, and opinion for the greater Almaguin, Ontario region.
Read our profile on the
[Canadian government’s influence on media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/).
Funded by / Ownership
The publisher of Almaguin News is Metroland Media Group Ltd., a subsidiary of Nordstar Capital LP. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In 2015 and
[2021](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2021/09/19/the-stars-editorial-board-endorses-liberals-for-2021-federal-election.html), the [Toronto Star](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/toronto-star/) (Nordstar), which owns the Guelph Mercury Tribune’s publisher, Metroland Media Group, [endorsed](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2015/10/09/for-justin-trudeau-and-the-liberal-party-editorial.html) the Liberal Party under Justin Trudeau.
In review, the Almaguin News uses low biased wording when reporting local news such as this:
[Special weather statement for high winds has ended for North Bay, Almaguin March 31](https://www.northbaynipissing.com/news-story/10597408-special-weather-statement-for-high-winds-has-ended-for-north-bay-almaguin-march-31/). The website does not cover national or world news.
Editorially, a review of op-eds slightly favors the left, such as this
[Eco-friendly fan for wood stoves and a battery-powered vacuum](https://www.northbaynipissing.com/opinion-story/10599084-eco-friendly-fan-for-wood-stoves-and-a-battery-powered-vacuum/). In general, they are fact-based and hold a left-leaning editorial bias.
