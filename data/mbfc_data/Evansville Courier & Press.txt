History
The Evansville Courier & Press serves Evansville, Indiana, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. courierpress.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Evansville Courier & Press. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Evansville Courier & Press covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Evansville, Indiana area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Law firm says Deaconess doc viewed women’s personal, medical data without cause](https://www.courierpress.com/story/news/2022/04/16/deaconess-vanderburgh-county-womens-personal-medical-data/7335573001/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Evansville Courier & Press does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Commentary: Step up the search for a ‘universal’ flu vaccine](https://www.courierpress.com/story/opinion/editorials/2018/01/23/commentary-step-up-search-universal-flu-vaccine/1057303001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
