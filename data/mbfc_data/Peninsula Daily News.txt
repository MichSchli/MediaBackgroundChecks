History
[Founded in 1916](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsula_Daily_News), The Peninsula Daily News is a daily newspaper printed Sundays through Fridays, covering the northern Olympic Peninsula in the state of Washington, United States. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Peninsula Daily News is owned by Sound Publishing Inc. (
[Black Press Ltd.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Press)), which is a Canadian publisher that publishes papers in Hawaii, Ohio, and Washington State. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, local news is covered through reporters and with minimal bias such as this
[Port Angeles homeless shelter lease extended](https://www.peninsuladailynews.com/news/port-angeles-homeless-shelter-lease-extended/). National and world news is republished through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, most op-eds are balanced though more tend to lean slightly right. In general, the news is reported factually and with a slight right-leaning bias.
