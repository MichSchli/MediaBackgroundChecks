History
KCFC – NPR – Boulder is an NPR FM radio station (1490 AM) based in Boulder, Colorado, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KCFC – NPR – Boulder is owned and broadcast by Public Broadcasting of Colorado. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KCFC – NPR – Boulder website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [LGBTQ+ community resource center opening in Colorado Springs](https://www.cpr.org/2023/10/09/lgbtq-community-resource-center-opening-in-colorado-springs/). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[As insects flee warming for higher ground, new Colorado research shows the ones with wings struggle to make the escape](https://www.cpr.org/2023/09/26/flying-insects-migration-challenges-cooler-environments/). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
