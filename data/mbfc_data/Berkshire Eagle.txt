History
[Founded in 1789](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Berkshire_Eagle), The Berkshire Eagle is an American daily newspaper published in Pittsfield, Massachusetts, and covers all of Berkshire County and four New York communities near Pittsfield. It is considered a newspaper of record for Berkshire County, Massachusetts.
In 2022,
[The Berkshire Eagle won 43 awards](https://newsfactsnetwork.com/2022/05/01/the-berkshire-eagle-wins-general-excellence-42-more-awards-for-its-journalism/) — including General Excellence — in the 2021 New England Newspaper & Press Association’s Better Newspaper Competition. Funded by / Ownership
Digital First Media owned the Berkshire Eagle until 2016, when four local private investors purchased it. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Berkshire Eagle covers local news through the use of journalists with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[$11.2M overhaul at YMCA in Pittsfield to begin in spring](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/stories/112m-overhaul-at-ymca-in-pittsfield-to-begin-in-spring,598045). National and world news is derived primarily from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, The Berkshire Eagle has endorsed Democratic Presidential candidates since 1940. The op-ed pages feature diverse commentary with more opinions favoring the left. In general, this paper is factual with a left-leaning editorial bias.
