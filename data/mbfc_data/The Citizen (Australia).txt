History
Founded in 2013,
[The Citizen](https://www.thecitizen.org.au/themes/science) is an online news and analysis website launched by the University of Melbourne’s Centre for Advancing Journalism. According to its [“About page,”](https://www.thecitizen.org.au/about) The Citizen “has several aims. Foremost, it is a teaching tool that showcases the students’ work in the [Master of Journalism](http://caj.unimelb.edu.au/teaching/master-of-journalism) and [Master of International Journalism](https://coursesearch.unimelb.edu.au/undergrad/2189-master-of-international-journalism) programs, giving them real-world experience in working for an industry-standard, independent masthead.” The Editor-In-Chief is Andrew Dodd. The Citizen focuses on topics such as Culture, Health, Money, People, Politics, Science, Society, Podcasts, and more. [Read our profile on Australian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/australia-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Citizen states on its about page that it is funded through a mix of university and grant monies, and public donations and contributions from businessman and philanthropist Paul Bendat.
Analysis / Bias
Under the category “politics,” The Citizen publishes news stories utilizing minimal emotionally loaded headlines such as
[“Malaysian students in Australia fearful someone is watching them”](https://www.thecitizen.org.au/articles/malaysian-students-in-australia-fearful-someone-is-watching-them) and sources to credible media outlets such as the [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [News.com.au](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/news-com-au/), [Amnesty International](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/amnesty-international/), [ABC News Australia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news-australia/) as well as local Australian newspapers such as [The New Daily](https://thenewdaily.com.au/news/national/2017/12/10/chinese-donations-australia/) and [Channel News Asia](https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/mahathir-bersih-criticise-decision-malaysia-election-wednesday-10123112), which we have yet to review.
Under The category society, they publish opinion pieces such as
[“Yes, Prime Minister! How Mr. Turnbull radicalized me”](https://www.thecitizen.org.au/articles/yes-prime-minister-how-mr-turnbull-radicalised-me) and source through quotations and interviews. The Citizen reports national news with a relatively neutral tone, such as [“Eyes on the town hall prize: Who will be Melbourne’s next lord mayor?”](https://www.thecitizen.org.au/articles/eyes-on-the-prize-who-will-be-melbournes-next-lord-mayor) and sources to verifiable media sources such as [The Age (Australia).](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-age-australia/)
