History
KISU – NPR – Pocatello is an NPR FM radio station (91.1 FM) based in Pocatello, Idaho, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KISU – NPR – Pocatello is owned and broadcast by Idaho State University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias

[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [ISU Innovation in Nuclear Medicine, National Security and Energy Production Comes Together at the Idaho Accelerator Center](https://www.kisu.org/podcast/isu-research-innovation-and-creativity-insights/2024-02-07/isu-innovation-in-nuclear-medicine-national-security-and-energy-production-comes-together-at-the-idaho-accelerator-center). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[Food Insecurity and Student Athletes](https://www.kisu.org/podcast/bengal-health-chat/2024-02-09/food-insecurity-and-student-athletes). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
