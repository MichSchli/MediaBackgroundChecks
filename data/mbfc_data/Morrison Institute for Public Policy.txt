History
The Morrison Institute for Public Policy is a research organization based at Arizona State University. According to their
[about page](https://morrisoninstitute.asu.edu/about), “Morrison Institute produces research relevant to the public needs of today and tomorrow and stays on the leading edge of research principles and methods.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Morrison Institute for Public Policy is a part of Arizona State University. Funding comes from the University.
Analysis / Bias
Morrison Institute for Public Policy conducts political research and polling on issues affecting Arizona via various mediums. The website publishes research and reports that slightly align with a liberal perspective, such as this
[Untangling Housing Affordability & Groundwater Regulation](https://morrisoninstitute.asu.edu/publication/untangling-housing-affordability-groundwater-regulation). All research is well-sourced. [FiveThirtyEight](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fivethirtyeight/), an expert on measuring and rating pollster performance, has [evaluated 8 polls](https://projects.fivethirtyeight.com/pollster-ratings/) by the Morrison Institute for Public Policy, earning 1.7 stars for accuracy, indicating they are Mostly Factual by MBFC’s criteria. They also conclude that their polling slightly favors the Left with a score of +0.5, which equates to Least Biased overall in polling bias. In general, the Morrison Institute for Public Policy is considered reasonably accurate and demonstrates minimal bias in polling.
