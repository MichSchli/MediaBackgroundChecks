History
[Founded in 1829](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stamford_Advocate), the Stamford Advocate is a daily broadsheet newspaper serving Stamford, Connecticut, and the surrounding area. The paper covers news, sports, crime, and lifestyle. The current editor is Tom Mellana. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Stamford Advocate is owned by
[Hearst Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearst_Communications), which owns more than 300 media outlets across the United States, including the [San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Cosmopolitan](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cosmopolitan/). Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Stamford Advocate reports local news through reporters with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Man pleads guilty to stealing van, burglarizing Stamford post office](https://www.stamfordadvocate.com/policereports/article/Man-pleads-guilty-to-stealing-van-burglarizing-15542587.php?src=sthpcp). National news is published from various sources, including the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [Business Insider](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/business-insider/), and the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/).
Editorially, the Stamford Advocate most often endorses Democratic presidential candidates such as
[Barack Obama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspaper_endorsements_in_the_2012_United_States_presidential_election) in 2012 and [Hillary Clinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspaper_endorsements_in_the_2012_United_States_presidential_election) in 2016. A review of editorial pieces demonstrates a reasonable balance of opinions with slightly more favoring the left, such as this [Opinion: COVID successes had nothing to do with luck](https://www.stamfordadvocate.com/opinion/article/Opinion-COVID-successes-had-nothing-to-do-with-15540226.php). The news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
