History
Launched in 1953, WHEC – Rochester News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Rochester, New York, United States, serving the Rochester television market. The associated website WHEC.com publishes news and station information. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WHEC – Rochester News is owned by
[Hubbard Broadcasting, Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubbard_Broadcasting), an American television and radio broadcasting corporation based in St. Paul, Minnesota. Stanley E. Hubbard founded it. Revenue is derived through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WHEC – Rochester News covers news, sports, traffic, weather, and crime for the Rochester, NY area. Articles and headlines rarely use load language such as this:
[Man hit and killed walking on W. Ridge Rd.](https://www.whec.com/rochester-new-york-news/man-hit-and-killed-walking-on-west-ridge-road/6524515/?cat=565) A video accompanies many news stories from the TV station. Staff reporters and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) write national and international news.
Editorially, WHEC – Rochester News does not publish opinion pieces; however, they cover politics with minimal bias, such as this:
[Jill Biden apologizes after remark about Latinos causes stir](https://www.whec.com/politics/jill-biden-apologizes-for-saying-latinos-unique-as-tacos/6523797/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
