History
Founded in 1924, Commonweal Magazine is a progressive American Catholic journal of opinion, edited and managed by lay Catholics, headquartered in
[The Interchurch Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Interchurch_Center) in New York City. It is the oldest independent Roman Catholic journal of opinion in the United States. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Commonweal is published by the nonprofit
[Bainum Family Foundation](https://bainumfdn.org/bainum-family-foundation-name-transition-press-release/), formerly the Commonweal Foundation. Donations and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Commonweal Magazine publishes news and opinion with minimally loaded language and proper sourcing to credible media such as this
[Learning from Las Hermanas](https://www.commonwealmagazine.org/learning-las-hermanas). Although Commonweal is [anti-abortion](https://www.commonwealmagazine.org/abortion) due to Catholic beliefs, they are progressive on other issues such as human rights, [climate change](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), income inequality, etc.
