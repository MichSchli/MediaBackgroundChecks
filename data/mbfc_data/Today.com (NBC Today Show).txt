History
Today.com is the website for NBC’s morning and talk show named Today. The Today Show
[debuted in 1952](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Today_(American_TV_program)) and is the fifth longest-running television program in history. The website covers news, food, health, wellness, and video streams of their TV programming.
Over its history, the Today Show has faced some controversy. For example, in 2012, they were found to have
[edited an audio recording](https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/erik-wemple/post/nbc-to-do-internal-investigation-on-zimmerman-segment/2012/03/31/gIQAc4HhnS_blog.html?hpid=z6) that selectively edited the 9-1-1 call that George Zimmerman made before shooting and killing Martin, which made Zimmerman appear racist. Funded by / Ownership
Today is owned by NBC Universal is owned by
[Comcast](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-comcast-nbc/comcast-completes-nbc-universal-merger-idUSTRE70S2WZ20110129). The Chairman and Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Comcast are [Brian L. Roberts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_L._Roberts), founded by his father, Ralph J. Roberts. Today’s revenue is mainly derived from advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Today.com offers original reporting on national news, lifestyle, celebrities, and video segments from the Today show. Articles and headlines typically utilize minimally loaded language such as this
[Coronavirus treatments, and vaccines show hopeful new signs](https://www.today.com/video/coronavirus-treatments-and-vaccines-show-hopeful-new-signs-89568325741).
When reporting on political stories, they often report in a straightforward nonbiased manner, such as this
[Longtime Rep. William Lacy Clay defeated by progressive activist Cori Bush](https://www.today.com/video/longtime-rep-william-lacy-clay-defeated-by-progressive-activist-cori-bush-89567813716). However, when reporting on conservatives and the former Trump Administration, more news stories are negative than positive such as this [After husband dies of coronavirus, woman writes obituary blaming Trump, Texas governor](https://www.today.com/health/wife-blames-trump-texas-governor-after-husband-dies-coronavirus-t188524).
While Today.com does not publish original articles that advocate pseudoscience, they frequently have
[Dr. Oz](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/doctoroz-com/) on as a guest who is a known purveyor of debunked pseudoscience: [Dr. Oz reveals the foods that help you live longer](https://www.today.com/video/dr-oz-reveals-the-foods-that-help-you-live-longer-63524933581). In general, Today reports news mostly factually with a moderate left-leaning editorial bias based on negative story selection when covering the right.
