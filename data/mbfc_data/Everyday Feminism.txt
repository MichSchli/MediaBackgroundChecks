History
[Founded in 2012](https://whois.domaintools.com/everydayfeminism.com), Everyday Feminism is a feminist online educational platform that features articles on gender, race, religion, sex, health, and class. It has a stated mission to help dismantle violence, discrimination, and marginalization.
The website was founded by
[Sandra Kim](https://everydayfeminism.com/about-ef/founder/).
Funded by / Ownership
Everyday Feminism does not openly disclose ownership; however, it seems Sandra Kim is the owner based on this
[searchable link](https://everydayfeminism.com/about-ef/founder/). Revenue is derived through advertising and membership fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Everyday Feminism covers news and opinions for those supporting a pro-feminist agenda. Stories include
[How Misogyny Impacts Lesbians](https://everydayfeminism.com/2014/09/how-misogyny-impacts-lesbians/) and publications from sources such as [Alternet](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/alternet/), which has failed fact checks, [10 Insidious Ways White Supremacy Shows Up in Our Everyday Lives](https://everydayfeminism.com/2015/09/white-supremacy-everyday-life/). While this source no longer produces new content, its archive is still available. In general, this is not a reliable source that no longer publishes new information.
