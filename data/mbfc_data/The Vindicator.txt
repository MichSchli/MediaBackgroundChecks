History
[Founded in 1869](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Vindicator_(Ohio_newspaper)), The Vindicator is a daily broadsheet newspaper serving Youngstown, Ohio, and the Mahoning County region, as well as southern Trumbull County and northern Columbiana County. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Vindicator is owned by
[Ogden Newspapers Inc.,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ogden_Newspapers_Inc.) which publishes numerous small-market newspapers around the eastern half of the USA. Ogden has historically donated to Republican candidates. According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), since 1992, they have [donated 93% to Republicans](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000036358&cycle=2018). [Robert Nutting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Nutting) is the current CEO. The paper is funded through both advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Vindicator produces original news covering the Youngstown, Ohio, region. Headlines and articles contain minimal emotional language, such as this:
[Laying of the Roses ceremony honors service members killed in Vietnam](https://www.vindy.com/news/local-news/2019/11/laying-of-the-roses-ceremony-honors-service-members-killed-in-vietnam/). National and international news is republished by the [Associated Press.](https://www.post-journal.com/news/local-news/2019/10/police-investigating-saturday-stabbing/)
Editorially, commentary shows a reasonable balance between right and left without delving into extremes. In general, the news is reported factually with a balance in editorial positions.
