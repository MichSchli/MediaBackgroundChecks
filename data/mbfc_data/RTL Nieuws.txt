History
Established in 1995,
[RTL Nieuws](https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/pagina/4262551/volg-ons) is a commercial news broadcaster in the Netherlands. Its official website is rtlnieuws.nl. RTL Nieuws offers political, economic, and business news programming and collaborates primarily with the Flemish commercial television channel VTM and RTL [Germany](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/germany-media-profile/) for international news coverage. The company’s headquarters is located in Amsterdam, Netherlands. [Read our profile on the Netherlands government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/netherlands-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
RTL News operates as a
[subsidiary](https://www.rtl.nl/over/over-rtl/V8gmwCgAAL1tBaEY) of the [RTL Group](https://company.rtl.com/en/), a multinational media conglomerate based in Luxembourg, with an additional corporate center in Cologne, Germany. RTL Nieuws generates revenue through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
RTL Nieuws utilizes strong emotional language in its headlines, such as, “
[Geert Wilders lashes out at Sinterklaas filmmakers: ‘Woke freaks!”](https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/entertainment/artikel/5339267/geert-wilders-boos-op-makers-paard-van-sinterklaas-woke-freaks) The article reports on Geert Wilders, a Dutch far-right politician and activist. In the article, Wilders criticizes a Dutch children’s show for featuring a multicultural cast; The article portrays Geert Wilders negatively by presenting him as angry.
Another article includes quotes from various political parties”
[From happiness to a bitter pill’: this is how politicians and media respond to election results.”](https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/nieuws/politiek/artikel/5371975/reacties-verkiezingsuitslag-provinciale-staten-bbb-winst-partijen) The article presents their perspectives without any apparent editorial bias. Regarding sourcing, RTL draws information from various sources, including public statements and interviews with politicians, and they use sources such as [de Volkskrant](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/de-volkskrant/), [Algemeen Dagblad](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/algemeen-dagblad/), Trouw, and [Reuters. ](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/)
On the issues such as immigration, RTL publishes articles with strong emotional wording, such as “
[Asylum seeker arrested for rape of girl in Kampen.”](https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/nederland/artikel/433916/asielzoeker-aangehouden-voor-verkrachting-meisje-kampen) The article emphasizes the suspect’s asylum seeker status before mentioning the crime itself. This type of framing could be seen as biased, as it may create a [negative](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/02673231211035873) association between asylum seekers and criminal behavior in readers’ minds. In general, they report fact-based news and hold slightly right-leaning editorial biases.
