History
The Orangeville Banner is a newspaper published in Orangeville, Ontario, Canada. orangeville.com provides online local, national, and world news for the greater Orangeville, Ontario region.

Analysis / Bias
In 2015 and
[2021](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2021/09/19/the-stars-editorial-board-endorses-liberals-for-2021-federal-election.html), the [Toronto Star](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/toronto-star/) (Nordstar), which owns the Orangeville Banner publisher Metroland Media Group, [endorsed](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2015/10/09/for-justin-trudeau-and-the-liberal-party-editorial.html) the Liberal Party under Justin Trudeau.
In review, the Orangeville Banner uses low-biased wording when reporting local news such as this:
[Orangeville Snow-Pitch tourney returns this weekend](https://www.orangeville.com/news-story/10845666-orangeville-snow-pitch-tourney-returns-this-weekend-/). National news is derived from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-canadian-press/), while world news comes from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, a review of op-eds slightly favors the left. In general, they are fact-based and hold a left-leaning editorial bias.
