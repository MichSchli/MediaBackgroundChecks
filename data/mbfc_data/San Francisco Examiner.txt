History
The San Francisco Examiner is a longtime
[daily newspaper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_San_Francisco_Examiner) distributed in and around San Francisco, California. The paper has been published continuously since 1863. The newspaper has switched ownership several times with the most recent in 2011. [Clarity Media Group](https://www.sourcewatch.org/index.php/Clarity_Media_Group) bought the paper in 2004 and quickly changed the paper to reflect a strong right-wing editorial bias. Clarity Media Group also owns the conservative [Washington Examiner.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-examiner/)
Funded by / Ownership
The San Francisco Examiner is owned by Clint Reilly Communications. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The San Francisco Examiner uses minimal loaded words in their headlines, such as
[Challengers emerge to battle for newly open Sunset District supervisorial seat](http://www.sfexaminer.com/challengers-emerge-battle-newly-open-sunset-district-supervisor-seat/). Local news covering the Bay Area is done through journalists, while national news is from the [Tribune News Service](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/tribune-news-service/), which we have rated least biased. All information is properly sourced to credible media outlets.
Editorially, there is a clear left-leaning bias as the paper frequently
[endorses progressive candidates](http://www.sfexaminer.com/examiner-endorsements-june-5-2018/).
