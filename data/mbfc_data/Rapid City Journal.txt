History
Founded in 1879, The Rapid City Journal is a print newspaper serving the greater Rapid City, South Dakota region.
[Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by /Ownership
The paper is owned by
[Lee Enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_Enterprises), which, according to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/) has donated 88% of it money to [Republican politicians](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052990&cycle=2014) since 1990.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Rapid City Journal covers local news with journalists and reporters throughout the Rapid City region. For national news, they use the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). All news is reported with minimal loaded words and bias. The Rapid City Journal always sources its information properly. [Editorially](http://rapidcityjournal.com/opinion/editorial/#tracking-source=menu-nav), there is a balance between right and left-leaning opinions with just a slight edge to the left.
