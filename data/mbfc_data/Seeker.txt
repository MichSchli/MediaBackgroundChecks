History
[Founded in 2015](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seeker_(media_company)), Seeker.com is an American digital media network and content publisher based in San Francisco, California. Seeker produces online video and editorial content for the digital media landscape, with an emphasis on social platforms and YouTube. The primary focus is on science and exploration. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Seeker is owned by
[Group Nine Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_Nine_Media), a subsidiary of Discovery, Inc. Group Nine Media also features [NowThis](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nowthis-news/), [Thrillist](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/thrillist-bias-credibility/), and [The Dodo](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/dodo-bias-rating/). The website generates revenue through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Seeker.com primarily produces news and information videos that pertain to science, such as this
[Nose Swabs vs. Saliva: Which COVID Test Should You Take?](https://www.seeker.com/videos/health/nose-swabs-vs-saliva-which-covid-test-should-you-take) Other stories cover space and technology such as [This Nuclear Battery Could Power Deep Space Missions for Decades](https://www.seeker.com/videos/space-innovation/this-nuclear-battery-could-power-deep-space-missions-for-decades) and earth/conservation news. All information is properly sourced and minimally biased. In general, this is a pro-science website.
