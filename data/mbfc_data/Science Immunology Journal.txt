History
Science Immunology Journal (Science.org) is a peer-reviewed academic journal of the
[American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-association-for-the-advancement-of-science-aaas/) and one of the world’s top academic journals. According to their [about page](https://www.science.org/content/page/science-immunology-mission-and-scope), they are “A trailblazer in online publishing.”
Five other peer-reviewed journals are published under the science.org domain:
[Science Magazine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/science-magazine/), [Science Advances](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/science-magazine/scienceadvances-journal-bias/), [Science Robotics](https://www.science.org/journal/scirobotics), [Science Signaling](https://www.science.org/journal/signaling), [Science Translational Medicine](https://www.science.org/journal/stm). Science.org publishes articles from all publications on the website. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS) owns Science.org and all its magazines. Donations, advertising, and subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the site publishes original news and coverage of the broad field of Immunology. Articles related to scientific studies do not contain loaded languages such as this,
[Lack of CD8+ T cell effector differentiation during priming mediates checkpoint blockade resistance in non–small cell lung cancer](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciimmunol.abi8800). The majority of content on this website and the magazine are peer-reviewed.
Editorially, Science Immunology Journal does not report on politics or produce opinion pieces. Almost all information found on the website and magazine is peer-reviewed scientific studies and discoveries. In general, they are a pro-science source that is very high in factual reporting.
