History
The Greenfield Recorder, based in Greenfield, Massachusetts, is a news website that focuses on arts, entertainment, and culture in the Franklin County region. The website covers a variety of topics, including local news, music, theater, art exhibits, and social issues.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Greenfield Recorder is owned by
[Newspapers of New England, Inc. (NNE)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspapers_of_New_England,_Inc.), which is a privately owned publisher of nine daily and weekly newspapers in New Hampshire and Massachusetts. The paper is funded through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
The Greenfield Recorder publishes local news through staff journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Montague Selectboard endorses cemetery position; Town Meeting approval still needed](https://articles.recorder.com/Montague-Selectboard-endorses-cemetery-position-Town-Meeting-approval-still-needed-53179606). The paper and website do not cover national and international news.
Editorially, a review of articles classified as “opinion” indicates more favor a left-leaning perspective, such as
[Laurel Facey: Are we prepared for climate change?](https://www.recorder.com/ltr-Facey-Are-We-Prepared-for-Climate-Change-53127158) In general, the news is reported factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
