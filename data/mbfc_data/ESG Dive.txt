History
Founded by Sean Griffey, Eli Dickinson, and Ryan Willumson, ESG Dive is an online news platform that focuses on Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance and is operated by Industry Dive, based in Washington, DC. According to its
[about page](https://www.esgdive.com/about/), “ESG Dive provides in-depth journalism and insight into the most impactful news and trends shaping the ESG space. The newsletter and website cover topics such as sustainability, regulation, governance, risk and compliance, finance, and more.” [Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Industry Dive,](https://www.industrydive.com/who-we-are/about) now a subsidiary of [Informa Tech](https://www.informatech.com/brands/)—a London-based British group specializing in publishing, business intelligence, and exhibitions—was acquired by Informa PLC and operates ESG Dive along with [other verticals](https://www.industrydive.com/publications) like FoodDive, BioPharmaDive, CFODive, and ESG Dive. ESG Dive generates revenue from ads and sponsored content. [Read our profile on UK media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-kingdom-media-profile/)
Analysis/Bias
ESG Dive publishes news and information related to Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance. Articles and headlines are minimally biased in word choices, such as this
[$104M for clean energy projects in 31 federal buildings announced by DOE](https://www.esgdive.com/news/doe-104-million-clean-energy-projects-in-federal-buildings/705474/). This story, like all others reviewed, is properly sourced from credible media and information outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they do report on political issues related to the industry, like this
[ExxonMobil sues ESG investors to block climate proposals](https://www.esgdive.com/news/exxonmobil-sues-arjuna-capital-follow-this-block-climate-proposals/705257/). In reviewing dozens, most articles are pro-business in nature. In general, all content is factual and low-biased.
