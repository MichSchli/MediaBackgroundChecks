History
Founded in 2006, Skeptoid is a podcast and website run by
[Brian Dunning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Dunning_(author)) who also contributes to SkepticBlog. Skeptoid is a myth, urban myth, and pseudo-science debunking site. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Skeptoid is owned by Skeptoid Media, Inc through Brian Dunning, which is a 501(c)(3) public charity. Revenue is derived through donations and store that sells branded merchandise.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Skeptoid primarily debunks four types of pseudoscience such as consumer fraud like magic jewelry, urban legends such as hauntings,
[alternative medicine](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-alternative-medicine/) such as [detoxification](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-detoxification/), and conspiracy theories covering [chemtrails](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-chemtrails/), 911, and others. Articles on the website are transcripts/summaries of the [podcasts](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-aroma-therapy/) such as this [Aromatherapy: Sniffing Essential Oils](https://skeptoid.com/episodes/4405). In general, they debunk pseudoscience and conspiracies with evidence and align with our Very-High factual category.
