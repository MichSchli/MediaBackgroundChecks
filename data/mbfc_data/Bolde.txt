History
Launched in 2014,
[Bolde](https://www.bolde.com/about/) is a lifestyle and relationship advice website that provides advice on life and love to single millennial and Gen Z women worldwide. The content ranges from relationship insights to guidance on navigating dating apps. Bolde is headquartered in New York. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership

Analysis / Bias
Bolde features stories on relationships, sex, health, LGBTQIA+ issues, self-improvement, and beauty. Articles and headlines often contain loaded emotional language, evident in titles like
[“I Almost Fell For A Die-Hard Trump Supporter But Dodged A Bullet”](https://www.bolde.com/almost-fell-die-hard-trump-supporter-dodged-bullet/) and [“There Is No “Guy” In My Lesbian Relationship, People.”](https://www.bolde.com/no-guy-lesbian-relationship-people/)
The site also publishes pseudoscience-related content, such as horoscopes, “
[How Toxic Is He Based On His Zodiac Sign?”](https://www.bolde.com/how-toxic-is-he-based-on-his-zodiac-sign/) Regarding sources, they use reputable ones like ohchr.org and [New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/).
Finally, while they do not usually cover politics, they have negative opinions of former President Trump, such as this
[Waxwork Of Donald Trump Removed From Museum Because People Wouldn’t Stop Punching It In The Face](https://www.bolde.com/waxwork-donald-trump-removed-museum-punching-face/). In general, Bolde holds left-biased editorial positions and promotes mild pseudoscience resulting in a mixed factual rating.
