History
[Founded in 1955](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KCRA-TV), KCRA 3 is an NBC-affiliated television station licensed to Sacramento, California, United States. KCRA.com is the associated news and information website for the TV station. KCRA has won several regional [Murrow Awards](https://www.kcra.com/article/kcra-3-wins-5th-consecutive-regional-murrow-award-for-overall-news-excellence/20090259). [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KCRA is owned by Hearst Communications, which owns newspapers, magazines, television channels, and television stations, including the
[San Francisco Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/san-francisco-chronicle/), the [Houston Chronicle](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/houston-chronicle/), and [Esquire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/esquire-magazine/). According to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Hearst donated [70% to Republican candidates](https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/recipients?id=D000034391) and causes in 2020. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KCRA.com reports news, crime, sports, weather, and entertainment for the greater Sacramento area. Local news is covered through reporters with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Stockton man hospitalized after being shot by deputies](https://www.kcra.com/article/stockton-man-hospitalized-shot-by-deputies/33414046). A video accompanies most stories from the TV station. The national news is typically covered by [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/), [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other Hearst affiliates.
Editorially, KCRA does not produce Op-Eds; however, they report on politics with minimal bias as the primary source is the Associated Press. In general, the news is reported factually with minimal editorializing.
