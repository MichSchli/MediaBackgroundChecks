History
The Monadnock Ledger-Transcript, based in Peterborough, New Hampshire, is a news website that focuses on arts, entertainment, and culture in the Monadnock Valley region. The website covers a variety of topics, including local news, music, theater, art exhibits, and social issues.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Monadnock Ledger-Transcript is owned by
[Newspapers of New England, Inc. (NNE)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspapers_of_New_England,_Inc.), which is a privately owned publisher of nine daily and weekly newspapers in New Hampshire and Massachusetts. The paper is funded through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
The Monadnock Ledger-Transcript publishes local news through staff journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Temple audit studies current housing and needs of residents](https://articles.ledgertranscript.com/Temple-continues-talks-on-housing-52796238). The paper and website do not cover national and international news.
Editorially, a review of articles classified as “commentary” indicates more favor a left-leaning perspective, such as
[Viewpoint: State Rep. Molly Howard – Looking back on year one](https://www.ledgertranscript.com/Howard-column-on-first-year-52745067). In general, the news is reported factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
