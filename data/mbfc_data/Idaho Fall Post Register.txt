History
The Idaho Falls Post Register is a daily newspaper published in Idaho Falls, Idaho. The paper covers news and crime reports in the Idaho Falls region.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The newspaper is owned by
[Adams Publishing Group](https://www.poynter.org/business-work/2017/who-are-the-adams-family-and-why-are-they-buying-newspapers-by-the-dozen/), which holds over one hundred papers across the USA. Stephen Adams is the owner and [donates to Republicans](https://web.archive.org/web/20080724023422/http://www.madcowprod.com/billboardenvy.htm), raising 1 million dollars for George W. Bush in 2000. Advertising and subscription fees generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Idaho Falls Post Register covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[Potato growers celebrate ruling allowing full-market access to Mexico](https://www.postregister.com/news/local/potato-growers-celebrate-ruling-allowing-full-market-access-to-mexico/article_63e6bbe6-429a-5249-b4da-24eeffee5271.html). Local news relies on quotes, interviews, and accounts as sourcing. National and world news comes directly from the [Associated Press](https://www.bozemandailychronicle.com/news/city/bozeman-continues-to-foster-midtown-development/article_51464c29-97fa-57b9-9535-05375f0853c8.html).
Editorially, the Idaho Falls Post Register rarely publishes op-eds and generally reports news with minimal opining. Finally, they are fact-based and demonstrate low bias in reporting.
