History
WFSU – NPR – Tallahassee is an NPR FM radio station (88.9 FM) based in Tallahassee, Florida, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WFSU – NPR – Tallahassee is owned and broadcast by Florida State University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WFSU – NPR – Tallahassee website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Florida university system leaders order FAU to restart its presidential search](https://news.wfsu.org/state-news/2023-12-15/florida-university-system-leaders-order-fau-to-restart-its-presidential-search). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[The Climate Change Link To More And Bigger Wildfires](https://news.wfsu.org/npr-news/2021-07-27/the-climate-change-link-to-more-and-bigger-wildfires). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
