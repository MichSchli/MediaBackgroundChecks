History
Founded in 1947, WDTN – Dayton News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Dayton, Ohio. The associated website is wdtn.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WDTN – Dayton News is owned by
[Nexstar Media Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nexstar_Media_Group), which owns around 200 TV stations nationwide. The founder, chairman, president, and CEO of Nexstar is Perry A. Sook. [According](https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cmte=C00567388&cycle=2018) to [Open Secrets](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), Nexstar Media group gave more money to Republicans in the 2018 election cycle (45% to Democrats, 55% to Republicans). However, in 2020 they gave more to Democrats (55% Democrats, 45% to Republicans). Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The WDTN – Dayton News website publishes news for the greater Dayton, Ohio area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias such as this:
[Family sues over Canton high school pizza incident](https://www.wdtn.com/news/ohio/family-sues-over-canton-high-school-pizza-incident/). Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through Nexstar Media Wire, the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Biden firsthand sees tornado toll across Kentucky.](https://www.wdtn.com/news/u-s-world/biden-to-visit-storm-ravaged-kentucky-to-offer-aid-support/) Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoidings using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
