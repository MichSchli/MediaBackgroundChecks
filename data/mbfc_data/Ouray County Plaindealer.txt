History
The Ouray County Plaindealer is a weekly newspaper published in Ouray County, Colorado. ouraynews.com is the associated website covering local and crime news for the area.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Ouray County Plaindealer is owned by Mike Wiggins & Erin McIntyre. Revenue is generated through advertising, subscriptions, and newsstand sales.
Analysis / Bias
The Ouray County Plaindealer focuses on local news and community issues. Local news is covered with minimal bias such as this
[Councilor fills vacant Fire District seat](https://www.ouraynews.com/2024/01/17/councilor-fills-vacant-fire-district-seat/) The newspaper does not cover national news.
A review of the op-ed page demonstrates minimal bias, as the commentary is usually unrelated to politics.
The Ouray County Plaindealer made
[national headlines](https://www.denverpost.com/2024/01/18/ouray-plaindealer-stolen-newspapers-rape-allegations-police-chief-house/?share=e1dilh2riiu2enyoric2) on 01/19/2024 when it reported that all its newspapers were stolen. Coincidently, this theft occurred right after they reported on an alleged rape that occurred at the police chief’s house: [Girl: Rapes occurred at chief’s house](https://www.ouraynews.com/2024/01/17/girl-rapes-occurred-chiefs-house/).
