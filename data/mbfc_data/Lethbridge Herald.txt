Funded by / Ownership
Alta Newspaper Group owns the Lethbridge Herald through the Glacier media group, which is a Vancouver-based publisher of newspapers in Western Canada and Quebec. It owns three small daily newspapers and more than a dozen weeklies. Revenue is derived through advertising, subscription fees, and a paywall.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Lethbridge Herald reports local news through reporters with minimal bias, such as this
[Man charged in standoff with RCMP makes first appearance](https://lethbridgeherald.com/news/lethbridge-news/2020/10/07/man-charged-in-standoff-with-rcmp-makes-first-appearance/). National news comes from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-canadian-press/), and world news from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, more op-eds favor the left, with very few negative articles regarding liberal Prime Minister Justin Trudeau and many supporting
[pro-environmental](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/) changes such as this [Alberta needs more clean-energy projects](https://lethbridgeherald.com/commentary/opinions/2020/08/26/alberta-needs-more-clean-energy-projects/). In general, the news is reported factually and with a left-leaning editorial bias.
