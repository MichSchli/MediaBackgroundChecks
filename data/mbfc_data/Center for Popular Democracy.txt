History
The
[Center for Popular Democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_for_Popular_Democracy) (CPD) is a left-leaning nonprofit advocacy group in the United States. CPD is a federation of groups with a stated goal to “envision and win an innovative pro-worker, pro-immigrant, racial and economic justice agenda.” The organization is allied with teachers’ unions and has published studies criticizing charter schools. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
CPD is a nonprofit based in New York City. Donations derive revenue from notable donors, including the Bauman Foundation, the Ford Foundation, the Democracy Alliance, and the
[Open Society Foundations](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/open-society-foundations/).
Analysis / Bias
The Center for Popular Democracy advocates for progressive policies such as pro-worker, pro-immigrant, racial, and economic justice initiatives. The website features a blog that displays minimal bias in headlines and articles but clearly favors a left perspective, such as this
[Overhauling Our Unemployment Insurance System](https://www.populardemocracy.org/blog/overhauling-our-unemployment-insurance-system).
In general, CPD holds a left-leaning progressive bias through story selection and advocacy. Further, they report news factually.
