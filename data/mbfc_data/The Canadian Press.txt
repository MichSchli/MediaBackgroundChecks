History
The Canadian Press is a
[national news agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Canadian_Press) headquartered in Toronto. It was established in 1917 as a vehicle to permit Canadian newspapers to exchange their news and information. It currently offers a wide variety of text, audio, photographic, video, and graphic content to websites, radio, television, and commercial clients in addition to newspapers and its long-standing ally, The Associated Press (AP), a global news service based in the United States. In review, The Canadian Press is similar to
[Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/) and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) in that they report the news with minimal bias and excellent sourcing.
