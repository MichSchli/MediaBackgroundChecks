History
[Founded in 2013](http://whois.domaintools.com/conservativepost.com), The Conservative Post is a news and opinion website with a far-right/conservative perspective that covers politics, culture, entertainment, faith, and end times. Like most Questionable sources, there isn’t an about page nor any indication of who owns the site or writes the articles. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Although the Conservative Post does not list ownership, a simple Google search reveals the website is held by Conservative Post LLC and registered to
[Robert Saunders](https://www.corporationwiki.com/Texas/Dallas/robert-m-saunders/38863446.aspx) from Dallas, Texas. The website is funded through online advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Conservative Post always utilizes over-the-top sensational headlines such as these:
[Despicable Lowlifes Disrupt Marine’s Funeral but Get a BRUTAL Surprise](https://conservativepost.com/despicable-lowlifes-disrupt-marines-funeral-but-get-a-brutal-surprise/) [Ben Carson Enters Abortion Debate and Drops a Truth Bomb on Democrats](https://conservativepost.com/ben-carson-enters-abortion-debate-and-drops-a-truth-bomb-on-democrats/) [Liberals are LIVID After Rev. Franklin Graham Drops Major Truth Bomb on America](https://conservativepost.com/liberals-are-livid-after-rev-franklin-graham-drops-major-truth-bomb-on-america/)
All stories strongly favor the right and denigrate the left. The Conservative Post disproportionately publishes negative articles about
[Muslims and minorities](http://conservativepost.com/watch-violent-thug-gunned-down-by-quick-draw-store-clerk/) in many cases. Regarding sourcing, the Conservative Post often uses far-right or Questionable sources such as [Western Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/western-journalism/) or [RedState](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/red-state/). The Conservative Post is also listed on factcheck.org’s [fake news list](https://www.factcheck.org/2017/07/websites-post-fake-satirical-stories/).
