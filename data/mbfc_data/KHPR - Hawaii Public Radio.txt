History
WHPR – Hawaii Public Radio is an NPR FM radio station (88.1 FM) based in Honolulu, Hawaii, serving the surrounding area. The website hawaiipubicradio.org is the website for numerous NPR radio stations within Hawaii. These include KIPH, KANO, KIPO, KHPH, KJFH, KIPL, KAHU, KIPM, KKUA.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WHPR – Hawaii Public Radio is owned and broadcast by Hawaii Public Radio, Inc. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WHPR – Hawaii Public Radio website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [What do Oʻahu’s latest power outages mean for grid reliability?](https://www.hawaiipublicradio.org/local-news/2024-01-25/what-do-oahus-latest-power-outages-mean-for-grid-reliability) This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[How Hawaiʻi universities are responding after SCOTUS affirmative action ruling](https://www.hawaiipublicradio.org/local-news/2023-06-30/how-hawaii-universities-are-responding-after-scotus-affirmative-action-ruling). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
