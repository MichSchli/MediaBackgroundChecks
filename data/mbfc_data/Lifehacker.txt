History
Lifehacker is a
[weblog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lifehacker) about life hacks and software launched on January 31, 2005. Gawker Media initially launched the site.
Funded by / Ownership
Lifehacker is owned by
[G/O Media Inc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G/O_Media)., a media company that runs [Gizmodo](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/gizmodo/), [Deadspin](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/deadspin/), [Jezebel](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/jezebel/), [The Onion](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-onion/), [The Root](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-root/), and other sister websites. G/O was formed in early 2019 when Great Hill Partners, a private equity firm, purchased the websites from [Univision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Univision). Advertising generates revenue for Lifehacker.
Analysis / Bias
Lifehacker primarily covers life hacks on topics such as finance, technology, and health. The website features the tagline “Do Everything Better.” Stories and headlines reflect the article’s content with minimal bias, such as this
[Our Best Glass Cleaning Hacks for a Streak-Free Shine.](https://lifehacker.com/our-best-glass-cleaning-hacks-for-a-streak-free-shine-1847732023) Videos accompany many pieces. and stories are properly sourced from credible media outlets.
While Lifehacker does not produce political editorials, they report on politics related to their genre. For example, in this story,
[How an Anti-Mask Screed Got Spun as a ‘Study,”](https://lifehacker.com/how-an-anti-mask-screed-got-spun-as-a-study-1846748781) there is a left-leaning political perspective regarding the Covid-19. In another story, they report negatively on former president Trump [How Are Your Trump-loving Relatives Dealing With Recent Events?](https://lifehacker.com/how-are-your-trump-loving-relatives-dealing-with-recent-1846042012) In general, Lifehacker presents news with a left-leaning bias that is well-sourced and factual.
