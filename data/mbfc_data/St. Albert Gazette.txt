History
The St. Albert Gazette is an English-language newspaper in St. Albert, Alberta, Canada. stalbertgazette.com is the online domain covering news, sports, business, and lifestyle.
[Read our profile on Canada’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Glacier Media Group](https://www.glaciermedia.ca/brands/media-directory#Community+Media) owns the St. Albert Gazette. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
The St. Albert Gazette publishes sports, weather, national, world, and local news for the greater St. Albert, Alberta region. Local news is reported factually and with minimal bias, such as this
[St. Albert Community Band appoints interim conductor](https://www.stalbertgazette.com/local-entertainment/st-albert-community-band-appoints-interim-conductor-7472969). The national news is typically republished from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2018/06/15/fact-check-of-trumps-canadian-trade-stats/), while world news is from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, St. Albert Gazette’s Op-eds favor the left with stories such as this
[Carbon capture to help reduce emissions from hydrogen](https://www.stalbertgazette.com/local-news/carbon-capture-to-help-reduce-emissions-from-hydrogen-5363503). The paper generally offers a moderately liberal perspective and reports news factually.
