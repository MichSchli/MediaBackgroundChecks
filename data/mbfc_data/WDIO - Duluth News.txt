History
Launched in 1966, WDIO – Duluth News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Duluth, Minnesota, United States, serving the Duluth television market. The associated website WDIO.com publishes news and station information. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WDIO – Duluth News is owned by
[Hubbard Broadcasting, Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubbard_Broadcasting), an American television and radio broadcasting corporation based in St. Paul, Minnesota. Stanley E. Hubbard founded it. Revenue is derived through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, WDIO – Duluth News covers news, sports, traffic, weather, and crime for the Duluth, MN area. Articles and headlines rarely use load language such as this:
[Ex-MPD officer convicted of violating Floyd’s civil rights, sentenced to 2.5 years in prison](https://www.wdio.com/neighborhood/thomas-lane-sentenced-violating-george-floyd-civil-rights/6530985/?cat=10335). A video accompanies many news stories from the TV station. Staff reporters and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) write national and international news.
Editorially, WDIO – Duluth News does not publish opinion pieces; however, they cover politics with minimal bias, such as this:
[Top Wisconsin Democrats focus on Sen. Johnson in debate](https://www.wdio.com/politics-news/top-wisconsin-democrats-focus-ron-johnson-debate/6528071/?cat=12095). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
