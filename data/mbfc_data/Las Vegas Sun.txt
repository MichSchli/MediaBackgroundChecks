History
[Founded in 1950](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Sun), the Las Vegas Sun was a daily newspaper published in the afternoons until 2005. In October of 2005, the Las Vegas Sun was included as a section inside the pages of the morning [Las Vegas Review-Journal](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/las-vegas-review-journal/) but continues operating exclusively on its website. The [about page](https://lasvegassun.com/about/) provides details on their history and the names of those associated with the paper. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Las Vegas Sun is owned by the Greenspun Media Group, which is an independent company and is a wholly-owned subsidiary of
[The Greenspun Corporation](https://www.gmgvegas.com/digital). The company owns more than 30 publications. The newspaper is funded through subscriptions and advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Las Vegas Sun covers local news through Sun writers and with minimal bias, such as this:
[Making solar power more accessible to low-income Nevadans](https://lasvegassun.com/news/2019/aug/26/solar-power-more-accessible-low-income-nevadans/). This story is sourced from first-hand accounts, interviews, and quotes. National and international news is covered by the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, the Sun almost
[exclusively endorses Democratic candidates](https://lasvegassun.com/news/2016/oct/22/the-suns-endorsements-for-nov-8-election/) such as Hilary Clinton in 2016 and Barack Obama in 2008 and 2012. They also announced in 2024 that they are [endorsing](https://lasvegassun.com/news/2024/jan/28/biden-deserves-his-partys-renomination-then-reelec/) current Democratic President [Joe Biden](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/joe-biden-democratic-presidential-candidate-bias-profile/). Opinion pieces tend to lean left with advocacy for [clean energy](https://lasvegassun.com/news/2019/aug/25/benefits-of-solar-power-development-cross-party-li/) and [education reform](https://lasvegassun.com/news/2019/aug/25/constitutional-changes-will-spur-higher-ed-reforms/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual, while op-eds tend to moderately favor the left.
