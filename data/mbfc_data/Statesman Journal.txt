History
[Founded in 1851](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statesman_Journal), The Statesman Journal is the major daily newspaper published in Salem, Oregon, United States. The current editor is Cherrill Crosby. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Statesman Journal is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Statesman Journal covers local news through journalists with minimal bias, such as this
[Salem City Council votes to extend emergency declaration for COVID-19](https://www.statesmanjournal.com/story/news/2020/04/28/coronavirus-salem-city-council-extends-emergency-declaration/3039389001/). National and international news is republished through the USA Today Network and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
