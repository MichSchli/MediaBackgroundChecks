History
Launched by
[Dotdash](https://www.wsj.com/articles/about-com-changes-its-name-to-dotdash-1493697840) (formerly About.com) in 2016, [Lifewire](https://www.lifewire.com/about-us-5081224#toc-about-dotdash-meredith) offers tech advice and solutions in a user-friendly manner. The site features an extensive library of educational articles and product insights. Organized into sections like News, How to, and Features, it covers diverse tech topics. Tracing back to its origins, in 2012, IAC, owner of entities like [The Daily Beast](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-beast/) and [Investopedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/investopedia/), [acquired About.com](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iac-about-purchase/iac-acquires-about-com-from-new-york-times-company-idUSBRE87Q00V20120827) from [The New York Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/) Company for $300 million. Subsequently, in 2016, [About.com launched](https://techcrunch.com/2016/10/25/about-com-launches-lifewire-a-tech-information-website/) Lifewire, which is now headquartered in New York City. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Lifewire is part of
[Dotdash Meredith](https://www.iac.com/brands/dotdash); (formerly Dotdash, before acquiring Meredith’s magazine assets) and falls under the broader parent company, [IAC (InterActiveCorp)](http://www.iac.com/about/overview) network. [Barry Diller](https://www.iac.com/leadership/barry-diller), known for his involvement with the Fox network, leads IAC, with [Joey Levin](http://www.iac.com/about/leadership/iac-senior-management/joey-levin) as its CEO. IAC’s portfolio includes brands such as [People](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/people-magazine/), Better Homes & Gardens, [Verywell](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/verywell/), Food & Wine, [Daily Beast](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-beast/), and many more. Lifewire’s revenue is derived from advertising and affiliate links.
Analysis / Bias
The platform refrains from publishing politically-oriented op-eds, aiming to maintain a neutral stance in its tech coverage. However, they address tech topics with political relevance, such as
[Why Cybersecurity Should Be a Priority for the Biden Administration. ](https://www.lifewire.com/why-cybersecurity-should-be-a-priority-for-the-biden-administration-5087811) The article cites various sources, including surveys and expert opinions, maintaining a neutral tone.
Lifewire covers diverse areas in technology, such as
[Instagram Now Lets 3 People Collaborate on Posts](https://www.lifewire.com/instagram-collaborate-with-friends-7642407). This article discusses a new feature on Instagram that allows three people to collaborate on posts. The article explains how the new feature works and its implications for users, citing official sources from [Meta](https://about.fb.com/news/2023/08/music-and-collabs-on-instagram/) and Instagram. It provides factual information without editorializing.
Another example is the article titled
[Why Titanium Is Better for the iPhone 15 Pro](https://www.lifewire.com/iphone-15-pro-titanium-7642337) discusses the properties of titanium and how they enhance the phone’s durability and aesthetics. The article cites other tech sites, such as 9to5Mac, tech ahead, and MacRumors, Japanese sword company [Mini Katana](https://minikatana.com/), [Yahoo News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/yahoo-news/), [Wikipedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wikipedia/), and technical specifications and announcements related to the iPhone 15 Pro. It focuses on the technical aspects and advantages of the material.
In general, Lifewire offers neutral and factual tech reporting across various subjects with minimal bias. This review does not cover technology bias, such as favoring certain brands, and deals only with right-left political bias.
