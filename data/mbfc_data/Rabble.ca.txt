History
[Rabble.ca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rabble.ca) is a non-profit alternative Canadian online magazine founded in 2001. Rabble.ca works in partnership with the Canadian Centre for Policy Alternatives and the Centre for Social Justice. It features podcasts, videos, and a discussion board called Babble. According to their [about page](https://rabble.ca/about/), “rabble.ca was built on the efforts of progressive journalists, writers, artists and activists across the country and has a unique role of reporting on stories from civil society while providing a counterbalance to corporate-owned media.” Rabble provides a [writers guidelines](http://rabble.ca/about/writers-guidelines) and [Journalistic Policy](http://www.rabble.ca/about/journalistic-policy) on the website. [Read our profile on Canadian media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canadaigua-daily-messenger-bias/)
Funded by / Ownership
Rabble.ca is a registered not-for-profit organization. Upon its launch, the website raised $200,000, which included $120,000 from the
[Atkinson Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atkinson_Foundation). The Atkinson Foundation “Provides grants focusing on “early childhood education and development and/or economic justice”. Today the website is funded through “individual and organization donors.”
Analysis / Bias
In review, Rabble is a progressive news and opinion website that occasionally uses moderately loaded language in headlines, and within the articles such as this
[When does plant and animal species loss become a societal crisis?](http://rabble.ca/blogs/bloggers/david-suzuki/2019/04/when-does-plant-and-animal-species-loss-become-societal-crisis) This story is written by [David Suzuki,](https://davidsuzuki.org/) a respected environmental activist with a Ph.D. in Zoology, and is properly sourced to credible outlets such as [Science Direct](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/sciencedirect/) and the [CBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbc-news-canadian-broadcasting/). In fact, all information reviewed on this website is properly sourced to credible media outlets.
Editorially, Rabble presents a progressive left bias with advocacy for the
[Environment](http://rabble.ca/issues/environment), [Feminism](http://rabble.ca/issues/feminism), [Civil Liberties](http://rabble.ca/issues/civil-liberties-watch), [Anti-Racism](http://rabble.ca/issues/anti-racism), and [LGBTIQ](http://rabble.ca/issues/lgbtiq). In general, story selection favors the Left, which should not be surprising, as they freely disclaim they are a progressive source.
