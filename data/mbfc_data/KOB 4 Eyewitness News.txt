History
Launched in 1948, KOB 4 Eyewitness News is an
[NBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/nbc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to Albuquerque, New Mexico, United States, serving the Albuquerque television market. The associated website Kob.com publishes news and station information. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KOB 4 Eyewitness News is owned by
[Hubbard Broadcasting, Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubbard_Broadcasting), an American television and radio broadcasting corporation based in St. Paul, Minnesota. Stanley E. Hubbard founded it. Revenue is derived through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KOB 4 Eyewitness News covers news, sports, traffic, weather, and crime for the Albuquerque, NM area. Articles and headlines rarely use load language such as this:
[4 Investigates: 12-year-old boy dies of fentanyl overdose](https://www.kob.com/new-mexico/4-investigates-12-year-old-boy-dies-of-fentanyl-overdose/). A video accompanies many news stories from the TV station. Staff reporters and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) write national and international news.
Editorially, KOB 4 Eyewitness News does not publish opinion pieces; however, they cover politics with minimal bias, such as this:
[US judge OKs online publication of New Mexico voter records](https://www.kob.com/news/us-and-world-news/us-judge-oks-online-publication-of-new-mexico-voter-records/). In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
