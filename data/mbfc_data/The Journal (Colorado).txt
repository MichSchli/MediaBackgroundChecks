History
The Journal is a daily newspaper based in Cortez, Colorado, serving the Four Corners area of Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona, and Utah. The newspaper covers local news, sports, politics, and events, as well as national and international news.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Journal is owned by
[Ballantine Communications](https://ballantinecommunicationsinc.com/bci-brands/print-online-news), a family-owned media company based in Durango, Colorado. The company also owns several other newspapers, magazines, and websites in the region. The newspaper’s revenue comes from advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In reviewing The Journal, the newspaper primarily reports national news through credible sources such as the
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). The reporting is factual, and the headlines are neutral. Journalists do the paper’s reporting on local news with minimal bias, and sources are properly cited, such as this [Mancos will inventory trees in town as part of Community Forestry Program](https://www.the-journal.com/articles/mancos-will-inventory-trees-in-town-as-part-of-community-forestry-program/).
Editorially, The Journal presents a mix of left and right-leaning opinions with a reasonable balance. The paper has supported Democrats in the past, including former President Barack Obama and
[Colorado Governor Jared Polis](https://www.the-journal.com/articles/our-view-stay-the-course-with-polis/), who is a member of the Democratic Party. However, they also publish conservative viewpoints, such as an opinion piece from Republican Senator [Cory Gardner](https://www.the-journal.com/articles/sen-cory-gardner-says-he-plans-to-confirm-trumps-u-s-supreme-court-nominee/). Overall, the editorial page is not dogmatically ideological and publishes various opinions.
