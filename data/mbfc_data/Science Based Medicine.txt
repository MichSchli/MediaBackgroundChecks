History
[Founded in 2007](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science-Based_Medicine), Science-Based Medicine is a website dedicated to evaluating medical treatments and products of interest to the public in a scientific light and promoting pro-science consensus-based information. [Steve Novella, MD](https://sciencebasedmedicine.org/contact-us/), a clinical neurologist at [Yale Un](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yale_University)i [versity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yale_University), is the founder and current editor. All editors are MDs. [Read our country media profile of the USA.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The New England Skeptical Society owns Science-Based Medicine. Revenue is derived through donations.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Science-Based Medicine primarily debunks false medical claims such as this
[A Poorly Conceived Study Fails to Prove Ayurveda Works](https://sciencebasedmedicine.org/a-poorly-conceived-study-fails-to-prove-ayurveda-works/). This story is very well-sourced. In general, Science-Based Medicine aligns with the consensus of science, which we rate as Pro-Science.
