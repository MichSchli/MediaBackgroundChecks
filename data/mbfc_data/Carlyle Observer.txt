History
Carlyle Observer is an English-language newspaper in Carlyle, Saskatchewan, Canada. sasktoday.ca/south/carlyle-courier is the online domain covering news, sports, business, and lifestyle.
[Read our profile on Canada’s media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Glacier Media Group](https://www.glaciermedia.ca/brands/media-directory#Community+Media) owns the Carlyle Observer. Revenue is derived through advertising and subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Carlyle Observer publishes sports, weather, national, world, and local news for the greater Carlyle, Saskatchewan region. Local news is reported factually and with minimal bias, such as this
[Carlyle Cubs Learning Centre is now open](https://www.sasktoday.ca/south/local-news/carlyle-cubs-learning-centre-is-now-open-6679291). The national news is typically republished from the [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2018/06/15/fact-check-of-trumps-canadian-trade-stats/), while world news is from the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/).
Editorially, Carlyle Observer’s Op-eds favor the left with stories such as this
[Column: Holidays don’t have to hurt the environment](https://www.sasktoday.ca/south/opinion/column-holidays-dont-have-to-hurt-the-environment-6275752). The paper generally offers a moderately liberal perspective and reports news factually.
