History
[Founded in 1884](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Falls_Tribune), The Great Falls Tribune is a daily morning broadsheet newspaper printed in Great Falls, Montana. The Great Falls Tribune won the Pulitzer Prize for explanatory journalism in 2000 for a yearlong series on alcoholism. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Great Falls Tribune is owned by the
[Gannett Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gannett), which owns numerous newspapers around the USA, including their flagship paper [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). The newspaper is funded through a subscription fee and online advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Great Falls Tribune covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in wording such as this:
[Shooting near Walmart leads to kidnapping charges for two men.](https://www.greatfallstribune.com/story/news/2019/03/22/shooting-near-walmart-leads-kidnapping-charges-two-men/3245608002/) The national news is covered via the USA Today Network. All information is properly sourced.
Editorially, a review of the
[opinion page](https://www.greatfallstribune.com/opinion/) reveals more left-leaning pieces but retains balance. Generally, they are fact-based.
