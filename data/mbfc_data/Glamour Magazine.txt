History
[Glamour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glamour_(magazine)) is a women’s magazine published by Condé Nast Publications. Founded in 1939 and first published in April 1939 in the United States, it was originally called Glamour of Hollywood. The Glamour website has a news and culture section in which they publish political news. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Glamour is owned by Conde Naste, an American mass media company founded in 1909 by
[Condé Montrose Naste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cond%C3%A9_Montrose_Nast), based in New York City and owned by [Advance Publications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advance_Publications). They own 18 brands, including Allure, Architectural Digest, [Ars Technica](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/ars-technica/), Backchannel, Bon Appétit, Condé Nast Traveler, Epicurious, Golf Digest, Pitchfork, Self, [Teen Vogue](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/teen-vogue/), [The New Yorker](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-yorker/), [Vanity Fai](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/vanity-fair/)r, Vogue, and [Wired](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/wired-magazine/). Advertising and sponsored content derive revenue.
Analysis / Bias
There is a left-center bias in reporting choices that sometimes utilizes loaded words to convey emotion. Many political news articles are not favorable toward conservatives. For example,
[Don’t Just Celebrate the End of the Trump Era. Savor the Beginning of Something Better](https://www.glamour.com/story/dont-just-celebrate-the-end-of-the-trump-era-savor-the-beginning-of-something-better). While this source is not overtly political, the articles they publish about politics usually align with the Democratic left. In general, it has a left-leaning bias. Furthermore, it is factual.
