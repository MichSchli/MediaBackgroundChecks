History
[Founded in 1865](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Antonio_Express-News), The San Antonio Express-News is a daily newspaper in [San Antonio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Antonio), Texas. The Express-News is the fourth-largest newspaper in the state of Texas, with a daily circulation of nearly 100,000 copies. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The San Antonio Express-News is owned by the
[Hearst Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearst_Corporation) and has offices in Austin, the Rio Grande Valley, and Washington, D.C. Hearst, is an American mass media and business information conglomerate based in New York City, New York. Hearst Corporation owns numerous [Left-Center](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/leftcenter/) and [Least Biased](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center/) newspapers throughout the USA. The newspaper is funded through a subscription and advertising model.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the San Antonio Express-News utilizes local journalists for news coverage in the greater San Antonio area and republishes
[Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) stories for national and international news. All news stories are well-sourced and use minimally loaded language. Editorially, the San Antonio Express-News slightly favors the left with [endorsements](https://ballotpedia.org/San_Antonio_Express-News) of the Democratic Candidate for President in the last two elections and slightly more Democrats on the local level.
