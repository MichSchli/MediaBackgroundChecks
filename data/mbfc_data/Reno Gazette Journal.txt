History
[Founded in 1870](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reno_Gazette-Journal) as the Nevada State Journal, The Reno Gazette-Journal is the main daily broadsheet newspaper for Reno, Nevada. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Reno Gazette-Journal is owned by the
[Gannett Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gannett) and is a part of the [USA TODAY](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) Network. The paper generates revenue through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Reno Gazette-Journal covers local news through journalists with minimal bias such as this:
[BLM denies Burning Man growth to 100,000 people, may implement drug screens in future](https://www.rgj.com/story/life/arts/burning-man/2019/06/14/burning-man-blm-restricts-growth/1453109001/). National and international news is covered by [USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/) and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). Editorially, the Reno Gazette-Journal tends to [endorse Democratic candidates](https://ballotpedia.org/Reno_Gazette-Journal) such as Barack Obama in both 2008 and 2012. A review of their opinion pages also demonstrates a slight left-leaning bias in story selection. In general, they report factual news with a slight left lean, editorially.
