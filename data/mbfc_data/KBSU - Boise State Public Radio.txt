History
KBSU – Boise State Public Radio is an NPR FM radio station (90.3 FM) based in Boise, Idaho, serving the surrounding area. The website boisestatepublicradio.org is the website for numerous NPR radio stations within Idaho. These include KBSX, KBSY, KLCZ, KBSQ, KBSM, KBSK, KBSW, KBSS.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KBSU – Boise State Public Radio is owned and broadcast by Boise State University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KBSU – Boise State Public Radio website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [Bill allowing armed teachers, volunteers in Idaho schools advances](https://www.boisestatepublicradio.org/politics-government/2024-01-24/idaho-lawmakers-armed-teachers-volunteers-school). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[Transgender Troops On Protecting Their Right To Serve](https://www.boisestatepublicradio.org/2021-11-29/transgender-troops-on-protecting-their-right-to-serve). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
