History
The News is a Mexican English-language newspaper from Mexico. The News does not have an about page, but the
[contact page](https://www.thenews.mx/contacto/) indicates they are based in Mexico City and lists a group of sites they are affiliated with: Capital México, Efekto TV, Reporte Indigo, Estadio Deportes.
It appears they have not published a news story since 2019.
[View our country profile on Mexico.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/mexico-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to an
[article](https://www.thenews.mx/living/lebanese-week-slated-for-early-september/) published by The News, their owner is Capital Media which is owned by the Anuar Maccise family. According to an El Pais [article](https://elpais.com/sociedad/2013/07/31/actualidad/1375301944_091721.html), the family also owns Grupo Mac Multimedia and publishes a wide variety of newspapers such as “Capital de México, the sports stadium Estadio, Diario DF, the weekly Cambio, in addition to the English-language newspaper The News” The CEO is Luis Maccise. The News (Mexico) is funded through advertising; however, the ownership information is not transparent.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The News (Mexico) publishes headlines that typically utilize minimally loaded language such as this
[“Attorney: Hunger-striking immigrants forced to hydrate”](https://www.thenews.mx/latest-news/attorney-hunger-striking-immigrants-forced-to-hydrate/). World news is typically republished through wires like the [AP](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/).
Editorially, they do not publish op-eds, and when reporting on politics, they tend not to editorialize content:
[Trump predicts sharp rise in US-UK trade under Boris Johnson](https://www.thenews.mx/business/trump-predicts-sharp-rise-in-us-uk-trade-under-boris-johnson/).
