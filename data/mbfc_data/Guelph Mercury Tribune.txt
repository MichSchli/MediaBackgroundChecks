History
Founded in 1853, the Guelph Mercury Tribune is a community newspaper published (online) in Guelph, Ontario, Canada. Guelphmercury.com provides local news, lifestyle, things to do, and opinion for the greater Guelph, Ontario region.

Funded by / Ownership
The publisher of the Guelph Mercury Tribune is Metroland Media Group Ltd., a subsidiary of Torstar Corporation. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In 2015 and
[2021](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2021/09/19/the-stars-editorial-board-endorses-liberals-for-2021-federal-election.html), the [Toronto Star](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/toronto-star/) (Torstar), which owns the Guelph Mercury Tribune’s publisher, Metroland Media Group, [endorsed](https://www.thestar.com/opinion/editorials/2015/10/09/for-justin-trudeau-and-the-liberal-party-editorial.html) the Liberal Party under Justin Trudeau.
In review, the Guelph Mercury Tribune uses low biased wording when reporting local news such as this:
[Three teens arrested after alleged schoolyard assault in Guelph sends adult to hospital](https://www.guelphmercury.com/news-story/10595636-three-teens-arrested-after-alleged-schoolyard-assault-in-guelph-sends-adult-to-hospital/). The website does not cover national or world news.
Editorially, a review of op-eds indicates that slightly more favor the left such as this
[We must acknowledge pandemic trauma as we chart a course toward ‘normal](https://www.guelphmercury.com/opinion-story/10595543-we-must-acknowledge-pandemic-trauma-as-we-chart-a-course-toward-normal-/).’ In general, they are fact based and hold a left leaning editorial bias.
