The company engages in the collection, publication, and distribution of public opinion polling information. Analysis / Bias
In review, Rasmussen Reports has received
[praise for accuracy](https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/tracking-poll-primer/) in the past. They have also been criticized for having a right-leaning bias. Some of those called Rasmussen Reports right-leaning are [FiveThirtyEight](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fivethirtyeight/), [New Republic](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-republic/), [Time](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/time/), [Center for Public Integrity](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-public-integrity/), [CNN](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cnn/), and the [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/). Further, founder Scott Rasmussen has written a conservative book called [In Search of Self-Governance](https://www.amazon.com/Search-Self-Governance-Scott-W-Rasmussen/dp/1449593542/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1274811590&sr=8-1). [FiveThirtyEight](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fivethirtyeight/), an expert on measuring and rating pollster performance, has [evaluated 772 polls by Rasmussen](https://projects.fivethirtyeight.com/pollster-ratings/rasmussen-reports/), indicating they are accurate 78% of the time or Mostly Factual. They also conclude that their polling favors the right with a score of +1.4, which equates to a Right-Center bias in polling. In general, Rasmussen is a reasonably accurate pollster that slightly favors the right (Republican Candidates).
