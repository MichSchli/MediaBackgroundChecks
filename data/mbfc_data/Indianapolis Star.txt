History
The Indianapolis Star is a morning daily broadsheet newspaper that began publishing on
[June 6, 1903](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Indianapolis_Star). It has won the Pulitzer Prize for Investigative Reporting twice, in 1975 and 1991. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Indianapolis Star is owned by Gannett (Gatehouse Media), which publishes
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/). In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger the companies retained the Gannett name. The newspaper is funded through advertising and paid subscriptions.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Indianapolis Star covers local news through journalists with minimal bias in reporting such as this,
[Indiana coronavirus updates: State reports 7 new deaths, 251 more cases](https://www.indystar.com/story/news/health/2020/03/28/indiana-coronavirus-updates-latest-news-covid-19-pandemic-indianapolis/2926934001/). National and international news comes from other Gatehouse/Gannett publications and the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). In general, news reporting is low-biased and factual.
Editorially, the Indianapolis Star has long been known as a conservative paper; however, since 2012, they have not endorsed Presidential Candidates. A thorough review of editorials and opinion pieces reveals a mostly balanced left-right selection, with a few more opinion pieces favoring the left.
