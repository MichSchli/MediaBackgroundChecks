History
Launched in 1952, KTBC – Fox 7 – Austin is a
[Fox](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news-bias/)-owned-and-operated television station licensed to Austin, Texas, United States. fox7austin.com is the associated website covering news, sports, crime, and weather for the Austin area. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KTBC – Fox 7 – Austin is owned by Fox Television Stations, a subsidiary of Fox Corporation and, in turn, owned by Rupert Murdoch. Fox Corporation owns 30 Fox Television Stations. Revenue is derived through advertising and sponsored content.
Analysis / Bias
In review, KTBC – Fox 7 – Austin reports the Austin area’s news, sports, crime, and weather. Local news is provided with minimal bias in wording, such as
[Firefighter injured, building partially collapsed in Avery Ranch Golf Course fire](https://www.fox7austin.com/news/fire-austin-texas-avery-ranch-golf-course). Most news reports also contain video footage from the TV station. The [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and other Fox properties provide national and international news.
Editorially, they do not offer opinion content and cover politics with minimal bias, often citing the Associated Press for national politics. In general, the news is reported factually and with minimal bias.
