History
Launched in 2018, The Bipartisan Press is a news and opinion website that displays the bias of its articles. According to their about page, part of their
[mission](https://www.thebipartisanpress.com/about-us/) is “publishing different opinions from people who have different views, not having a left-leaning press team or vice versa.” The Bipartisan Press is headquartered in San Jose, California, United States. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Bipartisan Press was founded by
[Julian Connor,](https://www.thebipartisanpress.com/our-staff/) who is also the publisher. Winston Wang is the managing editor and Janet Ybarra senior contributing editor. The Bipartisan Press is funded mainly through advertising revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The Bipartisan Press has a rating system for every article they publish, further they state their bias for each article and also display a graph that shows the author’s political spectrum based on the
[Political Compass](https://www.politicalcompass.org/) quiz. The Bipartisan Press describes their [rating system](https://www.thebipartisanpress.com/bp-rating/) but does not go into detail how they determine the bias of the article.
In review, The Bipartisan Press presents the issue “Fact” and then they publish the opinions of Democrats, Republicans, and Independents on the issue. For example, in this article titled
[“What should be done about Ralph Northam’s yearbook photo?”,](https://www.thebipartisanpress.com/politics/what-should-be-done-about-ralph-northams-yearbook-photo/) you can find Democratic and Republican opinions regarding the issue and “Our verdict” which states the sites overall opinion on the issue. The Bipartisan Press also publishes news/opinion articles and labels them according to their bias. We looked at 50 random articles and calculated that 45 out of 50 or 90% were self-described as leaning left. When it comes to sourcing, The Bipartisan Press sources their information to credible media outlets such as [The Atlantic](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-atlantic/), [NY Times](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/new-york-times/), [Pew Research](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pew-research/), [Reuters](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/reuters/), [cpb.org](https://www.cpb.org/), and [The Washington Post.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/) The Bipartisan Press generally reports news that favors the left; however, they occasionally provide right-leaning opinions that provide some balance.
