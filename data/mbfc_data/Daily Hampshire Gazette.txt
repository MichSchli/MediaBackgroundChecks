History
[Founded in 1786](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daily_Hampshire_Gazette), The Daily Hampshire Gazette is a daily broadsheet newspaper based in Northampton, Massachusetts, and covers all of Hampshire County and the southern towns of Franklin County, Massachusetts. The current editor is Brooke Hauser. [Read our profile on United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The Daily Hampshire Gazette is owned by
[Newspapers of New England, Inc. (NNE)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspapers_of_New_England,_Inc.), which is a privately owned publisher of nine daily and weekly newspapers in New Hampshire and Massachusetts. The paper is funded through advertising and subscription fees.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the Daily Hampshire Gazette publishes local news through staff journalists with minimal bias, such as this:
[Should Northampton, Florence ban smoking downtown?](https://www.gazettenet.com/Smoking-ban-26009508) The national news is covered through wire services such as the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/). Editorially, there is a moderate left bias through the endorsement of Democratic candidates such as [Hillary Clinton in 2016.](https://www.gazettenet.com/Editorial-endorses-Hillary-Clinton-for-president-5667239) A review of multiple editorials and opinion pieces reveals a left-leaning bias in story selection and wording that favors the left. In general, the news is reported factually with a left-leaning editorial bias.
