History
Founded in 1956, KVIA – El Paso News is an
[ABC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/abc-news/)-affiliated television station licensed to El Paso, Texas. The associated website is krdo.com which provides news, sports, weather, politics, and crime reporting. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
The News-Press & Gazette Company (NPG) owns KVIA – El Paso News and the associated website. NPG owns and publishes several TV and Radio stations and newspapers, primarily in the midwest USA. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
The KVIA – El Paso News website publishes news for the greater El Paso, Texas area. Local news features original reporting with minimal bias, such as this:
[Crisis Triage Center has now served more than 600 people in Doña Ana County](https://kvia.com/news/2022/05/24/crisis-triage-center-has-now-served-nearly-600-people-in-dona-ana-county/) Many stories also contain videos from the TV Station. National and international news is covered through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/), [CBS News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/cbs-news/), [CNN Newswire](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/left/cnn-bias/), and other large credible media outlets.
Editorially, they do not produce op-eds; however, they report political news with minimal bias, such as this
[Supreme Court has 33 opinions remaining in one-month sprint to end controversial term](https://kvia.com/news/2022/06/05/supreme-court-has-33-opinions-remaining-in-one-month-sprint-to-end-controversial-term/) Political reporting is reasonably balanced, covering both sides and avoiding using emotional language. In general, they report news factually and with minimal bias.
