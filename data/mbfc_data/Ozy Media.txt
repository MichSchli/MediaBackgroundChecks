History
[OZY Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OZY_Media) is an international online magazine that focuses on news, arts, culture, politics, business, and sports. OZY was launched in September 2013 by former MSNBC news anchor, journalist, and businessman Carlos Watson. Ozy also produces TV shows for notable outlets such as [PBS](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/) and [BBC](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/bbc/), as well as podcasts.
On 10/1/2021, OZY Media announced they were
[shutting down](https://www.nytimes.com/2021/10/01/business/media/ozy-media-carlos-watson.html) after scrutiny over business practices and the loss of investors. As of this update, the website is still up. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Ozy has been funded by Axel Springer as well as GVS Capital. Revenue is derived through advertising.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the website publishes TV episodes, Podcasts, and news. The focus of this report is on their news content. News is reported with moderately strong loaded language such as this
[THE BBC’S KATTY KAY SHUTS DOWN MEN WHO ‘TELL ME TO SHUT UP](https://www.ozy.com/news-and-politics/making-headlines-with-katty-kay/416944/).’ Although they report from a diverse point of view, most stories favor a left progressive perspective. For example, they generally support universal healthcare [WHY 1 MILLION AMERICAN KIDS COULD SOON LOSE ACCESS TO HEALTH INSURANCE](https://www.ozy.com/news-and-politics/why-1-million-american-kids-could-soon-lose-access-to-health-insurance/91063/) and LGBTQ rights [GAY MARRIAGE: UNDER ATTACK?](https://www.ozy.com/news-and-politics/gay-marriage-under-attack/75035/) In general, they report news factually and with a left-leaning editorial position.
