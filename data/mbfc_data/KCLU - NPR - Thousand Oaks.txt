History
KCLU – NPR – Thousand Oaks is an NPR FM radio station (88.3 FM) based in Thousand, California, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
KCLU – NPR – Thousand Oaks is owned and broadcast by California Lutheran University. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the KCLU – NPR – Thousand Oaks website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [A close call: Tour bus catches on fire on Highway 101 in Santa Barbara County](https://www.kclu.org/2023-09-17/a-close-call-tour-bus-catches-on-fire-on-highway-101-in-santa-barbara-county). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as
[Santa Barbara Transgender Day of Visibility](https://www.kclu.org/community-calendar/event/santa-barbara-transgender-day-of-visibility-23-03-2023-17-18-00). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
