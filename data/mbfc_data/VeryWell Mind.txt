History
VeryWell Mind is a medical and health website
[launched by About.com](https://www.forbes.com/sites/parulguliani/2016/04/26/about-com-unveils-new-health-vertical-verywell/#19447dca7599) in 2017. The website provides factual, evidence-based information on mental health and psychology. According to their [about page](https://www.verywellmind.com/about-us-5184564), “We are committed to helping you explore mental health and psychology topics in a way that’s approachable, personal, and stigma-free.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
VeryWell Mind is owned and published by
[Dotdash Meredith](https://www.verywellhealth.com/about-us), formerly About.com. Dotdash Meredithowns the VeryWell network, which consists of the websites [Verywell Health](https://www.verywellhealth.com/), [Verywell Fit](https://www.verywellfit.com/), and [Verywell](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/verywell/). They also own the website [Investopedia](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/investopedia/). Revenue is derived through advertising and affiliate commissions for some of the products they recommend.
Analysis / Bias
In review, VeryWell Mind provides news and information about mental health and psychology. They also publish news with minimal bias, such as this
[New Research Uncovers Why We All See the World So Differently](https://www.verywellmind.com/new-research-explains-why-we-see-the-world-differently-5496927). This article is properly sourced from the Psychological Review, and other credible journals. The website also provides a [Mental Health A-Z](https://www.verywellmind.com/mental-health-a-z-4797402) list linking to information on such topics as Addiction. Gender Identity and Panic Disorder.
Editorially, VeryWell Mind employs editors involved in medicine or the related field and fact-checkers who ensure the articles are accurate and evidence-based. In general, information is factual and presented with limited bias.
