History
The Lafayette Daily Advertiser is a newspaper that serves Lafayette, Louisana, and the surrounding area. The paper covers local, national, and international news, sports, entertainment, lifestyle, and opinion. theadvertiser.com serves as the online domain for the newspaper.
[Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
Gannett, the publisher of
[USA Today](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/usa-today-2/), owns The Lafayette Daily Advertiser. In August 2019, [Gatehouse Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/2019/08/06/gatehouse-gannett-to-merge-for-1-4b-build-newspaper-giant/) purchased and merged with Gannett becoming the largest newspaper chain in the USA. After the merger, the companies retained the Gannett name. Advertising and paid subscriptions generate revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, The Lafayette Daily Advertiser covers news, sports, lifestyle, and entertainment for the greater Lafayette, Louisiana area. They report local news with minimal loaded emotional language
[Lafayette blocked Vermilion River without Army Corps permit for spoil banks removal](https://www.theadvertiser.com/story/news/local/2022/07/18/lafayette-blocked-vermilion-river-hours-likely-violating-law-army-corps-permit-spoil-banks/10057849002/). National and international news comes from other Gannett publications and the [Associated Press.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/)
Editorially, The Lafayette Daily Advertiser does not make Presidential endorsements; however, a review of op-eds shows that both sides are represented, such as this that favors the left:
[Redrawing legislative map based on assumptions is a non-starter for Northwest Louisiana](https://www.theadvertiser.com/story/opinion/2022/03/25/redrawing-legislative-map-based-assumptions-non-starter-northwest-louisiana/7153917001/). After reviewing dozens of op-eds, we found slightly more favor a liberal perspective. Finally, the news is generally reported factually and with a slight left-leaning editorial bias.
