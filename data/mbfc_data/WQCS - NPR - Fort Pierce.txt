History
WQCS – NPR – Fort Pierce is an NPR FM radio station (88.9 FM) based in Fort Pierce, Florida, serving the surrounding area.
[Read our profile on the United States media and government.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
WQCS – NPR – Fort Pierce is owned and broadcast by Indian River State College. Revenue is derived through institutional grants, individual contributions, and fees paid by users of the Public Radio Satellite System. NPR stations receive
[less than 1%](https://gigafact.org/fact-briefs/does-npr-receive-less-1-its-funding-directly-federal-government) of their funding from the federal government.
Analysis / Bias
In review, the WQCS – NPR – Fort Pierce website reports news and radio programming information with a slight liberal bias. News on the website is derived from
[NPR](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/npr/) and often utilizes minimally loaded language, such as this [UNF Poll: Florida Voters Most Concerned About Housing Costs, Economy](https://www.wqcs.org/wqcs-news/2023-11-30/unf-poll-florida-voters-most-concerned-about-housing-costs-economy). This story is fact-based and, like all others reviewed, relies on credible sources of information. The website also publishes content from [American Public Media](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/american-public-media/) and the [Corporation for Public Broadcasting](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pbs-news-hour/).
In general, story selection tends to lean slightly left, as does editorial content, such as this:
[Climate change and extreme heat; Unguarded, an investigation](https://www.wqcs.org/show/the-florida-roundup/2023-07-07/climate-change-extreme-heat-unguarded). Finally, news reporting is fact-based and leans left from an editorial perspective.
