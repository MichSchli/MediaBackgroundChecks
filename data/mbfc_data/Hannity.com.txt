History
[Founded in 1998](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sean_Hannity), Hannity.com is the website for conservative radio and talk show host Sean Hannity. The website features a live stream of his radio show and summarized news articles from other sources.
Sean Hannity is a staunch supporter of former President Donald Trump and spoke to him almost
[daily via telephone](https://www.businessinsider.com/trump-sean-hannity-talk-before-bed-2018-5) during his presidency. Hannity’s relation with Trump has been described as [“shadow” chief-of-staff](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/hannitys-rising-role-in-trumps-world-he-basically-has-a-desk-in-the-place/2018/04/17/e2483018-4260-11e8-8569-26fda6b404c7_story.html). Funded by / Ownership
Hannity.com is owned by Premiere Radio Networks, which iHeartMedia owns. iHeartMedia owns approximately 850 radio stations across the USA. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
In review, Hannity.com publishes summarized conservative news stories and a podcast of Sean Hannity’s radio show. Articles and headlines often contain emotionally loaded wording such as
[WHO’S THE BOSS? Trump ‘Terminates’ US Relationship with World Health Organization](https://hannity.com/media-room/whos-the-boss-trump-terminates-us-relationship-with-world-health-organization/). This news summary leads to the full [Fox News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/fox-news/) article. Other stories are sourced to the [Daily Caller](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/daily-caller/), [Real Clear Politics](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/real-clear-politics/), and questionable sources such as the [Gateway Pundit](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-gateway-pundit/). Story selection always favors the right, particularly former President Trump, and denigrates the left such as this: [FANCY NANCY on SHUTDOWN: ‘This is an Opportunity, Every Crisis Is!’](https://hannity.com/media-room/fancy-nancy-on-shutdown-this-is-an-opportunity-every-crisis-is/).
Sean Hannity promotes pro-Trump propaganda and has advanced many conspiracies such as
[Obama Birtherism](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/11/28/magazine/how-far-will-sean-hannity-go.html), the [Seth Rich murder conspiracy](https://www.iheart.com/podcast/51-the-sean-hannity-show-24392822/episode/morning-minute-seth-rich-517-28758472/), and claiming [Hillary Clinton](https://apnews.com/2c8dbf3a7ed44e40be52fade3cfe2900/video-proves-clinton-suffering-seizures-not-so-i-was-there) has health problems. Other conspiracies espoused are [Ukraine and the Biden’s](https://www.thedailybeast.com/fox-news-internal-document-bashes-john-solomon-joe-digenova-and-rudy-giuliani-for-spreading-disinformation), [Climate change denialism](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/pseudoscience-dictionary-climate-change-denialism/), and [claiming](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/11/us/politics/coronavirus-conservative-media.html) that the Coronavirus may be a “fraud” perpetrated by the “deep state.” In general, Hannity.com reports news with a far-right bias that often is not factually based due to a poor track record with fact-checkers.
