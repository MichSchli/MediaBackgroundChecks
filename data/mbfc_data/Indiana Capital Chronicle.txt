History
The Indiana Capital Chronicle is an online news website from Indiana. Their
[about](https://arkansasadvocate.com/about/) statement reads, “The Indiana Capital Chronicle is an independent, nonprofit news organization dedicated to giving Hoosiers a comprehensive look inside state government, policy, and elections. More importantly, we will show how actions at the state level impact your everyday lives.” [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
According to their about page, the Indiana Capital Chronicle is part of
[States Newsroom](https://statesnewsroom.com/about/), a national 501(c)(3) nonprofit [supported by grants and a coalition of donors and readers](https://statesnewsroom.com/support/). States Newsroom publishes 28 other [publications](https://statesnewsroom.com/newsrooms/). The Indiana Capital Chronicle is transparent as they link to the States Newsroom donors page. Initially, when they launched, they got fiscal sponsorship from the Hopewell Foundation; however, once the IRS approved their application for 501(c)(3) charity certification, they severed their ties with Hopewell Foundation. There has been criticism of their involvement with [the Hopewell Foundation,](https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/news/mysterious-funders-behind-new-arizona-media-outlet-10870282) and they have responded to the criticism [here](https://www.azmirror.com/blog/a-few-friday-thoughts-on-what-the-arizona-mirror-is-and-isnt/).
Analysis / Bias
The Indiana Capital Chronicle publishes original news reporting on politics and policy from Indiana. Coverage focuses on the legislature, the environment, and how national policy affects the people of Indiana. Articles and headlines promote liberal perspectives through moderate, loaded language
[What does Indiana’s transgender athlete ban mean for schools?](https://indianacapitalchronicle.com/2022/12/05/what-does-indianas-transgender-athlete-ban-mean-for-schools/)
They properly source, linking to credible sources such as
[whitehouse.gov](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/whitehouse-gov/), [Washington Post](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/washington-post/), [Politico](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/politico/), [Center for Responsive Politics (Open Secrets)](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/center-for-responsive-politics-open-secrets/), and bls.gov (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics).
Editorially, opinion pieces favor the left, such as this
[Indiana’s horrific public health standing is a choice it’s willing to make](https://indianacapitalchronicle.com/2022/11/30/indianas-horrific-public-health-standing-is-a-choice-its-willing-to-make/). On the issues, the Indiana Capital Chronicle promotes LGBT equality and has concerns about climate change. Finally, they frequently report negatively on Republican politicians, such as this [Republicans in charge of Indiana have been no friend to women](https://indianacapitalchronicle.com/2022/11/22/republicans-in-charge-of-indiana-have-been-no-friend-to-women/). The Indiana Capital Chronicle generally reports news factually; however, story selection and op-eds strongly favor a liberal perspective.
