History
Founded in 1996,
[Jewish Voice for Peace (JVP)](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/about/#mission) is a progressive, anti-Zionist non-profit organization that promotes social justice, human rights, and peace. The group is an influential voice in the Jewish community, particularly among those who hold progressive, [left](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/about/)-leaning views. The organization is recognized for its active engagement in advocacy, often addressing contentious topics such as the Israeli occupation of Palestinian territories while advocating for [Palestinian](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/resource/first-steps/) rights. Jethro Eisenstein serves as the [Board Chair,](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/JVP-Newsletter-2014-Fall.pdf) Cynthia Greenberg as the Vice Chair, and Karen Ackerman as the organization’s Treasurer. JVP is based in Berkeley, California. [Read our profile on the United States government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/united-states-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
JVP is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization primarily funded through individual donations. Additionally, revenue is generated through its online store, which offers products related to Jewish Voice for Peace.
[Charity Navigator](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/charity-navigator/) gives Jewish Voice for Peace (JVP) a [four-star rating,](https://www.charitynavigator.org/ein/900018359) reflecting excellence with an overall score of 95%. This score is based on strong financial health, governance practices, and transparency, with 90% accountability and finance and 10% leadership and adaptability.
Analysis / Bias
Jewish Voice for Peace (JVP) advocates for progressive, left-wing ideologies promoting peace, justice, and human rights. They focus particularly on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict; their campaigns and content reflect this commitment. One of their current campaigns,
[“President Biden, call a ceasefire now,”](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/resource/ceasefire/) aims to mobilize public support for an immediate ceasefire and policy intervention. This campaign is positioned within JVP’s broader advocacy for resolving conflicts through peaceful means and dialogue while respecting the rights and dignity of all parties involved.
The organization is openly critical of Israeli government policies, particularly concerning the occupation of Palestinian territories, and supports movements like
[Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS)](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/category/boycott-divestment-and-sanctions/) against Israel.
The editorial stance of JVP is clearly in support of Palestinian rights and critical of the Israeli government. This perspective is reflected in its campaigns, articles, and educational resources. For instance, the article
[“Is a ceasefire imminent? What you need to know”](https://www.jewishvoiceforpeace.org/2024/02/07/is-a-ceasefire-imminent-what-you-need-to-know/) aligns with advocating for Palestinian rights and critiquing the Israeli government’s actions and policies. The article uses sources like [The Guardian](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-guardian/), [Al Jazeera](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/al-jazeera/), social media, [Jacobin](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/jacobin/), senate.gov, and [AP News](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) to substantiate its viewpoints, reflecting a narrative supported by externally verified information. The use of emotionally loaded language, such as describing funding for Israel’s military as “genocidal,” indicates a strong editorial stance, which can suggest a one-sided perspective by potentially overshadowing a more balanced discussion of the issue.
While JVP presents its views as being in line with Jewish values of justice and peace, its positions are often at odds with more mainstream or conservative Jewish organizations.
Regarding factual accuracy, JVP’s content is generally well-researched and includes references to support its claims. However, given its advocacy nature, the information presented is often framed within its ideological perspective. This framing can influence the selection and presentation of facts to align with its advocacy goals.
In general, Jewish Voice for Peace (JVP) is a notable organization in the landscape of Jewish and pro-Palestinian activism, known for its progressive stance on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. While it provides a critical perspective on these issues, readers must be aware of JVP’s ideological framework, which shapes its content and advocacy.
