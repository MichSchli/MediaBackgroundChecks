History
Eckville Echo is a newspaper published in Eckville, Alberta, Canada. The website eckvilleeacho.com covers news, sports, crime, and business for the greater Eckville, Alberta, area.
[Read our profile on the Canadian government and media.](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/canada-media-profile/)
Funded by / Ownership
[Black Press Media](https://www.blackpress.ca/about-us/) owns Eckville Echo. Black Press, based in British Columbia, Canada, publishes over 170 titles across Canada and the western United States. Advertising generates revenue.
Analysis / Bias
Eckville Echo covers news for Eckville and the surrounding area with minimal bias in wording, such as this
[MP Blaine Calkins: use of Emergency Act was ‘gross overreach of power’](https://www.eckvilleecho.com/local-news/mp-blaine-calkins-use-of-emergency-act-was-gross-overreach-of-power-7301309). National and international news is published through the [Associated Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/associated-press/) and [Canadian Press](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/the-canadian-press/).
Editorially, Eckville Echo does not regularly produce op-eds. However, political news coverage tends to lean left. Generally, news reporting is factual, with a left-leaning bias in story selection.
